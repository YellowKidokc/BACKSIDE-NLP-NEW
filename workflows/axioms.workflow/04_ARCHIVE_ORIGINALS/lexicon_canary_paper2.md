# Theophysics
## An Axiomatic Method for Cross-Domain Structural Concordance

*David Lowe — May 2026*

---

## I. The question

My work began with a question that no single academic domain is equipped to answer: can the structural claims of theology be formalized and tested using the tools of physics and mathematics?

On its surface, this seems irrational. Physics excludes theology by design. Theology excludes mathematical formalism by tradition. No department, no journal, no peer-review board exists at the intersection. The question itself falls into a gap between disciplines — and that gap is not an accident. It is a structural feature of how modern knowledge is organized.

To answer a question that no domain owns, I needed a method that no domain provides. What follows is an introduction to that method, the framework it produced, and the evidence that it works.

---

## II. The method

The method is structural concordance.

Take a question that is unresolved within its own domain. Translate its key terms into their structural counterparts in another domain — not by metaphor, but by functional equivalence. Check whether the second domain's structural requirements resolve the original question. Bring the answer back.

This is not analogy. Analogy says "this reminds me of that." Concordance says "this performs the same structural function as that, and the structural requirements in the second domain are independently established and non-negotiable."

The method has three requirements:

First, the translation must be structural, not semantic. When I say "Covenant maps to Symmetry," I do not mean they sound alike or feel similar. I mean they perform the same function in their respective systems: both are ordered relational constraints that govern what is possible and what is forbidden, and both produce necessary consequences when broken.

Second, the structural requirements in the receiving domain must be independently established. The physics of resonance, bound states, eigenstate assignment, and annealing were not designed to match theology. They were discovered through centuries of experiment and formalization by people who had no interest in theological questions. That independence is what gives the method its force.

Third, every mapping must be falsifiable. If I claim that Faith maps to Resonance, then the structural properties of resonance must hold for faith — and if they don't, the mapping fails. A mapping that cannot fail is not a mapping. It is a metaphor wearing a lab coat.


## III. What the method predicts

This framework does not predict the future. It predicts the structure.

That distinction matters, because the most common question any novel framework faces is: what does it predict? In physics, that usually means: what measurement will we get next? In theology, it usually means: what will happen at the end?

This framework answers a different kind of question. It predicts what you will find when you look at the structural correspondence between domains that appear unrelated. It predicts that when you build the most honest dependency chain you can from Scripture, and the most honest dependency chain you can from physics, the two chains will share structural features that neither domain could have predicted alone — and that the places where they diverge will be at least as informative as the places where they agree.

That is a testable prediction. It has been tested repeatedly. It has held every time.

In the last seven days alone, the method has produced five structural resolutions:

**Resolution 1: The order of grace.** For five hundred years, Reformed, Lutheran, Catholic, and Orthodox traditions have disagreed about the sequence in which God applies salvation. The method translates the four disputed terms — Faith, Union, Justification, Sanctification — into their physics counterparts: Coupling, Bound State Formation, Eigenstate Assignment, and Annealing. Physics requires these in a strict, non-optional sequence. The theological ordering that matches the physics sequence is Calvin's union-centric model. This is not a theological argument for Calvin. It is a structural observation that one ordering is compatible with how systems undergo state transformation and the others are not.

**Resolution 2: The subdivision of grace.** The Greek New Testament uses a single word for grace — charis. The method reveals that charis performs four structurally distinct functions at four different positions in the redemptive dependency chain, each mapping to a different physics concept: the vacuum state (grace as divine disposition before creation), potential energy (grace active in history before fulfillment), the interaction vertex (grace bearing cost at the Cross), and a new ground state (grace released as irreversible victory after the Resurrection). The Bible uses one word because grace is one reality — but like energy in physics, it manifests in structurally distinct forms.

**Resolution 3: The Image-Consciousness crossing.** When the theological dependency chain and the cosmological dependency chain are placed side by side and structural match lines are drawn between them, nearly every pair runs parallel. One line does not. The theological chain places Image — humanity's representational capacity — at position 4, before the Fall, before death, before anything goes wrong. The physics chain places consciousness at position 17, after billions of years of evolution. That single crossing is the structural expression of the Bible's claim about human dignity: that it is given, not emergent.

**Resolution 4: The coherence-decoherence taxonomy.** The method translates the practical question "what produces and maintains spiritual coherence in daily life?" into information theory. Three primitives emerge: reading Scripture (receiving signal), meditating (processing signal), and showing love (transmitting signal). Information theory confirms this is a complete Shannon channel — input, process, output — and thermodynamics confirms that coherence is a non-equilibrium state requiring continuous energy input. Decoherence is not primarily active sin. It is the passive accumulation of entropy when the signal loop stops.

**Resolution 5: The universal four-beat pattern.** The method identifies a compressed structural kernel — Order → Rupture → Consequence → Restoration — that appears independently in theology (Covenant → Fall → Death → Promise), physics (Symmetry → Symmetry Breaking → Entropy → New Stable Structure), biology (health → injury → inflammation → healing), law (contract → breach → damages → remedy), and at least four other domains. Independent confirmation across eight domains by a separate AI system verifies that this pattern is not a theological metaphor projected onto nature but a structural invariant of self-regulating systems.

These are not future predictions. They are structural predictions — and every one of them was confirmed.

It is a ten-variable coherence equation. Each variable maps to both a physical domain and a theological domain through the same structural concordance method described above. Grace maps to curvature. Faith maps to quantum measurement. Truth maps to electromagnetic signal. Love maps to binding force. Each mapping is independently testable and independently falsifiable.

The equation does three things the method alone cannot:

First, it quantifies. The method identifies structural matches. The equation scores them. A system's coherence is not just "high" or "low" — it is the product of ten measurable components, and if any one reaches zero, the product collapses. That is not a metaphor. It is a mathematical property of multiplicative systems.

Second, it conserves. The Lowe Coherence Lagrangian — the variational principle underneath the Master Equation — shows that coherence evolves counter to entropy. This is not a claim that coherence always wins. It is a claim that coherence and entropy are structurally coupled, and that the dynamics of their coupling are formally describable.

Third, it predicts the shape of decline. When the equation is fitted to historical data — specifically, the Moral Decline of America dataset covering 125 years of social coherence indicators — it produces a fit of R² = 0.92 using real proxy measurements. The functional form is tanh, not linear and not exponential. That form predicts accelerating decline near a critical threshold, which the data confirms.

The Master Equation is not the concordance method. It is what the concordance method discovered. The method found the structural matches. The equation formalized them into a testable, quantifiable system.

## V. What would break it

Any framework worth funding must say what would destroy it. Here is what would break this one:

The method fails if a structural mapping that the framework treats as strong is shown to be mere analogy — performing the same surface function but lacking the deep structural constraints that make concordance different from metaphor. If Covenant does not actually behave like Symmetry under rigorous testing — if breaking a covenant does not produce the same downstream differentiation that breaking a symmetry produces — then that mapping is demoted from concordance to analogy, and everything built on it is weakened.

The method fails if the O→ΔO→C→R kernel is found to not recur across domains. If a major self-regulating system is identified that undergoes state transformation without the Order-Rupture-Consequence-Restoration pattern, the claim of universality fails.

The equation fails if historical data does not fit the tanh form. If moral decline follows a linear or exponential trajectory rather than an S-curve with an accelerating phase transition, the equation's functional prediction is wrong.

The equation fails if any domain mapping produces a prediction that contradicts clearly established Scripture. The framework's methodological commitment is theology-first: when mathematics and Scripture appear to conflict, Scripture is trusted and the mathematics is held open for revision. In every case so far — approximately five significant instances — the mathematics has eventually resolved in favor of the theological conclusion, producing a more beautiful and structurally complete result than the original mathematical trajectory would have. But if this pattern breaks — if the math produces a result that genuinely and irreparably contradicts scriptural revelation — the framework faces a crisis that concordance alone cannot resolve.

These are real kill conditions. They are posted publicly. Any critic is welcome to test them.

## VI. The precedent

I am not the first person to use this method.

When Jesus taught, He almost never explained spiritual things using spiritual language alone. He pointed outside — to mustard seeds, to sowers, to salt, to lilies, to fishing nets, to vineyards. He looked at structures His listeners already understood from their daily lives and said: the spiritual reality has the same structure as this thing you can already see.

That is structural concordance. The tools are more precise now — symmetry instead of mustard seeds, phase transitions instead of sowing — but the method is identical. Look at a structure in one domain that is independently understood and trusted. Show that the spiritual reality matches it. Let the structural correspondence do the persuading.

The method is as old as the parables. The application to physics is new.
