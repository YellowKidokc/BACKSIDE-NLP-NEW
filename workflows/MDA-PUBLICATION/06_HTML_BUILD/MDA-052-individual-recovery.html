The AA Pattern

Alcoholics Anonymous has helped millions of people restore personal coherence.

Founded 1935. Still operating. Largest recovery program in history.

Here's what they discovered:

Step 1: Admission of Powerlessness

"We admitted we were powerless over alcohol—that our lives had become unmanageable."

Translation: Self-correction is impossible. I am below threshold. I cannot fix myself.

This isn't weakness. It's accuracy.

A system below threshold cannot generate enough internal coherence to reverse its own decline.

The first step is admitting this.

Step 2: Belief in External Power

"Came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity."

Translation: The β coefficient exists. There is an external source of coherence that can do what I cannot.

Not "might exist." Not "would be nice."

Came to believe.

Step 3: Surrender

"Made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him."

Translation: Activating β requires submission.

Not just believing it exists. Not just hoping it helps.

Turning will and life over.

This is the mechanism. This is what unlocks the grace coefficient.

The variable? Submission to a higher power.

The grace coefficient (β) is real. It's not metaphor. When humans submit to God, actual power flows that enables what was previously impossible.

AA doesn't require you to choose.

"God as we understood Him" allows multiple interpretations.

But the data is clear: submission to something greater than self correlates with recovery.
