Is America Coming Apart?

The Physics Idea That Turns “Moral Decline” Into a Measurable Question

83 percent of Americans say our values are getting worse. The big fight is over why. Some blame new freedoms, others blame old prejudices. They yell past each other because they lack a testable plan.

This series asks a sharper question: Is the nation losing coherence — like a jigsaw puzzle whose pieces still exist but no longer lock together? When the pieces stop locking, nothing big can be built, no matter who is in charge.

What “Cultural Decoherence” Means

In physics, a tiny system stays organized only if outside forces leave it alone. Shake the table and it scatters. That scatter is called “decoherence — like a puzzle that crumbles when the table gets bumped by loud music”. The bump is the “environmental noise — like a drummer practicing next door and vibrating your puzzle board”.

Culture can scatter too. Language, thinking, identity, trust, and money can all stop lining up at the same time. When that happens we get a “cascading system failure — like a row of dominoes where one tilt knocks down every other row in the room”. The point is not that people vanish. It is that the pieces can’t work together.

The Five Places We See the Scatter

  1. Words Shrink
    Google data shows big ideas like “justice” lose their shared meaning. Without clear words, we soon can’t agree on anything complex.

  2. Minds Overload
    SAT scores slide even after adjusting for who takes the test. Phones chop our focus into ten-second bites. Holding a deep thought feels heavier each year.

  3. Souls Drift
    Faith drops. Online life turns identity into a costume you change daily. A moving target can’t steer a life for long.

  4. Institutions Go Silent
    Trust in government, media, schools, and medicine dives under 30 percent. When every news source sounds crooked, even true alarms get ignored.

  5. Dollars Detach
    Debt balloons faster than real work. Prices feel fake to both rich and poor. If money no longer mirrors value, markets turn into guessing games.

When all five wobble together, society can’t plan, can’t build, and can’t heal. That, not simple “decline,” is the crisis.

Definition

What Is Cultural Decoherence?

In physics, decoherence describes how a quantum system loses its internal alignment when exposed to environmental noise. The system doesn't disappear — it fragments into disconnected pieces that can no longer coordinate.

Applied to culture, decoherence means the loss of shared meaning, coordinated behavior, and institutional signal — not through a single cause, but through cascading system failure across multiple domains.

This framework identifies five domains where decoherence can be measured: semantic (language), cognitive (reasoning), spiritual (identity), institutional (trust transmission), and economic (value-reality coupling). When all five decohere simultaneously, society doesn't just decline — it loses the capacity for collective action.
