# The Coherence Score

## Quick Overview

Imagine a giant puzzle. When every piece locks in, the picture is clear. When pieces fall out, the picture shreds fast. This paper says our societies act the same way. We can **measure** how many pieces are still in place, watch when they pop out, and even predict when the whole puzzle could drop.

---

## 1. Can Physics Measure Moral Decay?  

**Bold claim:** Yes, and here’s how.

### Key Idea  
**Coherence** — **Bold term:** **coherence** is like a puzzle whose pieces snap together; the tighter they fit, the clearer the image.  
The opposite tug is **entropy** — **bold term:** **entropy** is like a bedroom that keeps getting messier unless someone tidies up.

Physics already studies how order and mess battle inside stars, ice cubes, and computers. We argue the same math tracks order and mess inside families, schools, economies, and even hearts.

When a system holds together, nothing seems wrong. Then, right near a tipping point, it suddenly cracks. That sudden flip is a **phase transition** — **bold term:** **phase transition** is like water staying liquid until one last degree turns it to ice.

---

## 2. The Four Numbers Behind Every Collapse

1. **Constraint** — **bold term:** **constraint** is like guard-rails on a mountain road keeping cars from plunging.  
2. **Control parameter (P)** — **bold term:** **control parameter** is like the volume knob on a speaker; twist it and everything else responds.  
3. **Critical threshold (P₍c₎)** — **bold term:** **critical threshold** is the red line on a fever thermometer; cross it and you must rush to the doctor.  
4. **Scaling exponent (β)** — **bold term:** **scaling exponent** is like the steepness of a hill; near the top one extra step feels ten times harder.

The lesson from physics goes like this:

• As long as guard-rails (constraints) are strong enough, order holds.  
• Remove them past the red line, and disorder floods in, fast not slow.  
• How fast is set by β, the hill’s steepness.

We hide the exact equation here; picture it as a story about guard-rails, red lines, and downhill speed.

## 3. Nine Worlds, One Story

The rule is **domain-general** — **bold term:** **domain-general** is like a Swiss-army knife that works in kitchens, camps, and classrooms. The same math shows up across nine very different worlds:

| World | Guard-rail (P) | Order Sign (χ) | Messy Sign (entropy) |
|-------|----------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Moral | Divine / Natural law | Social trust | Crime spikes |
| Body  | Daily rhythms | Stable weight | Addiction, obesity |
| Words | Fixed meanings | Clear talk | Polarized noise |
| School | Tough standards | Real skill | Empty credentials |
| Family | Marriage vows | Stable homes | Divorce, breakup |

Wherever guard-rails weaken, the same downhill curve appears.

## 4. The Great Jump: 1968–1973

If the nine worlds fell apart for random reasons, their cracks would appear in random years. They do not.

Between 1968 and 1973, all nine crossed their red lines. The odds of that lining up by chance are less than one in a million.

Average break year: 1970  
Spread: only 3.2 years  
Chance: 0.000001

We call that synchronization a system failure, not nine separate accidents.

---

## 5. How We Checked

1. Data: Public records from 1900–2025—health surveys, crime logs, economics, language databases.  
2. Normalizing: We turned every metric into Z-scores so unlike numbers could share one ruler.  
3. Curve-fitting: We used **bootstrap** — **bold term:** **bootstrap** is like pulling colored marbles from a bag, putting each back, and repeating to estimate the whole mix. A thousand repeats showed how steady the curve is.  
4. We matched every curve to the guard-rail story above. Fit quality averaged R² = 0.88.

## 6. What the Numbers Say

### Family  
Divorce rocketed after California’s 1970 no-fault law. Births outside marriage jumped from 11 % to 40 %.

### Trust in Government  
Public trust slid from 77 % in 1964 to 16 % today.

### Language  
Seventy-four percent of words that name virtues now appear less often in books than in 1960.

### Money  
A 1971 dollar buys 14 cents today—the moment Nixon closed the gold window, our monetary guard-rail.

Average β = 0.41, matching the Ising **universality class** — **bold term:** **universality class** is like grouping soccer and hockey because both award one point per goal even though the games look different.

## 7. Five Big Claims

1. Order can be measured in every domain.  
2. The same math rule fits each curve, and that rule comes from physics, not poetry.  
3. The 1968–1973 window marks a single phase transition across society.  
4. Collapse in one world bleeds into the next through **percolation transition** — **bold term:** **percolation transition** is like coffee dripping through grounds; once enough holes open, the brew gushes everywhere.  
5. Real repair needs guard-rails back, not just patching potholes.

## 8. How to Prove Us Wrong

Science invites hits. Kill any test below and the whole frame crumbles.

| Test | Prediction | Kill switch |
|------|------------|-------------|
| T1 | Nine curves bend in 1968–73 | Bends scatter randomly |
| T2 | Guard-rail story fits R² > 0.85 | Fit drops below 0.70 |
| T3 | Domains cluster statistically | Domains act independent |
| T4 | Guard-rails loosen **before** cracks (checked with **Granger causality** — **bold term:** **Granger causality** is like seeing if flipping the switch predicts the light) | Cracks show up first |
| T5 | Add guard-rails, order climbs | Order ignores new rails |

• **Second Law of Thermodynamics** — **bold term:** **Second Law of Thermodynamics** says ice cream on a hot porch always melts unless cooled; societies melt too when guard-rails vanish.  

• **Decalogue** — **bold term:** **Decalogue** is like ten house rules taped on the fridge; in Scripture they form a minimum guard-rail set. Remove them and the puzzle pieces slip.

## 10. What Happened 1965–1973?

Below are key guard-rails pulled during the cluster:

1965 – Griswold v. Connecticut loosened marriage-sexuality ties.  
1968 – Vietnam unrest shattered trust in authority.  
1970 – No-fault divorce erased permanence from vows.  
1971 – Gold window closed; money unhitched from metal.  
1972 – Eisenstadt v. Baird spread contraception outside marriage.  
1973 – Roe v. Wade finalized the uncoupling.  

All nine domains tipped within this window.

## 11. Limits We Admit

1. Synchronization doesn’t auto-prove the cause chain; more work than **Granger causality** is needed.  
2. Measuring order is tricky; other metrics may sharpen or dull the picture.  
3. The shared β value must be tested on fresh data.

## 12. The Amish Counter-Example

The Amish kept the old guard-rails:

• Divorce ≈ 0 %  
• 85 % youth stay in the community  
• Population doubles every 20 years  

Their coherence score stays high. Same math, different inputs: keep rails, keep order.

## 13. Closing Words

The Coherence Score treats bridges, bodies, and civilizations with one tape measure. The 1968–1973 jump tells us guard-rails matter more than we thought. Restore them and the puzzle can lock again. Refuse, and entropy keeps messing the room.

The math does not take sides. It only counts missing pieces.
