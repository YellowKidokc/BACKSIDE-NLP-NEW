#### Governing Equation

**Original text (unchanged):**

> • **When β = 0:** Coherence declines toward zero.  
> • **When β > δχ:** Coherence stabilizes or increases.  
> • **When β < δχ:** Decline continues, even with positive input.

**Academic insertion**

Definition block (first use):

* β (“Grace source term”) = externally supplied coherence-restoring energy, usually in the form of **voluntary constraint adoption** — individuals freely binding themselves to limiting norms that increase systemic order.  
* δχ (“drift differential”) = the current rate at which disorder outpaces existing coherence structures.  
* Coherence = “the degree to which parts of a system hold together in stable, mutually intelligible relationship” (information-theoretic usage) [See: Shannon, 1948].  

Equation formalization:

1.  χ(t + 1) = χ(t) + β − δχ   
2.  Plain-English: tomorrow’s coherence equals today’s coherence plus externally supplied order minus internally generated drift.  
3.  Predictive leverage: increasing β by one unit raises χ one-for-one; decreasing δχ by regulation or cultural steering has the same numerical effect; if β ≤ 0, χ declines monotonically.  

Claim classification: *“without some positive external input, decline is mathematically inevitable.”* — [STRUCTURAL]
