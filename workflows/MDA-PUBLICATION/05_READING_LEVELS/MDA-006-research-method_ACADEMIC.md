## Opening Frame  

This is not "deep research by prompt."  

This workflow was designed for **human-led research with AI as a collaborator** — not an authority, not a conclusion machine, and not a shortcut past hard thinking.

[Definition — Artificial Intelligence (AI): any computational system capable of generating language-level or analytical output that imitates human cognition; here restricted to large language models such as GPT-4. Domain: computer science. First use in Opening Frame. Complexity: medium.]

AI is used here to map intellectual terrain quickly, surface contradictions, organize competing interpretations, and suggest where deeper study is needed. AI is **not** used to decide what is true. Some outputs may be incomplete or wrong. That is expected.  

[Definition — Contradiction: the simultaneous presence of mutually exclusive claims within the same evidentiary field. Domain: logic / epistemology. First use in Opening Frame. Complexity: low.]

The purpose of this method is to **compress time without compressing understanding** — by forcing disagreement, structure, and coherence to surface early.  

[Definition — Coherence: the degree to which component propositions of a system mutually support each other to form a non-self-contradictory whole. Domain: epistemology / information theory. First use in Opening Frame. Complexity: medium.]

"Truth is rarely found where everyone agrees. It is found where disagreement is sharpest and still survivable." [STRUCTURAL]

## The Five-Page System  

Each inquiry in this project uses **five pages**. They are not optional and they are not decorative. Each page performs a distinct cognitive function. Duplicate all five for each new question.

[Definition — Cognitive architecture (research-context): an intentionally designed workflow that structures the researcher’s information-processing steps so as to minimise bias and maximise evidential integrity. Domain: cognitive science / methodology. First use in The Five-Page System. Complexity: high.]

### 01 — Inquiry Seed [STRUCTURAL]  

States the research question precisely. Captures the researcher's initial intuition or belief before any AI input contaminates it. This matters: your pre-AI thinking is load-bearing. If you let AI frame the question, you've already lost authorship of the answer.

File naming convention: Inquiry_Seed_[Question].md.  

### 02 — AI Prompt Engine [STRUCTURAL]  

Documents the exact prompts used, the AI models consulted, and the outputs received — without yet evaluating them. The AI output is pasted or summarised here raw. No judgment at this stage.  

The engine maps the terrain: competing interpretations, key names, major fault lines, suggested search domains. It does not decide what is true.

### 03 — Research Workspace [STRUCTURAL]  

The living document where actual evidence accumulates. Primary sources, data links, study citations, statistics with dates and sourcing.

[Definition — Primary source: an artefact, dataset, or original document created contemporaneously with the event or phenomenon under study, unmediated by secondary interpretation. Domain: historiography / empirical research. First use in Research Workspace. Complexity: low.]

### 04 — Contradictions & Resolution [STRUCTURAL]  

![Story Illustration](../images/mda/The_Observer_Collapsed conv 5.webp)

This is the most important page in the system. Where do the sources disagree? Where does the AI output contradict the primary literature? Where does your intuition conflict with the data?  

Truth is most often found in the contradiction that refuses to resolve cleanly, not in the consensus that resolves too easily.

### 05 — Quotes, Domains, & Links [STRUCTURAL]  

A shared reference library built across all inquiries. Key quotes with attribution and date. Domain categories surfaced during research. External links with brief context annotations. This page is cumulative — it grows across every inquiry and builds the raw material from which publications are drawn.

## What AI Does — and Does Not Do  

### AI Is Used To [STRUCTURAL]  
• Map intellectual terrain quickly across multiple disciplines  
• Surface contradictions between competing interpretations  
• Organise competing frameworks side by side  
• Suggest where deeper primary-source study is needed  
• Stress-test early conclusions against counterarguments  

### AI Is Not Used To [BOUNDARY]  
• Decide what is true  
• Replace primary-source verification  
• Frame the initial question before the researcher does  
• Select which evidence counts as significant  
• Resolve contradictions that require human judgment  

Some AI outputs in this project are incomplete. Some are wrong. That is expected and is not a failure of the method. The method treats AI error as a signal — it tells you where the intellectual terrain is genuinely contested, where consensus has been manufactured rather than earned, and where you need to dig deeper into primary sources.

[Definition — Observer effect (research-workflow sense): the alteration of a phenomenon caused by the act of observing or measuring it; here, the researcher’s prompts may change the AI’s subsequent outputs, necessitating explicit documentation. Domain: physics analogy / methodology. First use in What AI Does. Complexity: medium.]

## How to Use This System  

Workflow Sequence  

1. Start with the **Inquiry Seed**. Write your question and your initial intuition *before* running any AI prompt.  
2. Run the **AI Prompt Engine**. Paste the output. Do not judge it yet — just collect it.  
3. Open the **Research Workspace**. Follow the AI's leads into primary sources. Cite everything. Record what the data actually says.  
4. Build the **Contradictions page**. Force the disagreements into the open. Sit with them. Do not paper over them.  
5. Maintain the **Quotes, Domains, & Links** library as you go. Every session adds to it.

Duplicate all five pages for each new question. The structure is not bureaucracy — it is the cognitive architecture that prevents AI-assisted research from collapsing into AI-generated opinion.
