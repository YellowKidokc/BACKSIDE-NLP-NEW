## A Note on Methodology  

“This series applies thermodynamic modeling⁵ to social data — not as metaphor, but as structural isomorphism⁶.” [BOUNDARY]  

⁵ Thermodynamic modeling: mapping state variables (e.g., entropy S, free energy F) onto analogue social variables (e.g., institutional trust).  
⁶ Structural isomorphism: a one-to-one correspondence between relational patterns in two formally distinct domains (physics and sociology).  

### Governing Equation (E₁)  

1. ΔS ≥ 0  
2. Plain English: The change in entropy (disorder) of an isolated system cannot be negative.  
3. Implication: Without external energy input, social-order metrics will not self-correct; increasing ΔS accelerates decline, while external “energy” (moral renewal movements, institutional reform) is required to reduce or stabilise entropy.  

The same mathematics that governs phase transitions in physics (ice→water→steam) maps onto the observed social data: gradual degradation, acceleration past critical thresholds, and irreversibility without external energy input [STRUCTURAL].  

Every claim is sourced; every metric is longitudinal⁷. The FACTS framework was constructed to be falsifiable⁸: if any pillar showed sustained recovery, the thesis would weaken. None has [EMPIRICAL].  

⁷ Longitudinal: data collected on the same variables repeatedly over time, allowing trend detection.  
⁸ Falsifiable: capable of being empirically disproven, following Popperian scientific standards.
