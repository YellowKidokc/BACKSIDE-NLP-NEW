# Part I — Before We Begin

“I’m biased,” I say first. 

**Bias** is like wearing tinted sunglasses; every scene you look at picks up that color. I know my tint: I believe in God, and I believe **consciousness**—think of it as the movie screen where every moment of your life appears—is real and basic, not an after-effect of chemicals.

I did not lose my faith while writing. I gained spine. Now it is your turn.

### Name Your Ground

Say out loud what lens you already wear. Are you a materialist, a Christian, an atheist, something else? Stick with that answer as you read. You do not get to swap sunglasses halfway through the debate.


# Part II — The Founders Knew

### A Label on the Lens

The American rebels practiced **Biaxiosum**, which is like putting an ingredient label on your thoughts before serving them. In the Declaration they wrote, “We hold these truths to be self-evident.” That was their label.

They made four core claims.

1. **The Source**  
   Rights come from a Creator, not from Congress.

2. **The Rights Themselves**  
   Life, Liberty, and the pursuit of Happiness—not just safety or sameness.

3. **Why Government Exists**  
   Government’s job is to guard those rights, not hand them out.

4. **The Backup Plan**  
   If government flips and attacks the rights, the people may tear it down.

Because the rebels named their tints first, their Biaxiosum score is 100%.

# Part III — The Modern Flip

### The Unelected Maze

Fast-forward to now. The **administrative state** is like a maze of referees who can change the rules mid-game even though nobody elected them. These agencies claim to be neutral while undoing every rebel axiom.

Result: a Biaxiosum score of zero.

### The Academy’s Meta-Mistakes

Scientists and scholars insist they see clearly, yet research shows four traps:

1. **Bias Blind Spot** — spotting everyone else’s typo but missing the one in your own sentence.  
2. **Naïve Realism** — believing your phone camera shows the world without filters.  
3. **Introspection Illusion** — trusting a bathroom mirror in the dark to tell you if you have spinach on your teeth.  
4. **Ideological Homogeneity** — a playlist that plays only one artist all day; on many campuses the political ratio is 12.7 to 1 or worse.

Add one more rule: the **Hull Principle** says science stays honest when many different biases crash into each other, like an orchestra that needs strings, drums, and horns. When nearly everyone thinks the same, the self-correction breaks.

So the modern system hides its slant yet doubles down on it. That is more dangerous than open subjectivity.

# Part IV — My Turn

### Naming My Ground, Again

You are about to meet the **Logos Papers**, a treasure map with 188 marked spots where physics, faith, and math meet. Before you open them, know this: I am biased toward God, toward consciousness, toward meaning. I lead with that. Just like the rebels in 1776.

I have labeled my lens. Now you know it. Let’s talk.
