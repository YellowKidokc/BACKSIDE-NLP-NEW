David Lowe • Theophysics Institute

## A Single Number on the Dashboard  

Imagine every part of a car talking to every other part. When the engine, brakes, and lights work in sync, the ride is smooth. When they fight, the car shakes apart.  
**Coherence Factor (χ)** is like that dashboard light that tells us if the whole car—our nation—is running smoothly. A perfect score is 1.0. A dead-car score is 0.0.  

Today the United States sits at about 0.24. History says we must stay above 0.35 or the car stalls for good.

---

## What Gets Measured  

A civilization is built from many smaller machines. Each one is a **Subsystem**, like the engine or the radio in our car, steering its own corner of life. We track nine of them, because trouble in any corner can start a **Cascade**—a row of dominoes that fall one after another.  

Below are the nine subsystems, why they matter, and how far they have slid since the year 1900.

---

### 1. Family Structure (F)  
Are families forming and holding together?  
1900: 0.92 → 2024: 0.31  
Why it matters: The family is the smallest team that still plays every day. Break the team and the league falls apart.

### 2. Social Trust (T)  
Do people believe strangers will play fair?  
1900: 0.85 → 2024: 0.22  
Why it matters: Trust is social oil. Without it every handshake needs a lawyer.

### 3. Safety (S)  
Can you walk outside without fear?  
1900: 0.75 → 2024: 0.45  
Why it matters: No one builds a sandcastle while bullets fly.

### 4. Self-Control (C)  
Can people rule their own cravings?  
1900: 0.80 → 2024: 0.28  
Why it matters: If I can’t steer myself, I can’t steer a community.

### 5. Mental Health (M)  
Are minds staying clear and hopeful?  
1900: 0.82 → 2024: 0.25  
Why it matters: Broken spirits struggle to bind anything together.

### 6. Economic Stability (E)  
Can ordinary families save, buy homes, and pass wealth on?  
1900: 0.78 → 2024: 0.35  
Why it matters: Long plans need steady ground.

### 7. Civic Participation (P)  
Do people volunteer, vote, and join clubs?  
1900: 0.88 → 2024: 0.22  
Why it matters: Democracy is a potluck—you only eat if everyone brings food.

### 8. Shared Meaning (Σ)  
Do we agree on basic stories, truths, and morals?  
1900: 0.90 → 2024: 0.18  
Why it matters: Shared meaning is a map; without it, every step sparks an argument.

### 9. Intergenerational Transfer (I)  
Is wisdom passing from grandparents to kids?  
1900: 0.92 → 2024: 0.20  
Why it matters: A culture that forgets to teach is one bad exam from extinction.

---

## How the Single Score Is Built  

First, each domain gets a **Normalized Score**—like resizing all photos to the same height so we can compare them.  
Then we take a **Weighted Average**—imagine giving each of the nine paints the same-sized spoon before we stir. Add them up, divide by nine, and we get χ.  

The math whispers a story: when any paint darkens, the final color darkens, too.

---

## The Shape of the Fall  

Trust down 74 %. Shared Meaning down 80 %. Civic life down 75 %. The drops are not gentle slopes; they are cliffs.  
Falling together creates more social **Entropy**—a classroom that grows noisier and messier when no teacher shows up. To fight that chaos we need **Negentropy**—the friend who walks in, cleans the desks, and restores calm. At 0.24 we are losing that tug-of-war.

---

## The Red Line We Crossed  

A **Critical Threshold** is like the thin ice line on a frozen lake—step past it and everything cracks at once. History places that line at χ = 0.35. Above it, societies have self-corrected. Below it, only outside rescue or a miracle reversed the slide.  
We are at 0.24. The ice is broken.  

Each subsystem is wobbling. Their wobbles feed each other in a widening Cascade. This is how a complex machine unwinds.
