# The Coherence Cascade  
Tracking a Century of Cracks in America’s Glue

---

## 1. The Big Claim

**Social coherence** — **like the glue that keeps a sports team moving in sync** — is sliding downhill.  
Words lose their punch first. Then families weaken, public trust shatters, and paychecks drift from real security.  
Across six generations, the numbers line up with scary precision.

We did not study a **closed system** — **like a room with no doors where nothing new can come in**.  
America faced wars, tech booms, and policy shocks. Even with all that noise, the same four-step collapse shows up every time.

---

## 2. Reading the Scoreboard

Researchers chose one main “score” in each area, an **order parameter** — **like a single grade that sums up your whole report card**.  
The outside forces pushing that score are the **control parameters** — **like the thermostat dial that decides when the heat kicks on**.

Year | Words | Family | Institutions | Economy
---- | ----- | ------ | ------------ | -------
1900 | 98 | 95 | 92 | 90  
1926 | 90 | 93 | 90 | 85  
1950 | 75 | 88 | 90 | 92  
1974 | 40 | 50 | 45 | 65  
1998 | 25 | 35 | 35 | 45  
2025 | 18 | 20 | 18 | 25  

A dramatic slide shows up in 1974. That sudden drop is a **phase transition** — **like water snapping into ice once it hits 32 °F**. After the snap, everything races downhill faster.

---

## 3. Step One: Words Fail

**Semantic drift** — **like a slang word that means one thing in sixth grade and the opposite by ninth**—starts the chain.  
By 1926 Henry had to explain “duty” to his sons.  
By 1950 advertisers bent words into slogans.  
By 1974 “integrity” turned into a courtroom insult.  
By 2025 every word choice feels like picking a team.

Underneath, rising **linguistic entropy** — **like a backpack so messy you can’t guess where anything is**—makes speech unpredictable. When words wobble, planning cracks.

---

## 4. Step Two: Families Loosen

**Familial coherence** — **like a thick rope made of long-term promises between kin**—held at 95 in 1900.  
After World War II, sitcom jokes about the “ball and chain” mocked marriage.  
California’s 1969 no-fault divorce law spread nationwide.  
By 2025 almost half of thirty-somethings are unmarried.  
Loose words made it easier to loosen vows.

---

## 5. Step Three: Trust Evaporates

**Institutional trust** — **like believing the referee will call the game fairly**—peaked at 73 % in 1958.  
Watergate chopped it in half.  
Scandals piled on.  
Today fewer than one in five adults trusts Congress, courts, or the evening news.  
If families are shaky, why expect strangers in suits to act better?

---

## 6. Step Four: Paychecks Drift

An **economic anchor** — **like tying your boat to a solid dock so waves can’t sweep it away**—used to link wages to real costs.  
In 1971 the gold standard vanished; prices spun loose.  
Inflation hit 12 % in 1974.  
By 1998 two incomes bought what one income bought in 1950.  
By 2025 gig-workers chase tips with no pensions.

When trust is low, you hedge. Hedging spreads risk but also thins anchors. The cascade completes.

---

## 7. Why the Order Matters

Words set the rules. When the rulebook smudges, families, courts, and markets all guess.  
Fixing paychecks without fixing language is like repainting a house with a rotten frame.  
That is why the project’s core warning is simple: mend meaning first.

---

## 8. Can We Reverse the Slide?

The data show direction, not destiny.  
If a phrase once drifted, it can settle.  
If ice can melt, a bad **phase transition** can unfreeze.  
But glue works only when everyone presses the pieces together at the same time.
