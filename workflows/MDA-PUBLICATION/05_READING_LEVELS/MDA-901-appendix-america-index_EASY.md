# The Moral Decline of America  
How We Measure the Slide, 1958–2025

Ten parts. Sixty-seven years of numbers. Five pillars. Seven family stories.  
The question: Is the United States quietly coming apart, piece by piece?  
The ache at 2 A.M. says yes. This series shows why.

---

## A Slow Unraveling

**Civilizational entropy** is like a sandcastle that loses shape as each wave hits it.  
When the castle is gone, you still see sand, but no clear design.  
Our data say the nation’s moral castle is washing away.

We track the sand with five beams that once held up the roof.  
These beams form the **FACTS** set—like five planks stamped “Faith, Authority, Covenant, Trust, Stewardship.”  
When any plank rots, the roof sags. All five are now bending.

---

## Five Alarms on the Timeline

Below are the moments when the beams cracked loud enough for everyone to hear.

1962 – Prayer banned in public schools. Every FACTS number tilts downward soon after.  

1968 – **Phase transition** is like ice hitting 32 °F and melting at once.  
SAT scores peak, trust fades, divorce soars. Society changes state.

1971 – Dollar cut from gold. Paychecks stop rising with productivity.  
Money keeps moving, yet workers feel lighter, like coins made from tin.

1973 – Roe v. Wade redraws life’s boundary. Choice and consequence drift apart.  
The legal map no longer matches the moral ground.

2008 – Phones in every palm, banks in free fall.  
**Observer** is like a referee whose eyes keep the game fair; now the referee looks away.  
Trust drops below the line where recovery is easy.

## How We Turn Physics into Social Science

We use **thermodynamic modeling**, like borrowing a baking recipe to predict how clay hardens.  
The math for heat and ice also fits our moral numbers.  
That link is a **structural isomorphism**—like two different keys cut with the same ridges.  
When entropy rises in a closed box, it never reverses by itself; societies act the same.

Our charts are **longitudinal**, like snapping a photo of the same tree every year.  
Each claim is **falsifiable**, like making a bet you can lose if wrong.  
So far, no one has collected.
