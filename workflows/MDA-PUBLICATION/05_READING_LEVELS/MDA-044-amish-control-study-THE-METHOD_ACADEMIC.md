## The Setup  

Two populations. Same country. Same century. Same legal rights. Same access to modern medicine, modern law, modern infrastructure.

**Group A (mainstream America):** began removing **constraints** *(constraint: a rule-based limitation on the allowable state-space of a system, thereby reducing its entropy; domain = systems theory)* in 1968. Embraced "freedom" as the highest value.

**Group B (the Amish):** kept the constraints. Refused most of what their neighbors called "progress."

If the **Theophysics thesis** *(Theophysics: an interdisciplinary framework that treats theological axioms as boundary conditions on physical/information-theoretic models of reality; domain = framework science)* is right — if constraint produces **order** *(order: the statistically unlikely alignment of system components that permits reliable function; the inverse of entropy)* and unconstrained freedom produces **entropy** *(entropy: a quantitative measure of disorder or uncertainty in a system’s micro-states; domain = thermodynamics/information theory)* — then Group B should outperform Group A on the metrics that actually matter for **human flourishing** *(human flourishing: the composite state in which physiological, psychological, social, and spiritual needs are sustainably met; domain = virtue ethics/public health)*, *despite* having less technology, less formal education, and less money.

The result is not subtle. They win on everything that can be counted. **[EMPIRICAL]**
