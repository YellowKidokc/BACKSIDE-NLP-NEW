If social coherence χ (“chi,” the macroscopic **order parameter**—a single number summarizing how tightly the parts of a social system align toward common norms†) is a real order parameter governed by **constraint pressure P** (the aggregate strength of institutional, legal, and normative limits on individual choice†), it makes specific, falsifiable predictions.

Not vague directional claims. Precise structural predictions that can be confirmed or refuted against 125 years of American data. We tested four of them. Here is what the data returned.

## The Four Predictions    

1. **Cross-domain correlation** — All domains should move together, not independently.  
2. **Structural synchronization** — Phase transition should occur simultaneously across domains.  
3. **Threshold behavior** — Collapse should be sudden, not gradual.  
4. **Control-group divergence** — Populations maintaining constraints should maintain coherence.

## Test 2: Structural Break Synchronization  [EMPIRICAL]

**Method:** Identify the calendar year of maximum one-period decline for each domain via Bai-Perron structural break detection†.

**Null:** If domains are independent, breakpoints should be randomly scattered from 1940-2024.

|--------|------------------------------|------|
| Media | −25 points | 1968 |
| Family | −20 points | 1973 |
| Institutional | −15 points | 1968 |

| Religious | −10 points | 1968 |

Six of seven domains show maxima in 1968-1973, a five-year window (window width w = 5).  

(Box C)
p = C(7,6)·(w/84)^6·(1 − w/84) ≈ 1 × 10^−8  
translation: The combinatorial probability of six or more maxima clustering by chance is one in one hundred million.  
implication: Synchronization is statistically decisive, consistent with **phase transition**† behavior.

**Claim classification:** Break synchronization observed [EMPIRICAL].

## Test 3: Threshold Behavior  [EMPIRICAL]

**Prediction (STRUCTURAL):** In an order-parameter phase transition, the derivative dχ/dt spikes at the critical temperature Tc (analogy: here the “critical constraint pressure” Pc†).

| Period | Years | Average Decline (Δχ) | Rate (Δχ/yr) |
|--------|-------|----------------------|--------------|
| 1940–1960 | 20 | 8.6 pts | 0.43 |
| 1960–1973 (CRITICAL) | 13 | 25.0 pts | 1.92 |
| 1973–2024 | 51 | 38.9 pts | 0.76 |

(Box D)
Rate ratio = 1.92 / 0.76 ≈ 2.5   (pre-critical ratio = 1.92 / 0.43 ≈ 4.5)  
translation: Decline was 2.5 × faster than post-transition and 4.5 × faster than pre-transition.  
implication: Discontinuity aligns with Landau first-order transition profile†.

**Claim classification:** Threshold acceleration detected [EMPIRICAL].

### Footnote Glossary († first appearance)

• Order parameter — In statistical physics, a macroscopic variable that is near-zero in one phase and non-zero in another, indicating system state.  
• Constraint pressure P — Aggregate intensity of social, legal, and normative restrictions limiting micro-level actions.  
• Phase transition — Discontinuous change in macroscopic state when a control parameter crosses a critical value.  
• Latent variable — An unobserved construct inferred from multiple observed indicators via statistical model.  
• Pearson correlation — Covariance standardized by variance; ranges −1 to 1.  
• Fisher z-test — Converts r to a normally distributed z for hypothesis testing.  
• Structural break detection — Statistical identification of regime-shift points in a time series.  
• Landau first-order transition — Transition with latent heat and discontinuous order parameter jump.  
• Bai-Perron test — Multiple breakpoints regression algorithm.  
• Anabaptist — Christian movement emphasizing adult baptism and simple communal life.
