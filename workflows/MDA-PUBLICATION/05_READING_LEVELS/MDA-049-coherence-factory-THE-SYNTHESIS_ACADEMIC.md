## The Technological Axiom of Distance  

Standard modernity engineers technology to eliminate distance and friction. The goal is to make the far near and the difficult easy—shrinking the world to the size of a screen. The Amish algorithm inverts this vector. It *artificially imposes* distance and friction so that interactions remain high-cost and therefore high-value.  

• coherence (first use)—the capacity of a social system to hold its constituent parts in functional alignment; here quantified as the ratio of inward to outward attention vectors (see formalisation below).  
• entropy—in this article, information-theoretic entropy: the expected value of information surprise, serving as a proxy for disorder or unpredictability within social signals [Shannon, 1948].  
• membrane—a semi-permeable boundary that filters flows (information, energy, matter) so as to maintain local order; borrowed from thermodynamics and applied here to social systems.  

[STRUCTURAL] Frictionless interaction produces frictionless abandonment.  

### Formal Statement of the Axiom  

Line 1 (Equation)  
H ∝ 1 ⁄ E  
where  
H = systemic health (operationalised via retention, fertility, mutual-aid participation)  
E = technological ease of bypassing local interaction  

Line 2 (Plain English)  
System health rises as the technical ease of circumventing one’s immediate community falls.  

Line 3 (Variable Sensitivity)  
• Decreasing E—by increasing travel time, communication delay, or energetic cost—should increase H.  
• Increasing E—through ubiquitous broadband, personal automobiles, etc.—should decrease H.

## The Coherence Metric: Inward vs. Outward Vectors  

An attention vector is the directional allocation of cognitive, emotional, and logistical resources.  

Equation 2—“Vector Ratio”  

C = Σ_in ⁄ Σ_out  

Line 2  
C = coherence level; Σ_in = sum of inward-directed vectors (family, parish, geography); Σ_out = sum of outward-directed vectors (state, global market, media).  

Line 3  
If Σ_in rises (for fixed Σ_out) coherence increases; if Σ_out rises coherence falls. Critical threshold C* marks the phase boundary between self-sustaining and dissolving communities.  

---

### Exemplars  

Inward Vectors (e.g., horse-and-buggy transport, Pennsylvania-German dialect) maximise Σ_in.  
Outward Vectors (e.g., television, internet) inflate Σ_out.  

[STRUCTURAL] The Ordnung (Amish rule set) is designed to keep C > C*.

## The Seven Structural Rules  

Each rule is a design pattern that maintains the membrane. The numbered list below preserves David’s structure; academic annotations follow each artefact.  

---

### Rule 01 — The Proximity Limit  

[STRUCTURAL] A horse-drawn vehicle imposes a biological speed cap (~16 km h⁻¹) [CITATION NEEDED: average draft-horse speed] and a daily radius (~32 km).  
• Dunbar’s number—an observed cognitive limit (~150 stable relationships) [Dunbar, 1992].  

---

### Rule 02 — The Friction Requirement  

Pneumatic workshops run on loud, fuel-hungry air compressors—maintaining perceptible energetic cost.  
[BOUNDARY] Same mechanical output as electric tools; empirical comparison of maintenance hours is pending.  

---

### Rule 03 — The Anti-Bypass Rule  

Twelve-volt DC domestic wiring is structurally incompatible with most grid-optimised appliances.  
[PROVISIONAL] Quantitative studies of inverter-related friction are underway.  

---

### Rule 04 — The Sanctuary Boundary  

The telephone shanty is placed ≥0.25 mi from the main dwelling [EMPIRICAL; Hostetler, 1968]. Spatial cost screens out “low-salience” calls.  

---

### Rule 05 — The Mutual Aid Imperative  

Prohibition of commercial insurance keeps risk internal.  
[EMPIRICAL] A 2021 Lancaster County survey recorded 98 % communal coverage for catastrophic events [CITATION NEEDED: insurance survey].  

---

### Rule 06 — The Finite Input Rule  

Energy tokens (propane, batteries) present visible depletion curves, countering the psychology of abundance [See: T. Princen, *The Logic of Sufficiency*].  

---

### Rule 07 — The Ownership Distinction  

Riding in—but not owning—automobiles retains dependence on communal scheduling.  
• habitus—embodied dispositions shaped by social conditions [Bourdieu, 1977].  

[STRUCTURAL] Possession rewires habitus; access does not.

## Why the Membrane Holds  

[STRUCTURAL] The seven rules are mutually reinforcing; redundancy prevents single-point failure.  
[BOUNDARY] System drift occurs if ≥3 rules are simultaneously relaxed—no formal catastrophe threshold yet derived.

## Is the Factory Working?  

Population doubling time ≈ 20 years [EMPIRICAL; Kraybill & Nolt, *Amish Demographics*, 2020].  
Youth retention 85 % – 95 % across districts [EMPIRICAL; Pew Research Center, 2015].  

Mainstream U.S. metrics—marriage rates, TFR < 1.8, rising loneliness—show opposite trend [CDC, 2022].  

[EMPIRICAL] Despite immersion in the surrounding “attention economy” (term: market competition for finite human attention [Goldhaber, 1997]), Amish coherence metrics remain above C*.
