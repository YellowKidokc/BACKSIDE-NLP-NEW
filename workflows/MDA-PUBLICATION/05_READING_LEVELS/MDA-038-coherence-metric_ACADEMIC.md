### The Coherence Master Equation  

**Line 1 – Formal statement**  
\[
\chi \;\approx\; \lVert P - P_c \rVert^{\beta}
\]  

**Line 2 – Plain-English translation**  
“Coherence (χ) equals the distance between the current constraint level (P, the ‘control parameter’) and the system’s tipping-point value (P₍c₎, the ‘critical threshold’), raised to the scaling exponent β.”  

**Line 3 – Variable-sensitivity commentary**  
• As P approaches P₍c₎ from above (constraints weaken), the magnitude of |P − P₍c₎| shrinks and χ falls precipitously.  
• Larger β values steepen the descent; smaller β values flatten it.  
• Increasing P (restoring constraints) grows the distance from P₍c₎ and therefore increases χ.

## 8. Discussion  

### The 1968–1973 Constraint Removal Cluster  

Synchronized inflection across nine domains demands a common cause. The 1968–1973 window produced an identifiable cluster of constraint-removal legislative and cultural events. These are not interpretations. They are dates.  

1965 — **Griswold v. Connecticut** — Decoupled contraception from the state's interest in marriage, weakening the familial constraint structure. [U.S. Supreme Court, 1965]  

1968 — **Vietnam / Civil Unrest** — Institutional authority collapsed publicly. Trust in government began its terminal decline from **62 %** toward **16 %**. [Pew, 2024]  

1970 — **No-Fault Divorce** — California's Family Law Act removed permanence from the marriage covenant. Divorce rate began vertical climb. [California Statutes, 1970]  

1971 — **Gold Window Closure** — Nixon severed the dollar's constraint anchor. Currency decoupled from physical reality. $1.00 became $0.14 over 53 years. [Federal Reserve Bulletin, 1971]  

1972 — **Eisenstadt v. Baird** — Extended contraceptive access to unmarried individuals, completing the legal decoupling of sexuality from covenant. [U.S. Supreme Court, 1972]  

1973 — **Roe v. Wade** — Removed the final institutional constraint on the consequences of decoupled sexuality. Nine domains show synchronized inflection within this window. [U.S. Supreme Court, 1973]
