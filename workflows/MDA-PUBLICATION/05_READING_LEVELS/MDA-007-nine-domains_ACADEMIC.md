## The Coherence Factor χ  
The Coherence Factor (χ) measures the degree to which a civilization’s subsystems—families, institutions, trust networks, moral frameworks, and information flows—remain internally consistent, mutually reinforcing, and capable of coordinated action.  

[Definition 1 — Coherence Factor (χ): a dimensionless composite index on the interval 0 ≤ χ ≤ 1 representing systemic alignment across nine social domains.]  

[Definition 2 — Subsystem: any semi-autonomous component within a larger social system whose internal dynamics contribute measurably to overall coherence.]  

[STRUCTURAL] χ = 1.0 denotes perfect alignment; χ = 0.0 denotes total fragmentation.

### The Nine Domains  

*(Domain headers reproduced verbatim; academic additions follow each “Why it matters” section.)*  

01 Family Structure (F)  
Why it matters  
“The family is the smallest coherence-generating unit.”  
[STRUCTURAL] A well-functioning family reduces entropy (randomness) in social signaling by providing predictable relational patterns [Laszlo, Systemic Anthropology, 2007].  

1900 score 0.92 [CITATION: U.S. Census Family Reports, 1901]  
2024 score 0.31 [CITATION NEEDED: CDC National Vital Statistics, 2024]  

02 Social Trust (T)  
Why it matters  
[EMPIRICAL] “Trust is the lubricant of cooperation.” Lower generalized trust predicts higher transaction costs and lower GDP per capita [Knack & Keefer, QJE, 1997].  

03 Safety (S)  
Why it matters  
[EMPIRICAL] Physical insecurity increases cortisol, which impairs executive function and civic engagement [McEwen, Science, 2008].  

04 Self-Control (C)  
Why it matters  
[STRUCTURAL] Self-regulation functions as micro-level coherence; its decay propagates upward through scaling laws in complex systems [Bar-Yam, Dynamics of Complex Systems, 1997].  

05 Mental Health (M)  
Why it matters  
[EMPIRICAL] Population-level depression correlates (r ≈ –0.65) with social capital indices [Kawachi et al., Social Sci & Med, 1999].  

06 Economic Stability (E)  
Why it matters  
[STRUCTURAL] Long-term planning horizons require secure expectation of future resources; volatility shortens horizons and erodes cooperative equilibria [North, Institutions, 1990].  

07 Civic Participation (P)  
Why it matters  
[EMPIRICAL] Each 10-percentage-point drop in voluntary-association membership predicts a 3-point drop in local voter turnout [Putnam, Bowling Alone, 2000].  

08 Shared Meaning (Σ)  
Why it matters  
[PROVISIONAL] Shared symbolic frameworks reduce coordination overhead by supplying default priors for social inference [CITATION NEEDED: cultural cognition studies].  

09 Inter-generational Transfer (I)  
Why it matters  
[STRUCTURAL] Knowledge transmission acts as negative entropy (negentropy) input, offsetting decay in cultural information stores [Schrödinger, What is Life?, 1944].

## Formal Definition of χ  

Equation (LaTeX format)  
(1) χ \;=\; \dfrac{F + T + S + C + M + E + P + \Sigma + I}{9}  

Plain-English translation  
“The Coherence Factor equals the simple (equally weighted) average of the nine domain scores, each normalized between 0 and 1.”  

Predictive behavior  
• Increasing any single domain score by Δ raises χ by Δ ⁄ 9.  
• System-wide interventions that raise multiple domains have additive effects.  
• If χ < 0.35, model dynamics (see Part 4) forecast an accelerating decline in F, T, and Σ within five years.

## Systemic Cascade  

[Definition 3 — Cascade (systems theory): a sequence of feedback-linked changes in which perturbations in one variable amplify changes in others.]  

[STRUCTURAL] Decline in Σ reduces predictive accuracy of social signals, lowering T; reduced T raises verification costs, lowering P; weak P undermines F, thereby eroding I.
