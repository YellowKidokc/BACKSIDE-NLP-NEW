### 1. The Problem of “Now”  

Quantum mechanics (QM, the contemporary physical theory describing matter–energy interactions at microscopic scales) presents a formal state vector called the wave-function **|ψ⟩** (Wave Function = “complete mathematical description of a system’s possibilities”). Within the Schrödinger framework it contains a superposition (simultaneous overlay) of all admissible outcomes.  
Our lived phenomenology, however, registers only one outcome per moment. The passage from plurality to singularity is termed **wave-function collapse** (“selection of a single eigenstate from |ψ⟩”). This disjunction constitutes the *measurement problem* (“why and how does collapse occur?”) [CITATION NEEDED: Standard history of the measurement problem, 1926-present].

[STRUCTURAL] Within the Theophysics program we hypothesise that the Christian Trinity—Father, Son, Spirit—supplies the minimal structural operators required for collapse. This is offered as an interpretive mapping, not a completed empirical proof.
