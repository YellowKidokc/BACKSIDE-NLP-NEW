# The Control Study  
**Part 9B: Is Freedom Making Us Sick?**

## The Setup

Two groups live inside the same United States.

1. Mainstream Americans started peeling away rules in 1968.  
2. The Amish kept most of their old rules.

The Amish own fewer gadgets, earn less cash, and stop school after eighth grade. Yet they beat modern America on every measure that matters for **human flourishing**—**human flourishing is like a garden where every plant has enough sun, water, and space**.

Why? The answer sits inside **Theophysics**—**Theophysics is like using a single map that blends city streets with hiking trails, mixing science and faith so you never feel lost**. The theory says clear rules, called **constraints**, fight chaos. **Constraint is like guardrails on a mountain road that keep the car from tumbling off the cliff**. Remove the guardrails and **entropy**—**entropy is like a bedroom that gets messier every day unless someone cleans**—takes over. Good rules create **order**, and **order is like rows of books neatly shelved so you can find the one you need**.

So, does the theory hold? Look at the scoreboard.

---

## 1. Staying Alive and Growing

• Amish women have about 6.8 children.  
• American women have about 1.7.  

The Amish have grown from 8 000 to 385 000 in one century—forty-eight times larger. Mainstream America needs immigration just to tread water.

---

## 2. Physical Health

• Obesity: 4 % Amish vs. 42 % America.  
• Diabetes: 3 % vs. 11 %.  
• Steps per day: 18 000 vs. 4 000.

No fitness apps, no Peloton bikes—just chores, barns, and daily walks.

---

## 3. Mental Health

• Depression: about three times lower among the Amish.  
• Suicide and loneliness: near zero.  
• Antidepressant use: almost none.

No therapy industry, yet better moods.

---

## 4. Money and Work

• Five-year business failure: 5 % Amish, 50 % America.  
• Household debt: near zero among the Amish.  
• Education cost per child: $500 vs. $16 000, yet every Amish adult can read contracts and run numbers.

---

## 5. Family Structure

• Divorce: almost unheard of.  
• Kids with both parents: nearly 100 %.  
• Elderly live at home, not in nursing centers.  
• Orphans stay inside the community.

The family is the welfare office.

---

## 6. Trust and Safety

• Violent crime and prison: effectively zero.  
• Neighbors trust score: about 100 %.  
• Need a barn after a fire? The whole district shows up tomorrow.

---

## 7. The Technology Relationship

The Amish do not hate machines—they run a **technology filter**, and **technology filter is like a bouncer who only lets helpful apps into your phone**. They ask, “Will this tool weaken our relationships?”

Examples:  
• Public pay phone in town—yes, because it keeps travel easy but brief.  
• Personal smartphone—no, because it drags eyes away from real faces.  
• Solar panels—yes, they power tools without tying the home to the outside grid.

Screens stay out; friendships stay in.

---

## Objections, Answered

1. “They’re genetically special.”  
   Their bloodlines suffer from the **founder effect**—**founder effect is like starting a garden from just a handful of seed packets, so some plants repeat too often**. Despite a few inherited diseases, they still win.

2. “It only works because they’re small.”  
   They split into new districts when the horse-and-buggy ride grows long. The pattern scales by division, not by sprawling cities.

3. “No one would choose that life.”  
   **Rumspringa**—**Rumspringa is like a trial run where you borrow your parents’ car before deciding to buy one**—lets teens taste the outside. About 85–90 % come back and commit.

4. “They mooch off the modern world.”  
   They pay taxes and take zero benefits. They produce more than they consume.

5. “Cherry-picking!”  
   Yes, they have problems: rare abuse cases, limited formal schooling. Yet their advantages stretch across every measured domain.

## Why It Works

The Amish don’t cite formulas, but their habits match them.

• **Open system**—**open system is like a house with open windows where fresh air keeps things from smelling**. God stays outside yet connected, feeding the group with moral oxygen.  
• **Negentropy**—**negentropy is like a friend who shows up and helps tidy the messy room**. Worship refuels the community’s will to stay ordered.  
• **Logos**—**Logos is like the instruction manual that comes with every Lego set, showing what the finished build should look like**. Scripture fixes meaning.  
• **Information primacy**—**information primacy is like saving the blueprint even if you must use cheaper bricks**. The story of who they are outranks any wish for shiny gadgets.  
• **Ordnung**—**Ordnung is like the house rules stuck on the fridge—everyone sees them, no one pretends they aren’t there**. Break them and the community steps in.  
• **Sabbath**—**Sabbath is like hitting the weekly pause button on a video game to rest your eyes and mind**. One day of real rest resets the body and the farm.

Together, these forces pull the group toward lower entropy and higher order.

---

## What Was Load-Bearing?

Here are the guardrails mainstream America ripped out:

• Marriage for life  
• Waiting now to gain later  
• Community needs before self wants  
• Screening tech by its social cost  
• Living under external authority  
• Weekly rest  
• Regular manual labor  
• Several generations under one roof  
• Face-to-face talk as the default

Pull the rails, the car skids. Put them back, the numbers rise. The Amish prove it with daily life.

No one asks you to trade jeans for plain dresses. But the data shouts: constraint works, aimless freedom doesn’t. Next, we’ll study the filter that decides which machines stay and which machines never cross the farm gate.
