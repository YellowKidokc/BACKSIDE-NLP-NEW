# The Moral Decay of America  
A 125-Year Story Told Through One Family  

## Why This Matters
We follow six members of the Lowe family, born from 1900 to 2025.  
Their lives let us watch America change in family life, community life, and money life.

First, a big idea. **Coherence** is like a jigsaw puzzle whose pieces lock neatly together. When the pieces fit—family, faith, work—life feels solid. When they don’t, the picture tears.

We track ten big numbers. Each one shows a different piece sliding out of place.

---

## 1900 — Samuel Lowe: A Small, Whole World
Samuel’s Ohio town had horses, candles, and silence after dark.  
The nearest big city was two days away by train.  

- **Divorce rate** (a scoreboard that counts how many marriages break per 1,000 people each year): 0.7  
- **Fertility rate** (like the average number of seeds a tree drops in its life): 3.5 children per woman  
- Church weddings were forever; leaving a spouse meant social exile.  
- **Constraint**—think of unbendable traffic rules—was everywhere. Crops grew only as fast as nature allowed.  
- Almost everyone attended church each week.

Puzzle status: Nearly every piece still fit.

---

## 1926 — Henry Lowe: The First Cracks
Henry bought the family’s first radio. **Radio penetration** is like counting how many homes own the same magic talking box; it hit 35%.  

Suddenly voices from New York filled the living room. The family still listened together, but the wall around home life now had a door.

Key numbers  
- Divorce rate: 1.6 (up 129%)  
- Fertility rate: 2.6 (down 26%)  
- Church attendance: around 90%  
- Phones, cars, and city lights started shortening distance and patience.  

The puzzle picture was still clear, but edges were loosening.

---

## 1950 — William Lowe: Peak Puzzle Fit
Post-war America felt unstoppable. Suburbs sprouted, factories roared, and the Baby Boom arrived.

- Weekly **church attendance** (like counting how many neighbors show up at the Sunday town hall): 55%—the highest ever recorded.  
- Divorce rate: 2.6  
- Fertility rate: 3.0  
- Trust in government soared to 77%.  
- Television arrived. Its spread followed an **adoption curve (logistic)**—like a stadium wave that starts slow, then sweeps every row, then settles. Only 9% of homes had a TV in 1950, but the wave was building.

For one bright moment, coherence peaked.

---

## 1974 — Thomas Lowe: The Sudden Shatter
War, Watergate, and the Pill rewrote the rules.

- **No-fault divorce** is like canceling a phone plan without giving a reason. States passed it, and the divorce rate hit 4.6.  
- Fertility rate fell to 1.8.  
- Church attendance dropped to 40%; trust in government to 36%.  
- Violent crime doubled; murder nearly did.  
- Families stared 6 hours a day at color TVs that now filled nearly every house.

The puzzle cracked. Pieces slid under the couch, and no one paused the show to look for them.

---

## 1998 — Jacob Lowe: Floating Pieces
The Cold War ended, wallets fattened, but roots thinned.

- Divorce rate: 4.0  
- One third of babies were now born outside marriage.  
- 25 million adults lived alone, up from 4 million in 1950.  
- Internet reached 26% of homes, promising endless answers.  
- The **GINI index** is like slicing a pizza and checking if everyone gets the same size. It climbed to 0.43—uneven slices everywhere.

People were richer, better entertained, and strangely adrift.

---

## 2025 — Jacob Again: Ultra-Connected, Deeply Alone
Phones in every pocket; friends in short supply.

- The **loneliness epidemic** is like standing in a packed bus where everyone wears noise-canceling headphones. Doctors say it kills as surely as smoking.  
- Fertility rate: 1.6—the lowest ever.  
- Weekly church attendance: 30%; “no religion” now 28%.  
- Less than 20% trust the government.  
- Americans stare at screens 7.5 hours daily; 30 million use AI companions.  

Digital glue links every piece, yet the picture still falls apart.

## The Long Slide in One Glance
Metric | 1900 | 1950 | 2025 | Direction  
---|---|---|---|---  
Divorce rate | 0.7 | 2.6 | 2.3* | 3× up  
Fertility rate | 3.5 | 3.0 | 1.6 | Half down  
Church attendance | ~95% | 55% | 30% | 2⁄3 down  
“No religion” | ~1% | 2% | 28% | 28× up  
Trust in government | — | 77% | <20% | 4× down  
Adults living alone | ~1 million | 4 million | 37 million | 37× up  
Daily media hours | 0 | 4.5 | 7.5 | From silence to saturation  

\*Looks lower than 1950 only because far fewer people marry.

---

## What the Numbers Whisper
America did not break overnight. Each new freedom erased an old **constraint**, and each erased constraint took some coherence with it. More choices. Fewer bonds. More voices. Less friendship.

The Lowe family shows us five generations of trade-offs: speed for patience, entertainment for presence, connection for commitment. The puzzle image is still on the box lid, waiting. Whether we find the lost pieces is now our choice.
