# Part 6: The Cascade (1973–2024)  
The Moral Decline of America

---

### A Single Number

**_Coherence Factor_ (χ)** is like a giant puzzle. When every piece—family, schools, faith, news, and government—locks together, you see the full picture. When pieces scatter, the picture shatters.

• Perfect puzzle: χ = 1.0  
• Pile of broken pieces: χ = 0.0  
• Today’s United States: χ ≈ 0.27

A **_Critical Threshold_** is like the moment a Jenga tower wobbles and tilts. Below χ ≈ 0.35, the tower starts falling on its own.

In 1973 we slipped past that wobble point. Since then the fall has sped up.

---

## Decade by Decade

### 1973–1983  |  The Fracturing  
χ : 0.55 → 0.48  

Divorces, inflation, and poisoned rivers cracked trust. Cable TV chopped shared news into tiny channels. People felt “malaise.” The puzzle pieces loosened.

Key metric: highest divorce rate in U.S. history.

---

### 1983–1993  |  The Substitution  
χ : 0.48 → 0.43  

Money flowed and malls boomed, so decay hid under bright lights. Crack cocaine, AIDS, and record crime showed the rot. Stuff replaced community.

Key metric: out-of-wedlock births rose to 30% (5% in 1960).

---

### 1993–2003  |  The Virtualization  
χ : 0.43 → 0.38  

The Internet promised one big room but became a maze of private screens. Trust in leaders dipped with scandals. 9/11 gave a short unity spike, then vanished.

Key metric: only a third of Americans said “most people can be trusted.”

---

### 2003–2013  |  The Fragmentation  
χ : 0.38 → 0.35  

Wars based on bad facts, a housing crash, and the birth of social media ripped open deeper wounds. Suicides climbed. “Deaths of despair” entered the dictionary. The Jenga tower now shook at threshold.

---

### 2013–2024  |  Below Threshold  
χ : 0.35 → 0.27  

Smartphones ruled pockets, algorithms fed outrage, and politics became a cage match. COVID lockdowns and street riots smashed the last shared stories. Youth depression exploded.

---

## Why the Fall Speeds Up

A **_Phase transition_** is like water that looks calm, then snaps into ice the instant it hits 32 °F. Our social water froze into something brittle in the early 1970s.

After that snap, **_feedback loops_** took over. A feedback loop is like a microphone squealing when its own sound hits the speaker and circles back. Good loops build more good; bad loops build more bad.

In healthy times  
• Trust → stronger families → more trust  

In sick times  
• Distrust → weaker families → more distrust  

Once the loops turned negative, every fix slipped. Strengthen families? Hard when no one trusts promises. Rebuild trust? Tough when leaders share no common values. Restore meaning? Impossible when facts are fluid.

That self-powered slide is the **_Cascade_**, a row of dominoes that keeps falling after the first push.

---

## The Rocket Fuel

A **_Technology accelerant_** is like pouring gasoline on a campfire. Each new gadget fanned the flames:

Television → passive watching  
Cable → splintered culture  
Internet → information overload  
Social media → 24/7 comparison and outrage  
Smartphones → no escape, even at dinner  
AI feeds → reality itself blurs

Tools that could connect us instead sped the dominoes.

---

## Where We Stand

Domain scores today:  

Family 0.31 Trust 0.22 Shared Meaning 0.18  
Five areas sit at “critical,” two have “collapsed,” none improve.

History shows no civilization has climbed back once χ drops below 0.35. The very muscles needed for the climb—trust, shared purpose, honest institutions—are now torn.

Yet a few tiny cultures have done the impossible. They rebuilt coherence after the fall. They point to a narrow path ahead.
