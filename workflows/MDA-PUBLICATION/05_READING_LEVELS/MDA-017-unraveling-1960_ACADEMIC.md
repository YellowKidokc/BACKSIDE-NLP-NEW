## Executive Summary  

The years **1960 – 1967** marked what can be analytically described as a *structural collapse* (structural collapse = a rapid, system-wide loss of functional integrity arising from internal stresses that exceed adaptive capacity) of the post-war, *high-trust* (high-trust = modal expectation that institutions and strangers will act predictably and in good faith) moral order. The driving variables were (i) *technological autonomy* (technological autonomy = the capacity of artefacts to give individuals discretionary control over previously regulated domains) [STRUCTURAL], (ii) mass secondary education, and (iii) cascading legitimacy shocks.  

Empirically, the decade begins at historical peaks of **institutional trust (77 % in 1964)** and **lowest female median marriage age (20.3 y in 1960)** [EMPIRICAL; NCES 2024, Pew 2024]. Within seven years, the arrival of *Enovid* (first oral contraceptive) granted *reproductive autonomy* (reproductive autonomy = the ability to separate sexual activity from procreation) [CITATION NEEDED: FDA Approval File, 1960], and a **69.5 % high-school graduation rate** supplied a *mass-educated* (mass-educated = > 2⁄3 of cohort with 12 y schooling) youth cohort capable of ideological critique [EMPIRICAL; NCES Table 219.10].  

Concurrently, *trust in government* entered continuous decline after the Gulf of Tonkin escalation [EMPIRICAL; Pew 2024]. The net effect was a rapid *transfer of moral authority* (moral authority = the socially recognized right to define normative boundaries) from centralized structures (Church, State, Nuclear Family = heterosexual, biologically related two-parent household ideal) to decentralized, individuated ethics.  

> The 1960s did not start with rebellion; they started at peak consensus **and exhausted it by 1967**. [STRUCTURAL]

## Part 2 · Expert Insights  

Each subsection isolates the **most influential causal driver**; claim tags appended.

### 2.1 Family — Enovid and the End of the Marriage Economy  

Enovid’s FDA approval (1960) functioned as a *moral technology* (moral technology = an artefact whose primary externality is normative realignment). By decoupling coitus from conception, it rendered early marriage economically unnecessary, thereby increasing the expected value of deferred or dissolved unions. Divorce rate rise from **2.1 → 2.6** (1955-63 → 1967) is directionally consistent [EMPIRICAL].  

Epistemic classification: Causal chain is **[PROVISIONAL]**—correlational evidence strong, mechanistic studies limited.

### 2.2 Sexuality — Kinsey and the Secularization of Desire  

Alfred Kinsey’s 1948 & 1953 reports quantified sexual variance, reframing “deviance” as statistical dispersion rather than moral failure. This *secularization* (secularization = transfer of normative arbitration from sacred to empirical discourse) empowered the educated cohort to reject purity narratives [STRUCTURAL].  

### 2.3 Education — The Mass-Educated Reckoning  

Crossing the 70 % graduation threshold constituted a *phase transition* (phase transition = non-linear qualitative change arising from quantitative parameter shift) in cognitive capacity distribution. Educated youth exposed contradictions between democratic ideals and segregation, catalyzing civil rights and anti-war protests [EMPIRICAL]^1.  

### 2.4 Economic Policy — The War on Poverty as Equality of Outcome Mandate  

The Economic Opportunity Act (1964) reframed federal responsibility from safety-net provision to *social justice engineering* (social justice = institutional pursuit of equitable outcome distributions). Poverty already falling (22 % → ≈ 15 % by 1968) [EMPIRICAL]^15, yet policy emphasis signaled new moral horizon [STRUCTURAL].  

### 2.5 Media — Television and the Death of Distance  

With **90 % penetration**, television collapsed spatial information gradients. Graphic coverage of Selma (1965) and Vietnam (post-1965) produced synchronized moral outrage, accelerating legitimacy erosion [EMPIRICAL]^16.  

### 2.6 Institutions — The Erosion of Trust  

Trust peaked at **77 % (1964)** and entered monotonic decline after Vietnam escalation [EMPIRICAL]^2. Loss of *legitimacy* (legitimacy = perceived rightful authority) is the pivotal variable explaining subsequent decentralization of moral frameworks [BOUNDARY]—data robust, precise causal accounting remains contested.

## Equation Formalization (Illustrative)  

To quantify trust decay, we model:

1)  T(t) = T₀ · e^(-λt)  

2)  Plain English: current trust T(t) equals initial trust T₀ (77 % in 1964) times an exponential decay governed by rate λ.  

3)  Prediction: Increasing λ accelerates decline; halving time τ½ = ln 2⁄λ. Empirically, trust reached 38 % by 1974, yielding λ ≈ 0.069 y⁻¹ (τ½ ≈ 10 y) [CITATION NEEDED: derived from Pew trend].
