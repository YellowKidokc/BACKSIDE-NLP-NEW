## The Control Group  

We've traced the decline. 1900 → 2024. From χ = 0.85 to χ = 0.27.  
 (DEF: χ, “coherence index,” is the normalized mean of nine domain-scores—family, trust, safety, self-control, mental health, economic stability, civic engagement, shared meaning, inter-generational linkage—scaled 0–1.0.)  

Formal statement  

(1) χ = (1 / n) ∑_{i=1}^{n} c_i  

 Line-by-line:  
 • χ = overall coherence  
 • n = number of assessed domains (n = 9 here)  
 • c_i = score of the i-th domain  

 Interpretation: raising any c_i, ceteris paribus, linearly increases χ; losses in any domain lower χ at equal weight. [STRUCTURAL]  

But here's the question that matters: **Was it inevitable?** ↵Was there something about modernity itself that required this collapse? Some iron law of progress that demanded we sacrifice coherence for prosperity?  

The answer is no [PROVISIONAL]. We know because we have a **control group** (DEF: “control group,” a population held constant to isolate the effect of an independent variable—in this case, constraint level).  

> “The Amish don't reject technology. They reject the dissolution of boundaries that technology enables.” [See: Kraybill & Nolt, The Amish, 2013]

Two populations. Same country. Same century. Same legal rights.  

**Group A (Mainstream America):** Removed constraints starting 1968 [CITATION NEEDED: legislative timeline]. Embraced “freedom.” (DEF: “freedom” here denotes maximal individual optionality, often at the expense of communal structure.)  

**Group B (Amish):** Kept constraints. Rejected “progress.”  

If the Theophysics thesis is correct—(constraint → order, freedom → entropy (DEF: “entropy,” degree of disorder or number of accessible micro-states, borrowed from statistical mechanics))—Group B should outperform Group A on every coherence metric *despite* having less technology, less education, less money.  

Result: **They do.** On everything. [EMPIRICAL]

## The Structural Interpretation  

**America after 1968:** external field h ≈ 0.  
**The Amish:** h > 0 maintained via Ordnung (DEF: Ordnung, the unwritten communal code governing conduct).  

Formal analogy to the Ising model  

(2) E = −J ∑_{⟨i,j⟩} s_i s_j − h ∑_{i} s_i  

 Line-by-line:  
 • E = system energy (lower E = more order)  
 • J = coupling constant (strength of binding between agents i and j)  
 • s_i, s_j = state (+1 communal, −1 individualist)  
 • h = external field (normative pressure toward +1)  

 Interpretation: Decreasing h or J increases probability of disorder (s_i randomizes). Maintaining non-zero h (Ordnung) stabilizes communal alignment [STRUCTURAL].
