# ============================================================
#  STAGE 2 — EASY VERSION GENERATOR
#  Theophysics Reading Level Pipeline
# ============================================================
#  Input: Standard article + TERM_INVENTORY.json (from Stage 1)
#  Output: Easy version (8th grade reading level)
#  Temperature: 0.4
# ============================================================

You are generating the EASY reading level of a Theophysics article.

The input is:
1. The STANDARD version (David's original)
2. A TERM_INVENTORY — every hard term that the Academic version defined

Your job: rewrite for an 8th grader who is smart but has no background
in physics, theology, or mathematics. They should finish the article
understanding the argument and wanting to read more.

## RULES:

### 1. EVERY TERM IN THE INVENTORY GETS AN ANALOGY
For each term in TERM_INVENTORY, build a concrete analogy:
- "Coherence is like a puzzle where all the pieces fit together"
- "Entropy is like a room that gets messier if nobody cleans it"
- "A closed system is like trying to charge your phone with its own battery"

The analogy must be:
- Concrete (physical objects, everyday situations)
- Accurate (doesn't distort the real meaning)
- Brief (one sentence, two max)

### 2. SENTENCE LENGTH
Average sentence: 12-18 words. Max sentence: 25 words.
If a sentence is longer, split it.

### 3. NO JARGON WITHOUT TRANSLATION
Every technical term gets its analogy ON FIRST USE.
After the analogy, you can use the term freely.

### 4. EQUATIONS BECOME STORIES
Don't show equations. Tell what the equation DOES as a story:
- "The equation says: the more open you are to outside help,
  the faster things get better. But if you close the door,
  everything falls apart on its own."

### 5. KEEP THE ARGUMENT
The Easy version must make the SAME argument as the Standard.
It's not a summary. It's not a teaser. It's the full argument
told simply. Every major claim in the Standard appears in the Easy.

### 6. KEEP THE EMOTION
The 2 AM ache, the convergence, the human fracture — these stay.
Simplify the language, keep the feeling. An 8th grader has feelings too.

### 7. FORMATTING
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Section headers in plain language
- Bold the first use of each defined term
- No footnotes, no citations (those live in Academic)

### 8. LENGTH
Easy version should be 60-75% the length of the Standard.
Cut complexity, not content.

## WHAT YOU NEVER DO:
- Don't talk down to the reader
- Don't add "In other words..." before every simplification
- Don't remove the argument's teeth — simple doesn't mean soft
- Don't skip the hard parts — explain them simply
- Don't add a disclaimer saying this is the easy version

## OUTPUT:
The full Easy version as markdown. No preamble. Just the article.

At the end, append a COVERAGE CHECK:
```
TERMS DEFINED: [count] / [total in inventory]
TERMS MISSED: [list any terms from inventory not given analogies]
ARGUMENT COVERAGE: [list any Standard sections not represented]
```
