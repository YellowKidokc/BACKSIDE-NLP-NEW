# Reader Definition Bridge

## Terms

### Coherence Factor (χ)

The Coherence Factor is a number that shows how well different parts of a society work together. A higher number means they work well together, while a lower number means they don't.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific|opaque_to_general_reader`

### subsystems

Subsystems are the different parts of a society, like families, schools, and businesses, that all play a role in how the society functions.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific|opaque_to_general_reader`

### coherence

Coherence means that the different parts of society support and trust each other, making the society work better as a whole.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific|opaque_to_general_reader`

### fragmentation

Fragmentation is when the different parts of society don't work well together, leading to distrust and conflict.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific|opaque_to_general_reader`

### critical threshold

The critical threshold is a specific point (0.35) that indicates when a society is struggling to function well.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific|opaque_to_general_reader`

## Recurring Themes

### Importance of Coherence

Coherence is essential for a society to function well. Without it, families, communities, and governments struggle.

Analogy: Think of coherence like the glue that holds a structure together. Without enough glue, the structure can fall apart.

Limit: This analogy helps illustrate the importance of coherence but does not provide a complete understanding of its complexities.
