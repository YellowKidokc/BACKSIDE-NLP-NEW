# Part 2: The Nine Domains

## Abstract

This paper examines the concept of the Coherence Factor (χ), a metric designed to evaluate the internal consistency and coordination capacity of a civilization's subsystems. The Coherence Factor is derived from nine domains that collectively represent different dimensions of social health. This study presents the current state of these domains in the United States, highlighting significant declines since 1900. The paper further discusses the implications of these declines, particularly the crossing of a critical threshold below which historical data suggests civilizations have not self-corrected.

## Keywords

Coherence Factor, social health, civilization, systemic failure, threshold, United States

## Introduction

The Coherence Factor (χ) is a measure of the degree to which a civilization's subsystems—such as families, institutions, trust networks, moral frameworks, and information flows—remain internally consistent, mutually reinforcing, and capable of coordinated action. A χ value of 1.0 indicates perfect alignment, while 0.0 signifies total fragmentation. The current U.S. Coherence Factor is approximately χ ≈ 0.24, which is below the critical threshold of χ = 0.35, indicating a significant systemic risk.

## The Nine Domains

### Domain 1: Family Structure (F)

**Research Question**: Are families forming and staying together?

**Indicators**: Marriage rate per 1,000 adults, divorce rate, percentage of children living with both biological parents, single-parent household rate, birth rate within marriage vs. outside.

**Significance**: The family is considered the smallest coherence-generating unit. Fragmentation at this level can lead to broader systemic incoherence.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.92; 2024: 0.31

### Domain 2: Social Trust (T)

**Research Question**: Do people trust each other and institutions?

**Indicators**: Survey responses on trust, institutional confidence, contract enforcement needs, neighbor-to-neighbor trust.

**Significance**: Trust facilitates cooperation and reduces the need for verification and enforcement in transactions.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.85; 2024: 0.22

### Domain 3: Safety (S)

**Research Question**: Are people safe from violence?

**Indicators**: Murder rate, violent crime rate, sexual assault rate, domestic violence rate, child abuse rate.

**Significance**: Physical safety is foundational for community coherence.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.75; 2024: 0.45

### Domain 4: Self-Control (C)

**Research Question**: Can people resist destructive impulses?

**Indicators**: Addiction rates, obesity rate, consumer debt levels, impulse-related bankruptcy, STD transmission rates.

**Significance**: Self-control is essential for individual coherence, which supports higher-order social coherence.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.80; 2024: 0.28

### Domain 5: Mental Health (M)

**Research Question**: Are people psychologically healthy?

**Indicators**: Suicide rate, depression rates, anxiety disorder rates, "deaths of despair," psychiatric medication usage.

**Significance**: Mental health reflects individual coherence and impacts social coherence.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.82; 2024: 0.25

### Domain 6: Economic Stability (E)

**Research Question**: Can people build and maintain wealth?

**Indicators**: Household savings rate, home ownership rate, bankruptcy rate, generational wealth transfer, economic mobility.

**Significance**: Economic stability supports long-term planning and commitment.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.78; 2024: 0.35

### Domain 7: Civic Participation (P)

**Research Question**: Do people engage in community?

**Indicators**: Volunteering rate, membership in civic organizations, voter participation, community event attendance, neighbor interaction frequency.

**Significance**: Civic participation is a manifestation of social coherence.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.88; 2024: 0.22

### Domain 8: Shared Meaning (Σ)

**Research Question**: Do people share a common framework?

**Indicators**: Religious participation rate, agreement on moral questions, shared cultural knowledge, common information sources, consensus on historical narrative.

**Significance**: Shared meaning facilitates coordination without explicit negotiation.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.90; 2024: 0.18

### Domain 9: Intergenerational Transfer (I)

**Research Question**: Is wisdom passing to the next generation?

**Indicators**: Children learning parents' trade/profession rate, family business survival, religious/cultural continuity, elder care by family, grandparent involvement in child-rearing.

**Significance**: Successful intergenerational transfer is crucial for societal continuity.

**Scores**: 1900: 0.92; 2024: 0.20

## The Coherence Factor Formula

The Coherence Factor (χ) is calculated as the weighted average of all nine domains:

\[ χ = \frac{F + T + S + C + M + E + P + Σ + I}{9} \]

Each domain is normalized to a 0-1 scale using historical benchmarks.

## Discussion

The analysis reveals significant declines across all domains, with the most pronounced decreases in Shared Meaning, Trust, Intergenerational Transfer, and Civic Participation. These declines are interrelated, forming a cascade effect where deterioration in one domain exacerbates others, leading to systemic failure.

## Conclusion

The threshold of χ = 0.35 is identified as critical, below which no civilization has historically self-corrected. The current U.S. Coherence Factor of χ = 0.27 suggests an urgent need for external intervention to alter the trajectory of decline.

## Limitations

- The study relies on historical benchmarks that may not fully capture contemporary complexities.
- The interdependencies between domains are hypothesized but not empirically validated.

## Defeat Conditions Preserved
