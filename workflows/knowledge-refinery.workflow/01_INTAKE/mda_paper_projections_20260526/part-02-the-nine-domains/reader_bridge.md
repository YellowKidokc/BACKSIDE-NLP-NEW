# Reader Definition Bridge

## Terms

### Coherence Factor (χ)

The Coherence Factor is a number that shows how well different parts of a society work together smoothly and support each other.

Flag: `above_8th_grade|technical|domain_specific`

### Threshold

A threshold is a point or level at which something changes. Here, it refers to a critical point below which societies have not been able to fix themselves.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific`

### Systemic Failure

Systemic failure means a breakdown that affects an entire system, not just one part.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific`

### Normalized

Normalized means adjusting values measured on different scales to a common scale.

Flag: `technical|domain_specific`

## Recurring Themes

### Coherence Factor and its impact

The Coherence Factor is a way to measure how well different parts of society work together. If the number is too low, it means the society is at risk of falling apart.

Analogy: Think of the Coherence Factor like a team score in a game. If the score is high, the team is playing well together. If it's low, the team is disorganized and likely to lose.

Limit: This analogy simplifies complex social dynamics to a game, which may not capture all nuances.
