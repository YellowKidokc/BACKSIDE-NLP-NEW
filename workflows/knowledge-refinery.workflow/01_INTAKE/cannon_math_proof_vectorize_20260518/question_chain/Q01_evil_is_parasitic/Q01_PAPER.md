# Q01 — Is Evil Equal To Good?

## Layer 1: The Hook

**"Good and evil are two sides of the same coin."**

Then why does evil need good to exist, but good doesn't need evil?

You can have health without disease. You can't have disease without health. You can have trust without betrayal. You can't have betrayal without trust. You can have life without death. You can't have death without life.

If they were equal, the dependency would run both ways. It doesn't. That's not two sides of a coin. That's a parasite and a host.

---

## Layer 2: The Human Story

Think about the worst betrayal you've ever experienced. Someone you trusted — really trusted — lied to you. Hurt you. Used your trust as a weapon.

Here's what made it hurt: the trust was real. If you'd never trusted them, the betrayal would have been nothing. A stranger can't betray you. Only someone you let in can.

The betrayal didn't create anything. It *destroyed* something that was already there. The trust came first. The betrayal was a corruption of something good.

Now run that in every direction. Cruelty is corrupted strength. Lust is corrupted desire. Greed is corrupted ambition. Pride is corrupted confidence. Addiction is corrupted pleasure. Every form of evil you can name is a good thing bent toward a wrong end.

Evil is not creative. It has never invented anything. It can only take what was already good and twist it.

---

## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Ontological priority: good is prior to evil the way metal is prior to rust. You cannot have rust without metal. You can have metal without rust. The dependency is asymmetric. This is not a claim about power (evil can be very powerful). It is a claim about existence-priority (evil requires prior good to corrupt).

This extends T1: if the reference state is prior, then deviation from it is derivative. All corruption is corruption *of* something. The something must exist first. Evil is not a substance. It is a relation — the relation of deviation from good.

---

## Layer 4: Theological Register

"God saw that it was good." Six times before anything goes wrong. The biblical narrative does not begin with a battle between equal forces. It begins with uncontested good, and evil enters as corruption of that good.

The serpent does not create an alternative world. He distorts the existing one. "Did God really say...?" — the lie works by bending truth, not by generating an independent reality.

Isaiah 45:7 ("I form the light and create darkness, I bring prosperity and create disaster") — even this is sovereignty over the consequences of a broken world, not co-creation of evil as an equal substance. The darkness is the absence of light, not a rival light.

---

## Layer 5: Scientific/Physics Isomorphism

**Entropy and order.** The Second Law says entropy increases in closed systems. But entropy is not a "force" — it is a statistical tendency toward disorder. Order is the organized state. Entropy is the loss of that organization. You cannot have entropy without prior order to lose.

**Signal and noise.** Shannon: noise degrades signal. Noise is not a competing signal — it is the corruption of signal. Without a signal to corrupt, noise is just randomness with no information content.

**Disease and health.** A virus cannot exist without a host cell. The virus hijacks the cell's own machinery — it corrupts existing biological order. Health is the reference. Disease is the deviation.

In every physical domain: the ordered/coherent state is prior, and the disordered/corrupted state is derivative. Evil mirrors this structure in the moral domain.
