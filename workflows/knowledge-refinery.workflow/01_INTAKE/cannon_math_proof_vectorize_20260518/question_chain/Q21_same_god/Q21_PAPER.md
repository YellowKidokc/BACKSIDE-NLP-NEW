# Q21 — Did God Change Between the Testaments?

## Layer 1: The Hook

**"God changed between the Old and New Testaments."**

The medicine changed. The doctor didn't.

You don't accuse a doctor of inconsistency because they prescribed aspirin yesterday and surgery today. The condition changed. The treatment changed. The goal — your health — never did.

The Old Testament is diagnosis and containment. The New Testament is cure and restoration. Same God. Different phase of treatment.

---

## Layer 2: The Human Story

You've known someone who was strict with their children when they were young and gentle when they were older. Not because the parent changed. Because the children changed. The strictness was appropriate for a toddler near a stove. The gentleness is appropriate for an adult making their own choices.

The parent's character is constant. The expression adapts to the developmental stage.

God's character didn't change. Humanity's condition did. The law was for childhood. Grace is for maturity.

---

## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Phase-appropriate intervention: a system in different phases of dysfunction requires different interventions. Early-phase corruption may be containable. Late-phase corruption may require radical intervention. The same reference state guides both decisions.

T1 extension: if the external operator is time-dependent (appearing at a specific point in history), then pre-operator and post-operator phases will look different. The pre-operator phase contains the error state. The post-operator phase introduces the restoration term.

The difference is not in God's character. It is in the availability of the restoration mechanism.

## Layer 4: Theological Register

"Jesus Christ is the same yesterday and today and forever." — Hebrews 13:8

**Category precision:** The epistle to the Hebrews explicitly anchors constancy in Christ. If Christ is the same, and Christ is the "exact representation" of God's being (Hebrews 1:3), then God's being is the same.

James 1:17 — "Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights, who does not change like shifting shadows."

The Greek for "does not change" (*parallage*) refers to astronomical variation — the apparent movement of heavenly bodies. God does not vary like that. There is no parallax, no apparent shift due to perspective.

Exodus 34:6-7 (the grace formula) is quoted or alluded to throughout the Bible: Numbers 14:18, Nehemiah 9:17, Psalms 86:15, 103:8, 145:8, Joel 2:13, Jonah 4:2, Nahum 1:3. The same character attributes appear in both testaments: compassionate, gracious, slow to anger, abounding in love and faithfulness.

**Critical structural point:** The OT contains both justice and mercy. The NT contains both justice and mercy. The proportions appear different because the phases are different, but the ingredients are the same.

**Jesus's own reading of the OT:** "Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law or the Prophets; I have not come to abolish them but to fulfill them." — Matthew 5:17

Fulfillment (*pleroo*) means completion, not cancellation. The law pointed forward. Christ is the pointer's destination. The destination does not contradict the signpost.

**Boundary:** This does not resolve every specific moral difficulty in the OT. It establishes the structural claim: the God of the OT and the God of the NT are the same character operating under different conditions.

**Category jump flag:** Moving from "same God" to "Christ is that God incarnate" requires the identity bridge (Q22-Q24).

## Layer 5: Scientific/Physics Isomorphism

**Developmental biology.** The same DNA produces radically different structures at different developmental stages: zygote, embryo, fetus, infant, adult. The organism is the same. The expression changes. The OT and NT are different developmental stages of the same redemptive history.

**Medicine.** The same doctor treats the same patient differently as the disease progresses: prevention, containment, treatment, rehabilitation. The doctor's goal is constant. The methods adapt.

## Layer 7: Objections / Kill Conditions

| Objection | Response | Status |
|---|---|---|
| "The NT God is love; the OT God is wrath" | The NT contains wrath (Revelation, Ananias and Sapphira). The OT contains love (Hosea, Ruth, Psalm 103). Both attributes are present in both. | SURVIVES |
| "Jesus rejected the OT law" | Matthew 5:17 — "I have not come to abolish but to fulfill." Jesus intensifies the law internally (anger = murder, lust = adultery). | SURVIVES |
| "The OT is violent; the NT is peaceful" | The NT includes persecution, martyrdom, and apocalyptic judgment. The difference is not violence but who bears it (Christ absorbs it). | BRIDGE |
| "Progressive revelation implies earlier error" | Progressive revelation implies earlier partialness, not error. The child is not wrong; the adult is more complete. | SURVIVES |

**Kill condition:** Show that the OT and NT describe fundamentally incompatible characters, not the same character under different conditions.

**Self-defeating test:** "God changed" assumes a standard of constancy by which to judge God — which standard, if valid, must be unchanging. But the critic's own moral framework is culturally relative and historically variable.
