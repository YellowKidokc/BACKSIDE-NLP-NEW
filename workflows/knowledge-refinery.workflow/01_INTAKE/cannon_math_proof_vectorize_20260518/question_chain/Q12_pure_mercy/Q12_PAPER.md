## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Unconditional mercy without restoration is *enabling*, not healing. If the agent's actual state remains in the error class, declaring them forgiven without changing their state imports the error class into the reference environment. Paradise becomes a venue for re-corruption; the conditions that produced the fall reappear because the agents who fell are unchanged.

The structural distinction: forgiveness is a *relational* operation; restoration is an *ontological* operation. Forgiveness alone changes the ledger; it does not change the agent. If the agent is unchanged, the ledger will re-fill.

Pure mercy is therefore not a sufficient solution to the universal-failure problem (Q10). It is half a solution — the half that addresses the legal status — without the other half that addresses the substrate. A repair that addresses only the symbolic side and not the material side will be recurrent.

**T1 extension:** Restoration is the operation that maps error-state agents to reference-state agents. A label change ("forgiven") that does not produce the mapping is not restoration. Operations from within the error class can change labels (because labels are symbolic) but not states (because states require reference-class operations, which by closure must come from outside).

## Layer 5: Physics / Science Isomorphism

**Partial antibiotic treatment and resistance.** A bacterial infection treated insufficiently selects for resistant strains. The pathogen is not eliminated; it is partially suppressed, and the survivors are the ones with the strongest resistance. The next round is harder. Forgiveness without restoration is structurally analogous: the corruption is symbolically suppressed (declared forgiven) but materially preserved, and recurrence selects for the most entrenched forms. (BRIDGE: partial treatment vs. cure is a tight process-isomorphism for mercy-without-restoration vs. justice-and-mercy integrated.)

**Error-correcting codes that mark vs. correct.** A code that flags errors but does not correct them does not restore the message; it just annotates the corruption. The downstream system receives marked-as-forgiven garbage, not clean data. Useful for diagnostics, useless for restoration.

**Renormalization without resolution.** In physics, when a theory produces infinities at every scale, declaring the infinities "renormalized away" without an actual mechanism is a label change, not a solution. A real theory either eliminates the infinities (mechanism) or is incomplete (in which case "renormalization" is a placeholder, not an answer).

**Status flag:** Antibiotic-resistance analogy is a strong process-isomorphism. The error-correction analogy is exact in information-theoretic terms. The renormalization point is illustrative.
