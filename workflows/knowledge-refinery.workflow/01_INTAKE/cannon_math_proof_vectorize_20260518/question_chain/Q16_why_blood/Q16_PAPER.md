## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Moral debt is conserved. A wrong does not vanish when committed; it has a real cost, and the cost has to be borne by someone. The candidates are: **(a)** the perpetrator pays (justice); **(b)** the victim absorbs the cost (typical default); **(c)** a third party absorbs the cost (sacrifice / atonement). Pretending the cost doesn't exist is option (b) by default — the cost goes to whoever is downstream, often the victim, often the future.

The "blood is barbaric" objection trades on disgust with the visible cost. But the visibility is the point. A framework that pretends costs are invisible relocates the cost; it does not eliminate it. Visible cost-bearing is the honest form of the conservation principle.

**T1 + Noether on Law 9 extension:** Moral conservation is structural, not optional. Time-translation symmetry in the moral domain gives a conserved quantity by Noether's theorem. The conserved quantity does not vanish on internal operations of the system; it can only be transferred. Atonement is the transfer mechanism. Blood is the symbolic register of the life-cost being transferred, not an arbitrary ritual cruelty.
