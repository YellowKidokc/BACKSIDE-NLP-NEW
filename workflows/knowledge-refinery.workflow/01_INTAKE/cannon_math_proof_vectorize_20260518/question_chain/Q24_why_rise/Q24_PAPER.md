## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

A claim about the defeat of death cannot be verified by anything internal to the death-domain. To verify that the cost has been paid in full and the system has been opened, the verifier must return from inside the system with the verification carried out. The resurrection, on the framework's reading, is exactly this: a domain-exit signature, in history, publicly available.

Without it, the claim that Christ's sacrifice resolved the moral conservation equation (Q23) is structurally unverifiable from outside theology. With it, the claim has a discriminating observable in the empirical record: a publicly attested return-from-death whose veracity can be argued over by historical methods.

Two distinct structural properties are doing work:
- **Closure of the ledger** — the cost was sufficient (Q23).
- **Verification of closure** — the closure is visible in our domain (Q24).

A solution to a conservation equation is not the same as evidence that the solution has been applied. The resurrection is the *applied* signature. It moves the claim from "this is what would resolve the equation if it occurred" to "the resolution has a witness in our domain."

**T1 extension:** The restoration operation has a publicly verifiable signature. This is what makes T1 a *discriminating* framework rather than a closed metaphysical system: it predicts that the actual restoration event must leave evidence in history, not merely in theory.

## Layer 5: Physics / Science Isomorphism

**Phase-transition completion check.** Superconductivity is verified by zero resistance — a binary observable that distinguishes the superconducting phase from the normal phase. A theory that predicts superconductivity is testable; the test is whether you find the zero-resistance state. The resurrection plays the analogous role for the atonement theory: it is the binary observable that distinguishes "the atonement occurred" from "the atonement is hypothetical." (BRIDGE: phase-transition completion test ↔ resurrection as completion test for the atonement; same architecture — both require a discriminating measurement to confirm the transition completed.)

**Falsifiable signatures vs. unfalsifiable theory.** A theory with no observable consequences is not science; a theology with no historical consequences is, on the framework's reading, similarly under-determined. The resurrection is the empirical horn of the Christian claim — without it, the claim is unfalsifiable (1 Corinthians 15:14 makes this explicit on the theology side). With it, the claim is exposed to historical falsification, which is exactly what makes it a *claim* rather than a position.

**Signal vs. theory.** Predicting a particle's existence is one thing; detecting it is another. The detection event is what converts the prediction from theory to confirmed measurement. The resurrection is the detection event for the atonement-prediction. Without the event, the theory remains theory. With the event, the theory has crossed the verification gate.

**Status flag:** The phase-transition-completion architecture is a tight process-isomorphism for "verification of a structural change." The falsifiability point is a general scientific-method claim applied to a theological claim — BRIDGE on the theological side, FORMAL on the methodological side.
