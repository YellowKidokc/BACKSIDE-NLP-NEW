# Q02 — Is Righteousness Just an Opinion?

## Layer 1: The Hook

**"Morality is just opinion."**

Whose opinion says that?

If morality is just opinion, then "morality is just opinion" is itself just an opinion. Why should I care about your opinion any more than anyone else's? If you say "my opinion is better" — you've just admitted there's a standard by which opinions can be better or worse. That's not opinion. That's structure.

Every attempt to reduce morality to preference ends up smuggling in a preference-hierarchy. The hierarchy is the thing you claimed didn't exist.

---

## Layer 2: The Human Story

Think about the last time you were treated unfairly. Not "I didn't get what I wanted" unfair. Genuinely unfair. Someone lied about you. Took credit for your work. Broke a promise that cost you something real.

You didn't think "I personally dislike this." You thought "This is WRONG." And you expected the other person to know it too. That's why betrayal hurts — not because they broke a rule you invented, but because they broke something both of you already knew.

If morality were pure opinion, injustice wouldn't feel like violation. It would feel like disagreement. But it doesn't. It feels like gravity pulling in the wrong direction. Because it is.

---

## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Moral realism: some states of affairs are genuinely better or worse than others, independent of any observer's preference. This is not the claim that everyone agrees — people disagree about math too. It is the claim that disagreement presupposes a correct answer.

T1 extension: if error is deviation from reference, then "wrong" is not arbitrary. It is measured distance from a reference state. The reference must exist for the deviation to be meaningful.

If there is no reference, there is no deviation. But everyone acts as if deviation exists. Therefore everyone acts as if reference exists — even those who deny it.

---

## Layer 4: Theological Register

"The law is written on their hearts." — Romans 2:15

Paul argues that Gentiles who never received the Mosaic law still "do by nature things required by the law" because conscience bears witness. This is not a claim that everyone is righteous. It is a claim that everyone has access to the reference — and suppresses it.

"The wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, who suppress the truth by their wickedness." — Romans 1:18

**Category precision:** Paul does not say people are ignorant of the moral law. He says they suppress the truth they already have. This is structurally identical to the metaphysical claim: the reference is known but denied, not absent but ignored.

Isaiah 5:20: "Woe to those who call evil good and good evil, who put darkness for light and light for darkness." — This presupposes that good/evil, light/darkness are real categories that can be mislabeled. The prophet doesn't say "your preferences differ from mine." He says you've swapped the labels on reality.

**Boundary:** This does not prove the Christian God. It proves moral reference is inescapable — which Scripture describes as "written on hearts" and "suppressed by wickedness." The bridge from inescapable reference to personal divine lawgiver requires later questions.

---

## Layer 5: Scientific/Physics Isomorphism

**Mathematics.** 2+2=4 is not a matter of opinion. It is a structural truth. Disagreement does not make it negotiable. Moral realism claims moral truths have the same structural status — not that they are equally precise or equally discoverable.

**Measurement theory.** Any measurement requires a standard. A meter stick is arbitrary in origin (it could have been a different length) but once fixed, deviations are real. Moral reference may have an origin (divine character, on the Christian account) but once fixed, deviations are real.

---

## Layer 6: Mathematical/Lean Skeleton

T1 (Closure.lean) proves reference-before-deviation. Q02 adds the claim that the reference is not merely logical but normative — it carries "ought" as well as "is."

This is a BRIDGE claim, not yet FORMAL. The Lean kernel proves structural dependency. The normative weight requires additional axioms (possibly agency/choice axioms in later modules).

Lane: FORMAL (structural dependency via T1) + BRIDGE (normative realism)

## Layer 7: Objections / Kill Conditions

| Objection | Response | Status |
|---|---|---|
| "Different cultures have different moral codes" | Different cultures have different measurements too. Doesn't mean length is arbitrary. | SURVIVES |
| "Moral disagreement proves there's no fact of the matter" | People disagree about history, science, and religion. Disagreement doesn't eliminate the subject. | SURVIVES |
| "Evolution explains moral intuition" | Explains origin, not validity. Evolution also explains why we see faces in clouds. | HONEST |
| "Moral realism requires God" | Not proven at Q02. Divine grounding comes later in the chain. | HONEST |

**Kill condition:** Construct a coherent moral system where "wrong" means nothing more than "I don't prefer this" and the system remains functional for large-scale cooperation.

**Self-defeating test:** "Morality is just opinion" is itself presented as a moral claim about how we ought to regard morality.
