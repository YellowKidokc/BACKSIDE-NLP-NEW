## Step 0 — Does Truth Come Before Error?

Claim:

> Error, falsehood, corruption, and evil are derivative. A lie requires a truth to distort. Disease requires a healthy body. Entropy requires order.

Nearest wrong answers:

- dualism
- process theology
- co-eternal chaos
- impersonal Platonic coherence

Current status:

> `SURVIVES`, but only as priority of reference/coherence.

Exception to add:

> This does not yet prove a personal ground of truth. A deist or Platonist may accept impersonal coherence. Do not force personality at this step.

## Step 1 — Does Creation / Initial Coherence Have To Be High?

Claim:

> The system must begin above zero coherence for degradation to be meaningful.

Formal sketch:

```text
C ≈ 1
S·C requires C > 0
```

Current status:

> `SURVIVES` as an initial-condition requirement.

Exception to add:

> Does this require ex nihilo creation, or only a coherent initial condition? The chain should not assume Genesis 1 too early.

## Step 4 — Does Holiness Create A Threshold?

Claim:

> Human moral judgment is graded, but divine holiness compatibility is thresholded.

Formal sketch:

```text
H_divine(x) = 0 if x contains unmediated sin-condition
G_human(x) = graded by harm, intent, consequence
```

Current status:

> `STRONG BRIDGE-CLAIM`.

Exception to add:

> Could holiness be asymptotic rather than binary? Catholic/purgatorial models may treat proximity as gradual purification. Possible repair: graded in experience, binary in unmediated compatibility/status.

## Step 5 — Can Justice And Mercy Separate?

Claim:

> Pure justice preserves standard but loses people. Pure mercy admits people unchanged and corrupts the destination. Restoration requires justice, mercy, and transformation together.

Formal sketch:

```text
J = 1, M = 1, T = 1
```

Current status:

> `STRONG BRIDGE-CLAIM`.

Exception to add:

> Decree-forgiveness objection: God forgives by authority without cost transfer. Repair must show decree-forgiveness may solve legal liability but not ontological transformation if the person remains unchanged.

## Step 6 — Does Law Diagnose Without Curing?

Claim:

> Law reveals, restrains, tutors, and condemns, but does not regenerate corrupted orientation.

Formal sketch:

```text
E_detect ↑
G_internal = 0
```

Current status:

> `BRIDGE-CLAIM`.

Exception to add:

> Jewish theology may contest “diagnostic only”; Torah is described as life-giving. Repair: law is good/perfect information and covenant light, but information alone is not internal regenerative power. The defect is in the patient, not the law.

## Step 7 — Does Sacrifice Cover But Not Complete?

Claim:

> OT sacrifice is covenantally real and provisional; it dampens exposure/decoherence but does not permanently complete transformation.

Formal sketch:

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·(1-α)·C
0 < α < 1
```

Current status:

> `STRONGER BRIDGE-CLAIM`.

Exception to add:

> Can `α < 1` be argued from OT alone without importing Hebrews? Proposed answer: repeated sacrifice, especially annual Day of Atonement, implies temporary operation. If `α = 1`, one sacrifice would suffice permanently.

## Step 8 — Is OT Triage Falsifiable?

Claim:

> Hard OT texts reflect containment, covenant preservation, signal-chain protection, consequence, judgment, or typology before cure.

Current status:

> `HIGH-RISK BRIDGE-CLAIM`.

Exception / kill condition:

> Find an OT act of divine judgment that cannot be mapped to containment, covenant preservation, signal-chain protection, consequence of violated natural law, judgment, discipline, or typology, and is still presented approvingly in the text. If found, the triage model is insufficient.

## Step 9 — Is Self-Repair Impossible?

Claim:

> A corrupted system cannot flip its own orientation using only self-generated operations.

Formal support:

```text
Sign invariance theorem
```

Current status:

> `STRONG IF FORMAL DEFINITIONS HOLD`.

Exception to add:

> What about repentance? Repair: repentance is `O > 0`, the turning/opening toward help, not the surgery itself. Without `G`, openness alone does not restore.
