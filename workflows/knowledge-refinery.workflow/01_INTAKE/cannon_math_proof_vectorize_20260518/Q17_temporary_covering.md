# Q17 — Why Didn't the Old Testament Sacrifices Work?

## Layer 1: The Hook

**"Why sacrifice again and again? If it works, once is enough."**

Exactly.

If the medicine works, you stop taking it. If the sacrifice worked, they'd have stopped sacrificing. The repetition is the confession that it didn't work yet. It covered. It didn't cure.

The Old Testament sacrificial system was a Band-Aid on a bullet wound. Real. Necessary. But not sufficient. It kept the patient alive until the surgeon arrived.

---

## Layer 2: The Human Story

You've taken medicine that treats symptoms but doesn't cure the disease. The pain goes away for a while. Then it comes back. You take it again. And again.

You don't resent the medicine. You're grateful for it. But you know it's not the cure. You keep looking for the real thing.

The sacrifices were like that. They addressed the symptom (guilt, separation, uncleanness) but not the disease (corrupted orientation, broken relationship, universal failure). They were real. They mattered. But they were provisional.

## Layer 3: Metaphysical Structure

Covering (α < 1) vs. completion (α = 1): if the covering coefficient α is less than 1, then the restoration term `O*G*(1-C)` dampens decoherence but does not complete restoration. The system remains in a covered-but-incomplete state.

Repetition is evidence of provisionality: if an operation must be repeated to maintain the covered state, then the operation did not produce permanent completion.

T1 extension: covering operations that are self-generated (within the error class) cannot produce completion. They can only delay or dampen the decay. True completion requires an external operator with sufficient magnitude to drive the system to convergence.

## Layer 4: Theological Register

"The law is only a shadow of the good things that are coming — not the realities themselves. For this reason it can never, by the same sacrifices repeated endlessly year after year, make perfect those who draw near to worship. Otherwise, would they not have stopped being offered? For the worshipers would have been cleansed once for all, and would no longer have felt guilty for their sins. But those sacrifices are an annual reminder of sins. It is impossible for the blood of bulls and goats to take away sins." — Hebrews 10:1-4

**Category precision:** The author of Hebrews is explicit: repetition proves provisionality. If the sacrifices took away sins, they would have stopped. The fact that they continued year after year is evidence that they did not complete the work.

"An annual reminder of sins" — the sacrifices did not erase guilt. They reminded Israel that guilt remained. The system was not designed to cure. It was designed to maintain relationship while the cure was prepared.

Leviticus 16 (Day of Atonement): The high priest enters the Most Holy Place once a year. Blood is sprinkled. Sins are covered. But next year, the ritual repeats. The covering is real but temporary.

Hebrews 9:25-26 — "Nor did he enter heaven to offer himself again and again, the way the high priest enters the Most Holy Place every year with blood that is not his own. Otherwise Christ would have had to suffer many times since the creation of the world. But he has appeared once for all at the culmination of the ages to do away with sin by the sacrifice of himself."

The contrast is structural: repetition vs. once-for-all. Animal blood vs. Christ's own blood. Annual vs. culminating. The Old Testament system was a pointer; Christ is the reality.

**Boundary:** This does not prove that Christ's sacrifice was sufficient. It proves that the Old Testament system was insufficient — which the Old Testament itself acknowledges through its repetition. The sufficiency of Christ requires the identity bridge (Q22-Q24).

**Category jump flag:** Moving from "OT sacrifices were provisional" to "Christ's sacrifice is final" requires the typological bridge (type/antitype) and the identity claim about Christ.

## Layer 5: Scientific/Physics Isomorphism

**Palliative care.** Pain management keeps the patient alive and comfortable while the cure is sought. It is real medicine, genuinely helpful, but not the cure. The Old Testament sacrifices were palliative — maintaining the covenant relationship while the ultimate remedy was prepared.

**Vaccine development.** Early treatments (quarantine, variolation) reduced mortality before the actual vaccine was developed. They were provisional, genuinely helpful, but not the solution. The sacrificial system was spiritual variolation.

## Layer 7: Objections / Kill Conditions

| Objection | Response | Status |
|---|---|---|
| "This makes the OT system a failure" | No. It makes it provisional — genuinely valuable but not final. Like childhood is provisional but not failed. | SURVIVES |
| "Why would God institute an insufficient system?" | Because humans needed covering while the cure was prepared. The system taught the cost of sin and the need for a substitute. | SURVIVES |
| "This is supersessionism" | The framework does not reject Israel. It claims the OT system pointed forward to fulfillment. The pointer is honored. | BRIDGE |
| "Other religions have sacrifice too" | Yes. The universality of sacrifice points to a universal intuition: sin costs, and cost must be paid. Christianity claims the universal intuition is fulfilled in a specific event. | SURVIVES |

**Kill condition:** Show that a repeated provisional system can produce permanent completion without an external, definitive intervention.

**Self-defeating test:** "If it worked, once is enough" applies equally to the critic's own moral efforts. If self-improvement worked, you'd be done by now.
