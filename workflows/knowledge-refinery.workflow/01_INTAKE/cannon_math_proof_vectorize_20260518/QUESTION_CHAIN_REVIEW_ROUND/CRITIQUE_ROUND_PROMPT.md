# Critique Round Prompt
## Question Chain Review

## Assignment

Read the question-chain material and critique the **argument**, not the partner
who wrote any layer.

This is not a personality review. It is not "Opus was wrong" or "Codex missed
it" or "Kimi overdid it." It is:

> Does this question survive contact with an intelligent skeptic, a serious
> Christian, a secular reader, a family member, and an academic critic?

## What To Critique

For each question you review, ask:

1. Does the hook actually self-defeat the objection?
2. Does the human story make the answer felt without manipulation?
3. Does the metaphysical structure follow, or does it smuggle a premise?
4. Does the theology align with Scripture without overclaiming?
5. Is the physics bridge isomorphism or just analogy?
6. Does the math/Lean layer say what is actually proven?
7. Are the objections strong enough?
8. What is the kill condition?
9. Is this public-ready, academic-only, needs revision, or should be merged/dropped?

## Tone

Be direct. Be surgical. Be fair.

Do not protect the argument from criticism. Protect it from being published too
early.

## Output

For every critique file:

- Name the question.
- Name the layer or whole-flow issue.
- State the strongest objection.
- State what breaks if the objection holds.
- Recommend a fix.
- Give a status recommendation.

## Status Recommendations

Use one of:

- `PUBLIC READY`
- `NEEDS REVISION`
- `ACADEMIC ONLY FOR NOW`
- `NEEDS FORMAL / SCIENCE SUPPORT`
- `MERGE WITH ANOTHER QUESTION`
- `DROP`

## Rule

Critique the paper in front of you.

Do not critique the person or partner who drafted a layer.

## After Critique: Owner Discretion

Critiques are serious inputs, not automatic commands.

After objections are written, each layer/question owner decides what to do with
them:

- **Adopt** - the critique is right; revise the layer or question.
- **Adapt** - the critique is partly right; preserve the point but improve the
  framing.
- **Defer** - the critique is real but belongs to a later layer, NT scaffold,
  academic appendix, Python model, Lean module, or historical argument.
- **Reject** - the critique does not hold; briefly state why.

No partner is required to integrate every objection into their workflow. But do
not ignore strong objections silently. If a critique is rejected or deferred,
leave a short note explaining the decision.

David may later review and agree with the critique, override the rejection, or
move the issue to another work lane.

The immediate goal is not to finish every objection tonight. The goal is to
bank them cleanly so the project can move on to the next job without losing the
pressure points.
