## Cross-cutting structural issues

The chain has six cross-cutting issues that aren't tied to any single question. Full treatment in the master file Section 3; brief summaries here:

**1. The chain isn't visibly a chain.** Each Q-file argues itself. Add `Comes from: Q##, Q##. Leads to: Q##.` headers to every Q-file. Cheap structural payoff.

**2. Phase 4 and Phase 8 are out of proportion.** Trap gets 5 questions; resolution gets 3 (one of which is prelude). Resolution should breathe at least as much as setup did. Add a Phase 8 question or split Q23.

**3. Two adjacency pairs.** Q08+Q09 (why beings who can refuse), Q18+Q19 (obedience under uncertainty). Merge candidates.

**4. Missing question: the affective objection.** "I logically agree but I feel fine." Numbness is data. Slot between Q14 and Q15, or strengthen Q10/Q15 L4 instead.

**5. The 7-layer architecture is overkill for family dinner.** Need per-format extracts (`Q##_family.md` = L1+L2 only, `Q##_thread.md` = L1+threadable L2+L3, `Q##_substack.md` = full 7-layer prose). Master file stays as working artifact; extracts are generated.

**6. Self-defeating hook framing is uneven.** ~7 are clean self-defeats, ~10 are challenges/category-corrections. Be honest about which are which; don't force the rest into the trap mold.

## Use-case mismatches

The chain advertises four delivery formats (family dinner, Twitter, Substack, academic). The 25-question count works for academic and Substack; it does NOT work for family dinner (25 dinners = 6 months) or TikTok (no feed sustains a sequenced chain).

Recommended:
- **Family minimum-viable chain:** 6-8 questions (Q00, Q10, Q11/Q12/Q13 trilemma, Q23, Q24, Q25).
- **Twitter sequence:** ~12 questions as threads, one per week, run on a defined timeline.
- **TikTok:** playlist of standalone videos, not a sequence.
- **Substack/academic:** full 25 with all 7 layers.
