## Section 2 — Per-Question Objections

### Q00 — truth before error
*   **Physics Objection:** The Second Law analogy is strong, but a skeptic will argue that entropy only increases in *closed* systems. They will claim the universe is not a closed system or that local order (life) is an "eddy" in the entropic flow that doesn't require a prior "Truth."
*   **Recommendation:** Strengthen L5 by specifying that even an "eddy" requires a flow to be part of. Truth is the "Flow"; error is the "Eddy."

### Q04 — resurrection uniqueness
*   **Opus Integration:** I agree with Opus. The "Five Features" is a debate-nerd move. 
*   **Narrative Objection:** The "Human Story" (L2) uses a "Magic Trick" analogy. Skeptics hate this because it implies God is a magician. 
*   **Recommendation:** Change the L2 analogy to a "Phase Change" or a "Broken Rule of Nature." A man walking through a fire that burns everyone else is a better structural isomorphism for a "New Phase" than a magic trick.

### Q07 — design god
*   **Logical Objection:** The "Constant of Integration (+C)" is brilliant but potentially opaque to non-math people. 
*   **Theological Objection:** Convergent design could be argued as "Convergent Evolutionary Psychology." Just because we all imagine a "Justice-God" doesn't mean He exists; it could mean our brains are hard-wired to want justice for survival.
*   **Recommendation:** The L3 Lock must explicitly differentiate "Hard-wired Desire" from "Structural Necessity."

### Q09 — worship robots
*   **Metaphysical Objection:** The argument "Forced praise is a mirror, not love" assumes God *wants* love. 
*   **Kill Condition:** If God is truly self-sufficient (Q08), why does He care if the love is "real" or "forced"? The chain needs to define God's "Relational Nature" as an axiom or a derivation before this question works.

### Q12 — pure mercy / Q13 — pure justice
*   **UX Objection:** These two are the most "theological-sounding" but they are actually the most "legal/structural." 
*   **Recommendation:** Ensure L2 stays in the "Parent/Child" or "Marriage" frame. "Just forgive your spouse for cheating with no change" (Q12) vs "Divorce them and take everything" (Q13). This makes the JMT constraint feel visceral.

### Q18 — trust precedes comprehension
*   **Structural Objection:** This is often used to shut down legitimate questions.
*   **Fix:** Ensure L7 (GPT) adds a kill condition for "Blind Credulity." Trust is rational calibrated commitment (Q25), not "shut up and obey."

### Q20 — ot triage
*   **Strong Objection:** The "Same Surgeon" analogy (L2) is the best we have, but it struggles with "Amputation of the innocent" (conquest narratives).
*   **Recommendation:** Don't try to solve the moral horror. Solve the *Signal Chain*. If the one village with the cure is infected, the whole world dies. The OT judgments are about **Signal Chain Integrity**, not ethnic preference.

### Q22 — why not teacher
*   **Physics Objection:** The "Information vs hardware" bridge is perfect. 
*   **Recommendation:** Lean into the "Computer Virus" analogy. You can't fix a BIOS-level virus by typing a "BE GOOD" text document into Notepad. You need a hardware reset.
