## Repeated Critique Clusters

### 1. Too Many Questions

The 25-question chain is too long for family dinner, TikTok, or a first-contact public doorway.

**Decision:** Keep the 25-question master chain. Generate shorter delivery variants:

- Minimum viable family chain: 6–8 questions.
- Public/X chain: 8–12 strongest gates.
- Academic/Substack chain: full 25 with all layers.
- Master artifact: all 7 layers retained for audit and reuse.

### 2. Formal Strength Is Uneven

Some steps are Lean-adjacent or formally locked; others are theological, historical, rhetorical, or bridge claims.

**Decision:** Add visible status labels to every question:

- `FORMAL`
- `BRIDGE`
- `HISTORICAL`
- `THEOLOGICAL AXIOM`
- `OPEN / NEEDS WORK`

This prevents the chain from pretending every step has the same proof weight.

### 3. Title / Hook Semantics Need Tightening

Some hooks sound like semantic traps instead of structural locks.

**Decision:** Stop advertising every question as self-defeating. Use three hook classes:

- `SELF-DEFEATING`
- `STRUCTURAL CHALLENGE`
- `CATEGORY CORRECTION`

This preserves the strong hooks without forcing weaker ones into the wrong mold.

### 4. Personal God Is Not Yet Forced

The chain narrows toward coherence, reference, externality, restoration, and relational repair. It does not yet by itself force personal God, Trinity, Christ, or incarnation.

**Decision:** Add a dedicated personhood/covenant gate before leaning too hard on marriage, love, agency, or relational restoration.

### 5. Jesus / Gospel Must Be Proclaimed, Not Smuggled

The formal chain diagnoses the need and narrows the solution profile. It does not automatically make someone believe in God or Christ by formal necessity alone.

**Decision:** Add an explicit gospel/proclamation lane:

> The formal chain shows the wound and the shape of the cure. The Christian claim is that Jesus is the cure.

This is not a weakness. It is boundary discipline.

### 6. Resolution Is Underbuilt

The chain spends several questions building the justice/mercy/transformation trap, then asks Q23/Q24 to carry too much of the solution.

**Decision:** Split or expand the cross/resurrection resolution:

- Why death?
- What does the death accomplish?
- How is resurrection verification?
- Why this operator rather than rival operators?

### 7. Missing Affective Objection

The listener may grant the logic and still say, "I do not feel like I need this."

**Decision:** Add either a new question or an inserted beat:

> Numbness is data, not disproof.

This handles the most common real-world failure mode.

Satisfy the critiques by separating:

- master chain from public front door,
- formal theorem from bridge claim,

- theism profile from Christianity-specific fulfillment.

The repeated objections are not a death sentence. They are a revision map.
