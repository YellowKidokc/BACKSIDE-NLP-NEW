# Objection-First Proof Protocol

Run context: 2026-05-18  
Purpose: require every major claim in the Christianity Unique Convergence / OT Diagnostic paper to face its strongest nearby wrong answers before final proof, Lean 4 formalization, or public-facing claims.

## Core Rule

Do not start by proving the claim.

Start by writing what would make the claim wrong.

The proof gets stronger only when:

1. The best objection is stated without caricature.
2. The nearest plausible wrong answer is allowed to compete.
3. The claim either survives, weakens, or gets ejected.
4. The reason for survival is more specific than “it still feels right.”

## Claim Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `SURVIVES` | Objection fails under stated assumptions; claim can remain strong. |
| `WEAKENED` | Claim remains useful but must narrow scope or soften wording. |
| `BRIDGE-ONLY` | Claim can connect layers but cannot function as direct proof. |
| `DEFERRED` | Claim belongs to a later NT/historical/formal module. |
| `EJECTED` | Objection succeeds; claim should be removed or replaced. |

## Required Row Format

Every major claim needs this row:

| ID | Claim | Lane | Best Objection | Nearest Wrong Answer | What Would Prove Us Wrong | Current Reply | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Definitions:

- **Best Objection:** the serious critic’s strongest attack.
- **Nearest Wrong Answer:** the alternative explanation closest to the truth, not a strawman.
- **What Would Prove Us Wrong:** the kill condition.
- **Current Reply:** why the claim still holds, weakens, or dies.

## Objection Categories

Use all categories that apply. Some claims need one objection; some need ten.

### 1. Hidden Premise Objection

The claim smuggles in Christianity, theism, moral realism, or resurrection before deriving it.

Test:

```text
Could a non-Christian accept the premises without already accepting the conclusion?
```

### 2. Category Error Objection

The claim treats math, physics, theology, history, or narrative as if they prove the same kind of thing.

Test:

```text
Is this AUDITABLE-HOW, INTERPRETIVE-WHY, BRIDGE-CLAIM, HISTORICAL-WEIGHT, or NARRATIVE-REFRAIN?
```

### 3. Rival Explanation Objection

Another model explains the same data with fewer or better assumptions.

Test:

```text
What is the second-best answer?
Why does it fail?
```

### 4. Scope Inflation Objection

The claim is locally true but overstated globally.

Test:

```text
Does this prove all cases, most cases, this case, or only a possible pattern?
```

### 5. Scripture Interpretation Objection

The claim relies on proof-texting, ignores context, or contradicts orthodox boundaries.

Test:

```text
Does the verse support the exact claim, or only a weaker version?
```

### 6. Historical Underdetermination Objection

The evidence does not force one historical explanation.

Test:

```text
Does this establish fact, plausibility, uniqueness pressure, or only coherence?
```

### 7. Formal Mapping Objection

The math is true but not constrained by the story.

Test:

```text
What mathematical object is this?
What story event does it correspond to?
What would break the mapping?
```

### 8. Moral Intuition Objection

The claim violates moral intuition so strongly that it becomes implausible or monstrous.

Test:

```text
Have we separated human gradient from divine threshold?
```

### 9. Continuity Objection

The solution solves the problem by replacing the system/person rather than restoring them.

Test:

```text
Is continuity preserved across the repair?
```

### 10. Agency Objection

The solution overrides free will instead of preserving voluntary coupling.

Test:

```text
Can the repair be received or refused?
```

### 11. Justice/Mercy Objection

Justice, mercy, or transformation is dropped.

Test:

```text
Does the solution preserve justice, enact mercy, and transform the person?
```

### 12. OT Triage Objection

The containment model becomes a blanket excuse for hard texts.

Test:

```text
Which OT mechanism applies here: restraint, judgment, covenant discipline, typology, or prophetic exposure?
```

### 13. Sacrifice Objection

The covering/completion distinction is unclear or morally troubling.

Test:

```text
Is sacrifice dampening, shielding, substitution, typology, covenant sign, or completion?
```

### 14. Mediator Objection

External help does not necessarily imply incarnation/mediator.

Test:

```text
Can a non-incarnational operator satisfy external origin, internal validity, voluntary coupling, justice, mercy, and continuity?
```

### 15. Uniqueness Objection

The argument claims Christianity uniquely fits before all rivals are tested.

Test:

```text
Have rival systems been given their strongest possible version?
```

### 16. AI-Convergence Objection

AI agreement reflects training data or prompt direction, not truth.

Test:

```text
Is this being used only as internal structure testing?
```

### 17. Lean/Formalization Objection

Lean proves a formal theorem, not theological reality.

Test:

```text
What exactly does the theorem prove, and what does it not prove?
```

### 18. Why/How Objection

Science may answer how but not why.

Test:

```text
Is this a how-claim, why-claim, or bridge-claim?
```

### 19. Death-Boundary Objection

Death-boundary logic belongs to NT fulfillment and may be premature in OT diagnostic paper.

Test:

```text
Is this deriving the unsolved need, or already solving it?
```

### 20. Psychological Pattern-Matching Objection

The convergence may be human/AI pattern completion rather than objective structure.

Test:

```text
Which domains are genuinely independent, and where are we selecting matches?
```

## First Claims To Attack

Start with these because they carry the most load:

1. Truth before error.
2. Free will makes rejection possible.
3. Universal failure.
4. Human-gradient vs divine-threshold.
5. Justice/mercy simultaneous constraint.
6. Law diagnoses but does not cure.
7. Sacrifice covers but does not complete.
8. OT triage/containment.
9. External grace required.
10. Mediator specificity.
11. Death-boundary need.
12. Christianity as best-known fit.

## Proof Strength Rule

A claim only becomes stronger after objection if the reply narrows the claim to exactly what survived.

Bad:

```text
The objection failed, so the original broad claim stands.
```

Good:

```text
The objection forced us to weaken the claim from mathematical uniqueness to best-known fit under stated constraints.
```

## Final Proof Gate

Before final proof, every claim must have:

1. lane label
2. strongest objection
3. nearest wrong answer
4. kill condition
5. current reply
6. status
7. scope boundary

If a claim lacks any of those, it is not final-proof ready.
