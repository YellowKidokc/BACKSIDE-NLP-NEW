## Proposed Title

### Primary Title

**The Old Testament Diagnostic Chain: A Formal Coherence Derivation of Sin, Law, Sacrifice, Justice, Mercy, and the Need for External Grace**

### Subtitle

**A Lean 4–Oriented, Objection-First Map of the Master Equation Across Theology, Information, and Moral Coherence**

## Shorter Working Title

**OT Diagnostic Chain — Formal Coherence Derivation**

## Avoid For Now

Do not title the current version:

> Proof of Christianity

or:

> Mathematical Proof of the Gospel

Those may be future targets, but the current durable claim is narrower and stronger:

> The OT diagnostic chain formally derives the shape of the problem and the required solution-profile.

## Opening Claim Stack

This project should begin by saying what survives even if the maximal proof fails.

### Claim 1 — Theology Can Be Formally Structured

Christian theology is not merely poetic, emotional, or devotional language. Its core claims can be expressed as constraints, operators, thresholds, boundary conditions, and failure modes.

### Claim 2 — Spiritual Claims Can Be Pressure-Tested

Claims about sin, grace, sacrifice, justice, mercy, law, repentance, and restoration can be tested by objections, kill conditions, rival explanations, and formal mappings.

### Claim 3 — Information Theory Provides A Bridge Register

Information language gives a shared bridge between physics and theology:

- truth vs error
- signal vs noise
- coherence vs decoherence
- transmission vs distortion
- correction vs collapse
- bandwidth vs blockage

This does not prove theology by itself, but it gives theology a rational audit surface.

### Claim 4 — Error Is Derivative Of Prior Coherence

Falsehood, corruption, and disorder require a prior truth/order/coherence to distort. This can be stated philosophically, theologically, and information-theoretically without immediately forcing the full Christian conclusion.

### Claim 5 — Moral Coherence Behaves Like An Integrated System

The framework argues that moral/spiritual coherence is not merely additive. Some factors appear jointly necessary. If one required condition reaches zero, the integrated state may collapse.

This remains one of the most important claims to prove, not assume.

### Claim 6 — Human Moral Judgment And Divine Holiness Use Different Registers

Human beings rightly judge by graded harm, intent, knowledge, and consequence.

Divine holiness introduces a threshold question:

> Can this state survive unmediated contact with perfect holiness?

These are not the same register.

### Claim 7 — Justice And Mercy Are Not Rival Sentiments

Justice and mercy can be modeled as simultaneous constraints in a restoration problem. If justice is dropped, wrong is ignored. If mercy is dropped, people are lost. If transformation is dropped, the destination is corrupted again.

### Claim 8 — Law Can Diagnose Without Regenerating

Law can reveal, name, restrain, instruct, and condemn. That does not mean law is bad. It means information alone does not supply regenerative power to a corrupted orientation.

### Claim 9 — Sacrifice Can Be Modeled As Real But Provisional Covering

The covering/completion distinction can be formalized:

```text
covering = dampening/restraint of S
completion = restoration through O·G(1-C)
```

This allows OT sacrifice to be treated as covenantally real without making it final-form completion.

### Claim 10 — Self-Repair Has A Formal Failure Mode

The framework can express why a corrupted system cannot reverse its own deepest orientation by operations generated only from the corrupted state.

This does not eliminate repentance; it locates repentance as openness/reorientation toward external grace, not the external grace itself.

### Claim 11 — The OT Can Derive A Solution-Profile Without Solving The NT

The Old Testament diagnostic chain can derive the shape of the required solution:

```text
external origin
internal validity
voluntary coupling
continuity preservation
justice preservation
mercy enactment
person transformation
death-boundary answer
```

It does not need to fully prove incarnation, cross, resurrection, Pentecost, or New Creation in the OT paper.

### Claim 12 — The Master Equation Gives A Shared Story-Form

The Master Equation frame:

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·C
```

does not merely decorate theology with math. It proposes a shared structure:

- corruption degrades existing coherence
- grace enters as non-self-generated restoration
- openness governs reception
- distance from completion creates restoration potential
- final coherence is approached through transformation, not relocation

This is a bridge claim until fully formalized.

### Claim 13 — The Framework Produces Rational Falsification Surfaces

Even if the maximal derivation fails, the framework succeeds in generating concrete places where theology can be attacked:

- product vs additive coherence
- decree-forgiveness
- impersonal coherence
- law as life versus law as cure
- sacrifice covering versus completion
- triage falsification
- mediator uniqueness
- grace operator ontology
- resurrection verification
- internal correction / Pentecost prediction

That is already a major rational contribution.

### Claim 14 — The Maximal Claim Is Not Required For The Lower Claims To Matter

If the full Christian uniqueness proof fails, the lower claims may still survive.

The project can still show:

> Christian theology has a deep formal coherence structure that can be mapped, tested, narrowed, and challenged using information theory, moral logic, mathematical dynamics, and objection-first reasoning.

That claim alone is significant.

## Recommended Opening Paragraph

This work does not begin by claiming that Christianity has been mathematically proven. It begins with a more modest and more testable claim: that the theological structure of sin, law, sacrifice, justice, mercy, repentance, grace, and restoration can be expressed as a formal coherence problem. The Old Testament diagnostic chain is treated as a pre-cure system: it names the rupture, exposes the insufficiency of self-repair, distinguishes covering from completion, and derives the profile of the needed solution. Whether the New Testament uniquely fulfills that profile is a later and harder question. But even if the maximal proof fails, the lower result remains: Christian theology can be given a rational audit surface where spiritual claims are tested by operators, thresholds, boundaries, objections, and kill conditions rather than protected by vagueness.

## Proof Posture

The desired outcome is not “make the proof pass.”

The desired outcome is:

```text
state the strongest claim that survives the strongest objection
```

If the maximal proof survives, promote it.

If it fails, preserve every lower-level claim that still holds.
