## Critical Audit Warnings

This draft should not be used publicly as-is.

### 1. Source Gaps

Many numeric tolerances and probability bounds need citations.

Before proof use, each row needs:

```text
claim | source | citation | current consensus? | contested? | confidence
```

### 2. Probability Overclaim Risk

Compound probability is dangerous.

Even when parameters are independent in current models, probability distributions depend on measure assumptions.

Avoid saying:

> absolute empirical impossibility

until the measure problem is addressed.

Safer:

> under standard naive independence assumptions, the chance estimate becomes unimaginably small; however, exact probabilities depend on the assumed measure.

### 3. Independence Audit Needed

The draft correctly notes derivative vs independent conditions, but the count of 34–40 independent variables needs verification.

Required audit:

- truly independent
- derivative
- partially dependent
- local historical contingency
- biological/evolutionary optimization
- theorem/limit rather than parameter

### 4. Fine-Tuning Does Not Equal Teleology By Itself

Fine-tuning can pressure naturalism, but it does not automatically prove purpose, God, or Christian theology.

Lane label:

> `HISTORICAL/SCIENTIFIC-WEIGHT + BRIDGE-CLAIM`

not:

> final theological theorem.

### 5. Biology Claims Need Special Care

Claims like genetic-code optimality, quantum biology error correction, and near-100% photosynthetic efficiency are nuanced and contested in strength.

These should be checked carefully before publication.
