# Opus Attack Brief — OT Derivation / Lane-4 Proof

Purpose: give a fresh reviewer the strongest case against the Old Testament diagnostic derivation before any final proof or Lean 4 submission.

## What This Is

This is not the final proof.

This is the attack surface.

The current thesis is that the Old Testament can be read as a diagnostic/pre-cure structure:

> truth/coherence → creation/free will → failure → holiness threshold → justice/mercy → law/sacrifice/triage → need for external mediator

The job is to find where that chain breaks, overclaims, imports Christianity too early, or maps math onto story without enough constraint.

## Current Strongest Version

The derivation should be treated as an OT diagnostic argument, not a full NT derivation.

It may derive the shape of the needed solution:

```text
External(G) ∧ JusticePreserved ∧ MercyEnacted ∧ PersonTransformed ∧ DeathBoundaryAddressed
```

It should not yet claim to fully derive:

- incarnation
- cross
- resurrection
- Pentecost / distributed Spirit
- sanctification
- New Creation

Those belong to the NT fulfillment pass unless clearly marked as preview.

## Master Equation Frame

Current bridge equation:

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·C
```

Working meanings:

- `C` = coherence / completion variable
- `S` = sin/decoherence pressure
- `G` = grace / external restoration operator
- `O` = openness / voluntary coupling
- `(1-C)` = distance from full coherence

Attack question:

> Does the story actually require this equation, or are we fitting a powerful equation onto the story after the fact?

## Lane Labels

Every claim must be labeled before judging it:

- `AUDITABLE-HOW`: mechanism/prediction/falsifier available.
- `INTERPRETIVE-WHY`: theological/teleological meaning claim.
- `BRIDGE-CLAIM`: says the how-pattern and why-pattern are structurally isomorphic.
- `HISTORICAL-WEIGHT`: raises/lowers plausibility but does not compel certainty.
- `NARRATIVE-REFRAIN`: rhetorically important but not proof by itself.

Attack question:

> Which claims are being scored in the wrong lane?

## Main Claims To Break

### 1. Truth Before Error

Claim:

> Error, falsehood, corruption, and evil are derivative of prior truth/coherence/goodness.

Attack:

- Does this prove only logical priority, not moral priority?
- Does it secretly import classical theism?
- Can a dualist or process-theology model resist this?
- Does coherence imply personal righteousness, or only impersonal order?
- Could a Platonist/deist accept impersonal coherence without accepting personal God?

### 2. Creation / Coherence

Claim:

> The system begins in a high-coherence/good state.

Attack:

- Is `C ≈ 1` literal, Eden-local, or idealized?
- Does the math require an initial perfect state, or merely a local coherent state?
- Can a non-theological cosmology satisfy the same formal condition?
- Does this require ex nihilo creation, or only a high-coherence initial condition?

### 3. Free Will / Agency

Claim:

> Genuine relationship requires freedom, and freedom permits rejection.

Attack:

- Are `W` agency and `O` openness wrongly collapsed?
- Can love be meaningful without libertarian freedom?
- Does the argument depend on a disputed free-will model?
- What is the minimum freedom threshold: libertarian choice, compatibilist ownership, or functional capacity for genuine misalignment?

### 4. Universal Failure

Claim:

> Under genuine freedom, human failure is structurally universal.

Attack:

- Product collapse proves what failure does, not that everyone fails.
- Is failure logically inevitable or only historically universal?
- What about children, incapacity, ignorance, moral development?
- Does inherited condition / corporate solidarity need a separate formal argument from personal-act failure?

### 5. Holiness Threshold

Claim:

> Human moral judgment is graded, but divine holiness has a threshold: all unmediated sin is incompatible with perfect holiness.

Attack:

- Does Scripture support threshold incompatibility without flattening all sins?
- Can the framework preserve degrees of guilt/consequence?
- Does the threshold make God appear morally disproportionate?
- Could holiness be asymptotic or purgatorial rather than binary?

Required boundary:

> All sin is equally incapable of surviving unmediated perfect holiness; sins are not identical in harm, intent, or temporal consequence.

### 6. Justice/Mercy Constraint

Claim:

> Pure justice loses the people; pure mercy corrupts the destination; restoration requires justice, mercy, and transformation together.

Attack:

- Is “someone always pays” too crude?
- What exactly is conserved: debt, moral displacement, relational rupture, consequence?
- Could God forgive by authority without cost transfer?
- Is the justice/mercy tension a Christian assumption?
- Does decree-forgiveness solve legal guilt but fail ontological transformation?

### 7. Law Diagnoses But Does Not Cure

Claim:

> Law reveals, restrains, tutors, and condemns, but does not regenerate.

Attack:

- Does this accidentally make the Law negative?
- Does it understate OT grace?
- Does Jewish theology contest the premise?
- Is this relying on NT interpretation too early?
- How does the chain handle Torah as life-giving and soul-reviving without collapsing law into regenerative power?

### 8. Sacrifice Covers But Does Not Complete

Claim:

> OT sacrifice is covenantally real and provisional; it covers/dampens but does not complete.

Proposed bridge:

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·(1-α)·C
0 < α < 1
```

Attack:

- What exactly is `α`: ritual, covenantal, communal, individual, temporal?
- Does sacrifice dampen sin, consequence, guilt, wrath, or relational exposure?
- Can we prove `α < 1` without importing Hebrews too early?
- Does this respect OT sacrifice as real rather than fake?
- Does repeated sacrifice, especially annual Day of Atonement, provide OT-internal evidence that `α < 1`?

### 9. OT Triage / Containment

Claim:

> Hard OT texts reflect pre-cure containment, judgment, covenant preservation, and triage.

Attack:

- Does this become a blanket excuse?
- Which hard text maps to which mechanism?
- Are flood, conquest, exile, sacrifice, and law too different for one operator?
- What would falsify the triage model?
- Can we name a strict kill condition instead of letting the model absorb every hard text?

Proposed kill condition:

> Find an OT act of divine judgment that cannot be mapped to containment, covenant preservation, signal-chain protection, consequence of violated natural law, judgment, discipline, or typology, and is still presented approvingly in the text.

### 10. Self-Repair Impossible

Claim:

> A corrupted system cannot flip its own orientation by self-generated operations.

Attack:

- What is the sign/orientation variable?
- Which operations count as self-generated?
- Does the theorem prove spiritual inability, or only formal invariance?
- Can repentance be self-generated, grace-enabled, or both?
- Is repentance `O > 0` rather than the restorative operation itself?

### 11. External Grace Required

Claim:

> Restoration requires a non-self-generated grace operator.

Attack:

- Does external help have to be personal?
- Could it be impersonal reset, cosmic law, enlightenment, or moral education?
- Does this prove grace, or only non-self-originating intervention?

### 12. Mediator Specificity

Claim:

> The needed operator must have external origin, internal validity, voluntary coupling, and continuity preservation.

Proposed bridge:

```text
External(G) ∧ SystemCompatible(G) ∧ VoluntarilyCoupled(G) ∧ ContinuityPreserving(G)
```

Attack:

- Can a non-incarnational operator satisfy the same constraints?
- Does internal validity require shared human nature?
- Could divine authority satisfy justice without incarnation?
- Does this prove mediator logic, or only narrow toward it?

Current safe status:

> BRIDGE-CLAIM, not final theorem.

### 13. Death Boundary

Claim:

> Death is the unsolved final boundary that the OT diagnostic chain points toward.

Attack:

- Is this OT derivation or NT fulfillment?
- Does death-boundary logic require resurrection evidence already?
- Should death be framed only as unresolved need in this paper?

### 14. Christianity Fit

Claim:

> Christianity is the best-known fit under the derived constraints.

Attack:

- Are rivals tested seriously?
- Is “unique” overclaimed?
- Is Jesus introduced before the profile is derived?
- Does history provide enough weight, or only coherence?

Safe wording:

> best-known fit under stated constraints

Not yet:

> mathematically unique proof of Christianity

## Additional Missing Attack Surfaces

These were identified after reading the attack brief against the full session thread. They should be tested before any positive proof attempt.

### 15. Why Multiplicative?

Claim:

> Total coherence is multiplicative/product-like, not additive/score-like.

Formal issue:

```text
χ = ∏ C_i
```

If any required factor reaches zero, total coherence collapses.

Attack:

- Why product rather than sum?
- Does product form smuggle in the claim that all domains must cohere simultaneously?
- Could moral coherence be additive, where failure in one domain lowers the score but does not collapse the system?
- Is “one zero collapses all” mathematically derived or chosen?

Nearest wrong answer:

```text
χ = Σ C_i
```

or a weighted additive/hybrid model where partial coherence survives local failure.

Kill condition:

> Find a case where genuine total moral/spiritual coherence survives while one required coherence variable is truly zero.

Repair if it survives:

> Product form is justified only if the variables are jointly necessary conditions for integrated coherence, not optional virtues in a moral scorecard.

Status:

> `UNTESTED / LOAD-BEARING`

### 16. Why Restoration Instead Of Re-Creation?

Claim:

> The solution must restore the existing broken system/persons rather than replace them with a new creation.

Attack:

- If God creates ex nihilo once, why not wipe and restart?
- Why is restoration required instead of replacement?
- Does continuity preservation come from theology, math, or moral intuition?
- Does the flood narrative function as evidence that restart does not solve the internal condition?

Nearest wrong answer:

> God can solve corruption by deleting the corrupted system and making a clean one.

Kill condition:

> Show that replacement satisfies justice, mercy, agency, and continuity as well as or better than restoration.

Repair if it survives:

> Replacement fails because the persons who sinned are not restored; continuity is broken, agency is bypassed, and mercy toward existing persons is not enacted.

Status:

> `BRIDGE-CLAIM / NEEDS FORMAL CONTINUITY CONDITION`

### 17. The `O = 0` Prediction

Claim:

> Grace is not coercive; if openness/voluntary coupling is zero, the restoration term vanishes.

Formal issue:

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·C
O = 0 -> dC/dt = -S·C
```

Attack:

- Is there biblical or empirical evidence of grace operating without voluntary coupling?
- What about infants, mental incapacity, pre-conscious states, severe trauma, or ignorance?
- Does Calvinist irresistible grace imply `O` is itself generated by `G`?
- If `G` can force `O > 0`, is the equation missing a prior term?

Nearest wrong answer:

> Grace can operate unilaterally without any receptive coupling.

Kill condition:

> Find a required case where restoration occurs while `O = 0` in every meaningful sense.

Repair if it survives:

> Distinguish ordinary voluntary coupling from grace-enabled openness, and add a separate account for incapacity cases.

Status:

> `UNTESTED / HIGH-RISK THEOLOGICAL BOUNDARY`

### 18. Covenant Continuity / Why Does God Not Walk Away?

Claim:

> God continues engagement with the broken system across the OT period.

Attack:

- The equation allows grace withdrawal: `dG/dt = 0` or `G = 0`.
- What formally requires continued divine engagement?
- Does triage float on narrative unless love/covenant is a field condition?
- Is continued engagement necessary, freely chosen, or covenantally promised?

Nearest wrong answer:

> God could abandon the corrupted system without contradiction.

Kill condition:

> Show that divine withdrawal satisfies holiness, justice, love, covenant, and purpose without remainder.

Repair if it survives:

> Continued engagement is required if love/covenant is not an optional term but the field condition in which the equation runs.

Status:

> `BRIDGE-CLAIM / NEEDS LOVE-FIELD OR COVENANT AXIOM`

### 19. Resurrection As Verification Signal

Claim:

> Resurrection is not only a solution to death but the valid confirmation signal that mediation succeeded.

Formal issue:

```text
χ ≠ 0 after the death event
```

Attack:

- Could divine testimony alone verify the payment?
- Could transformed followers verify it?
- Could prophecy fulfillment verify it?
- Could atonement succeed invisibly without resurrection?
- Does this belong to OT diagnostic scope or NT fulfillment scope?

Nearest wrong answer:

> The payment can be valid and verified without resurrection.

Kill condition:

> Provide a verification signal other than post-death coherence that confirms death-boundary defeat with equal or greater force.

Repair if it survives:

> OT diagnostic may derive the need for a death-boundary verification signal; NT fulfillment identifies resurrection as that signal.

Status:

> `DEFERRED-NT / BUT OT MAY PREDICT NEED`

### 20. Pentecost / Distributed Internal Correction Prediction

Claim:

> The OT diagnostic chain should predict the need for distributed internal correction, even if it cannot name Pentecost yet.

Formal issue:

External-only rescue produces oscillation:

```text
rescue -> coherence rise -> decay -> crisis -> rescue
```

Attack:

- Could external intervention alone stabilize the system?
- Does Judges actually function as evidence of an unstable oscillator?
- Is “distributed Spirit” being imported too early from NT theology?
- Can law, covenant, prophets, or sacrifice provide internal correction without Pentecost?

Nearest wrong answer:

> Repeated external intervention is sufficient for stable restoration.

Kill condition:

> Find an external-only OT mechanism that produces convergence rather than oscillation or decay.

Repair if it survives:

> OT diagnostic derives the need for an internal correction term; NT fulfillment later names the mechanism.

Status:

> `BRIDGE-CLAIM / OT-PREDICTIVE, NT-FULFILLED`
