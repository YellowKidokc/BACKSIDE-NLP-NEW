# Falsification Rules

Registry: `PREDICTION_REGISTRY_2026-05-18.md`  
Initialized UTC: 2026-05-19T00:31:00Z

## Immutable ID Rule

Once a prediction ID is published, do not edit the row in place.

Allowed:

- add a new amended prediction row,
- add a correction note with a new timestamp,
- mark a prediction as weakened, falsified, confirmed, or superseded.

Not allowed:

- silently altering the original claim,
- changing the falsifier after results are known,
- reusing a retired prediction ID.

## Confirmation Rule

A prediction is not confirmed by an anecdote alone.

Minimum confirmation requires:

1. observable case or dataset,
2. predeclared measurable outcome,
3. comparison against plausible rival explanation,
4. written result with date and method.

## Falsification Rule

A prediction is falsified or weakened when a case meets its stated falsifier under fair interpretation.

Do not rescue a prediction by redefining terms after the fact.

## Register Rule

Keep registers separate:

- Lean/formal proof,
- human-domain empirical prediction,
- theological structural prediction,
- physics-adjacent speculation,
- novel physics.

No result in one register automatically proves another register.

## Boundary Rule

If a prediction says "not yet ready," do not treat it as a hidden positive prediction.

TP-PRED-0010 is a restraint claim: no responsible novel particle-physics prediction is currently released.
