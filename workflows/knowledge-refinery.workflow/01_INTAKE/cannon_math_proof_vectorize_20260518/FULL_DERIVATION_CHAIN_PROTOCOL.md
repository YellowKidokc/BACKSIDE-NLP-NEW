Purpose: test Theophysics from axioms to end-claims by forcing every step to compete against the best wrong answer.

## Core Rule

Do not ask whether an AI partner believes the framework. Ask whether each step survives constraint pressure.

Each step must compare:

- the Theophysics claim;
- the strongest plausible rival;
- why that rival looks attractive;
- where the rival fails structurally;
- what the Theophysics claim preserves;
- what still needs formalization.

## Best-Wrong-Answer Principle

The rival answer must be good enough that a serious critic might prefer it. Weak objections do not count.

Examples:

- Infinite regress is too easy; test brute fact, self-grounding logic, and impersonal necessity.
- "Justice and mercy are opposites" is too easy; test restoration without cost, restorative justice without substitution, and debt cancellation by authority.
- "Fruits are just virtues" is too easy; test Aristotelian virtue ethics, Buddhist non-attachment, and secular flourishing as rival state-space models.
- "Moral conservation is metaphor" is too easy; test legal restitution, psychological trauma transfer, social cost displacement, and information-preservation analogs.

## Pass Conditions

A step earns confidence only if:

- independent AI partners reconstruct similar dependencies;
- the rival answer is strong;
- the rival fails by a named structural error;
- the Theophysics claim preserves more constraints;
- the surviving claim is not overstated.
