# Pause-Gap Bridge Notes — Sacrifice and Mediator

Run context: 2026-05-18  
Purpose: capture proposed bridge repairs for the two hardest pre-Lean gaps: sacrifice covering vs completion, and mediator specificity.

## Gap 1 — Sacrifice Covering vs Completion

### Problem

The story distinguishes OT sacrifice as real covering and Christ as final completion.

The math needed a distinction between:

- temporary protection / forbearance
- permanent transformation / completion

Without that distinction, “animal sacrifice covers but does not complete” sounds theological but not formal.

### Proposed Formal Bridge

Use two different mathematical states.

#### Covering

Covering dampens the sin/decoherence term but does not eliminate or reverse it.

```text
dC/dt = O·G(1-C) - S·(1-α)·C
```

Where:

- `S` = sin/decoherence pressure
- `α` = covering factor
- `0 < α < 1`

Interpretation:

```text
covering = S is suppressed, not removed
```

The infection remains, but damage is restrained. This maps to OT sacrifice as covenantally real but provisional.

#### Completion

Completion is not merely stronger suppression of `S`.

Completion introduces or activates the Grace operator as a restoration engine:

```text
O·G(1-C)
```

Interpretation:

```text
completion = system relationship to sin changes
```

The cross/resurrection layer does not simply make `S = 0` in ordinary lived experience. It changes whether `S` has final dominion and whether restoration can proceed from inside the healed covenantal state.

### Why This Helps

This gives a formal distinction:

| State | Math | Theology |
|---|---|---|
| Covering | dampened `S` | protected, restrained, provisional |
| Completion | activated `O·G(1-C)` | transformed, restored, final |

### Still Needs Testing

- Does OT sacrifice dampen `S`, delay consequence, shield covenant relation, or all three?
- Is `α` individual, communal, covenantal, or ritual-contextual?
- Does completion belong entirely to NT formalism, with OT only deriving the insufficiency of `α < 1`?

## Gap 2 — Mediator Specificity

### Problem

The math can show external help is required:

```text
self-generated repair cannot flip corrupted orientation
```

But that alone does not prove incarnation or mediator specificity. An objector can ask:

> Why not a divine reset?

### Proposed Triple Constraint

The needed operator must satisfy three constraints:

1. **External origin** — not generated by the corrupted system.
2. **Internal validity** — operates inside the system’s own justice/rule structure.
3. **Voluntary coupling** — preserves agency rather than overriding it.

Formal sketch:

```text
GraceOperator(G)
External(G)
SystemCompatible(G)
VoluntarilyCoupled(G)
```

### Why Reset Fails

| Reset Type | Failure |
|---|---|
| External erasure | bypasses justice rather than satisfying it |
| Forced repair | overrides agency/free will |
| Destroy-and-rebuild | breaks continuity of the person/world being restored |

So the operator cannot merely act from outside. It must enter the system without becoming reducible to the system.

### Mediator Shape

The mediator must be:

```text
outside-origin ∧ inside-valid ∧ agency-preserving ∧ continuity-preserving
```

The theological claim is that incarnation satisfies this operator profile:

```text
divine origin ∧ human participation ∧ voluntary obedience ∧ continuity of creation
```

### Strong Version

The strong version says:

> Incarnation is the unique operator class satisfying external origin, internal validity, voluntary coupling, justice preservation, mercy enactment, and continuity preservation.

### Required Caution

This uniqueness claim is not proven yet.

Before Lean, phrase it as:

> The triple constraint narrows the required solution toward mediator/incarnation logic.

Not yet:

> The triple constraint mathematically proves incarnation.

### Still Needs Testing

- Could a non-incarnational mediator satisfy the same constraints?
- Does “internal validity” require shared human nature, or only valid representation?
- Does justice require payment from inside the system, or can divine authority satisfy justice directly?
- How do covenant headship, representation, and union with Christ enter the formalism?
- Which parts belong to OT diagnostic derivation, and which belong to NT fulfillment derivation?

## Updated Readiness

These two gaps are now bridgeable but not fully closed.

### Strong Enough For Pre-Lean Ledger

- Covering as `S` dampening with `0 < α < 1`.
- Completion as restoration operator activation, not mere stronger covering.
- Mediator as triple-constraint solution class.

### Not Yet Strong Enough For Lean Theorem

- `α` semantics and scope.
- proof that `α < 1` must hold for animal sacrifice.
- proof that no non-incarnational operator satisfies mediator constraints.
- exact formal relationship between representation, substitution, union, and continuity.

## Next Move

Add these to the claim ledger as `BRIDGE-CLAIM`, not final theorem.

Then test:

```text
Can a serious objector give a non-incarnational operator satisfying all constraints?
```

If yes, weaken uniqueness.

If no, promote mediator specificity toward formal proof.
