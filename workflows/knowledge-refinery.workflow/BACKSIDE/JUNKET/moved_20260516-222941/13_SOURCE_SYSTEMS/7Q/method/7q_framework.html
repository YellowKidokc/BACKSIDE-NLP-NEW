The 7Q Framework

Seven questions forward: classify anything. Seven questions reversed: prove anything by exhaustive elimination. Same engine. Two directions. Nothing escapes.

Forward — Classify Anything

1. Name the object (identity)
2. Locate it in domain space
3. State what it claims
4. Show what supports it
5. Trace what it depends on
6. Map what it forces downstream
7. Define what kills it

Reverse — Prove Anything

1. Destroy — show every way it could die
2. Force — show what the survivor necessarily produces
3. Ground — show what it stands on
4. Support — bring evidence after survival is established
5. State — the claim is now a conclusion, not a hypothesis
6. Generalize — show it holds across domains
7. Name — identity earned through survival

Five Death Conditions

The traditional scientific method has one death condition: empirical contradiction. This method has five. A claim can die five different ways.

Self-Refutation
The claim destroys itself when stated. The denial requires what it denies.
Infinite Regress
The answer pushes the question backward without ever resolving it.
Empirical Contradiction
The claim contradicts what we observe. Data kills it.
Logical Incoherence
The claim contradicts something already established in the chain.
Explanatory Failure
The claim cannot answer forced downstream questions. It runs out of road.
