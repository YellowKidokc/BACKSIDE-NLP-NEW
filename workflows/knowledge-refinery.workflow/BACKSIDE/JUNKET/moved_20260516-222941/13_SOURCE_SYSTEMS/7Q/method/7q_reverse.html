The 7Q Framework — Reverse Direction

Start With Destruction.
End With a Name.

The traditional scientific method starts with a hypothesis and tries to confirm it. This method starts with everything that could kill your idea — and whatever survives earns its identity last. The reader doesn't have to trust you. They watched everything else die.

Traditional (Forward)

Start with hypothesis → gather evidence → try to confirm → announce conclusion.

Problem: Identity is assumed first. The hypothesis carries its name from the beginning. Confirmation bias is baked in. You're trying to prove something you already believe.

Reverse (7Q)

Start with destruction → extract consequences → trace foundations → attach evidence → state what survived → locate it → name it last.

Advantage: Identity is earned, not assumed. The conclusion is what's left standing after everything else failed. You didn't prove it — you watched it survive.

The traditional method starts with a seed and hopes it grows. The reverse method starts with a forest fire and names what's still standing.

Physics Example — General Relativity, Reversed

Before stating what GR claims, start here: GR dies if light doesn't bend around mass. GR dies if gravitational waves don't exist. GR dies if GPS doesn't need relativistic correction. GR dies if spacetime singularities are impossible. GR dies if the equivalence principle is wrong. Five kill conditions, laid out before any argument is made.

The question for the reader: "These are the ways this idea dies. Watch what happens next."

What GR Forces

If spacetime curves around mass, then: black holes must exist (extreme curvature). Gravitational waves must propagate (ripples in curvature). Time must run slower near mass (metric distortion). Light must bend around galaxies (geodesic paths through curved space). Einstein didn't predict most of these — the math forced them.

The question for the reader: "This is not a hypothesis anymore. This is what must follow."

What GR Stands On

Equivalence principle (gravity = acceleration). Riemannian geometry (smooth manifolds). Constancy of c. Conservation of energy-momentum. The chain terminates at the equivalence principle — an axiom, not a derived result. And the smooth manifold assumption is the hidden root that might be wrong at quantum scales.

The question for the reader: "Now you see what holds it up. You decide if those foundations are solid."
