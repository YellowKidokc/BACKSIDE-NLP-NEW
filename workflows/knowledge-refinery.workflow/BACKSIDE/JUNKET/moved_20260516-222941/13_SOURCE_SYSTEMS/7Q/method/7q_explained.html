The 7Q Framework — Explained

One Tree. Seven Lenses.
One Physics Example.

Every claim is a tree. We'll take one claim from physics and walk it through all seven questions — showing the classifications, the sub-questions, and what each stage actually produces.

Running Example
"Mass-energy curves spacetime, and objects follow geodesics through that curvature."
Q0
Before the First Question
The Empty Soil
Function: Establish posture. Not a question — a precondition.
Zone: Empty Ground
Before anything grows, there's ground. Not prepared ground. Not good ground. Just ground. The posture before inquiry is not a question — it's a precondition. You do not arrive already knowing what will grow here. You arrive open to whatever the soil produces.
"The inquirer cannot be the ground of inquiry."
Every dead framework in history refused this posture. Logical positivism said "meaning must be empirical" before looking. Behaviorism said "minds don't matter" before investigating. They arrived decided. The soil was poisoned before the seed landed.

Physics Example — General Relativity

Einstein in 1905 didn't start with "Newton is wrong." He started with a genuine puzzlement: what happens if the speed of light is the same for all observers? He didn't arrive to fix gravity. He arrived with a question he couldn't dismiss. The theory that killed Newtonian absolute space emerged from holding the question honestly, not from knowing the answer in advance.
Q1
Identity
The Seed — What Is It?
Function: Create a defined object. Name it. Classify it. Prevent category confusion.
Zone: The Seed
undefined→defined
Every tree starts as a seed. Not a generic seed — this specific seed. It has DNA. It's an oak, not a pine. It's a causal claim, not a descriptive one. Before anything else happens, you name it. You classify it. You say "I am holding THIS, and it is THIS KIND of thing."
"If you can't name what you're holding, you don't know what you planted."

Sub-questions

What type of claim is this? (descriptive, causal, ontological, mathematical, mechanistic, predictive, normative)
What tier? (foundational axiom, derived theorem, testable hypothesis, boundary condition)
Has it been named before — and does that name carry assumptions you haven't verified?

Classification — Filled In

LabelMass-energy curves spacetime
Typecausal — mass-energy CAUSES curvature
Tierfoundational — this is the core equation, not derived from something deeper within GR
Prior NamesGeneral Relativity, Einstein Field Equations — carries assumption that "relativity" means only physics, which is itself a domain constraint worth noting

What Q1 Caught

By forcing the claim type, we immediately see this is causal, not merely descriptive. Einstein isn't saying "spacetime happens to be curved." He's saying mass-energy causes the curvature. That's a much stronger claim than most people realize — it says matter tells space how to bend. If you'd classified this as "descriptive," you'd miss half the kill conditions.
Q2
Domain
The Soil — Where Does It Live?
Function: Anchor the object in domain, scale, and cross-domain presence.
Zone: The Soil
defined→located
An oak seed in sand grows differently than one in clay. Domain is destiny. A physics claim tested in a physics lab has different constraints than the same structural claim appearing in information theory. And if the same seed grows in two different soils and produces the same tree? That's not coincidence. That's isomorphism.
"The soil doesn't change the DNA. It reveals what the DNA can do."

Sub-questions

What domain owns this claim? (physics, mathematics, theology, consciousness, information, moral)
Does it appear in more than one domain? If so — surface analogy or structural isomorphism?
At what scale does it operate? (quantum, molecular, neural, individual, social, cosmic, universal)

Classification — Filled In

Primaryphysics
Additionalmathematics (differential geometry, Riemannian manifolds)
Scalecosmic → universal — applies from planetary orbits to cosmological expansion
IsomorphismISO-confirmed — the mathematical structure (Riemannian geometry) is independently valid. Physics and math confirm each other.

What Q2 Caught

The claim lives in physics but structurally depends on mathematics. Riemannian geometry existed before Einstein borrowed it. The math didn't need the physics — but the physics can't exist without the math. That's a real cross-domain dependency. And here's the thing: Riemannian geometry also appears in machine learning (manifold learning), in economics (curved utility surfaces), and in information geometry. The soil is more universal than the seed realized.
Q3
Assertion
The Sprout — What Does It Say?
Function: Force precision. The claim commits to a direction and can't take it back.
Zone: The Sprout
located→stated
The sprout breaks surface and goes up, not sideways. It commits. It picks a direction and can't take it back. A precise claim does the same — it says one thing clearly. No "it might relate to" or "there could be a connection." It states what it asserts and generates its own negation.
"If your opponent can't restate your claim accurately, it isn't precise enough to grow."

Sub-questions

State the claim in one sentence with zero hedging
Can a serious opponent restate it accurately? If not, it's not precise enough
Generate the negation — this becomes the seed for Q7's kill conditions

Classification — Filled In

Assertion"The distribution of mass-energy determines the curvature of spacetime, and free-falling objects follow geodesics through that curvature."
Precisionmathematical
Certaintyproven — confirmed to extraordinary precision
Scopeuniversal — applies everywhere gravity operates
Negation"Mass-energy does NOT determine spacetime curvature, OR objects do NOT follow geodesics."

What Q3 Caught

The negation reveals there are actually two claims bundled together: (1) mass-energy causes curvature, and (2) objects follow geodesics. Either could fail independently. Newton's gravity has mass causing attraction but NO curvature at all. Some modified gravity theories keep curvature but add extra forces so objects DON'T follow pure geodesics. Q3's negation split the claim into two killable parts.

Q4
Evidence
The Rain — What Supports It?
Function: Attach external justification. Show the claim doesn't feed itself.
Zone: Rain & Sun
stated→supported
A tree doesn't feed itself from nothing. Rain comes from outside. Evidence is external support — data, observation, replication, mathematical proof. Without it, the tree dries up. But here's the critical sub-question most people skip: could this same rain fall on a different tree and feed it equally well? If so, your evidence doesn't distinguish your claim from its competitors.
"If your evidence equally supports a competing explanation, you haven't proven anything yet."

Sub-questions

Is this evidence empirical, experimental, mathematical, logical, or inferential?
Has it been replicated? By whom? Under what conditions?
Could the same evidence equally support a competing model?

Classification — Filled In

Typeexperimental mathematical observational
Tiertier_1 — direct experimental, massively replicated
Replicationreplicated — across 100+ years, multiple independent teams, multiple methods
Competing?No — the specific predictions (e.g., exact Mercury precession value) are unique to GR

The Evidence — Four Independent Rainstorms

1. Mercury's Perihelion Precession (1915) — Newton predicted 5,557 arcseconds/century. Observed: 5,600. The 43-arcsecond gap is explained exactly by GR. No other theory predicted this value.

2. Gravitational Lensing (1919) — Eddington's eclipse expedition: starlight bends around the sun by exactly the amount GR predicted — twice the Newtonian value.

3. Gravitational Waves (2015) — LIGO detected spacetime ripples from merging black holes. Waveform matched GR prediction to extraordinary precision.

4. GPS Time Dilation (daily) — GPS satellites correct for relativistic time dilation every second. Remove the correction and GPS fails within hours. You use this evidence every time you open a map.
Q5
Dependencies
The Roots — What Does It Depend On?
Function: Expose hidden foundations. Trace the chain all the way down.
Zone: Root System
supported→grounded
Everything above ground depends on what's below it. Roots are the invisible dependencies — the things that must already be true for the claim to stand. Some roots go deep to bedrock (axioms). Some spread wide but stay shallow (assumptions). And some loop back into themselves — that's circularity, and it must be flagged.
"The tree you see is only as strong as the roots you can't."

Sub-questions

What must already be true for this claim to stand?
Trace the dependency chain all the way down — where does it end? Axiom, brute fact, or circularity?
If a root fails, does the whole tree fall immediately or just one branch?

Classification — Filled In

Depends onEquivalence Principle, Riemannian geometry, constancy of c, conservation of energy-momentum, smooth spacetime manifold
Chain terminusaxiom — equivalence principle is foundational (not derived from deeper physics)
Fragilitydegrade_gracefully — if smooth manifold assumption fails at Planck scale, GR still works everywhere else
Hiddensmooth spacetime — GR assumes spacetime is a continuous manifold. Quantum gravity theories challenge this at 10⁻³⁵ meters. Most people don't know this root exists.

What Q5 Caught — The Root Nobody Sees

GR depends on spacetime being a smooth, continuous manifold. That's an assumption, not a proven fact. At the Planck scale (~10⁻³⁵ m), spacetime might be discrete, foamy, or something else entirely. This hidden root is why GR and quantum mechanics can't be unified — they disagree about the nature of the ground they're standing on. Q5 found the crack that's been driving physics for 100 years.
Q6
Consequences
The Canopy — What Does It Force?
Function: If this is true, what else MUST be true? Generate predictions. Drop seeds into new soil.
Zone: Branches, Fruit & Falling Seeds
grounded→generative
A living tree doesn't just stand there — it produces. Branches reach new space. Fruit appears. Seeds drop into new soil. If your claim is true, what else MUST be true that nobody has checked? Those falling seeds are your untested predictions. And if a seed lands in a different domain's soil and grows the same tree — that's an isomorphism. A claim that produces nothing is a dead tree.
"A claim that forces nothing downstream explains nothing. It occupies space."

Sub-questions

If true, what else MUST follow that nobody has checked?
Does it generate testable predictions?
Does it force consequences in a different domain? (That's where isomorphisms live)

Classification — Filled In

ImpliesBlack holes must exist (BH) · Gravitational waves must propagate (GW) · Time runs slower near mass (TD) · Light bends around massive objects (LB)
PredictsExact Mercury precession value · Exact LIGO waveform shape · Exact GPS correction factor
Cross-domainyes — Forces consequences in cosmology (expansion), astrophysics (neutron stars), technology (GPS), and potentially information theory (holographic principle)

What Q6 Produced — Fruit That Became New Trees

Einstein published the field equations in 1915. He did NOT predict black holes — Schwarzschild derived them as a forced consequence within months. Einstein didn't predict gravitational waves either — they fell out of the math in 1916 and were detected 99 years later. GPS time dilation wasn't predicted by Einstein but was forced by the equations long before satellites existed. The tree bore fruit Einstein didn't plant. That's Q6 working. The claim forced consequences its own author hadn't imagined.
Q7
Falsification
The Axe — What Kills It?
Function: Attach death conditions. Every object carries its own death warrant.
Zone: The Axe
generative→testable (vulnerable)
Every tree can be cut down. The question is where to swing. Five ways to die: self-refutation (the tree's weight snaps its own trunk), infinite regress (roots never reach bedrock), empirical contradiction (the rain carries poison), logical incoherence (the rings contradict the bark), explanatory failure (the tree stops bearing fruit). A tree that can't be cut isn't a tree — it's a painting of one.
"Every object carries its own death warrant. If it doesn't, it isn't science."

Sub-questions

Name the death condition — which of the five types?
What breaks DOWNSTREAM if this fails? (link to Q6 consequences)
Has anyone actually TRIED to kill it — or has it only survived friendly examination?

Classification — Filled In

Death #1empirical — If light doesn't bend around massive objects (but it does — Eddington 1919, confirmed thousands of times since)
Death #2empirical — If gravitational waves aren't detected (but they were — LIGO 2015)
Death #3empirical — If GPS doesn't need relativistic correction (but it does — every single day)
Death #4explanatory — If GR cannot handle singularities or quantum scales (this is LIVE — GR breaks at black hole centers and the Big Bang)
Statusalive — survived 100+ years of adversarial testing. But Q5's hidden root (smooth manifold assumption) is the open wound.
Adversarial?survived_expert_attack — every major physicist for a century has tried to break it. Nobody has. Yet.

What Q7 Found — The Living Kill Condition

Three of the four death conditions have been tested and GR survived. But Death #4 is still live. GR produces infinities at black hole singularities and can't describe the first instant of the Big Bang. This isn't a hypothetical objection — it's a mathematical fact inside the theory itself. The smooth manifold assumption Q5 found IS the axe wound. The tree is standing, but the crack is real, and it's been there since 1915. Whoever unifies gravity and quantum mechanics will swing the axe that either kills GR or heals the wound.
