## IV. The Metaphysics of Privatio Boni and the Parasitic Mechanism of Evil

The argument for the ontological priority of goodness rests on the doctrine of _privatio boni_: evil is not a substance but a parasitic privation of a prior good.

- **The Mechanics of Privation:** Evil takes a "due good" and breaks it. Blindness is not a "thing" in the eye but the absence of sight where it ought to be.
    
- **Semantic and Structural Dependency:** One cannot describe an evil act without invoking the good it violated. Murder takes life; theft takes property; lying takes truth. Evil has no independent vocabulary.[User Query, 9, 318]
    
- **The Asymmetry of Growth:** Good is self-defining and can exist without evil. Love does not need hate to be love. In contrast, evil is inherently self-limiting; as it consumes its "host" (goodness), it moves toward non-existence.[3, User Query, 344]
    

## V. Refuting the "Evil-God Challenge" via Ontological Symmetry

The "Evil-God Challenge" suggests that arguments for a good God are symmetrical to those for an evil God. Metaphysical asymmetry provides the rebuttal:

1. **Completeness vs. Incompleteness:** A good God creates out of infinite fullness for the benefit of the other (other-regarding). An evil God must create to maximize evil, making it dependent on its creation and therefore incomplete.
    
2. **Subordination to the Standard:** To be "evil," a being must fail to comply with a standard of "good." This implies the standard (the Good) is more fundamental than the evil being.
    
3. **Foundational Deception as Self-Destruction:** If the ground of reality were deceptive, the ability to reason or do math would collapse.[User Query, 29] Our universe's stability and discoverable patterns prove the Ground is truthful.[4, User Query]
