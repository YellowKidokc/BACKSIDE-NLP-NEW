# Meta-Analysis

## Purpose
This section provides tools for quantitatively evaluating arguments across the entire vault. The goal is rigor — not just collecting arguments, but measuring their actual strength.

## Notes

- **[[Evaluation Criteria]]** — What counts as strong evidence? Definitions for the strength scale (1-10) used across all argument notes.
- **[[Evidence Weighting System]]** — Not all evidence is equal. How to weight historical vs. philosophical vs. experiential vs. scientific evidence.
- **[[Argument Scoring]]** — Aggregate scores by domain. Which domains carry the most weight? Where is the case strongest and weakest?
- **[[Logical Fallacy Tracker]]** — Common fallacies found in BOTH pro-Christian and anti-Christian arguments. Tag them honestly.
- **[[Debate Outcomes]]** — Records of formal debates (Craig vs. Hitchens, Craig vs. Ehrman, etc.) with outcome assessments.

## How to Use
After a domain has sufficient notes in both FOR/ and AGAINST/, use the scoring system to assess:
1. Which side has more load-bearing arguments?
2. Which arguments have the highest strength ratings?
3. Where are the genuine open questions?
4. Where has one side clearly won?

This is the section that turns the vault from a library into an analytical tool.
