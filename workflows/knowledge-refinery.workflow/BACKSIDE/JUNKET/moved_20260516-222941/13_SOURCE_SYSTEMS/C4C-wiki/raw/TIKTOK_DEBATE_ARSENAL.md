## THE OPENER — USE THIS FIRST

**"What's your evidence that God exists?"**

**Answer:** "That you have a mechanism in you that tells you what's right and wrong."

Then shut up. Let them respond. Every possible response proves your point:
- "That doesn't prove God" → You just evaluated. You used the mechanism.
- "That's just evolution" → You judged that explanation as BETTER. Evaluation.
- "That's ridiculous" → Judgment. You just did the thing.
- "There is no right and wrong" → Is that statement right? You just used right/wrong to deny right/wrong.

**There is no response that doesn't prove the claim.**

## THE GATEWAY — ASK THESE BEFORE ENGAGING

Don't waste energy on someone who can't grant basics. Ask first:

1. **Do you believe truth exists?** If no → "Then nothing you're about to say matters by your own standard. We're done."
2. **Do you believe some things are right and some things are wrong?** If no → "Then you can't object to anything I say. We're done."
3. **Do you believe reality can be known?** If no → "Then your objections are unknowable too. We're done."

If they say yes to all three → now you can talk.
If they say no to any → don't argue. Just point out the self-defeat and move on.

## THE DISCERNMENT KILL SHOT (Self-Referential Proof)

**Setup:** "Do you evaluate things as right or wrong?"
**They say yes:** "Good. So does every human who ever lived. That's not learned. That's structural."
**They say no:** "Then is my claim wrong? You just evaluated it."

**The one-liner:** "You can't disagree with this without proving it true."

**The chain (use in order):**
1. Every person evaluates — right/wrong, true/false, better/worse
2. This cannot be turned off — even the person denying it is evaluating
3. If it can't be turned off, it's not cultural — it's structural
4. If it's structural to ALL conscious agents, it's a feature of reality, not just brains
5. If reality has normative structure, then right and wrong aren't opinions — they're built in

**Descartes parallel:** "I think therefore I am" — you can't doubt existence without thinking, which proves you exist. Same structure: you can't deny evaluation without evaluating.

Best food you've ever had. Best time. Door's never locked.

## SCIENCE DOESN'T PRODUCE TRUTH

**Don't say:** "Science is wrong"
**Say:** "Science produces the current best model. Not truth. Models."

- Newtonian gravity was the best model for 250 years. Then Einstein.
- Steady-state universe was consensus. Then Hubble.
- Ulcers were caused by stress. Then bacteria.
- Every generation was empirically tested AND wrong about something.

**The move:** "When you say 'I believe in science,' you mean 'I believe in the current revision.' That's fine — it works, it builds bridges, it cures diseases. But it's not truth. It's the best model until the next one replaces it."

**Kill shot:** "Science assumes truth exists every time it runs an experiment. If there's no fact of the matter, there's nothing to measure. You're standing on my foundation every time you pick up a test tube."

## EVOLUTION — DON'T DENY, REFRAME

**Don't say:** "Evolution is fake"
**Say:** "We ran their numbers. Their mutation rates. Their population sizes. Their timelines. Five configurations. Every single one failed to close the gap."

- The selection coefficient required is 89x above the documented maximum
- That's using THEIR citations. Not ours.
- The mechanism works for small changes (bacteria, viruses — yes, observable)
- It does NOT work for major transitions (body plans, organ systems, 35 animal phyla)

**The line:** "Evolution describes what persists. My framework evaluates what's coherent. Those are different questions."

**If pressed:** "Every evolutionary model starts the clock AFTER the hard part. They assume a viable starting population already exists. That's a two-line disclaimer doing more work than the entire model."

## THE EVOLUTION CAN'T GET YOU THERE ARGUMENT

"A firefighter runs into a burning building and dies saving a stranger's child.
Evolution says: survive. Pass on your genes. That firefighter's genes just died.
Natural selection should have eliminated that impulse millions of years ago.
But it didn't. And every culture on earth honors that sacrifice.
Where did THAT come from in a system that only optimizes for survival?"

"Evolution explains survival instincts. Don't eat poison. Run from predators. Fine.
But evolution CANNOT explain moral evaluation.
Recognizing injustice doesn't help you survive — it often gets you killed.
People die for moral principles. That's the OPPOSITE of fitness.
Evolution produces 'avoid danger.' It does not produce 'that's unfair.'
So where did your moral evaluation come from?"

If they say "group selection / social cooperation":
"Cooperation explains 'help the group survive.' It does NOT explain
'what that dictator is doing to those people across the ocean is wrong.'
You have no survival stake in that judgment. Zero. But you make it anyway.
Every single time. That's not evolution. That's structure."

"We axiomatized 68 worldviews. Every single one either zeroes out variables mine keeps or assumes things mine derives. I'm not saying they're all stupid. I'm saying mine covers more and leaves fewer gaps."

**"You can't prove God exists"**
"You can't prove consciousness exists either, but you're using it right now. Some things are foundational — you can't derive them from below because they're what everything else stands on. The question isn't proof. It's whether reality makes more sense with the ground or without it."

## RULES FOR TIKTOK

1. Never be mean. Be precise.
2. Don't say "you're wrong." Say "look at what your own framework requires."
3. Ask questions more than make statements. Let them walk into it.
4. Never use the word "prove." Use "show" or "demonstrate."
5. Say "right and wrong" not "good and evil." Less religious packaging.
6. Lead with the question. End with the structure. Never lead with the equation.
7. If someone can't grant truth exists — walk away. No foundation, no conversation.
8. The neighbor analogy is your closer. Save it.
9. Never argue with someone who's performing. Only engage people who are actually thinking.
10. You're not trying to win. You're trying to create a divide. Two systems. Pick one.
