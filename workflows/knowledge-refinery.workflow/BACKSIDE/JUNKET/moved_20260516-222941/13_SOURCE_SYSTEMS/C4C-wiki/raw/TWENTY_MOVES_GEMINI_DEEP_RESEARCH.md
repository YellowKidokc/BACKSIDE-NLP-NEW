## MOVE 1 — HELL IS EVIL (115 hits — #1)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** A perfectly loving Being could never be satisfied while even one creature suffers eternally. If God is infinite and sin is finite, then eternal torment is a gross violation of proportionality and distributive justice.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Assumes that people in hell want to be in heaven with God, and that God is a cosmic tyrant forcing them into a dungeon against their will.

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "You're assuming God is a kidnapper. He's not. Look, if you ignored someone your entire life — never answered their calls, never acknowledged they existed — and then on your last day, that person forced you to move into their house and live with them forever, would you call that love or harassment? God doesn't send anyone to hell. He respects the choice you already made. The signal has been broadcasting your whole life; you just never picked up. If you don't want a relationship with Him now, why would He force you into one for eternity? Is honoring your free choice an act of evil, or an act of respect?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** The critic borrows the concept of "justice" and "love" from the Christian worldview to judge God, while their own worldview can't ground an objective standard for either. You're demanding God be more "loving" than the source of Love Himself.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Traditional theodicy from Anselm argues that the gravity of an offense is determined by the status of the Being offended. Scholars like Karlo Broussard note that if the sinner continues to sin eternally (ongoing rebellion), the punishment remains proportionate to the ongoing act.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** If you ignore someone your whole life, is it "loving" for them to force you to live with them?

## MOVE 2 — SLAVERY IN THE BIBLE (91 hits — #2)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** The Bible provides explicit instructions for the purchase and physical correction of human beings (Exodus 21), establishing a "property" status morally indistinguishable from American chattel slavery.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Assumes that because God regulated a practice in an ancient culture, He endorsed it as His ideal will.

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "This collapses the moment you ask: Did God show up? Because when Jesus showed up, He said some things were permitted in the Old Law only because of the 'hardness of human hearts,' not because it was the ideal. The Hebrew word for 'slave' (ebed) usually meant an indentured servant paying off debt, and the Law was the only one in the ancient world that gave those servants a mandatory day off and legal protection against abuse. But more importantly: if Jesus rose from the dead, He is the Author of the story. He gets to set the terms of the relationship, and He chose to 'buy' us with His own blood to show our value, not our worthlessness. Are you judging the Architect for how He managed a broken building before He finished the renovation?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** To call slavery "wrong," the atheist must presuppose the objective, intrinsic dignity of human beings — a concept only grounded in being made in the image of God. You're using God's moral compass to try and find God lost in the woods.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Biblical law uniquely mandated the death penalty for man-stealing (kidnapping for trade), which was the basis of New World chattel slavery. Unlike other Ancient Near Eastern codes, Exodus 21:26-27 required a master to free a servant for an injury as small as a lost tooth.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** If Jesus rose, does He have the authority to define what is "good"?

## MOVE 3 — SCIENCE VS BIBLE (81 hits — #3, 5x under-defended)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** The Darwinian mechanism provides a complete, self-contained explanation for the complexity of life, rendering the "God hypothesis" superfluous.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Assumes that a functional explanation of how a system works eliminates the need for an author who designed the system.

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "This isn't a conflict between science and a book; it's a question of whether the Universe has an Author. Science has actually made the 'God showed up' claim more plausible, not less. We've discovered that the universe had a beginning — the BGV theorem proves it. We've discovered that the laws of physics are fine-tuned to 1 part in 10^120. And we've found a 3-billion-letter digital code inside your cells that natural processes can't account for. If you found a poem in the sand, you wouldn't say 'the tide wrote this.' You'd look for the person who was walking there. Science found the poem; I'm just introducing you to the Poet. Why are you trying to use the laws of the house to prove the homeowner doesn't exist?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** Science requires the presupposition that the universe is rational, orderly, and that our minds are reliable — all "borrowed" from a theistic framework. You're using your mind to disprove the Designer who gave you the hardware to think.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** The Borde-Guth-Vilenkin (BGV) theorem (2003) mathematically demonstrates that any expanding universe must have a past spacetime boundary. Stephen Meyer's Signature in the Cell highlights that functionally specified information in DNA is always traced back to an intelligent cause.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** Does a functional explanation of a computer prove there was no programmer?

## MOVE 4 — RESURRECTION DIDN'T HAPPEN (62 hits — #4)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** The Resurrection is a physical impossibility. Given the low reliability of ancient testimony and the "extraordinary claims" standard, any naturalistic explanation is more probable than a miracle.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Presupposes philosophical naturalism — that miracles cannot happen — before even looking at the data.

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "Let's skip the side arguments. If Jesus didn't rise, I'll go home. But you have to explain the 'Minimal Facts' that even skeptical scholars grant: Jesus was crucified, the disciples truly believed they saw Him alive, and they were willing to die for that claim. People will die for a lie they think is true, but nobody dies for a lie they know they made up. The tomb was empty, the skeptics like Paul and James converted, and the Church exploded in the same city where He was executed. If God showed up, we should expect a footprint that changes history. That footprint is the empty tomb. What's your explanation for the data that doesn't involve 'I've already decided it's impossible'?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** The critic uses the historical-critical method to judge the Resurrection while presupposing that the "laws of nature" they rely on aren't themselves sustained by God. You're trying to use the shadow to prove there is no Light.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Over 75% of scholars (including non-believers) accept the empty tomb as historical fact. Historians like Bart Ehrman admit as "certain" that the disciples had experiences they believed were appearances of the risen Jesus.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** Did the disciples die for a lie they knew they invented?

## MOVE 5 — BIBLE CONTRADICTS ITSELF (49 hits — #5)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** The four Gospels cannot agree on the number of women at the tomb, the timing of the crucifixion, or the genealogy of Jesus, proving they are unreliable human inventions.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Assumes that "supplemental" details (one witness mentioning A and another mentioning B) constitute a "contradiction."

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "If four of us saw a car accident, and one of us talked about the driver while the other talked about the car, would you call us liars? That's not a contradiction; that's multiple perspectives on a real event. In the Resurrection accounts, the differences in detail actually prove there was no collusion — they didn't get together to get their stories straight. If God showed up in history, he did it in front of real people with real, different perspectives. Why would you expect a relationship with a real Person to look like a scripted legal deposition?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** You're demanding the Bible meet a standard of "modern historiography" that didn't exist for 1,800 years, yet you use the Bible's own standard of "Truth" to find its alleged "Errors." You're borrowing God's ruler to show His lines are crooked.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Textual criticism shows the New Testament is 99.5% textually pure. Archaeological finds like the "Pilate Stone" and "Caiaphas Ossuary" consistently vindicate the specific people and places mentioned.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** Does one witness seeing one angel and another seeing two make the tomb not empty?

## MOVE 6 — PROBLEM OF EVIL / SUFFERING (~45 hits — #6)

**A. STEEL-MANNED ATTACK:** The Epicurean Paradox: If God is willing but not able, He's not omnipotent. If able but not willing, He's malevolent. The sheer quantity of gratuitous suffering makes a good God improbable.

**B. HIDDEN ASSUMPTION:** Assumes that if we can't see a reason for suffering, then a reason must not exist.

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "This is the most honest question anyone can ask. But notice something: you only call it 'evil' because you're comparing it to a 'Good' that you think should exist. Where did you get that standard? If we're just biological accidents, suffering isn't 'evil,' it's just 'nature.' The only reason this is a problem for you is that you're borrowing the Christian idea that things are supposed to be better. And that's exactly what the Cross is. God didn't stay distant; He showed up and suffered with us. He doesn't give you an explanation; He gives you a relationship. He's the only God who has scars."

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** You can't have a "Problem of Evil" unless there is an objective "Good," which requires a Moral Lawgiver. You're using the existence of evil to disprove the only Being who provides a basis for calling it evil.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Alvin Plantinga's "Free Will Defense" is widely recognized by philosophers as a successful rebuttal to the logical problem of evil. Studies show that "positive religious coping" significantly reduces the risk of depression and suicide.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** If there is no God, why is suffering "evil" instead of just "natural selection"?

God waited 400 years for them to change before He acted as Judge. And the language of 'men, women, and children' was common military hyperbole — it meant a decisive victory, like saying a team 'annihilated' their rivals. If God showed up to stop a group from sacrificing their own children, would you call Him a monster or a Rescuer?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** To judge God's judgment, you must assume a moral high ground that doesn't exist in a godless universe. You're sitting in the Judge's seat using the Judge's own law to arrest the Judge.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Scholars like Paul Copan note that herem commands (utter destruction) were idiomatic and hyperbolic in the Ancient Near East. Archaeology confirms Canaanite religious sites were centers for child sacrifice and systemic cultic abuse.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** Is a judge "evil" for sentencing a criminal after 400 years of warnings?

## MOVE 11 — JESUS DIDN'T EXIST / MYTHICISM (~25 hits — #11)

**C. PROACTIVE DISMANTLE (60-90 sec):** "This is a move that even skeptical historians don't make. Virtually every serious scholar on earth agrees Jesus was a real man. We have Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny the Younger — all writing within a century of His life. If God showed up, you'd expect Him to leave a mark. Jesus left the largest mark in human history. He changed the calendar. If He didn't exist, where did the empty tomb come from?"

**D. PRESUPPOSITIONAL LAYER:** You're using "historical evidence" to disprove Jesus, yet the historical method confirms His existence. You're using the telescope to prove the star isn't there.

**E. DATA / EVIDENCE:** Josephus (c. 93 AD) and Tacitus (c. 116 AD) provide independent Roman and Jewish confirmation. The Osiris/Mithras parallels have been debunked by specialists — those "resurrections" are seasonal allegories, not historical claims.

**F. KILL QUESTION:** Why do even atheist historians agree that Jesus was a real person?
