# Historical Jesus

## The Question
Did Jesus of Nazareth exist as a historical figure, and what can we know about him from historical sources?

## Why This Matters
If Jesus never existed, Christianity collapses entirely. If he existed but was substantially different from the Gospel portraits, the theological claims built on those portraits are undermined. The historicity question is foundational.

## Scholarly Landscape
The vast majority of ancient historians and New Testament scholars — including non-Christian ones like Bart Ehrman — affirm that Jesus existed as a historical figure. The "mythicist" position (that Jesus never existed) is a fringe view in academia, though it has vocal popular advocates like Richard Carrier and Robert Price. The real scholarly debate is not *whether* Jesus existed but *what* we can reliably know about him.

## Key Sub-Questions
- What do non-Christian sources tell us about Jesus?
- How do criteria of authenticity (embarrassment, multiple attestation, dissimilarity) apply?
- What does archaeology confirm or challenge?
- How reliable is oral tradition in a first-century Jewish context?

## Strongest FOR Argument
Multiple independent sources — including hostile witnesses like Tacitus and Josephus — attest to Jesus' existence within decades of his life, which is remarkable for a non-elite figure in the ancient world.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
The mythicist argument that Paul's letters show no awareness of a historical Jesus and that the Gospel narratives were constructed from pre-existing mythological templates.
