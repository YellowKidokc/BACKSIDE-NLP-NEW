# Comparative Religion

## The Question
Did Christianity borrow its core ideas from earlier religions, and how does it compare structurally to other traditions?

## Why This Matters
The "Christianity is just recycled mythology" claim is one of the most popular objections in popular culture. If the dying-rising god motif predates Christianity, the uniqueness claim is weakened. If the parallels are superficial, the objection fails.

## Strongest FOR Argument
Judaism's strict monotheism and historical grounding are genuinely unique in the ancient Near East. The specific combination of historical claims (resurrection of a specific person at a specific time) with ethical demands and grace-based soteriology has no real parallel in pagan mystery religions, despite superficial similarities.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Dying-rising god motifs (Osiris, Attis, Adonis), virgin birth narratives, and sacred meal rituals all predate Christianity. Syncretism in late antiquity was pervasive, and early Christians operating in Hellenistic culture would naturally have absorbed and adapted existing religious forms.
