# APO-20: Cumulative Case
## "No single argument proves God alone. Together they form a converging explanation."

---

## The Hook (30 seconds)

I'm not asking you to accept one argument. I'm asking you to explain why nineteen independent lines of evidence — from math, from physics, from consciousness, from morality, from history, from information theory, from logic — all point in the same direction. One coincidence, fine. Five coincidences, suspicious. Nineteen independent convergences from unrelated domains? That's not luck. That's a signal.

---

## The Case (2-5 minutes)

The cumulative case is the meta-argument. It's not additive — it's MULTIPLICATIVE. Each independent line of evidence doesn't just ADD weight. It MULTIPLIES probability. The odds of all nineteen being coincidence approaches zero.

And crucially — these aren't nineteen versions of the same argument. They come from different domains:
- Mathematics (APO-05, APO-17)
- Physics (APO-02, APO-03, APO-10, APO-12)
- Philosophy (APO-01, APO-04, APO-19)
- Morality (APO-07, APO-08)
- Consciousness (APO-09, APO-11)
- Biology (APO-06, APO-13)
- History (APO-14)
- Experience (APO-15, APO-16)
- Meta (APO-18)

Independent domains. Independent methods. Same conclusion.

> [!tip] The Kill Shot
> "You need nineteen independent explanations for nineteen independent phenomena. I need one. Occam's razor says take the simpler hypothesis. God is simpler than nineteen coincidences."
