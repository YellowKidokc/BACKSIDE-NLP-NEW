# Early Christianity & Church History

## The Question
How did Christianity actually spread and form historically, and does the pattern of its growth support or undermine its truth claims?

## Why This Matters
Critics often claim Christianity was a later invention shaped by political power (Constantine) rather than authentic apostolic witness. The speed and nature of early Christian growth is itself evidential.

## Strongest FOR Argument
Christianity spread explosively across hostile cultures within decades, carried by witnesses who gained nothing materially and risked everything. The pre-Gospel creeds and early church fathers demonstrate doctrinal continuity from the earliest period.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Constantine's political adoption of Christianity in the 4th century transformed it from a persecuted sect into an imperial religion, and the resulting power dynamics shaped doctrine, canon, and practice in ways that may not reflect the original movement. Alternative Christianities (Gnostic, Ebionite, Marcionite) were suppressed, not refuted.
