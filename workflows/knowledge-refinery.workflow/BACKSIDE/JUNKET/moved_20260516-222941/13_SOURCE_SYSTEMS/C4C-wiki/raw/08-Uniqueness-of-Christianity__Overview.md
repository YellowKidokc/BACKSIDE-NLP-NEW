# Uniqueness of Christianity

## The Question
What distinguishes Christianity from other world religions, and does its uniqueness count as evidence for its truth?

## Why This Matters
If Christianity is simply one myth among many, its truth claims are undermined. If it has genuinely unique structural features — particularly its falsifiability — that changes its epistemic status.

## Strongest FOR Argument
Christianity alone among world religions stakes everything on a historical, falsifiable claim: that a specific man died and physically rose from the dead at a specific time and place. Additionally, the doctrine of grace (salvation as gift, not earned) is structurally unique among soteriological systems.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Every religion claims uniqueness. Islam claims a unique final revelation, Hinduism claims unique inclusivity, Buddhism claims unique liberation from desire. The mere fact of distinctive features doesn't establish truth. And 40,000+ Christian denominations suggest the tradition can't even agree with itself about what it teaches.
