Doubt represents a terrible threat to a Christian’s faith. Con-
sider the apostle Peter. When Jesus invited him to step out of the
boat on the Sea of Galilee and walk on the water (Matthew 14:28-
33), Peter began that walk with bold determination. But when he
glanced away and saw the treacherous wind and waves licking at
his heels, he doubted—and lost his faith in the divine power that
had borne him across the water to that point. As he drew near to
the boat—having been saved by the hand of the Master—he was
admonished by His Lord with these words: “O you of little faith,
why did you doubt?” (14:31). Throughout the Bible, doubt gener-
ally is mentioned in negative terms. When Thomas doubted,
Christ told him: “Be not faithless, but believing” (John 20:27). In
the end, doubt is extremely detrimental to faith.
In this book, young people can see and examine the vast
amount of evidence upon which biblical faith is based, so that
they can say “Out with doubt!”—and mean it. In the process, they
can build a rock-solid faith that will last a lifetime.
About the Author
Kyle Butt is a graduate of Freed-Hardeman University, where
he earned a B.A. degree with a double major in Bible and Com-
munication, and an M.A. in New Testament. Currently, he works
in the Bible Department at Apologetics Press and is editor of Dis-
covery, the monthly paper on Scripture and science for children
published by Apologetics Press.

Doubt represents a terrible threat to a Christian’s faith. Con-
sider the apostle Peter. When Jesus invited him to step out of the
boat on the Sea of Galilee and walk on the water (Matthew 14:28-
33), Peter began that walk with bold determination. But when he
glanced away and saw the treacherous wind and waves licking at
his heels, he doubted—and lost his faith in the divine power that
had borne him across the water to that point. As he drew near to
the boat—having been saved by the hand of the Master—he was
admonished by His Lord with these words: “O you of little faith,
why did you doubt?” (14:31). Throughout the Bible, doubt gener-
ally is mentioned in negative terms. When Thomas doubted,
Christ told him: “Be not faithless, but believing” (John 20:27). In
the end, doubt is extremely detrimental to faith.
In this book, young people can see and examine the vast
amount of evidence upon which biblical faith is based, so that
they can say “Out with doubt!”—and mean it. In the process, they
can build a rock-solid faith that will last a lifetime.
