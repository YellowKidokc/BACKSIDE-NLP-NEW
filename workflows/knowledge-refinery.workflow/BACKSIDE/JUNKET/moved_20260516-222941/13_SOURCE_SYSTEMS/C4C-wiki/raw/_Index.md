# Evil and Moral Agency — Research Index

## Topic Focus
This collection investigates the asymmetry between what evolutionary ethics can explain (cooperation, reciprocity, fairness) and what it struggles with (genuine, intentional evil; moral realism; free will). The goal is to surface the crack where materialism breaks down and contextualize how a theistic framework addresses this gap.

## Key Perspectives

### What Evolutionary Ethics CAN Explain
- Reciprocal altruism
- Kin selection
- Group cooperation
- Game-theoretic equilibria
- Moral emotions (disgust, guilt, shame)

### What It STRUGGLES With
- **Evil as active intention** — not just moral failure, but creative malice
- **Moral realism** — whether morality is mind-dependent or independent
- **Free will responsibility** — how genuine agency arises from physical processes
- **Gratuitous suffering** — harm that provides no fitness benefit
