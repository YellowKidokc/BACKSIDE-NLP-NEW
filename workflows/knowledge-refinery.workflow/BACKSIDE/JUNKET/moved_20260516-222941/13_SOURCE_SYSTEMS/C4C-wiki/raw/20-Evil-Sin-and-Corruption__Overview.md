# Evil, Sin, and Corruption

## The Question
Why does evil exist? Not bad decisions. Not mistakes. Not maladaptive behavior. Evil — the deliberate pursuit of destruction, cruelty for its own sake, the systematic corruption of what is good. Where does it come from, and why does it persist?

## Why It Matters
This is the question materialism cannot answer. Evolution selects for advantage. Evil frequently operates against the interest of the person doing it. Self-destructive cruelty has no evolutionary mechanism. The persistence of evil across every culture and every era, despite conferring no survival advantage, is one of the strongest arguments against purely naturalistic explanations of morality.

The Theophysics framework proposes: evil is the entropy-dominant regime of a fractured substrate. It is not a separate substance but a catastrophic failure mode of the same field that generates good. Lies compound. Noise accumulates. The system degrades toward death. Evil is what the substrate looks like when the Grace term approaches zero.

## Key Sub-Questions
- Is evil an objective feature of reality, or a human moral judgment?
- Is evil the absence of good (privation), or an active force?
- Can evil be explained through evolutionary psychology alone?
- Does the existence of evil require a personal adversary (Satan), or can it be explained as emergent entropy?
- Why does evil persist when it's self-destructive?
- Does information theory provide a better model of evil than traditional philosophy?

## Strongest FOR Argument (Evil requires deeper explanation than materialism provides)
Evil is self-destructive cruelty that persists across every culture despite conferring no evolutionary advantage. The direction of brokenness is specific — broken minds always break toward cruelty, not toward excessive kindness. Evil organizes, plans, and recruits. An absence doesn't do that. A force does. Information theory: lies compound exponentially. Every lie accelerates entropy. Evil is noise — but noise that propagates systematically, which distinguishes it from random decay.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument (Materialist account)
Psychopathy is a neurological condition. "Evil" is a moral label humans apply to extreme antisocial behavior. Evolutionary psychology explains aggression, selfishness, and violence through game theory and competing reproductive strategies. No supernatural explanation is needed.
