# Truth, Logic, and Reason

## The Question
What is truth? Is it a human invention, a description of reality, or the foundation of reality itself? And if truth is foundational — necessary, eternal, immutable — what kind of thing must exist for truth to be what it demonstrably is?

## Why It Matters
This is where the entire Theophysics framework either stands or falls. If truth is merely a human label for useful patterns, the framework is philosophy. If truth is a real structural feature of the substrate of reality — prior to matter, prior to time, prior to human minds — then the framework is pointing at something that exists whether anyone believes it or not.

DT-001 demonstrated that 24 properties of mathematical truth match 24 classical divine attributes. No alternative candidate achieves the same score. This section houses the evidence and counterarguments for that claim.

## Key Sub-Questions
- Are logical truths discovered or invented?
- Do abstract objects (numbers, theorems) exist independently of minds?
- Can truth exist without a mind to think it?
- Is the grounding of mathematical truth the same as the grounding of moral truth?
- Does truth require a personal source, or can it be an impersonal structural feature?

## Strongest FOR Argument
The 24-property isomorphism (DT-001): mathematical truth shares every ontological property with the classical ground of moral reality — necessary, eternal, immutable, consistent, universal, immaterial, foundational, non-deceptive, perfect, infinite, rational, beautiful, good, transcendent, omnipresent, self-existent, non-temporal, non-spatial, unique, all-constraining, self-sufficient, ordered, and generative. 24 for 24. The probability of coincidence approaches zero.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Mathematical Platonism without theism: logical truths exist as abstract structures that don't require a mind. They're features of logical space, not products of divine intellect. The step from "truth exists necessarily" to "truth requires a personal source" is the contested move.
