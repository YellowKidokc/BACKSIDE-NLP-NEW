## THE META-ARGUMENT: HOW TO ARGUE

Before content, understand the shape of what you're doing.

**Christians lose TikTok debates because they answer the first question.**

"What's your evidence for God?" — and they fumble into a single claim,
get interrupted, and spend the rest of the debate defending a position
they never got to build.

**The fix: You are a prosecutor, not a witness.**

A prosecutor doesn't answer one question. A prosecutor presents a CASE.
Multiple independent lines of evidence. Stacked. Interconnected.
The jury doesn't evaluate one piece — they evaluate the weight of all of it.

**Three phases. In order. Every time.**

```
PHASE 1: BUILD YOUR CASE        (90 seconds, uninterrupted)
PHASE 2: DISMANTLE THEIR CASE   (60 seconds)
PHASE 3: CLOSE                  (30 seconds)
```

---

## PHASE 1: THE OPENING SALVO (90 seconds)

### The Judgment Layer (Lead with this — it's your strongest move)

"Before I give you evidence, let me show you something you can't argue with.

You have a mechanism inside you right now that evaluates everything —
right or wrong, true or false, better or worse.
You can't turn it off. You're using it right now to decide whether
what I'm saying is valid.

Here's the thing — you can't deny this without proving it.
If you say 'that's wrong' — you just evaluated.
If you say 'that's not evidence' — you judged my claim as inferior.
If you dismiss me — you decided dismissal is the BETTER response.

Every possible reaction to this claim is an instance of the claim.
That's not faith. That's an observation. And it's more empirically
sound than almost anything either side has ever put forward.
Because denying it proves it."

### The Claim Stack (deliver all five before they can interrupt)

"Now let me give you five more things you can't get around.

ONE: Math is immaterial, universal, eternal, and necessary.
Your worldview says only material things exist.
So either math isn't real — and your argument just collapsed —
or something immaterial IS real, and you owe me an explanation.

TWO: Every evil is defined by the good it destroys.
Murder takes LIFE. Theft takes PROPERTY. Lying takes TRUTH.
Evil can't exist without good, but good exists without evil.
Good came first. That's God.

THREE: Science has replaced its own certainties thirteen times
in four centuries. Phlogiston. Aether. Steady-state universe.
Newton's gravity. Every generation was certain. Every generation was wrong.
Your method doesn't produce truth — it produces the best model until
the next one replaces it.

FOUR: The scientific method itself requires ten presuppositions it
can't prove. That the external world exists. That logic is valid.
That truth exists. That math works on reality. That senses are reliable.
You believe all ten. You can't test any of them.
You're standing on a foundation science didn't build.

FIVE: Every abolition movement in Western history was led by
Christians using Christian arguments. William Wilberforce.
Frederick Douglass. Martin Luther King Jr.
The trajectory of my book is toward human dignity.
Show me the trajectory of yours.

Now — which one do you want to start with?"

### Why This Works

- You set the frame. They're now RESPONDING to you.
- They have to pick which claim to attack, which means they implicitly
  concede the others (at least temporarily).
- You've demonstrated breadth. You're not a one-trick pony.
- The judgment layer is first because it's self-proving.
  It warms up the audience before the heavier material.

## PHASE 2: DISMANTLE THEIR CASE (60 seconds)

After they respond to your claims, don't defend — ATTACK their foundation.

### The Six Questions They Can't Answer

"Now let me ask YOU six questions about YOUR worldview.

ONE: Where does math live? It's not material. You can't weigh it.
On your view, where is it?

TWO: Why does logic bind? Why can't contradictions be true?
Logic isn't a physical thing. Where did it come from?

THREE: Why is the universe intelligible at all?
Why can equations written on paper predict what galaxies do?
Even Eugene Wigner called this 'unreasonable.' I can explain it. Can you?

FOUR: Why do you care about justice? Justice doesn't help you survive.
People die for moral principles. That's the OPPOSITE of fitness.
Evolution produces 'avoid danger.' It does NOT produce 'that's unfair.'
Where did your moral evaluation come from?

FIVE: What is consciousness? You can map every neuron.
You can tell me WHICH neurons fire when I see red.
You cannot tell me WHY THERE IS SOMETHING IT IS LIKE to see red.
Your worldview can't explain the one thing you're using to evaluate everything.

SIX: Why does anything exist instead of nothing?
Physics describes what exists and how it behaves.
It has never, in all of history, told you WHY there's a 'what' at all.

I have answers to all six. You have answers to zero.
And you're telling ME I need more evidence?"

## PHASE 3: THE CLOSE (30 seconds)

Pick ONE of these closers based on the room temperature:

### Closer A — The Floor (intellectual rooms)
"You're standing on my floor to tell me my floor doesn't exist.
Every time you reason, calculate, or evaluate — you're on Logos ground.
You're writing checks on an account you claim is empty."

### Closer B — The Courtroom (aggressive rooms)
"You put God on trial using logic, morality, and truth as your standards.
But those are His witnesses. You subpoenaed HIS witnesses to testify against Him."

### Closer C — The Neighbor (emotional rooms / hell questions)
"Your neighbor calls you every single day. Asks you to come over for dinner.
Best food you've ever had. Door's never locked.
You never go. Not once. For twenty years.
Now his house burns down. He shows up at your door.
'Can I live with you forever?'
Would you let him in?
God called. The signal was always there. The door was always open.
You never went. Not because He stopped asking.
Because you never answered."

### Closer D — The Deathbed (rooms attacking Christianity as harmful)
"Nobody on their deathbed says 'I wish I had more data.'
Nobody in their last moments reaches for a peer-reviewed journal.
In the moments when life gets absolutely real —
when you lose someone, when you're facing your own death —
science has nothing for you. Not because it's bad.
Because those questions aren't in its domain.
You're already living in my world. You just won't admit it."

### Closer E — The Mirror (rooms full of aggressive atheists)
"You treat science like a God. You don't question it. You don't doubt it.
You defend it when it's challenged. You get offended when someone
suggests it has limits. That's not a method. That's a faith.
The difference between your faith and mine?
Mine admits it's faith."

## THE THREE METAPHORS (use throughout — not just in closers)

### 1. The Floor
"You're standing on my floor to tell me my floor doesn't exist."
USE WHEN: They use logic, math, or morality against you.
The point: The tools they're using to attack you come from your domain.

### 2. The Checks
"You're writing checks on an account you claim is empty."
USE WHEN: They invoke truth, objectivity, or meaning.
The point: Their worldview can't cash what they're spending.

### 3. The Courtroom
"You subpoenaed God's own witnesses to testify against Him."
USE WHEN: They demand evidence using epistemic standards.
The point: The standards they demand are the evidence they deny.

## RULES OF ENGAGEMENT (from Combat Doctrine — compressed)

1. Never answer first. Always claim first.
2. Don't defend — EXPAND. Add claims. Make the pile bigger.
3. Never say "prove." Say "show" or "demonstrate."
4. Ask questions more than make statements. Let them walk into it.
5. If they can't grant truth exists — walk away.
6. Never argue with someone performing. Only engage thinkers.
7. Say "right and wrong" not "good and evil." Less religious packaging.
8. Lead with the judgment layer. End with a closer. Save the neighbor.
9. The person asking questions controls the conversation.
10. You're not trying to win. You're trying to divide. Two systems. Pick.

## THE JUDGMENT LAYER — FULL DEVELOPMENT

### What It Is
The observation that every conscious agent — human, AI, naturalist,
nihilist, theist, atheist — operates on an internal evaluation axis.
Right/wrong. Better/worse. Valid/invalid. True/false.

### Why It's Foundational
- It is pre-rational (fires before philosophy)
- It is pre-cultural (every culture has it)
- It is inescapable (denial proves it)
- It is structural (can't be turned off)
- It is universal (every conscious agent, every domain)

### The Self-Referential Trap
The claim cannot be engaged with without proving itself.
This is the Descartes structure expanded:
- Descartes: Can't doubt existence without thinking → proves existence
- Evaluation Axis: Can't evaluate the claim without evaluating → proves evaluation

### Deployment in TikTok Format
"You have a mechanism in you that tells you what's right and wrong."
Then shut up. Every possible response proves the claim:
- "That doesn't prove God" → You just evaluated. You used the mechanism.
- "That's just evolution" → You judged that explanation as BETTER. Evaluation.
- "That's ridiculous" → Judgment. You just did the thing.
- "There is no right and wrong" → Is that statement right?

There is no response that doesn't prove the claim.

### The Bridge to God
If the evaluation axis is:
- Universal (all agents have it)
- Structural (can't be turned off)
- Pre-rational (fires before philosophy)
- Normative (tracks better/worse, not just different)

Then reality itself has normative structure.
If reality has normative structure, right and wrong aren't opinions.
They're built in.

And if they're built in — who built them in?
