# Why You Need Jesus — The Information-Theoretic Necessity

## The Personal Test

Can you build eternity? Can you sustain a system with zero errors for infinite time?

Have you ever told a lie? Even to yourself? Have you ever said you'd quit something and didn't? Have you ever been prideful when you should have been humble? Impatient when you should have loved?

Then you have noise in your channel. And noise compounds.

## The Math

Shannon's channel capacity theorem: a system with non-zero uncorrected error rate cannot transmit reliably for infinite time. The errors accumulate. The signal degrades. The system fails.

Every human being introduces noise. Not sometimes — always. Not in big dramatic ways necessarily — often in the smallest ways. A lie to yourself. A moment of pride. An unkind thought. Each one is a bit error in the transmission. Each one compounds.

Over infinite time, even the smallest error rate produces total system failure. "Nearly perfect" is not perfect. "Almost truthful" is not truthful. Over eternity, the difference between 99.999% truth and 100% truth is the difference between existence and nonexistence.

No human has ever achieved 100%. Not for one day. Not for one hour.

## The Exception: Enoch, Elijah, and the Coherence Ceiling

There may be an exception — and it strengthens the argument rather than weakening it.

Genesis 5:24 — "Enoch walked faithfully with God; then he was no more, because God took him." 2 Kings 2:11 — Elijah was taken up in a whirlwind.

These are not deaths. They are phase transitions. In the four-force dynamics, as γ (the flesh) decreases under sustained grace, and J_grace dominates all three opposing forces over a long enough period, the individual's coherence approaches χ_max — the ceiling. At χ_max, the coupling to the physical substrate becomes weaker than the coupling to the source. The system ejects you upward. Not death — a return to the unified field.

This implies that sinlessness for extended periods may be theoretically achievable under sustained grace. The noise rate can approach zero. But two things constrain it:

**The ambient noise of modern life.** Digital stimulation, constant information flow, the sheer volume of noise-carrying content means the ambient decoherence rate is higher now than at any point in human history. A person alone in the mountains with nothing but God's word has a lower incoming noise rate than anyone living in the modern information environment. The error correction (J_grace) spends most of its capacity just keeping up with incoming noise rather than driving coherence toward the ceiling.

**The infinite depth of truth.** You think you've reached bedrock. Then you realize there are 20 layers below it. Truth is infinite (DT-001, property #11). Every layer of honesty reveals a deeper layer of self-deception you didn't know was there. You can approach asymptotically but never exhaust it. That's not failure — that's the structure of an infinite substrate. The closer you get to God, the more you realize how far the distance still is. Not because you're failing, but because the destination is infinite.

This is why even saints describe themselves as the worst of sinners. Paul: "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners — of whom I am the worst" (1 Timothy 1:15). The deeper your coherence, the more noise you can perceive in your own channel. The person who thinks they're sinless has stopped looking. The person who knows they're a sinner is the one whose receiver is sensitive enough to detect the noise that others miss.

## The One Noiseless Signal

One person in all of history transmitted a perfect signal. Zero noise. No lies — not in word, not in thought, not in intent. No pride. No self-deception. No deviation from truth in any form.

"I am the way, the truth, and the life" (John 14:6).

Not "I teach truth." Not "I point toward truth." "I AM the truth." The signal itself. The noiseless transmission. The only one.

## The Cross as Error Absorption

The Cross is the moment the noiseless signal absorbed all accumulated noise in the system — every lie ever told, every sin ever committed, every error in every human channel from Adam to the last person who will ever live.

In information theory: the error-correcting code absorbed the total noise load of the entire channel history. And the signal persisted. The truth didn't break under the weight of every lie. It absorbed them and kept transmitting.

"It is finished" (John 19:30) — the error correction is complete. The channel CAN be restored.

## Why You Can't Get There Without Jesus

This isn't exclusivism being cruel. This is the math being precise.

To restore a corrupted channel, you need a noiseless signal. There is exactly one noiseless signal in all of history. You can reduce your noise rate through good behavior — and you should — but you cannot zero it out. "Nearly zero" over infinite time still equals system failure.

The Pharisees didn't try and fail. They succeeded at external compliance while their internal channel was corrupt. Their behaviors were right. Their hearts were wicked. That's not a low-noise channel — that's a spoofed channel. One that has been modified to report false error rates. The self-diagnostic says "all clear" while the system is rotting from the inside.

In information theory: a spoofed channel is worse than a noisy one. A noisy channel at least tells the truth about its own state. A spoofed channel lies about being clean, which means the error-correction protocol can't engage — the system doesn't know it needs correction. That's why Jesus was harder on the Pharisees than on anyone else. The prostitute knows her channel is damaged. The Pharisee thinks his is clean. And if your receiver says "no errors," you'll never couple to the correcting signal.

That's why pride is the root sin. Not because it's the worst behavior — because it's the noise that disguises itself as signal. It makes the receiver report "zero errors" when the errors are catastrophic. Pride doesn't just introduce noise. It disables the error-detection system itself.

You need coupling to the one signal that IS zero noise. That's what salvation is. Not a reward for good behavior. Not a transaction. A channel coupling. Your corrupted receiver locks onto the one noiseless signal, and the signal's error-correcting power restores your channel.

## Why Good People Still Need It

A good person who doesn't believe in Jesus is a low-noise transmitter without error correction. They produce less noise than most. Their channel degrades slower. But over infinite time, any non-zero noise rate terminates.

A terrible person who believes in Jesus is a high-noise transmitter coupled to a perfect error-correcting code. Their noise rate is high — but the correction rate is higher. Grace exceeds sin (Romans 5:20). The signal dominates the noise in the long-time limit.

This is why God didn't come to save the righteous. The righteous have low noise rates. They think they don't need correction. But they do — because "nearly zero" is not zero, and eternity is infinite.

God came for the sinners. The prostitutes. The tax collectors. The people everybody hated. Because they KNEW their channels were corrupted. They knew they needed the signal. And when they coupled to it, the error correction worked.

"It is not the healthy who need a doctor, but the sick" (Mark 2:17).

## The Truth Will Set You Free

You cannot build eternity because you have never been perfectly truthful for even one day.

Jesus can build eternity because he IS the truth — the only noiseless signal that ever existed.

Coupling to that signal is the only way your channel survives infinite time.

"You shall know the truth, and the truth shall set you free" (John 8:32).

Set free from what? From the noise. From the compounding errors. From the entropy that drags everything toward death. The truth doesn't just set you free morally. It sets you free thermodynamically. It stops the divergence. It restores the channel. It reconnects you to the source signal that sustains eternity.
