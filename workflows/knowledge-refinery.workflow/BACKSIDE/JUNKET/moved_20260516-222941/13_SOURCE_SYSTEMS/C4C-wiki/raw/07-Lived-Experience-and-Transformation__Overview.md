# Lived Experience and Transformation

## The Question
Do personal experiences of God — transformed lives, answered prayers, near-death experiences, martyrdom — constitute evidence for Christianity?

## Why This Matters
For most believers, personal experience is the primary reason they believe. The question is whether subjective experience carries any evidential weight in a public argument, or whether it's entirely reducible to psychology.

## Strongest FOR Argument
The sheer volume and cross-cultural consistency of religious experience, combined with documented cases of radical life transformation (addiction recovery, criminal reform) that coincide with conversion, constitutes a pattern that demands explanation. The early martyrs died for claims they were in a position to know were true or false.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Every religion claims identical experiential evidence. Hindus, Muslims, Mormons, and atheists who meditate all report powerful transformative experiences. If experience validates Christianity, it equally validates contradictory truth claims. Additionally, psychology provides well-documented mechanisms (confirmation bias, placebo effect, cognitive dissonance) that explain religious experience without requiring the supernatural.
