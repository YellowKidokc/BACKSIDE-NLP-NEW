# APO-14: Historical Case (Jesus)
## "Did He rise or didn't He? Everything else is a side argument."

---

## The Hook (30 seconds)

Forget every philosophical argument on this list. One question settles it: did Jesus rise from the dead? If He did, He gets to set the terms. If He didn't, I'll go home. That claim is historically testable. Multiple independent sources. Hostile witnesses. A movement that shouldn't have survived its founder's execution but exploded instead. Either something happened in that tomb or it didn't. Let's talk about THAT instead of the side arguments.

### Common Objections & Responses

**"You can't prove historical events"**
"Correct — you can't REPLICATE them. But you can evaluate evidence. Same method that establishes Caesar crossed the Rubicon. The evidence for the resurrection is actually stronger by standard historical criteria."

**"The Bible is biased"**
"Every historical source has a perspective. That's not bias — that's testimony. You don't throw out Roman records because Romans wrote them. You cross-reference. And the NT has more manuscript support than any other ancient document."

**"Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence"**
"Define 'extraordinary evidence.' If you mean 'more evidence than normal claims' — fine, I've got more. If you mean 'evidence that would be impossible under naturalism' — you've defined away the possibility of ever being convinced. That's not skepticism. That's unfalsifiability."
