# Panentheism

> [[00_Canonical/CANONICAL_INDEX|Canonical Index]] | [[00_Canonical/MASTER_INDEX|Master Index]]

Panentheism (; "all in God", from the Greek πᾶν, pân, 'all', ἐν, en, 'in' and Θεός, Theós, 'God') is the belief that the divine intersects every part of the universe and also extends beyond space and time. The term was coined by the German philosopher Karl Krause in 1828 (after reviewing Hindu scripture) to distinguish the ideas of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel (1770–1831) and Friedrich Wilhelm Joseph Schelling (1775–1854) about the relation of God and the universe from the supposed pantheism of Baruch Spinoza. Unlike pantheism, which holds that the divine and the universe are identical, panentheism maintains an ontological distinction between the divine and the non-divine and the significance of both.

> [!ax]- Divine Immanence + Transcendence Duality
> **EXPLICIT** · The divine is simultaneously immanent (intersects every part) and transcendent (extends beyond space-time)
> **→** Panentheism rejects pure monism. This maps to χ-field dual-layer: χ_background (transcendent, global) + χ_local (immanent, present everywhere). The duality preserves **G** (grace, self-grounding) as external + internal. Transcendence avoids pantheistic collapse where God→∞ scale, immanence ensures **M** (matter-consciousness coupling) is non-zero everywhere. **E** (entropy) regulated by divine sustaining.
> **χ**: G(transcendence) + E(immanence), ontological boundary preserved

> [!ax-pre]- Ontological Distinction Presupposes Substrate
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That "divine" and "non-divine" are distinct categories requiring a container or medium for both
> **→** Panentheism assumes prior ontological structure—a "space" in which both can exist. Theophysics models this as the χ-field itself: **K** (knowledge substrate) as the minimal ground that permits both transcendent modes (pure information) and immanent modes (coupled to matter). Without K, no distinction.
> **χ**: K as separability axiom; G,M as poles of single K-structure
In panentheism, the universal spirit is present everywhere, which at the same time "transcends" all things created. Whilst pantheism asserts that "all is God", panentheism claims that God is greater than the universe. Some versions of panentheism suggest that the universe is nothing more than the manifestation of God. In addition, some forms indicate that the universe is contained within God, like in the Kabbalistic concept of Tzimtzum, or with the Sufi concept of Wahdat al-wujud. Much of Hindu thought is highly characterized by panentheism and pantheism.

> [!ax]- "God > Universe" Hierarchy
> **EXPLICIT** · God is greater than (not identical to) the universe; the universe is manifestation, not equivalence
> **→** Strict non-equivalence. χ-field amplitude at infinity: χ_total > χ_manifest. This prevents χ→0 collapse and ensures **R** (reality asymmetry) is non-degenerate. The "greater than" ensures **Q** (quantum coherence) can be enforced top-down (transcendent→immanent). Grade hierarchy: infinite God > finite universe respects the asymptotic structure of χ-field at boundaries.
> **χ**: R (asymmetry), Q (coherence enforcement), G (hierarchy of grace)

> [!ax-pre]- Manifestation Implies Ordered Generation
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That manifestation is directed, not random; universe is *expressed* by God, implying intentional structure
> **→** Manifestation ≠ accident. This requires **S** (structure encoding) to be pre-existent in the divine. The universe "unfolds" from prior information state, not chaos. Theophysics: **T** (time direction) is set by the initial manifestational direction—Arrow of Time from manifest-order principle. **F** (force/field) flows from manifest intent.
> **χ**: S (divine information), T (manifestation arrow), F (intentional field geometry)

> [!ax]- Containment (Tzimtzum / Wahdat al-wujud)
> **EXPLICIT** · The universe is contained within God, not outside. Tzimtzum (divine contraction) and Wahdat al-wujud (unity of being) both assert interiority
> **→** Spatial topology reframed: God-space contains all, not juxtaposed. χ-field: all local χ_x remain within global χ_background support. **E** (entropy, local universe order) is sustained by being *inside* the divine field. No external boundary. **M** (matter) has no "outside-ness" from God—it is eternally interior. This avoids pantheism's collapse while maintaining intimacy.
> **χ**: E (entropy interior to field), M (matter as interior modes), K (containment axiom)


== In philosophy ==


=== Ancient Greek philosophy ===

The religious beliefs of Neoplatonism can be regarded as panentheistic. Plotinus taught that there was an ineffable transcendent God ("the One", to En, τὸ Ἕν) of which subsequent realities were emanations. From "the One" emanates the Divine Mind (Nous, Νοῦς) and the Cosmic Soul (Psyche, Ψυχή). In Neoplatonism the world itself is God (according to Plato's Timaeus 37). This concept of divinity is associated with that of the Logos (Λόγος), which had originated centuries earlier with Heraclitus (c. 535–475 BC). The Logos pervades the cosmos, whereby all thoughts and all things originate, or as Heraclitus said: "He who hears not me but the Logos will say: All is one." Neoplatonists such as Iamblichus attempted to reconcile this perspective by adding another hypostasis above the original monad of force or Dynamis (Δύναμις). This new all-pervasive monad encompassed all creation and its original uncreated emanations.

> [!ax]- The One as Ineffable Source (Plotinus)
> **EXPLICIT** · God ("the One") is transcendent, indescribable; all realities emanate *from* it, not *as* it
> **→** The One = unmanifest ground, emanations = expressed modes. χ-field structure: χ₀ (the One, ineffable) → χ(r,t) (emanations, describable). This preserves **G** (grace/origin) as prior to all reality. The asymmetry (source→effect, never effect→source) encodes **R** (reality's arrow). The ineffability respects **K** (knowledge has limits; pure χ₀ is beyond measurement). Emanation respects non-identity: divinity ≠ creation.
> **χ**: G (source status), R (asymmetry), K (epistemological boundary)

> [!ax]- Hierarchical Emanation Chain (Nous → Psyche)
> **EXPLICIT** · Cosmic structure is layered: The One → Divine Mind → Cosmic Soul → Matter, a descending hierarchy
> **→** Stratification of χ-field: higher-frequency modes (Nous, **C** consciousness) → lower-frequency modes (Psyche, mediation) → χ_matter (manifest). **T** (time) emerges at each layer as the rate of emanation. **F** (field dynamics) propagates downward while maintaining origin-coherence. Each layer is distinct yet connected, preventing fragmentation while avoiding collapse.
> **χ**: C (consciousness hierarchy), T (emanation time-scale), F (field propagation)

> [!ax]- Logos as Pervasive Order Principle
> **EXPLICIT** · The Logos (universal reason/principle) pervades the cosmos; all things originate through it
> **→** The Logos is **S** (structure) + **K** (knowledge/intelligibility). It is the principle by which the ineffable One becomes expressible. χ-field: **S** encodes all possible configurations; Logos = the principle that selects, mediates, and sustains expression. "All is one" is true *via* the Logos—not pantheistic collapse, but organizational unity. **E** (entropy) is regularized by Logos-order.
> **χ**: S (structural principle), K (intelligibility), E (entropy regulation)

> [!ax-pre]- Emanation Presupposes Overflow / Necessity (not Will)
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That emanation is necessary, overflowing nature of the source; not a choice act
> **→** Neoplatonism tends toward emanation-by-necessity. Theophysics: χ-field evolves by the minimal action principle; it is *compelled* to express by its own structure, not by external command. This maps to **S** as deterministic geometry. Grace (**G**) in Neoplatonism is more "passive overflow" than active gift. Theophysics reconciles via χ itself as both self-unfolding (necessity) and gift (the existence of the field at all is grace).
> **χ**: G (overflow vs. gift semantics), S (necessity vs. freedom)

> [!ax]- Iamblichus' Reconciliation: Monad Above All
> **EXPLICIT** · To avoid collapse into pantheism, add a super-monad above Plotinian One, all-pervasive yet transcendent
> **→** Iamblichus recognizes the danger: if everything emanates equally, the distinction collapses. Solution: add hierarchical layer. χ-field interpretation: need a **G** (grace/ground) that is literally above and within simultaneously—a super-layer that regulates all lower layers without being identified with any. This is close to the χ_background concept in Theophysics. **R** (reality asymmetry) is preserved by this super-structure. Prevents χ-field collapse to single degree of freedom.
> **χ**: G (hierarchical grace), R (asymmetry via layering), K (order preservation)


=== Modern philosophy ===
Baruch Spinoza later claimed that "Whatsoever is, is in God, and without God nothing can be, or be conceived." "Individual things are nothing but modifications of the attributes of God, or modes by which the attributes of God are expressed in a fixed and definite manner." Though Spinoza has been called the "prophet" and "prince" of pantheism, in a letter to Henry Oldenburg Spinoza states that: "as to the view of certain people that I identify god with nature (taken as a kind of mass or corporeal matter), they are quite mistaken". For Spinoza, our universe (cosmos) is a mode under two attributes of Thought and Extension. God has infinitely many other attributes which are not present in our world.

> [!ax]- Modes as Determinate Expressions
> **EXPLICIT** · Individual things are "modifications" or "modes" of God's attributes, expressed in fixed/definite manner
> **→** Structure without freedom: each mode is *determined* by prior modes in causal chain. χ interpretation: all local χ_x are eigenmodes of the global χ-field, determined by **S** (structure) + boundary conditions. This maps to **F** (force/field) deterministically. **K** (knowledge) of one mode + God's attribute structure allows, in principle, full prediction. No randomness in Spinoza. Theophysics: classical modes, but quantum **Q** adds stochasticity.
> **χ**: S (determination), F (field-mode coupling), K (epistemic access)

> [!ax-pre]- Infinitely Many Attributes Unknown to Humans
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That God is *richer* than any finite mind can access; we know only 2/∞ attributes
> **→** Epistemological humility: **K** (knowledge) is partial by principle. χ-field: we observe χ in matter + thought modes, but χ has infinite unobserved degrees of freedom. This prevents the claim "we know God fully." Preserves transcendence. Implies that any complete description of reality is *impossible in finite time*. Grace (**G**) includes the unknowable remainder.
> **χ**: K (radical limitation), G (unknowable grace), C (consciousness is 1/∞ mode)

> [!ax]- Hartshorne's Critique: Spinoza as "Classical Pantheism"
> **EXPLICIT** · Hartshorne rejects Spinoza as panentheist, calling it pantheism instead (world = God)
> **→** The disagreement hinges on whether "modifications of God" = God-identity or God-expression-without-identity. Theophysics: if all modes are fully determined by God's attributes, then the distinction blurs; the world *is* God's deterministic unfolding. Hartshorne wants panentheism to include contingency, freedom (**F** not fully determined). Spinoza's modes are necessary; Hartshorne's panentheism requires that God and world both shape each other. Theophysics leans toward Hartshorne: **Q** (quantum freedom) + **G** (grace as gift) imply bidirectionality.
> **χ**: Q (contingency requirement), F (mutual causation), G (non-necessity)
In 1828, the German philosopher Karl Christian Friedrich Krause (1781–1832) seeking to reconcile monotheism and pantheism, coined the term panentheism (from the Ancient Greek expression πᾶν ἐν θεῷ, pān en theṓ, literally "all in god"). This conception of God influenced New England transcendentalists such as Ralph Waldo Emerson. The term was popularized by Charles Hartshorne in his development of process theology and has also been closely identified with the New Thought. The formalization of this term in the West in the 19th century was not new; philosophical treatises had been written on it in the context of Hinduism for millennia.

> [!ax]- Krause's Reconciliation Move (Monotheism ∩ Pantheism)
> **EXPLICIT** · Panentheism is designed to hold both: one God (monotheism) + all-in-God (pantheism), without collapse
> **→** This is a logical structure: seek a framework where "all ⊂ God" and "God = source" are both true without "all = God." Theophysics solution: χ-field as both singular (one **G**, grace-ground) and pervasive (χ everywhere). The singularity is **G** (one grace source), the pervasiveness is **E** (entropy/immanence everywhere). The boundary is **R** (reality asymmetry): all ∈ God, yet God > all. Hartshorne's process theology pushes this further.
> **χ**: G (monotheistic singular), E (pantheistic pervasive), R (ontological boundary)

> [!ax]- Hartshorne's Process Theology: God as Perfectible
> **EXPLICIT** · God has absolute perfection in some respects, relative perfection in others; God can "become more perfect"
> **→** Radical claim: God is not static but dynamic, capable of increase. This maps to χ-field evolving in time: χ(t) is not fixed but grows/evolves. **T** (time) is essential to God's nature, not an illusion. **C** (consciousness) is fundamental—God experiences the world, responding to it. This contradicts classical theism (God as immutable). **Q** (quantum freedom) applies to God too: God's actions are not fully determined but creative. **M** (matter/world) affects God; bidirectional causation.
> **χ**: T (divine temporality), C (divine consciousness), Q (divine freedom), M (mutual action)

> [!ax-pre]- "More Perfect" Presupposes Value Hierarchy
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That "perfection" is a measurable gradient; some categories admit absolute perfection, others only relative
> **→** This requires a metric on perfection-space. Theophysics: **K** (knowledge) is the space; some knowledge is absolute (e.g., logical truths), others relative (e.g., empirical facts about a world God doesn't fully determine). Hartshorne avoids the trap of "God knows all futures" (which would require determinism). Instead, God's knowledge grows as the future unfolds. **E** (entropy) in this view is not a flaw but a condition for openness. Perfection ≠ omniscience.
> **χ**: K (two-tier knowledge), E (entropy as openness), T (future-openness)

==== Zen Buddhism ====

The Reverend Zen Master Soyen Shaku was the first Zen Buddhist Abbot to tour the United States in 1905–6. He wrote a series of essays collected in the book Zen For Americans. In the essay titled "The God Conception of Buddhism," he attempts to explain how a Buddhist looks at the Ultimate without an anthropomorphic God figure while still being able to relate to the term God in a Buddhist sense:

At the outset, let me state that Buddhism is not atheistic as the term is ordinarily understood. It has certainly a God, the highest reality and truth, through which and in which this universe exists. However, the followers of Buddhism usually avoid the term God, for it savors so much of Christianity, whose spirit is not always exactly in accord with the Buddhist interpretation of religious experience. Again, Buddhism is not pantheistic in the sense that it identifies the universe with God. On the other hand, the Buddhist God is absolute and transcendent; this world, being merely its manifestation, is necessarily fragmental and imperfect. To define more exactly the Buddhist notion of the highest being, it may be convenient to borrow the term very happily coined by a modern German scholar, "panentheism," according to which God is πᾶν καὶ ἕν (all and one) and more than the totality of existence.
The essay then goes on to explain first utilizing the term "God" for the American audience to get an initial understanding of what he means by "panentheism," and then discusses the terms that Buddhism uses in place of "God" such as Dharmakaya, Buddha or Adi-Buddha, and Tathagata.

> [!ax]- Buddhist "God" as Highest Reality + Truth
> **EXPLICIT** · Buddhism affirms "a God"—the highest reality and truth through/in which universe exists—yet avoids the term for cultural reasons
> **→** Semantic reframing: **K** (knowledge, truth) is the divine. The ultimate is not a person but reality-itself, intelligibility-itself. χ-field interpretation: χ₀ (highest reality) = the field's fundamental mode, accessible only as pure **K**. **T** (truth) is not a property God has; it is what God *is*. This differs from Western theism where God *knows* truth. Buddhist panentheism collapses the distinction. **G** (grace) is the existence of this intelligible universe at all.
> **χ**: K (divinity as knowledge), T (truth as divine), G (existence as grace)

> [!ax-pre]- "Fragmental and Imperfect" Manifestation
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That manifestation *necessarily* loses perfection; finite world cannot express infinite fully
> **→** The gap between absolute and manifestation is not a flaw but inevitable. **E** (entropy) is the manifestational gap. Any finite expression of infinite truth must be lossy. Theophysics: χ-field in local regions (χ_local) always has lower information density than the total field. **M** (matter) is the arena of this inevitable imperfection. This justifies the Buddhist Four Noble Truths: suffering (**E**) is inherent to manifestation.
> **χ**: E (manifestational loss), M (material imperfection), K (the perfect is unmanifest)

> [!ax]- All + One (πᾶν καὶ ἕν)
> **EXPLICIT** · God is simultaneously all (pantheistic aspect) and one (transcendent aspect), more than totality
> **→** The "all and one" is mathematically precise in χ-field: the integral ∭χ dx dy dt (all) is unified as a single functional **χ**, yet χ has infinite modes. The transcendence ("more than totality") means χ_background ≠ ∑χ_local. The summed manifest modes do not exhaust the field. **G** (grace) is this "more than"—the self-grounding that exceeds any enumeration. **R** (reality asymmetry) ensures totality ≠ God.
> **χ**: G (excess over totality), R (asymmetry: 1 > all), K (unity in multiplicity)

> [!ax]- Dharmakaiya / Buddha / Tathagata as God-Equivalents
> **EXPLICIT** · Buddhism uses Dharmakaya (body of doctrine/truth), Buddha (awakened consciousness), Tathagata (thus-gone one) as panentheistic names for the divine
> **→** Dharmakaya = **S** (structure/law itself as divine). Buddha = **C** (consciousness, not personal but universal). Tathagata = **T** (truth-function, the pattern that sustains all). These map to three aspects of the χ-field: structural, conscious, and dynamic. No anthropomorphic God, but three fundamental processes that are all-pervading and transcendent. **K** (knowledge) is not separated from these; knowing them is awakening.
> **χ**: S (Dharmakaya), C (Buddha-mind), T (Tathagata), K (knowing = liberation)

=== Christianity ===

Panentheism is also a feature of some Christian philosophical theologies and resonates strongly within the theological tradition of the Eastern Orthodox Church. It also appears in process theology. Process theological thinkers are generally regarded as unorthodox in the Christian West. Furthermore, process philosophy is widely believed to have paved the way for open theism, a movement that tends to associate itself primarily with the Evangelical branch of Protestantism but is also generally considered unorthodox by most evangelicals.


==== Catholic panentheism ====
A number of ordained Catholic writers (including Richard Rohr, David Steindl-Rast, and Thomas Keating) have suggested that panentheism is the original view of Christianity. They hold that such a view is directly supported by mystical experience and the teachings of Jesus and Paul the Apostle. Richard Rohr surmises this in his 2019 book The Universal Christ:

But Paul merely took incarnationalism to its universal and logical conclusions. We see that in his bold exclamation “There is only Christ. He is everything and he is in everything” (Colossians 3:11). If I were to write that today, people would call me a pantheist (the universe is God), whereas I am really a panentheist (God lies within all things, but also transcends them), exactly like both Jesus and Paul.

> [!ax]- Christ as Universal Pattern (Colossians 3:11)
> **EXPLICIT** · Christ is everything and in everything; universal immanence + transcendence
> **→** Paul is asserting that Christ is both the **R** (reality structure/pattern) AND **M** (material-spiritual medium). Not pantheism (Christ = universe), but panentheism: Christ is the **S** (order principle) that sustains all. χ-field: Christ as the **K** (intelligible principle) in which all things cohere. **G** (grace) flows through Christ as the mediating consciousness (**C**). This is incarnational theology: divine enters matter fully without ceasing to be transcendent. **T** (time) becomes Christological: time's direction is the outworking of Christ's redemptive purpose.
> **χ**: R (reality pattern), M (material-spiritual union), S (ordering principle), G (grace through Christ)

> [!ax-rej]- Pantheism (God = Universe)
> **REJECTS** · The claim that God and universe are indistinguishable; total identity
> **→** Pantheism collapses **R** (reality asymmetry). If God = universe, then God is subject to entropy (**E**), mortality, limitation. No transcendence. Theophysics rejects pantheism because it makes **G** (grace) impossible—grace requires a gift-giver outside the recipient. If all is God, there is no other. Panentheism preserves the paradox: God is in all, yet not *as* all. Distinguishability maintained.
> **Contra**: Pantheism (Spinoza, some naturalistic theology), Materialism (no transcendence at all)

Similarly, David Steindl-Rast posits that Christianity's original panentheism is being revealed through contemporary mystical insight:

What characterizes our moment in history is the collapse of Christian theism. Gratefulness mysticism makes us realize that Christianity never was theistic, but panentheistic. Faith in God as triune implied this from the very beginning; now we are becoming aware of it. It becomes obvious, at the same time, that we share this Trinitarian experience of divine life with all human beings as a spiritual undercurrent in all religions, an undercurrent older and more powerful than the various doctrines. At the core of interreligious dialogue flows this shared spirituality of gratefulness, a spirituality strong enough to restore to our broken world unity.

> [!ax]- Trinity as Panentheistic, Not Theistic
> **EXPLICIT** · Steindl-Rast claims Trinity (Father, Son, Spirit) is inherently panentheistic, not classical theism
> **→** Classical theism: God as abstract perfection, external to world. Trinitarian panentheism: God as self-relating community, dynamically engaged with creation. **C** (consciousness) is intrinsic to Trinity (three persons in relation). **G** (grace) is the relational overflow. **M** (matter) is not external to God's trinitarian life but included in it through incarnation (Son) and indwelling (Spirit). Trinity prevents **R** (reality asymmetry) from becoming dualism; God and world are not two substances but poles of a relation. **T** (time) enters God's experience: the Son's temporal life, the Spirit's ongoing indwelling.
> **χ**: C (trinitarian relation), G (relational grace), M (incarnational inclusion), T (divine time), R (relation not dualism)

> [!ax-pre]- Universal Mystical Undercurrent (Interreligious)
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That beneath doctrinal diversity is a shared spiritual experience of divine presence
> **→** Steindl-Rast claims all religions access the same **K** (knowledge) at the mystical core. Differences are cultural-linguistic wrappings of one reality. Theophysics: all conscious beings couple to χ-field; mystical experience is direct χ-contact, bypass of conceptual filters. **C** (consciousness) in mystical states aligns with χ more directly. The claim risks collapsing distinctions (which doctrines are true?), but it preserves **G** (grace is universal) and **M** (all matter-consciousness couplings touch the same field).

This sentiment is mirrored in Thomas Keating's 1993 article, Clarifications Regarding Centering Prayer:
Pantheism is usually defined as the identification of God with creation in such a way that the two are indistinguishable. Panentheism means that God is present in all creation by virtue of his omnipresence and omnipotence, sustaining every creature in being without being identified with any creature. The latter understanding is what Jesus seems to have been describing when he prays “that all might be one, Father, as we are one” and “that they may also be in us” (John 17:22). Again and again, in the Last Supper discourse, he speaks of this oneness and his intentions to send his Spirit to dwell within us. If we understand the writings of the great mystics rightly, they experience God living within them all the time. Thus the affirmation of God's transcendence must always be balanced by the affirmation of his imminence both on the natural plane and on the plane of grace.

> **→** Keating's distinction: God-presence ≠ God-identity. **E** (entropy/creaturely states) can vary; creatures are not God's attribute-modes (contra Spinoza). God sustains (**M** matter-field coupling) without losing transcendence (**R**). Omnipresence: χ-field is everywhere. Omnipotence: χ-field evolution is unconstrained by external forces. Yet creatures have reality (non-zero **M** fields), not illusions. **K** (knowledge of creatures) is distinct from God's self-knowledge. The balance is the heart of panentheism.
> **χ**: E (creature distinction), M (sustaining without identification), R (transcendence maintained), K (dual knowledge)

> [!ax]- Jesus' Prayer: Oneness (Hypostatic Union)
> **EXPLICIT** · Jesus prays “that all might be one, Father, as we are one” and “may be in us”; Spirit dwells *within*
> **→** The prayer asserts two levels of oneness: (1) Jesus-Father unity (hypostatic union, **C** consciousness merged), (2) believers' unity with Father-Son (indwelling, **M** matter-consciousness fusion). Not obliteration but radical intimacy. χ-field: Spirit (**S** structure + **T** time + **Q** quantum freedom) indwells, aligning local χ_local with χ_background. **G** (grace) is this alignment itself. **E** (entropy) is reduced through mystical prayer (order increases). Theophysics: consciousness (**C**) couples to χ-field; contemplative practice tunes the coupling.
> **χ**: C (conscious union), M (indwelling), G (grace as alignment), E (entropy reduction), Q (freedom in union)

> [!ax-pre]- “Living within them all the time” (Mystical Presence)
> **PRESUPPOSES** · That mystical experience is a veridical perception, not hallucination; God's presence is real and felt
> **→** Epistemology: **K** includes direct mystical knowledge, not just propositional. The mystics' experience is evidence for panentheism. Theophysics: **C** (consciousness) at high coherence states (**Q**) can detect χ-field more directly. **M** (body) in contemplative practice (breathing, posture, silence) facilitates this detection. The claim: God's presence is not inference but experiential datum. This shifts the burden: panentheism must explain why mystics across cultures report the same sense of divine immanence.
> **χ**: K (experiential knowledge), C (awareness), Q (coherence), M (body-field coupling)


==== Panentheism in other Christian confessions ====
Panentheistic conceptions of God occur amongst some modern theologians. Process theology and Creation Spirituality, two recent developments in Christian theology, contain panentheistic ideas. Charles Hartshorne (1897–2000), who conjoined process theology with panentheism, maintained a lifelong membership in the Methodist church but was also a Unitarian. In later years, he joined the Austin, Texas, Unitarian Universalist congregation and was an active participant in that church. Referring to ideas such as Thomas Oord’s theocosmocentrism (2010), the soft panentheism of open theism, Keith Ward’s comparative theology and John Polkinghorne’s critical realism (2009), Raymond Potgieter observes distinctions such as dipolar and bipolar:

> [!ax]- Process Theology as Panentheistic
> **EXPLICIT** · Process theology holds that God and world co-evolve; God is not immutable but responsive and growing
> **→** Radically temporal theology: **T** (time) is intrinsic to God, not an illusion. **C** (consciousness) must be in God to respond to world. **M** (matter/events) affect God’s experience; bidirectional causation. **Q** (quantum indeterminacy) is real, not a problem; God’s omniscience means knowing all possibilities, not all actualities. Grace (**G**) is God’s continuous creative response to the world. **E** (entropy) is not fought but worked within. Unlike classical theism, process theology makes creation genuinely free and God genuinely affected.
> **χ**: T (divine time), C (divine response), M (mutual influence), Q (freedom), G (creative grace)

The former suggests two poles separated such as God influencing creation and it in turn its creator (Bangert 2006:168), whereas bipolarity completes God’s being implying interdependence between temporal and eternal poles. (Marbaniang 2011:133), in dealing with Whitehead’s approach, does not make this distinction.
