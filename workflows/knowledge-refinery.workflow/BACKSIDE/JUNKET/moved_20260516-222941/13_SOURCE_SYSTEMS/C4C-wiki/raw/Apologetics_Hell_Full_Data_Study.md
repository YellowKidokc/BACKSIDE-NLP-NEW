## The Core Question

The atheist TikTok claim (Madalyn Murray O'Hair originally, Jon Cohen and others now): **"Jesus preaches hell more than anyone else in the Bible. You can't retreat to 'that's Old Testament' — Jesus is the primary hell-preacher."**

Partial truth. Full picture requires data.

## THE NUMBERS

### Jesus's hell references as % of his recorded speech

| Metric | Count |
|---|---|
| Jesus hell-threat verses (excluding Mt 16:18 promise) | **23** |
| Total words in those verses (KJV) | **672** |
| Est. total Jesus words in 4 Gospels (KJV) | 42,000 |
| **% of Jesus's speech that is hell-threat** | **1.60%** |

**Jesus spent ~1.6% of his recorded words warning about hell.** The other 98.4% was kingdom, grace (through action, not the word), parables, healing dialogue, teaching about love, forgiveness, prayer, discipleship, and confrontation of hypocrisy. The "Jesus preaches hell constantly" framing is numerically false.

### Who was Jesus warning?

| Audience | Verses | % of hell-threats |
|---|---|---|
| **Disciples (private teaching)** | 11 | **47.8%** |
| Disciples + crowd (public teaching with disciples present) | 4 | 17.4% |
| Crowd / specific cities | 4 | 17.4% |
| Pharisees (direct confrontation) | 3 | 13.0% |
| Pharisees + mixed audience | 1 | 4.3% |

**65.2% of Jesus's hell teaching was directed at his own disciples.** Private instruction to insiders who had already committed to following him. Another 17.4% was confrontation of the Pharisees and their proselytes — religious professionals who used religion for abuse.

Only ~17% was directed at outsider crowds. Of that 17%, most were warnings to cities that had already seen Jesus's miracles and rejected them (Capernaum, Chorazin, Bethsaida) — not "believe or burn" threats to seekers.

### Where Jesus preached hell

| Gospel | Jesus hell-threat verses | % of gospel verses | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Matthew | 16 | 1.5% | Carries 70% of all gospel hell content |
| Luke | 4 | 0.3% | |
| Mark | 3 | 0.4% | |
| **John** | **0** | **0%** | Zero. None. |

**John's zero is the single most important data point.** The gospel most devoted to love — the gospel of "God so loved the world that he gave his only begotten Son" (John 3:16), the gospel of "I am the good shepherd," the gospel of "Jesus wept," the gospel of "love one another as I have loved you" — has no hell threats from Jesus.

The "loving NT Jesus" the atheist tries to separate from "hell Jesus" is literally the Jesus of John. And John's Jesus never mentions hell.

## THE VOCABULARY

Hell in the NT is not one word. It's at least seven distinct terms, and atheists routinely conflate them:

| Term | Meaning | Used by Jesus? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gehenna** | Valley of Hinnom — Jerusalem's dump, historic child-sacrifice site to Molech | 11× by Jesus | Real geographic place. Jer 7:31 prophesied it would become "Valley of Slaughter." Jesus weaponizes the image. |
| **Hades** | Generic death-realm; Greek equivalent of Hebrew Sheol | 3× by Jesus | NOT torment-specific. In Rev 20:14 hades itself gets thrown into the lake of fire. |
| **Outer darkness** | Exclusion from the wedding feast | 3× by Jesus, Matthew only | Image is *separation*, not *fire*. |
| **Weeping and gnashing** | Emotional state of the damned | 6× (always by Jesus) | Grief + rage formula. |
| **Eternal/everlasting fire** | Furnace of judgment | 2× by Jesus | Mt 25:41: "prepared for the devil and his angels" — NOT prepared for humans. |
| **Lake of fire** | Final apocalyptic judgment | **0× by Jesus** | Only in Revelation (5 times). Not in any Gospel, not in any Epistle. |
| **Tartarus** | Greek-myth underworld | **0× by Jesus** | Only in 2 Peter 2:4, for fallen ANGELS, not humans. |

### Who else uses these words?

| Speaker | Hell references |
|---|---|
| Jesus | 22-23 (across all terms) |
| John the Baptist | 2 (Mt 3:12, Lk 3:17 — unquenchable fire) |
| James | 1 (James 3:6 — tongue set on fire of gehenna) |
| Peter | 1 (2 Pet 2:4 — tartarus, for fallen angels) |
| Jude | 1 (Jude 1:7 — eternal fire) |
| John the Revelator | 5 (lake of fire, only in Revelation) |
| **Paul** | **0 uses of gehenna. Zero.** Uses apoleia (destruction) and olethros (ruin), not hell-fire language. |

**Paul — the apostle to the Gentiles, writer of half the NT — never uses the word gehenna.** If hell-threat evangelism were the gospel's core, Paul should use it constantly. He doesn't use it once. His vocabulary is "destruction," "ruin," "eternal death" — which some scholars argue supports annihilationism rather than eternal conscious torment.

## THE 23 JESUS HELL-THREAT VERSES

### Sermon on the Mount (to disciples + crowd)
1. **Matthew 5:22** — gehenna. Anger at brother = danger of hell fire.
2. **Matthew 5:29** — gehenna. Better to lose an eye than whole body to hell.
3. **Matthew 5:30** — gehenna. Parallel, for the hand.

### Centurion episode (to crowd)
4. **Matthew 8:12** — outer darkness. "The *children of the kingdom* shall be cast out." Anti-presumption warning to ETHNIC/RELIGIOUS insiders who assumed lineage saved them.

### Commissioning the Twelve (to disciples privately)
5. **Matthew 10:28** — gehenna. "Fear him who is able to destroy both soul and body." Argues for annihilationist reading — key word is "destroy" (apolesai).

### Woes on Galilean cities (to crowds who had seen miracles)
6. **Matthew 11:23** — hades. To Capernaum, Jesus's own ministry base. Generic death-realm language from Isaiah 14:15.

### Parable explanations (private to disciples)
7. **Matthew 13:42** — furnace of fire. Wheat and tares explanation.
8. **Matthew 13:50** — furnace of fire. Dragnet parable.

### Teaching on stumbling (to disciples)
9. **Matthew 18:8** — eternal fire. Warning to disciples about what stumbling-blocks cost.
10. **Matthew 18:9** — gehenna. Parallel.
11. **Mark 9:43** — gehenna. Mark's parallel to Mt 18.
12. **Mark 9:45** — gehenna. Foot parallel.
13. **Mark 9:47** — gehenna. Eye parallel.

### Wedding feast parable (to Pharisees + crowd)
14. **Matthew 22:13** — outer darkness. The wedding guest without garment.

### Woes to Pharisees (direct confrontation)
15. **Matthew 23:15** — gehenna. "Twofold more the child of hell than yourselves."
16. **Matthew 23:33** — gehenna. "Ye serpents, generation of vipers."

### Olivet discourse (to disciples)
17. **Matthew 24:51** — weeping/gnashing. Faithful vs unfaithful servant.
18. **Matthew 25:30** — outer darkness. Parable of talents.
19. **Matthew 25:41** — eternal fire. Sheep and goats. Fire "prepared for the devil and his angels" — not originally for humans.

### Luke material
20. **Luke 10:15** — hades. Capernaum parallel.
21. **Luke 12:5** — gehenna. Public-teaching parallel to Mt 10:28.
22. **Luke 13:28** — weeping/gnashing. To crowd: presumption of lineage won't save.
23. **Luke 16:23** — hades. Rich man and Lazarus parable, told to Pharisees (Lk 16:14).

**And the one promise often miscounted as threat:**
- **Matthew 16:18** — "gates of *hades* shall not prevail against it." Not a threat. A promise that death itself cannot overpower the church.

## WHAT THIS DOES TO THE ATTACK

### Atheist claim: "Jesus preaches hell more than anyone else."
**Response:** Partial truth. By raw count, Jesus mentions hell-terms more than any other single speaker. But:
- It's ~1.6% of his recorded speech. Kingdom/grace/love material is 4-5× more frequent.
- 65% was directed at his own disciples, not outsiders.
- John's Gospel — the most loving of the four — has zero.
- Paul doesn't use the word "gehenna" once in 13 letters.

### Atheist claim: "Jesus threatened unbelievers with eternal torment to scare them into believing."
**Response:** Factually backwards. 65% of Jesus's hell teaching was private instruction to his OWN disciples about walking in integrity. 17% was confrontation of religious hypocrites. Only ~17% was crowd teaching, and most of that was to cities that had seen miracles and rejected them — not generic outsider threats.

### Atheist claim: "The loving NT Jesus contradicts the hell-preaching Jesus."
**Response:** There is no "hell-preaching Jesus" in John's Gospel. The Jesus of "God so loved the world" is the same Jesus. The "loving NT" and the "hell NT" aren't two different Jesuses — they're the same speaker emphasizing different things to different audiences.

### Atheist claim: "Jesus wanted people to suffer in hell — hell is on his lips more than anyone else's."
**Response (Mt 25:41):** The only place Jesus identifies *who* hell was prepared for: "everlasting fire, **prepared for the devil and his angels**." Hell wasn't prepared for humans. Humans who end up there align themselves with the rebellion it was made for.
