# Biblical Archaeology

## The Question
Does the physical, archaeological evidence corroborate or contradict the biblical accounts?

## Why This Matters
Archaeology provides the only independent, physical verification of biblical claims. Confirmed details build cumulative credibility. Contradicted details do the opposite.

## Strongest FOR Argument
Multiple specific details from the New Testament have been archaeologically confirmed: the Pilate inscription at Caesarea, the Caiaphas ossuary, the Pool of Bethesda with its five porticoes, first-century synagogues in Galilee, and the accuracy of Luke's references to local titles and geography. The Dead Sea Scrolls confirmed the antiquity and textual stability of OT manuscripts.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
There is no archaeological evidence for the Exodus as described (mass migration of millions through Sinai), the Conquest of Canaan shows a different pattern than Joshua describes, and "biblical archaeology" has historically been driven by confirmation bias — looking for evidence to support predetermined conclusions rather than following the evidence.
