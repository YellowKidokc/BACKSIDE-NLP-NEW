# Debate Combat Framework

## Three Types of Response (every answer needs all three)

1. **Bible/Principle** — grounded in Scripture and God's character
2. **Relatable Analogy** — something the person can feel in their own life
3. **Rhetorical Judo** — flip their own logic back on them

## Six Combat Rules

### Rule 1: Information Overload (3-4 points)
Stack 3-4 points. They might win one, but you carry the distance.

### Rule 2: Establish Knowledge Depth
Show you've done the homework. "If you go down this road, I'm probably going to win."

### Rule 3: The Motivation Flip
"I understand why I'm in this room trying to convince you. I don't understand why you're trying to convince me to change my mind."

### Rule 4: Separate Yourself from Christian History
"I'm not making the Christian history case. I'm not the Crusades. Debate me."

### Rule 5: The Witness Close
"Have you ever met somebody sold out for Jesus for 10, 20 years — and every time you meet them, you know they love the Lord? You felt it in 10 seconds. You felt it after 10 years."

You can't fake that for 20 years. Something happened to that person or it didn't.

### Rule 6: The Church Hurt Diagnostic
"I imagine you probably had some church hurt before you turned atheist. Is that true?"

Research says 60-70% for explicit deconversions. Name it with genuine care, not as a weapon.

## Pre-Moves (before any verse lands)

### The Posture Question
"How do you read the Bible?" — make them declare their interpretive rules before attacking.

### The Four Speech Modes (kills 25-30% instantly)
"Who's holding the microphone in that verse?"

| Mode | Example |
|------|---------|
| God speaking | "Thou shalt not murder" |
| Human to God | Psalm 137:9 — exile's grief |
| Narrator recording | Judges 19 — horror condemned |
| Human to human | Job's friends — wrong theology |

### The Justice/Mercy Diagnostic
"Where does your justice lie? Where does your mercy lie?" Force them to expose their own framework first.

## One-Liners (conversation-enders)

| Attack | One-liner |
|--------|-----------|
| Hell is cruel | "If there's no hell, there's no justice. And you just told me you believe in justice." |
| God is cruel | "You're angry at God for not stopping evil. And angry at God for stopping evil. Pick one." |
| Free will | "You want God to stop all evil but never interfere with your choices. Those are the same sentence." |
| Morality | "You borrowed your moral standard from the book you're attacking." |
| Suffering | "You're not asking why God allows suffering. You're saying you'd do better. That's Genesis 3." |
| Science vs God | "Science tells you HOW the engine runs. It's never once told you WHY there's an engine." |
| Justice + mercy | "How do you get perfect justice and perfect mercy at the same time? One place in history. And you're arguing against it." |
