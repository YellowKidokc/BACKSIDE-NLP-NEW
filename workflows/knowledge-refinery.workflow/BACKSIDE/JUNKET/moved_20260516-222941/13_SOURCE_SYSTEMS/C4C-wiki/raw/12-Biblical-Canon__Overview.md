# Biblical Canon

## The Question
How did the books of the Bible get selected, and does the process of canon formation undermine or support the authority of Scripture?

## Why This Matters
The canon question is one of the most common objections: "Men decided which books to include." If the canon was a political product, the foundation shifts. If it was a recognition of texts already functioning as authoritative, the picture changes.

## Strongest FOR Argument
The canonical books were recognized early — not invented at Nicaea. The Muratorian Fragment (c. 170 AD), the Church Fathers' citations, and the consistency across early manuscript collections show that the core NT canon was functionally settled long before any council voted on it.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Multiple Christian communities used different texts. The Gnostic Gospels, the Gospel of Thomas, and other texts were actively suppressed. Council decisions (Carthage 397, etc.) were influenced by political and institutional interests. The "winners wrote the history."
