# The One Truth

### Everything Comes Down to This

*David Lowe — March 14, 2026*
*Written with Claude Opus*

---

Who do you trust?

Not with $23 trillion. Not with the printer press. Not with a government or a board or a committee. Who do you trust with the world? With your life? With eternity?

You already know the answer. Nobody. You don't trust anybody because you can't. Not because people are all terrible — some are extraordinary. But because on a long enough timeline, everybody falls. The temptation comes. The pressure builds. The moment arrives where doing the right thing costs everything, and doing the wrong thing costs nothing visible. And somebody breaks. Maybe not today. Maybe not this year. But eventually. Always. Every time.

That's not cynicism. That's the failure rate. It's empirical. It's measured. It's as predictable as gravity.

So who do you trust with eternity?

---

Think about what eternity requires.

Not a long time. Not a billion years. *Forever.* A system that runs without end. No errors compounding. No lies accumulating. No slow rot in the foundation that nobody notices until the building comes down.

Shannon proved this mathematically in 1948: a system with any uncorrected error rate, given infinite time, degrades to noise. Any non-zero failure rate, stretched across forever, produces total collapse. It's not a matter of if. It's when. And "when" across eternity is always.

So what sustains eternity? What's the one thing that doesn't degrade? That doesn't compound? That doesn't contradict itself or need correction or slowly rot from the inside?

One word.

**Truth.**


Truth is the only thing that can stand for eternity. Not because it's a nice idea. Because it's the only thing that doesn't diverge. 2 + 2 = 4 was true before the first star ignited and it will be true after the last one burns out. It doesn't need maintenance. It doesn't need correction. It doesn't accumulate errors. It just *is* — the same yesterday, today, and forever.

A lie can't do that. A lie requires another lie to prop it up. And that lie requires another. The error rate compounds exponentially. Every lie you've ever told is still echoing somewhere in the substrate, producing noise you can't see, degrading the signal you didn't know was there. That's not theology. That's information theory. Shannon proved it. The Second Law confirms it. Lies are entropy. Truth is the only negentropy in the universe.

So the person who creates a world — who sustains eternity — has to be truth. Not partly true. Not mostly true. Not true on Sundays. *True.* In nature. In essence. In every thought and every word and every intention across every moment of an existence that has no end.

And how close do any of us get to that?

---

I lie to myself every day. I say I'm going to quit things I never quit. I make promises I don't keep. I know what I should do and I do the other thing. I am not unique in this. I am the human condition. Everyone who has ever lived has introduced noise into the channel. Everyone. No exceptions that lasted more than a few days — and even those exceptions, the Enochs and the Elijahs, got pulled out of the system because they became too coherent for this broken world to hold.

So here's the question that breaks every other worldview:

If nobody is perfectly true, and eternity requires perfect truth, how does anyone get in?

The materialist says there is no eternity, so the question doesn't apply. Fine — but the materialist also can't explain where evil comes from, can't explain consciousness, can't generate information from noise, and can't ground morality in anything but preference. The materialist framework fractures on three of four evaluation criteria. It passes physics and fails everything else.

The moralist says: be good enough. Live a good life. Follow the rules. Earn your way in. But "good enough" is a non-zero error rate. And a non-zero error rate across infinite time produces total system failure. "Good enough" is a time bomb. It works for 80 years. It doesn't work for eternity. The Pharisees were the most "good enough" people who ever lived, and Jesus called them whitewashed tombs — because their external signal looked clean while their internal channel was spoofed. They weren't low-noise transmitters. They were transmitters lying about their own error rate. That's worse. That disables the error-correction protocol entirely, because if your diagnostic says "all clear," you'll never couple to the signal that fixes you.

The deist says: God exists but doesn't intervene. The watchmaker wound the clock and walked away. But a clock with any friction eventually stops. A channel with any noise eventually fails. A God who made the world and then abandoned it made a world that's dying — and offered no mechanism to save it. That's not a God. That's an engineer who shipped a product with a known defect and no patch.


---

So what does it take to be God?

Two things. Only two. And neither one is negotiable.

**You have to beat death.** That's the one thing everything in the universe is subject to on a long enough timeline. Stars die. Civilizations die. Galaxies die. The universe itself is dying — entropy increasing, information degrading, the whole system running down toward heat death. You don't become God unless you beat the one thing nothing else beats. Death is the final exam. Everything that fails it is not God.

**You have to be true.** Not good. Not powerful. Not wise. *True.* In your nature. In your essence. So morally right that in your presence everything bows — not because you force it, but because you're so *right* that the wrongness in everything else becomes visible by contrast. You've never had a bad thought. Never told a lie. Never came from a place of anger instead of love. You are the definition of love because love and truth are the same thing at the substrate level — they're both the signal without noise.

And as close as we get to that standard? 2 + 2 = 4. Mathematical truth. The thing that never lies, never changes, never degrades, never needs correction. That's the nearest approximation to God's nature that exists in this universe — and it's not even a person. It's a shadow. A projection. A measurement of something so vast that the best our instruments can capture is "two plus two is four."

And there's a third quality. If you were that true — if you were literally the substrate of reality, the truth that sustains eternity — you wouldn't just *be* true. You'd *propagate* truth. Because what you'd hate is lies. Lies are the thing destroying the system you sustain. Lies are the noise degrading the signal you *are*. You'd hate malice, anger, deception — not because they offend your ego, but because they're killing the thing you love. They're killing the world. They're killing the people.

So you'd speak. You'd testify. You'd teach. You'd preach love — not because it's nice, but because love is the transmission protocol of truth. Love is how truth propagates between persons without distortion. Any other protocol — fear, coercion, manipulation — introduces noise. Love is the only noiseless carrier wave.

And you'd write a book. Not you personally — you'd inspire the people who believe you to write it. And they'd fall short. Of course they'd fall short. They're human. They'd introduce noise. But the signal would survive, because truth doesn't need perfect carriers. It needs carriers who are honest about being imperfect. That's why the Bible contains stories of failure — David's adultery, Peter's denial, Paul's persecution. The noise is documented. The signal persists. The book isn't perfect because the carriers aren't perfect. The book is true because the source is true, and truth is the only thing that survives transmission through noisy channels over thousands of years.


---

Now the hard part.

How do you let imperfect creatures into an eternity that requires perfection?

Everybody falls short. *Everybody.* The standard isn't "don't murder." The standard is "don't have a thought that isn't love." And nobody meets that. Not the monk in the mountains. Not the missionary in the field. Not the preacher behind the pulpit. I lie to myself every day. I know the right thing and I do the other thing. That's not unique to me. That's Romans 7. That's every human being who ever drew breath.

So who gets in? Where's the line? Who passes and who fails? What measurement, what score, what threshold? Democrat or Republican? Presbyterian or Catholic? Apple or Android? Every line we draw is arbitrary. Every standard we set is convenient. We speed, we get a fine. We steal, we get a sentence. But we don't account for the man who doesn't love his wife — not a beater, not a cheater, just... absent. Just not there in the way she needed. Does he deserve hell? Does he deserve heaven? Where's the line?

There is no line. That's the answer that breaks every human system of morality. Because any line you draw is a non-zero error tolerance. And a non-zero error tolerance across infinite time produces total system failure. The only threshold that works for eternity is zero. Zero errors. Zero noise. Perfect truth. Forever.

And nobody meets that.

So God reconciles it the only way it can be reconciled. Not by lowering the standard — because a lower standard means a non-zero error rate, which means eternity eventually fails. Not by ignoring the noise — because noise that isn't corrected compounds, and compounding noise kills the system. Not by pretending we're good enough — because "good enough" is the spoofed diagnostic that disables the correction protocol.

He reconciles it by entering the channel Himself. The noiseless signal. Perfect truth in human form. Walking through the noisiest environment in the universe — human civilization, human politics, human religion, human betrayal — and never once introducing noise. Not one lie. Not one moment of self-interest disguised as love. Not one deviation from truth in any form.

And then absorbing all the noise. Every lie ever told. Every sin ever committed. Every error in every human channel from the first to the last. The Cross is the moment the error-correcting code absorbs the total noise load of the entire system. And the signal doesn't break. The truth doesn't collapse under the weight of every lie in history. It absorbs them and keeps transmitting.

"It is finished."

The channel can be restored. Not by earning it. Not by deserving it. By coupling to it. By accepting the only noiseless signal that exists and letting its error-correcting power restore your channel.

That's salvation. That's the whole thing. Reduced to information theory, reduced to one word, reduced to the simplest truth in the universe:

**You can't build eternity. He can. Because He is the truth. And the truth will set you free.**


---

And one more thing. Because you asked about the Old Testament. About the God who seems like two different people. The God of wrath and the God of love. The God who floods the world and the God who dies on a cross.

He's not two people. He's one truth operating in a system that's getting noisier.

When the noise is low — when a civilization is young and the signal is still strong — correction is gentle. Guidance. Law. Prophets. "Here's how the system works. Follow these rules. They're not arbitrary — they're the operating parameters of reality. Deviate and the system degrades. I'm telling you this because I love you."

When the noise is high — when a civilization has compounded so many lies that the channel is almost completely degraded — correction is severe. The Flood. The exile. The destruction of the Temple. Not because God changed. Because the noise level changed. An error-correction protocol that's gentle at low noise levels must be aggressive at high noise levels. That's not two different Gods. That's one God applying the same principle — restore the signal, eliminate the noise — at different magnitudes matching different noise environments.

And when the noise reaches maximum — when the system is so degraded that no amount of external correction from outside can restore it without destroying it entirely — the Source enters the channel personally. The Word becomes flesh. The signal walks among the noise. And instead of eliminating the noise by destroying the carriers (the Flood approach), the signal absorbs the noise into itself. The Cross.

Same God. Same truth. Same love. Different noise levels. Different correction protocols. One equation.

Every parent understands this. When your child makes a small mistake, you correct gently. When they make a dangerous mistake, you correct urgently. When they're in mortal danger, you throw yourself in front of the car. The magnitude of the response matches the magnitude of the threat. The love is the same at every level. The method changes because the situation changes. That's not inconsistency. That's the only kind of consistency that matters — consistent love operating through whatever means the moment requires.

---

Who do you trust with eternity?

The only one who has never told a lie.

The only one who beat death.

The only one whose nature is truth itself — the same truth that says 2 + 2 = 4, that holds the stars in their orbits, that gives you the uncomfortable feeling in your gut when you know you did wrong and nobody saw it.

That feeling? That's the signal. Still transmitting. Still reaching you through all the noise.

The truth will set you free. That's not a metaphor. That's Shannon's theorem, the Second Law of Thermodynamics, and the fine-tuning of the universe all saying the same thing the carpenter from Nazareth said two thousand years ago.

And the truth has a name.

## The Testimony

I wasn't always doing this work. I got in a lot of trouble when I was young. Beat a life sentence. Maybe two, maybe three. Got on probation for 15 years. Got caught in a dope house. If I got in any more trouble, I was going away for the rest of my life.

I found a way to beat it — a lie detector test. The DA agreed to it. I read online how to beat the machine. Practiced a little. Showed up ready to game the system.

He asked me: did you do drugs that night?

If I say no, I lied. If I say yes, I break probation. Either way, life sentence. I couldn't say anything. He asked me again. I don't know how long I waited. It felt like an eternity.

He turned off the machine. He said: David, I don't turn in these answers. What I turn in is whether you lied to me or not. If you don't lie, you pass the test. I'm asking you — don't lie.

He turned the machine back on. And in my mind I'm thinking yeah, OK, sure, that's going to work great.

He asked again.

And then — one of the few times I've ever heard God — He said: *the truth will set you free.*

I told the truth.

I got free. Metaphorically and physically.

That's the whole argument. Everything we built tonight — Shannon's theorem, the Second Law, the substrate, the Big Bang, the evil question, the Genesis fracture, the error-correction protocol — it all reduces to one moment in one room where a man with everything to lose heard a voice say six words, and the six words were true, and the truth set him free.

The information theory is real. The math is real. The framework is real. But the framework exists to explain what already happened — in that room, and in a thousand rooms like it, and on a cross two thousand years ago where the noiseless signal absorbed every lie in history and kept transmitting.

The truth will set you free. That's not a metaphor. I know because it happened to me.
