## How to read this

Every atheist, Muslim, or skeptic who steps to a mic in a TikTok debate room is running one of these twenty moves. That's it. Twenty. Some dress it up, some combine two, but the atomic unit is always one of these. Know all twenty cold, and nobody surprises you.

## THE TWENTY

### MOVE 1 — "A loving God wouldn't send people to hell"

**Frequency:** #1 overall — 115 theme hits, 45 keyword hits across all transcripts **What they actually say:** "Infinite punishment for finite sin." "A God who tortures people forever isn't loving." "Hell is God's concentration camp." **Category:** Doctrinal attack — they're attacking what you BELIEVE, not what the Bible SAYS **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 — was missing from the original 10 **Needs:** Full response card. This is the #1 gap.

### MOVE 2 — "The Bible endorses slavery"

**Frequency:** 91 theme hits, 33 keyword hits **What they actually say:** "Exodus 21 tells you how to beat your slaves." "God gave rules FOR slavery instead of abolishing it." "Leviticus 25:44." **Category:** OT Morality — they're reading ancient legal codes through modern ethics **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #2 — "Name one other Bronze Age legal code that commanded 'you shall not return an escaped slave.' I'll wait." **Needs:** Deeper card with Deut 23:15, 1 Tim 1:10, Philemon context, ebed vs chattel distinction

### MOVE 3 — "Science disproves God"

**Frequency:** 81 theme hits, 20 keyword hits (evolution, big bang, young earth) **What they actually say:** "Evolution is a fact, Genesis is a fairy tale." "The earth is 4.5 billion years old." "No educated person believes in creation." **Category:** Science vs Faith — usually conflates young-earth creationism with all Christianity **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #7 — "When you say 'the sun rose this morning,' are you endorsing geocentrism?" **Needs:** Stronger card. The kill is about literary genre, but the move is about AUTHORITY — who gets to define reality. Need the Lemaitre card (Big Bang inventor = Catholic priest), fine-tuning constants, and the category error (science answers HOW, not WHY).

### MOVE 4 — "There's no evidence God exists"

**Frequency:** 70 HUD-classified hits + pervasive in OTHER **What they actually say:** "Show me one piece of evidence." "The burden of proof is on you." "Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence." **Category:** Epistemological — they're defining what counts as "evidence" to exclude everything you have **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. Key move: flip the burden. What evidence would you accept? If no answer = unfalsifiable skepticism, which is itself a faith position.

### MOVE 5 — "Jesus didn't rise from the dead"

**Frequency:** 62 theme hits, 30 keyword hits **What they actually say:** "Dead people don't come back." "The disciples hallucinated." "The tomb story was added later." **Category:** Historical — this is the load-bearing wall. If resurrection falls, everything falls (Paul said it first — 1 Cor 15:14). **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. Minimal facts argument (Habermas/Licona). The 5 facts even skeptical historians grant. The die-for-a-lie problem. The James conversion problem. The Saul→Paul problem. The empty tomb + enemy attestation.

### MOVE 6 — "The Bible contradicts itself"

**Frequency:** 49 theme hits, 18 keyword hits, 115 HUD rows in SCRIBE category **What they actually say:** "How did Judas die — hanging or falling?" "Two different creation accounts." "The genealogies don't match." **Category:** Internal consistency — usually the weakest attack (copyist variants, literary genre confusion) **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #6 — "The best 'contradiction' you've got is whether Ahaziah was 22 or 42." **Needs:** Ready. Could use 3-4 more examples of the strongest "contradictions" pre-answered.

### MOVE 7 — "Why does God allow suffering?"

**Frequency:** 50+ keyword hits (evil, suffer, pain, innocent) + 190 HUD classified (though many are misclassified) **What they actually say:** "Children get cancer." "Where was God during the Holocaust?" "If God is all-powerful and all-good, evil shouldn't exist." **Category:** The Problem of Evil — the oldest philosophical objection, and the most emotionally loaded **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #9 (partial) — sovereignty + agency framing **Needs:** Separate from Kill #9. The suffering question deserves its own card because the EMOTIONAL register is different. Kill #9 answers the logic. This move needs the pastoral answer too.

### MOVE 8 — "Jesus is a myth / never existed"

**Frequency:** 36 theme hits, 25 keyword hits (historical, myth, pagan) **What they actually say:** "No contemporary sources mention Jesus." "He's a copy of Horus/Mithra/Dionysus." "Christianity is recycled paganism." **Category:** Historical — mythicist position (fringe in actual scholarship but huge on TikTok) **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny the Younger. The scholarly consensus card — even Bart Ehrman (skeptic) says mythicism is nonsense. The copycat myth debunk (Horus parallel claims are fabricated).

### MOVE 9 — "God commanded genocide"

**Frequency:** 35 theme hits, 34 keyword hits (kill, genocide) **What they actually say:** "The Canaanites." "1 Samuel 15 — kill the women and children." "Your God is worse than Hitler." **Category:** OT Morality — the hardest of the OT objections **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #1 — the "pick a lane" argument (text reports genocide AND reports it didn't fully happen) **Needs:** Deeper card. Archaeological evidence that Canaanite "destruction" language is ancient warfare hyperbole (common ANE literary convention). Jericho = military outpost, not civilian city. Plus the moral context: Canaanite child sacrifice (Molech worship) that prompted the judgment.

### MOVE 10 — "Prayer doesn't work"

**Frequency:** 32 HUD + 63 keyword hits (many are Christian prayer content, not attacks — estimate ~30 actual attacks) **What they actually say:** "I prayed and nothing happened." "Studies show prayer has no effect." "God has a plan anyway so why pray?" **Category:** Experiential — this is personal, not philosophical **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. The vending machine fallacy. Prayer as alignment, not wish fulfillment. The Gethsemane model — Jesus prayed and the answer was no. That's not failure; that's relationship.

### MOVE 11 — "The Bible is sexist"

**Frequency:** 26 theme hits, 47 keyword hits (women, patriar, misogyn) **What they actually say:** "Women should be silent in church." "Wives submit to your husbands." "Women are property in the OT." **Category:** Social ethics — reads ancient honor/shame culture through modern egalitarian lens **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #3 — Ruth, Deborah, midwives defying Pharaoh **Needs:** Add: first witnesses to resurrection = women (in a culture where women couldn't testify in court). If you're inventing a religion, you don't make women your star witnesses. That's either true or the worst propaganda ever written.

### MOVE 12 — "Your God is evil"

**Frequency:** 24 theme hits + significant overlap with moves 1, 7, 9 **What they actually say:** "The God of the OT is a monster." "He's jealous, wrathful, petty." "I wouldn't worship him even if he existed." **Category:** Character attack on God — the umbrella objection **TEN_KILLS status:** ✅ Kill #5 (jealousy), Kill #9 (moral worse than Satan) **Needs:** These kills are solid. Add: "By what standard are you judging God evil? If morality is objective, where does it come from? If it's subjective, you're just stating a preference."

### MOVE 13 — "I used to believe and I left"

**Frequency:** 23 HUD classified, 9 keyword hits (deconstr, used to believe) **What they actually say:** "I was a devout Christian for 20 years." "I know the Bible better than you." "I deconstructed and I'm happier." **Category:** Testimony — the hardest to argue against because it's personal experience **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. This ISN'T an argument — it's a story. You don't counter a story with logic. You counter it with a better question: "What did you think you were believing?" Most deconversions are deconversions from a caricature, not from Christ.

### MOVE 14 — "Christians are hypocrites"

**Frequency:** 22 HUD classified **What they actually say:** "Pedophile priests." "Mega-church pastors." "The church covered it up." "Christians are the worst people I know." **Category:** Institutional — attacking the church, not the faith **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. "You're right. And?" Christianity predicts its own adherents will fail (Romans 3:23). The existence of counterfeit money proves the existence of real money. Judge the claim, not the claimant.

### MOVE 15 — "You don't need God to be moral"

**Frequency:** 19 theme hits, 60+ keyword hits (moral, ethic) **What they actually say:** "Atheists are just as moral as Christians." "Secular humanism." "Morality evolved." "The Euthyphro dilemma." **Category:** Moral philosophy — this is actually the strongest atheist position and the weakest Christian response in most TikTok debates **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. The question isn't whether atheists CAN be moral — obviously they can. The question is whether morality has any foundation without a transcendent standard. If morality is just evolved preference, then "murder is wrong" has no more authority than "I prefer chocolate." You're smuggling in objective morality while denying its source.

### MOVE 16 — "Islam is the truth, not Christianity"

**Frequency:** ~70 keyword hits (islam, muslim, quran, muhammad) — NOT an atheist move, but huge in TikTok rooms **What they actually say:** "The Quran is perfectly preserved." "Jesus was a prophet, not God." "The Trinity is polytheism." "Muhammad is the final prophet." **Category:** Inter-faith — different opponent type entirely **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. This is a whole different fight. Key angles: the textual criticism argument (Quran has variants too — Sana'a manuscripts), the crucifixion historicity (Muslim denial vs. universal historical consensus), the character comparison (Muhammad's actions vs. Jesus's), the prophecy question. NOTE: Handle with respect — these are sincere believers, not trolls.

### MOVE 17 — "Religion is just psychology"

**Frequency:** 14 HUD, 15 keyword hits (psycholog, delusion, brainwash) **What they actually say:** "Religion is a coping mechanism." "God is a cosmic security blanket." "You were indoctrinated." "It's just wish fulfillment." **Category:** Reductive — explains religion AWAY rather than engaging its claims **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. The genetic fallacy: explaining how someone came to believe X says nothing about whether X is true. I can explain psychologically why you believe math works — doesn't make 2+2≠4. Also: if belief in God is wish fulfillment, why does God demand so much that humans DON'T wish for?

### MOVE 18 — "God hates gay people"

**Frequency:** ~17 keyword hits (gay, lgbtq, homosexual, transgender) **What they actually say:** "Leviticus says kill gay people." "The church causes suicide." "You can't love someone and condemn who they are." **Category:** Identity/ethics — the most emotionally charged move on the board **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. This requires extreme care. The pastoral response and the apologetic response are DIFFERENT. Apologetically: the text distinguishes between desire and action, all humans have disordered desires (Rom 3:23), and the church's historical cruelty toward LGBTQ people is a sin the church needs to own. The person in front of you matters more than winning the argument.

### MOVE 19 — "All religions basically teach the same thing"

**Frequency:** 8 HUD, 12 keyword hits (other religion, only way, exclusive) **What they actually say:** "Many paths to the same mountain." "Who are you to say yours is right?" "Religious exclusivity is arrogant." **Category:** Pluralism — sounds tolerant but is actually a truth claim itself **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. "All religions teach the same thing" is only said by people who haven't studied any of them. Buddhism: no God. Islam: one God, Jesus is not divine. Hinduism: many gods. Christianity: one God, three persons, Jesus IS God. These are mutually exclusive claims. They CANNOT all be true. Saying they're all the same is the most disrespectful possible position — it says none of them matter enough to take seriously.

### MOVE 20 — "Free will can't exist if God knows everything"

**Frequency:** 5 HUD + 13 keyword hits (free will, predestination, omniscien) — low frequency but HIGH SOPHISTICATION **What they actually say:** "If God knows what I'll choose, I can't choose otherwise." "Predestination means we're puppets." "Omniscience and free will are logically incompatible." **Category:** Philosophical — this one actually requires careful thought **TEN_KILLS status:** ❌ Not in v1 **Needs:** Full card. Foreknowledge ≠ causation. I can know the sun will rise tomorrow without causing it. Also: the Theophysics framework addresses this directly — free will (W) is a variable in the Master Equation, and the mathematical structure requires it to be a genuine degree of freedom for the equation to work. God's knowledge is not a constraint on the system; it's a property of the observer outside the system.
