# The Root Assumption

## The Claim

Every atheist objection ultimately reduces to one assumption: **"I could do better than God."**

Three surface roots, one trunk:
1. **Church hurt** — "A Christian hurt me, therefore God is wrong"
2. **The hypocrite problem** — "Christians don't live it, therefore it's not real"
3. **The baby problem** — "I can't understand why God would let X happen"

All three say the same thing: "I expected God to perform better and He didn't." Which requires omniscience to assert. And they don't notice.

## The Garden Connection

"I could do better than God" = Genesis 3 verbatim. The serpent's pitch was "you will be like God, knowing good and evil." The atheist's sophisticated philosophical argument is the oldest recorded human mistake, word for word.

## The Reductio (Grand Canyon Move — Battle-Tested)

"OK, nothing can happen to a baby. Parent throws their kid off the Grand Canyon — flies right back up. Can't get run over. Can't get hurt."

Keep going with logical extremes until the room goes quiet. They go quiet not because they're convinced but because the reductio exposes they haven't thought it through.

**Terrain check:** Works in groups (audience watches them fail to answer). Backfires with a private person in genuine pain. Different conversations need different moves.

## The Convergence Principle

Every alternative to God, when followed carefully, describes God:
- "I believe in science" → can't ground its own axioms, can't derive ought from is
- "I believe in morality without God" → where does the standard come from?
- "I believe in nothing" → then why are you in this room arguing?

## The Inversion Pattern

All 11 hell attacks, all 10 atheist opening moves on contradictions — they're ALL the same measurement of God's love viewed from the flip side:
- Freedom to reject = "dictatorship"
- Consequences for rejection = "cruelty"
- Justice alongside mercy = "contradiction"

The debate strategy: name the inversion, then flip it back.

## The Scientism Reversal (Three Arguments)

1. **Self-refutation:** "Prove that claim using science." Logical positivism was abandoned by philosophers in the 1950s. Their epistemology is dead software.
2. **Is/ought gap:** Every moral claim they make has already stepped outside science. They left their own framework.
3. **Falsifiability flip:** Christianity makes MORE falsifiable predictions than materialism — named witnesses, specific dates, 300+ advance predictions. Materialism explains everything post-hoc and therefore explains nothing.

## The Measuring Stick

The moral vocabulary they're using to judge the Bible came FROM the Bible's cultural influence. Google Ngram data: virtue -74%, honor -78%, trauma +2,400% over the century Christian cultural saturation collapsed.

They borrowed their ruler from the carpenter's shop, then tried to demolish the shop.
