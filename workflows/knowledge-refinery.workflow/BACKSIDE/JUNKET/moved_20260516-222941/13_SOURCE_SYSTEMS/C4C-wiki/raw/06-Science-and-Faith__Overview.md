# Science and Faith

## The Question
Are scientific findings compatible with Christianity, and does science provide evidence for or against theistic belief?

## Why This Matters
The perceived conflict between science and religion is one of the primary reasons people leave or reject faith. If Christianity requires anti-scientific commitments, that's a serious problem. If science actually points toward features of reality best explained by theism, that changes the landscape entirely.

## Strongest FOR Argument
The Big Bang implies a cosmic beginning consistent with creation ex nihilo, the fine-tuning of physical constants demands explanation beyond blind chance, and the informational content of DNA suggests specified complexity — none of which naturalism explains without invoking entities (multiverses, eternal inflation) that are themselves untestable.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
God-of-the-gaps reasoning: every time science hasn't yet explained something, inserting God as the explanation has historically been a losing strategy. Evolution provides a sufficient mechanism for biological complexity, neuroscience is progressively explaining consciousness, and methodological naturalism works precisely because it doesn't invoke supernatural causes.
