# Christian Ethics

## The Question
Does Christianity provide a coherent and defensible moral framework, and has it been a net positive for human civilization?

## Why This Matters
Christianity claims objective moral grounding in the character of God. If this grounding holds, it solves the "is-ought" problem that plagues secular ethics. If it doesn't, or if Christianity's track record is morally worse than secular alternatives, the claim rings hollow.

## Strongest FOR Argument
The concept of universal human dignity — that every person bears inherent worth regardless of status, ability, or utility — is historically a Christian contribution. The abolition movement, the hospital system, universal education, and the framework of human rights all have deep Christian roots. Without a transcendent grounding for moral obligations, ethics reduces to social convention or individual preference.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
Secular moral systems (utilitarianism, Kantian ethics, contractualism, secular humanism) function perfectly well without God. Meanwhile, Christianity's historical record includes the Crusades, the Inquisition, colonialism justified by theology, defense of slavery, and ongoing institutional abuse scandals. Moral progress has often happened *despite* the church, not because of it.
