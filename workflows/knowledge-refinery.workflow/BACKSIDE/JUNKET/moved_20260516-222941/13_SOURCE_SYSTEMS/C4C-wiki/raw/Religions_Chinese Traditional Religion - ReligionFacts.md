**Chinese religion** is not an organized, unified system of beliefs and practices. It has no leadership, headquarters, founder, or denominations. Instead, "Chinese religion" is a term describing the complex interaction of different religious and philosophical traditions that have been influential in [China](https://religionfacts.com/china).

Chinese religion is composed of four main traditions: Chinese folk religion, [Confucianism](https://religionfacts.com/confucianism), [Taoism](https://religionfacts.com/taoism) and [Buddhism](https://religionfacts.com/buddhism). The religious outlook of most Chinese people consists of some combination of beliefs and practices from these four traditions. It is rare for only one to be practiced to the exclusion of the others. This section focuses on Chinese folk or indigenous religion, but reference is also made to the other traditions.
