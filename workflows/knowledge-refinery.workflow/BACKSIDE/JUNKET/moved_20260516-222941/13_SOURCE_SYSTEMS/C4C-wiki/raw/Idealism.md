# Idealism

> [[00_Canonical/CANONICAL_INDEX|Canonical Index]] | [[00_Canonical/MASTER_INDEX|Master Index]]

Idealism in philosophy, also known as philosophical idealism or metaphysical idealism, is the set of metaphysical perspectives asserting that, most fundamentally, reality is equivalent to mind, spirit, or consciousness; that reality or truth is entirely a mental construct; or that ideas are the highest type of reality or have the greatest claim to being considered "real".

> [!ax]- consciousness as fundamental reality
> **EXPLICIT** · Reality is most fundamentally equivalent to mind, spirit, or consciousness — the mental is ontologically primary
> **→** "most fundamentally, reality is equivalent to mind, spirit, or consciousness" — C is the sole fundamental variable. M, E, T are derived from or dependent on C. **Theophysics: agrees that C is fundamental but rejects that C is the ONLY fundamental. The Master Equation has 10 co-fundamental variables. Idealism collapses 9 of them into C. That's the inverse error of materialism — one makes M sole, the other makes C sole. Both are ontological compressions that fail.**
> **χ**: C (sole fundamental); M, E, T = derived

> [!ax-pre]- mental monism
> **PRESUPPOSES** · There is only one type of fundamental substance and it is mental — this is a form of metaphysical monism
> **→** Ontological idealism is monist — one substance (mind). This rejects both dualism (two substances) and materialism (matter as sole substance). **Theophysics: the Master Equation is neither monist nor dualist. It's a 10-variable integration where each variable is irreducible. The multiplication means if ANY goes to zero, χ collapses. That's structural pluralism, not monism.**
> **χ**: C only (monist compression)

> [!ax-rej]- materialism
> **REJECTS** · Matter exists independently of mind — the physical world has autonomous existence
> **→** "entails a rejection of materialism (or physicalism) as well as the rejection of the mind-independent existence of matter" — Idealism and materialism are exact inversions. **Theophysics rejects both inversions and holds C and M as co-fundamental.**
> **Contra**: Materialism, Physicalism, Naturalism

> [!ax-rej]- dualism
> **REJECTS** · Mind and matter are two distinct substances that both independently exist
> **→** "also entails a rejection of dualism" — Idealism's monism rules out Cartesian dualism. **Theophysics is neither dualist nor idealist monist — the 10-variable product integration is its own category.**
> **Contra**: Cartesian Dualism, Property Dualism

> [!ax-pre]- epistemic limitation
> **PRESUPPOSES** · We cannot know the existence of anything independent of mind — all knowledge is mental
> **→** "a rejection of the possibility of knowing the existence of any thing independent of mind" — This makes K (knowledge) dependent on C (consciousness) with no independent access to M (matter). **Theophysics: K couples to both C and M through the Master Equation. Knowledge IS observer-dependent (Q5 boundary) but the thing known is real, not mental projection.**
> **χ**: K→C only (no K→M path)

> [!ax]- Platonic forms as highest reality
> **EXPLICIT** · Ideal forms — abstract, perfect, unchanging — are more real than physical instances that participate in them
> **→** Plato: forms "exists-by-itself" (auto kath' auto), independently of physical instances. **Theophysics parallel: the axiom structure (188 nodes) exists as logical necessity independent of physical instantiation. But Theophysics doesn't make forms the ONLY reality — it makes them the structural skeleton that physical reality instantiates. Both layers are real.**
> **χ**: K (forms as information structure)

> [!ax-pre]- nous as cosmic ordering principle
> **PRESUPPOSES** · Mind or intelligence (nous) arranges and causes all things in the universe
> **→** Anaxagoras: "it is intelligence [nous] that arranges and causes all things" — This is structurally identical to the Logos field (Λ) in Theophysics. **The difference: Idealism makes nous the ONLY substance. Theophysics makes the Logos the ordering principle that operates THROUGH physical substrates, not instead of them.**
> **χ**: G, K (Logos parallel but not identical)

> [!ax]- Parmenides identity of thinking and being
> **EXPLICIT** · Thinking and being are the same — "For apprehending and being are the same"
> **→** Parmenides DK 28B3. **Theophysics /PROBE: this is either profound or trivially true depending on interpretation. If it means "consciousness is constitutive of reality" — that's the observer-collapse axiom (A5.1). If it means "only thought exists" — that's solipsism, which is self-refuting (who observes the thinker?). The Theophysics version: consciousness and physical reality are co-constitutive through χ.**
> **χ**: C, M (co-constitutive, not identical)

> [!ax]- Plotinus being equals intellect
> **EXPLICIT** · Being and Intellect are one nature — the first true Western idealism
> **→** Plotinus: "Being and Intellect are therefore one nature" (Enneads V.9.8). **Theophysics: this is close to the information-primacy axiom (A2.1) where reality IS structured information. But Plotinus still makes the One (to Hen) the source — which maps to G (Grace/God) as the ground. Neoplatonism is actually closer to Theophysics than standard idealism because it has an emanation hierarchy, not flat consciousness-monism.**
> **χ**: G, K, C (emanation structure)

> [!ax-pre]- hierarchical emanation from the One
> **PRESUPPOSES** · Everything derives from a single first principle (the One/the Good) through hierarchical procession
> **→** Neoplatonist: "the first cause or principle is the Idea of the Good, i.e. The One, from which everything is derived a hierarchical procession (proodos)" — **Theophysics structural isomorphism: this IS the Trinity architecture (Father=Potential, Son=Structure, Spirit=Actualization) flowing from the ground.
