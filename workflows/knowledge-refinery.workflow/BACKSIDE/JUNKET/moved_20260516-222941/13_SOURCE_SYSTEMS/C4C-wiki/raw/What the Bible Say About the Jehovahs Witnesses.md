A Brief History
In 1870, Charles Taze Russell formed a religious
group in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. He disputed many
beliefs of mainstream Christianity, including the
immortality of the soul, hell, the fleshly return of
Christ, the doctrine of the Trinity, and the eventual
dissolution of the world. In 1876, Russell met Nelson
Barbour and, together, they produced a book titled
Three Worlds on the subject of Dispensationalism.
They alleged that Jesus had returned as an invisible
spirit being in 1874. They further claimed that the
year 1914 would mark the end of a 2,520-year period
called “the Gentile Times,” and that God’s kingdom
on Earth would be established.
Within three years (1879), Russell and Barbour
separated from each other over doctrinal differences.
Russell began publishing Zion’s Watch Tower and
Herald of Christ’s Presence. The stated purpose for
these publications was to demonstrate that the world
was in “the last days” and that a new age of earthly
and human restitution under the reign of Christ was
imminent. The Zion’s Watch Tower Tract Society was
formed in 1881 to distribute religious tracts.
By 1879-1880, supporters and followers formed
30 congregations to promote Watch Tower teaching.
