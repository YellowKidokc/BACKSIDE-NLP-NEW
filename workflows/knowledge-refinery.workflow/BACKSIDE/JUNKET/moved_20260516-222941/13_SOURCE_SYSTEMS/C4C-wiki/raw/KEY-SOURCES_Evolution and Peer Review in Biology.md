Yes — there are thousands of peer-reviewed scientific papers about evolution. Evolutionary biology is one of the most heavily studied areas in modern biology, and it has dedicated journals, experiments, and mathematical models.
Below are examples of real peer-reviewed papers and journals so you can see what exists.
Examples of Peer-Reviewed Evolution Papers
1. Natural Selection in Humans
Paper: Natural selection in a contemporary human population
Journal: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)
Year: 2010
What it studied:
Whether natural selection can still be measured in humans today.
Researchers analyzed genetic and reproductive data from thousands of people.
Conclusion:
They found measurable selection pressures affecting traits like reproduction and survival. 
2. Evolution of Darwin’s Finches
Paper: Darwin's Galapagos finches in modern biology
Journal: Biological Reviews
What it studied:
Genetic changes responsible for the different beak shapes in finches.
Conclusion:
Demonstrated how natural selection drives diversification within species. 
3. Natural Selection Theory Review
Paper: Darwinian natural selection: its enduring explanatory power
What it studied:
A review of the evidence for natural selection since Darwin.
Conclusion:
Modern genetics, fossil records, and molecular biology have strengthened evolutionary theory since the 19th century. 
4. Modern Evolutionary Models
Paper: Natural selection and evolution: evolving concepts
Year: 2024
What it studied:
Updated evolutionary models based on molecular genetics and DNA change.
Conclusion:
Evolutionary theory now integrates genetic mutation, epigenetics, and population dynamics.

Important Point (Often Confusing)
A peer-reviewed evolution paper usually does NOT try to prove evolution from scratch.
Instead, they study specific mechanisms, like:
mutation rates
gene selection
fossil transitions
population genetics
speciation events
virus evolution
genetic adaptation
Scientists treat evolution as a framework, so most papers test parts of the mechanism, not the entire idea.
