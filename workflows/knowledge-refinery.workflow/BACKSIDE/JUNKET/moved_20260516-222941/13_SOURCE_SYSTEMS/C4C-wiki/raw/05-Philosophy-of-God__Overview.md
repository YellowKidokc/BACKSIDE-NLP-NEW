# Philosophy of God

## The Question
Do philosophical arguments support the existence of God, and specifically the kind of God that Christianity describes?

## Why This Matters
Historical evidence alone doesn't establish theism. Philosophy addresses whether the concept of God is coherent, whether God's existence is the best explanation for features of reality, and whether the major objections (evil, hiddenness) are decisive.

## Key Sub-Questions
- Does the universe require a cause? (Cosmological arguments)
- Does fine-tuning point to design? (Teleological arguments)
- Does objective morality require God? (Moral argument)
- Is consciousness best explained by theism? (Argument from consciousness)
- Does the problem of evil refute God? (Logical vs. evidential forms)
- Why doesn't God make himself more obvious? (Divine hiddenness)

## Strongest FOR Argument
The Kalam cosmological argument combined with fine-tuning: the universe began to exist, whatever begins to exist has a cause, and the physical constants are fine-tuned to a degree that demands explanation beyond chance or physical necessity.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
The evidential problem of evil: the sheer quantity and distribution of suffering in the world — particularly the suffering of children and animals — is better explained by the absence of God than by any theodicy.
