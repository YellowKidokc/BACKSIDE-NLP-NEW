# Textual Criticism

## The Question
How reliably has the New Testament text been transmitted across 2,000 years of manuscript copying?

## Why This Matters
This is the technical discipline underneath the reliability question. If the text we have today is substantially different from what was originally written, all historical arguments built on it are compromised.

## Strongest FOR Argument
Over 5,800 Greek manuscripts, 10,000+ Latin manuscripts, and thousands in other languages allow scholars to reconstruct the original text with a high degree of confidence. The earliest fragments (P52, c. 125 AD) and early papyri (P45, P46, P66, P75) show remarkable consistency with later manuscripts. No core Christian doctrine depends on a textually disputed passage.

## Strongest AGAINST Argument
There are more textual variants than words in the New Testament (approximately 400,000 variants across the manuscript tradition). Some variants affect doctrinally significant passages (the Comma Johanneum, the longer ending of Mark, the Pericope Adulterae). We don't have the originals, so "reconstruction" is always an inference. Scribal changes were sometimes theologically motivated.
