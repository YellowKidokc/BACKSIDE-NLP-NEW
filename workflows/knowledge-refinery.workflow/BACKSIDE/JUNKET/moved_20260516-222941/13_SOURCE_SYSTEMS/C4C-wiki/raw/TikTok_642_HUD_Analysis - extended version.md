## Ancient Near Eastern Comparison (the "Bronze-Age" killshot)

### The Slavery Killshot (Ch. 2)

Hammurabi §16: **Death** for harboring a runaway slave. Deut 23:15-16: **Command to shelter** runaway slaves, forbid returning them.

180° moral inversion on the most-attacked topic. Exod 21:20 making slave-killing a capital crime is also unique — Hammurabi allows masters to kill slaves freely.

### The Genocide Killshot (Ch. 1)

**Merneptah Stele (~1208 BC):** Pharaoh writes "Israel is laid waste, its seed is no more." Israel obviously survived. This is peer-era evidence, ON STONE, that "total destruction" language in the Bronze Age was standard diplomatic hyperbole, not literal body count.

When an atheist quotes "utterly destroy" from Deut 20 — cite the Merneptah Stele. Single fact. Settles the genre question. Published, in a museum, verifiable.

### Moral Innovations with No Peer-Era Parallel

Gleaning laws (Lev 19, Deut 24), cities of refuge (Num 35), sojourner protection (Exod 22:21), Jubilee (Lev 25), sabbath rest including slaves and animals (Exod 20:10).

1. **Decontextualization** (~40%) — context stripping, proof-texting, selective literalism
2. **Anachronism** (~20%) — chronological snobbery, modern moral intuition applied backwards

4. **False Framing** (~15%) — loaded question, strawman, false dichotomy
