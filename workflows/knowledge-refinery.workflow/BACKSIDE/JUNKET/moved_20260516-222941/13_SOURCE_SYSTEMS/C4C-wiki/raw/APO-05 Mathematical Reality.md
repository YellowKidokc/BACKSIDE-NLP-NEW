# APO-05: Mathematical Reality
## "Math shares 24 properties with God. Name one other thing that does."

---

## The Hook (30 seconds)

Math is immaterial — you can't weigh it. It's universal — 2+2=4 on Mars. It's eternal — it was true before humans existed. It's necessary — it can't NOT be true. It's non-deceptive — it never lies.

Your worldview says only material things are real. So either math isn't real — and every argument you've ever made collapses — or something immaterial IS real, and you owe me an explanation for where it lives.

I've got 24 properties that math and God share. Name one other candidate.

---

## The Case (2-5 minutes)

Here are the 24 properties math and God share. Not metaphor. Structural identity.

**Ontological:** Immaterial, eternal, omnipresent (universal), necessary, self-consistent, unchanging (immutable)

**Epistemological:** True, rational, discoverable (not invented), authoritative, sufficient (complete within domain), non-deceptive

**Relational:** Foundational to reality, sustaining (reality runs on it), ordering (imposes structure), generative (produces new truths), unifying (single coherent system)

**Functional:** Governing (constrains what's possible), judging (sorts valid from invalid), beautiful (elegant structure), simple (irreducible at base), infinite (unbounded), sovereign (not subject to external authority), personal — wait.

Personal. That's where it breaks. Math isn't personal. God is. That's not a weakness in the argument. That's the BOUNDARY. 18 properties are provable from pure algebra. 6 require something algebra can't express — personhood, relationship, mercy, faithfulness, creativity, love.

That boundary IS the definition of faith. Where the formal system ends and free commitment begins.

> "You just used math to argue with me. Math is immaterial, universal, eternal, and necessary. On YOUR worldview, where does it live? Because it's not physical. You can't weigh logic. You can't measure truth. And the universe ran on mathematical structure for 13.8 billion years before any human existed to 'invent' it. So either you believe in something immaterial — welcome to my world — or you just destroyed the foundation of every scientific claim you've ever made."
