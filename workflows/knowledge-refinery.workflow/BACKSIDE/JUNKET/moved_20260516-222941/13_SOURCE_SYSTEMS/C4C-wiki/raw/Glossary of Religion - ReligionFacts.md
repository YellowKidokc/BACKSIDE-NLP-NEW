# Glossary of Religion

A.H.

(Latin _anno hegirae_, "After Hijira"). Western abbreviation for the Islamic dating of years, beginning with Muhammad's flight to Medina in 622 CE.

[Abbasid](https://religionfacts.com/islam/history/abbasid-caliphate)

An Islamic dynasty established in 750, when it replaced the Umayyad caliphate. Originally based in Mesopotamia (the Abbasids founded Baghdad in 762), it remained dominant in the Islamic world until the mid-10th century.

[abhaya mudra](https://religionfacts.com/buddhism/symbols/abhaya-mudra)

Buddhist hand gesture representing fearlessness.

acolytes

(Greek, "follower"). A lay person, usually a child or young adult, who assists ministers in worship services.

acupuncture

Traditional Chinese medical treatment using needles to stimulate the flow of ch'i in the body.

AD

Abbreviation for Latin _anno domini_, "in the year of our Lord."

adar

(also _atar_). Sacred fire in Zoroastrianism.

[adhan](https://religionfacts.com/islam/practices/adhan)

(Arabic أَذَان, romanized Arabic _adhan_, Persian and Turkish _namaz_). In Islam, the call to prayer performed by a muezzin before each of five daily prayers (salat).

adharma

(also _papa_). Evil, sin, or misfortune, including both natural and moral evil.

[Adoptionism](https://religionfacts.com/christianity/beliefs/christology/adoptionism)

Christian doctrine that Jesus of Nazareth became the son of God by exaltation, not at birth.
