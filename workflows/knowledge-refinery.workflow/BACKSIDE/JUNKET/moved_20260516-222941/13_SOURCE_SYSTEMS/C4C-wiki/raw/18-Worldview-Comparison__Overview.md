# Worldview Comparison

## Purpose
This section doesn't follow the FOR/AGAINST structure. It provides systematic comparison frameworks for evaluating Christianity against other worldviews using consistent criteria.

## Notes

- **[[Christianity vs Naturalism]]** — The primary debate in the modern West. Does reality have a purposeful origin or is it brute fact?
- **[[Christianity vs Islam]]** — Two monotheisms with shared roots but radically different claims about Jesus, revelation, and salvation.
- **[[Christianity vs Hinduism]]** — Linear vs. cyclical history, personal vs. impersonal ultimate reality, creation vs. emanation.
- **[[Christianity vs Atheism]]** — Does the absence of God better explain the data than the presence of God?
- **[[Criteria for Evaluating Worldviews]]** — What makes a worldview rationally preferable? Internal coherence, explanatory scope, existential livability, predictive power.

## Evaluation Framework
Each comparison should address:
1. **Explanatory scope** — What does each worldview explain that the other can't?
2. **Internal coherence** — Does each worldview contradict itself?
3. **Correspondence to reality** — Which better fits what we observe?
4. **Existential livability** — Can you actually live consistently within this worldview?
5. **Predictive power** — Does the worldview generate testable predictions?
