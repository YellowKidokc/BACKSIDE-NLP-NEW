# APO-07: Moral Law
## "You can't deny this without proving it."

---

## The Hook (30 seconds)

You have a mechanism inside you that evaluates everything — right or wrong, true or false, better or worse. You can't turn it off. You're using it right now to decide whether what I'm saying is valid.

Try to deny it. "That's wrong" — you just evaluated. "That's not evidence" — you judged. "That's ridiculous" — judgment. There is no response that doesn't prove the claim.

If this evaluation is universal, structural, and inescapable — then reality itself has normative structure. Right and wrong aren't opinions. They're built in. Who built them in?

---

## The Case (2-5 minutes)

### The Evaluation Axis (Self-Referential Proof)

Every conscious agent operates on an internal evaluation axis. Right/wrong. Better/worse. Valid/invalid. True/false. This is:
- **Pre-rational** — it fires before philosophy
- **Pre-cultural** — every culture has it
- **Inescapable** — denial proves it (Descartes structure)
- **Structural** — can't be turned off
- **Universal** — every conscious agent, every domain

The Descartes parallel: "I think therefore I am" — you can't doubt existence without thinking, which proves you exist. Same structure: you can't evaluate the claim without evaluating, which proves evaluation.

### The Bridge to God

If the evaluation axis is universal, structural, pre-rational, and normative (tracks better/worse, not just different) — then reality itself has normative structure. Right and wrong aren't opinions. They're features of reality.

If they're features of reality — who featured them?

### The Evolution Kill

"A firefighter runs into a burning building and dies saving a stranger's child. Evolution says: survive. Pass on your genes. That firefighter's genes just died. Natural selection should have eliminated that impulse millions of years ago. But it didn't. And every culture on earth honors that sacrifice."

Evolution explains survival instincts. It does NOT explain moral evaluation. Recognizing injustice doesn't help you survive — it often gets you killed. People die for moral principles. That's the OPPOSITE of fitness.

If they say "group selection / social cooperation": cooperation explains "help the group survive." It does NOT explain "what that dictator is doing to those people across the ocean is wrong." You have no survival stake in that judgment. Zero. But you make it anyway. Every time.
