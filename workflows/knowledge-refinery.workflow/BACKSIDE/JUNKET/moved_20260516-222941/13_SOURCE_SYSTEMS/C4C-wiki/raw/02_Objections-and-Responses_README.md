## 02-Slavery-Objection-Response.md

**Biblical Slavery: A Comprehensive Response**

**The Major Objection:**
"The Bible permits and regulates slavery. Therefore, the God of the Bible is immoral."

**The Response Framework:**
1. **Definitional distinctions:** Ancient Near Eastern "slavery" ≠ transatlantic slave trade
2. **Legal protections:** Mosaic law granted unprecedented protections to servants
3. **Trajectory view:** Scripture shows movement toward freedom
4. **Cultural context:** Understanding Hebrew social economics
5. **Moral intuition check:** Why does this objection *feel* right even if misdirected?

**Key Points:**
- Hebrew slave law (Exodus 21) included mandatory release, compensation, legal personhood
- Prohibited chattel slavery; indentured servitude for debt relief
- Contrast with ANE equivalents (worse) and American slavery (worse)
- Shows ethical development within Scripture

**Relevance to Theophysics:**
- Demonstrates how divine wisdom works within human cultural constraints
- Relates to "grace working within human freedom" (open-system character)
- Shows historical progression (relates to covenant development)

**Scope:** Focused on Exodus-Leviticus law and responding to the strongest form of objection.
