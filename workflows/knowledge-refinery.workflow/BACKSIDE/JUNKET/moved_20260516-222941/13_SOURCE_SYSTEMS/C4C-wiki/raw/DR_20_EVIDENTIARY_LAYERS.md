To develop an overwhelming positive case—a strategy of "claim stacking" that forces a skeptical interlocutor to account for a vast web of data rather than isolated points—you can deploy these 20 distinct evidentiary layers. These are categorized by the "judgment layer" (logic/philosophy) and the "historical/forensic layer" (empirical data), concluding with the "relationship argument."

### The Philosophical Judgment Layer (Offensive Logic)

1. **The Argument from Change:** The material world is in a constant state of flux. Change requires an outside force to actualize potentiality, leading back to an "Unchanging Source of Change" that transcends matter, space, and time.
    
2. **The Kalam Cosmological Argument:** Logic dictates that everything that begins to exist has a cause. The universe began to exist (supported by the expansion of the universe and the Big Bang). Therefore, the universe has a cause that must be spaceless, timeless, and immaterial.
    
3. **The Fine-Tuning of Physics:** The physical constants of the universe (e.g., gravity, electromagnetism) are precisely calibrated for life to a degree of 1 part in $10^{something}$ infinitesimally small. This implies a "Fine-Tuner" rather than sheer accidental occurrence.
    
4. **The Moral Argument:** If objective moral values exist, God must exist as their foundation. Since even the skeptic relies on objective "oughts" to criticize religion, they inadvertently assume the existence of a moral Lawgiver.
    
5. **The Argument from Consciousness:** We experience a universe that is graspable by intelligence. The existence of a mind capable of rational thought is best explained by a transcendent, rational Source rather than blind, mindless chance.
    
6. **The Judgment Layer Foundation:** Human reason and the ability to distinguish truth from falsehood require a "docking point" in an eternal mind. Without this foundation, the very thoughts we use to argue are just the result of chemical reactions, making them untrustworthy.
    

### The Forensic Layer (Minimal Facts & History)

7. **The Five Minimal Facts:** Over 90% of credentialed scholars (including atheists) grant: (1) Jesus’ death by crucifixion, (2) the disciples' belief in appearances, (3) the conversion of the persecutor Paul, (4) the conversion of the skeptic James, and (5) the early proclamation of these events.
    
8. **The Jerusalem Factor:** Christianity was birthed in Jerusalem, the very city where Jesus was executed. If the body were still in the tomb, the authorities could have produced the corpse and ended the movement instantly.
    
9. **Criterion of Embarrassment (Women):** In the 1st century, women’s testimony was legally invalid. No one inventing a resurrection legend would make women the primary witnesses unless it were an unavoidable historical fact.
    
10. **Enemy Attestation (Theft Claim):** The earliest Jewish response was that "the disciples stole the body." This is an admission from hostile sources that the tomb was indeed empty; they were merely trying to explain why.
    
11. **Undesigned Coincidences:** The Gospels contain hundreds of "puzzle pieces"—incidental details in one book that casually explain a mystery in another. These interlock in ways that rule out collusion and point to genuine eyewitness memory.
    
12. **The 1 Corinthians 15 Creed:** This oral tradition dates to within 2–5 years of the crucifixion. This is "historical gold" because it proves the resurrection claim existed before any legendary development could occur.
    
13. **Bibliographical Superiority:** The New Testament is the best-attested work of antiquity, with over 25,000 manuscripts. By comparison, Homer's _Iliad_ has only 1,757. If the skeptic rejects the NT, they must also reject all other ancient history.
    
14. **Archaeological Vindication:** Discoveries like the Pilate Stone, the Caiaphas Ossuary, and the Pools of Bethesda and Siloam have repeatedly confirmed specific New Testament details once dismissed by skeptics.
    
15. **The Statistical Probability of Prophecy:** Professor Peter Stoner calculated that the odds of one man fulfilling just 8 Messianic prophecies is 1 in $10^{17}$—equivalent to a blindfolded man picking one specific silver dollar out of a pile covering the state of Texas two feet deep.
    
16. **The Dead Sea Scrolls TERMINUS:** The discovery of these scrolls proves that prophecies like Isaiah 53 (the "Suffering Servant") were in writing centuries before Jesus, eliminating the "later addition" objection.
    
17. **Conversion of James:** Siblings are notoriously difficult to convince of one's divinity. James, a pre-crucifixion skeptic, became a "Pillar" of the church and was martyred for believing his brother was God.
    
18. **The Transformation of the Apostles:** Men do not die for a lie they know they fabricated. The original witnesses faced ostracism, torture, and execution with no earthly gain, proving they were convinced of what they had seen.
    

### The Relationship Argument (The Experiential Core)

19. **The Relationship Argument:** If a personal, all-good God exists, His primary goal is relationship. Anyone who truly enjoys an experiential and personal connection with God possesses a certainty that is superior to abstract logic.
    
20. **The Challenge of Choice:** God provides enough evidence for those who want to see Him, but enough "hiddenness" to respect the free will of those who do not. A relationship cannot be forced; it must be chosen.
