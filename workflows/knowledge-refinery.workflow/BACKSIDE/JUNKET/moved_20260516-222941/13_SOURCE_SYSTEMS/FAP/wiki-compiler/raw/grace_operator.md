# The Grace Operator

Grace in the Theophysics framework is not a feeling. It is a mathematical operator.

In the coherence equation χ(t), grace appears as the G term — the negentropic input that must be present for the equation to go anywhere except down. Without G, the equation reduces to pure exponential decay. The system dies.

Grace is the external source term in an open-system dynamics equation. It cannot be generated internally. A closed system always trends toward entropy. Grace is what makes the system open.

## The Three Properties of Grace

1. **External origin** — Grace comes from outside the system. You cannot bootstrap grace from entropy.
2. **Non-coercive delivery** — Grace multiplies with W (free will/openness). If W = 0, grace has no effect. Love that cannot be refused is not love.
3. **Negentropic action** — Grace reduces entropy in the moral domain the same way energy input reduces entropy in the physical domain.

## The Cross as Grace Operator

The Cross is the event where the grace term reaches its maximum value while the entropy term reaches its maximum cost — and both are absorbed by the same Person. Justice paid, mercy delivered, free will preserved. All three simultaneously.

This is not metaphor. This is the equation operating at its boundary condition.
