## LEVEL ONE PAGE TEMPLATE

Every level one page follows this exact structure:

### 1. The Hook
"Hey, you that just came on my website, let me talk to you. My name is David Lowe. I imagine that you stumbled on my website today because you searched for [QUESTION], and as luck would have it, somehow I'm ranked high, and for whatever reason you landed on my page. Let me clarify a thing before we go on. I imagine the likelihood of you being here is because of something on TikTok. Good. I know exactly where you're coming from."

### 2. The Two Paths
"Now we can go about this two ways. I can give you the answer — and I'll tell you how to get the answer out of this website fast every time if you need to come back for anything — but that answer isn't going to get you to win. Or you can stay and learn why the answer works."

### 3. The Fast Answer
The one-liner from the arsenal. Deployable tonight. Screenshot-ready.

### 4. The Reality Check
Two concrete things they need to hear:
- Their posture matters more than their argument. Defending God while acting un-Christian is a loss regardless of the logic.
- Winning takes time. Discipline. Study. There are no cheat codes.

### 5. The Unpack
"Now I'll respect your time. Let me tell you what you have here and let me unpack it before I let you on your way."

Five to six pieces of evidence. Two sentences each. Dense. Quotable. Each one a standalone weapon.

### 6. The SEO Body
The full article. The math. The layered truth structure. The framework connections. This is where the keyword clusters live naturally. FAQ section at the bottom catching long-tail search variants.

### 7. The Hook to Next
"Now you have [QUESTION] handled. I've dissected more channels than anybody I know of. I ran the statistics, put it in Postgres. I've got the data, I've got the answers. From now on, you can skip level one. You can go to level two. But if you want the next question first — glad you made it this far. Next question:"

Poses the next question in the sequence. The reader clicks through to the next page.

## LEVEL TWO PAGE STRUCTURE

### 1. The Data Wall
Postgres statistics. 1,912 videos. 520 classified arguments. 35 channels. 20 attack vectors. Frequency distributions. Channel names. Database schema. Overwhelming. The reader scrolls through raw evidence of systematic work.

### 2. The Reveal
Two-thirds down. Photo or personal element. Direct address.

Amen again. Your faith has brought you this far. Now faith can take you everywhere, but there are some things that push you. Part of this was anger. I couldn't answer such a simple question, and they got me. They got me, and that started a lot of work."

### 3. The Return to Question One
"What is your evidence for God? Now I'm going to systematically lay out several different options. Pick which one feels the most natural to you."

Multiple response options. Each scored with difficulty rating, counterargument resistance, and best-use scenario. The hardest ones are original content found nowhere else on the internet.

### 4. The Note on Difficulty
"Now, as you can tell, the hardest ones are the most likely to win because they are nowhere on the internet. They are originally my content. They are a lot harder to learn. Kudos to you if you say you're sticking with the hard, but know this: you're not going to get it in one night. If you do — you're really smart, kudos, shoot me an email, say hi. I'll say hi back."

### 5. The Rules of the Game
Before they fight, they need to know the rules:

**Rule 1: Know when to walk away.** Do not argue with someone who denies Jesus existed historically. Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny the Younger — secular sources confirm it. If they reject basic scholarship, they are performing, not debating. Leave.

**Rule 2: Establish truth first.** Before arguing about what's true, make them admit truth exists. "There is no truth" is a truth claim. It refutes itself. Name it. Move on.

**Rule 3: Establish morality first.** Ask them if child abuse is wrong. They will say yes. Now they are a moral realist. Now the debate is on your field.

**Rule 4: Test their openness.** Ask if they are open to being wrong. Not to converting. Just to being wrong. If no, they are defending a position, not seeking truth. Do not engage.

**Rule 5: Guard your posture.** Do not get angry. Do not get frustrated. Do not act un-Christian while defending Christianity. The moment you raise your voice, they won. They did not beat your argument. They beat your character. And your character is the argument.

Each download gated behind email capture.
