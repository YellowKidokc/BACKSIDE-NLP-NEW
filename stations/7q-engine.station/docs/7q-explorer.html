0 Q0 — POSTURE BEFORE INQUIRY
Precondition
The inquirer cannot be the ground of inquiry.
Not a question — a posture. Cannot be scored, only acknowledged.
Operational
Arrive open or don't arrive at all.
Every dead framework — positivism, behaviorism, instrumentalism — arrived decided before investigating. Q0 prevents that error mode.
▼ undefined → defined
▲ STEP 7: Name it last — identity earned
1 Q1 — WHAT IS IT? (Identity)
Classification
Name it. Type it. Tier it.
Claim types: causal, descriptive, ontological, mathematical, predictive, mechanistic, normative.
Tier
Where in the hierarchy?
Foundational (axiom, postulate) · Derived (theorem, hypothesis) · Constraint · Support · Connection
GR Example
GR is causal, not descriptive.
Mass-energy causes curvature — a stronger commitment than standard presentation suggests. Reclassified at Q1.
▼ defined → located
▲ STEP 6: Generalize — show it holds across domains
2 Q2 — WHERE DOES IT LIVE? (Domain)
Primary Domain
Anchor in domain and scale.
Physics, theology, consciousness, information theory, mathematics, biology, economics, social science.
Isomorphism Test
Analogy or structural identity?
If the math preserves topology, boundary conditions, and conservation laws under variable substitution → isomorphism, not analogy.
GR Example
Riemannian geometry exists independently.
Appears in ML (manifold learning), economics (curved utility), information theory (Fisher geometry). ISO-confirmed.
▼ located → stated
▲ STEP 5: State — the claim is now a conclusion
3 Q3 — WHAT DOES IT SAY? (Assertion)
Precision
One sentence. No hedging.
Classify: certainty level, scope, mathematical vs verbal. Generate the opponent restatement test.
Negation
Every claim carries its own death warrant.
Auto-generate the logical opposite. If the negation decomposes the claim into independently testable components, you've found bundled claims.
GR Example
Negation splits GR into two claims.
"Mass-energy does NOT curve spacetime, OR objects do NOT follow geodesics." Two independently killable components found.
▼ stated → supported
▲ STEP 4: Support — evidence confirms what survived
4 Q4 — WHAT SUPPORTS IT? (Evidence)
Channel 1: PS
Phenomenon Strength
Reproducibility × 0.4 + Effect size × 0.3 + Measurement quality × 0.3. "What do we reliably observe?"
Channel 2: ED
Explanatory Depth
Mechanism clarity × 0.4 + Constraint consistency × 0.3 + Scope × 0.3. "Do we understand WHY?"
Channel 3: EC
Experiential Coherence
Internal consistency × .25 + Longitudinal stability × .25 + Behavioral transformation × .25 + Intersubjective pattern × .25
▼
Formula
The Completeness Factor
CF = (0.5 + 0.5 × ED) × (0.5 + 0.5 × EC)
E_final = PS × CF
If ED = 0, evidence capped at 50%.
Critical Sub-Q
Could this evidence support a competing model equally?
If yes → evidence doesn't distinguish. It supports a class, not a specific claim. This is information, not failure.

5 Q5 — WHAT DOES IT DEPEND ON? (Dependencies)
Chain Terminus
Where does the chain end?
Axiom (self-evident) · Brute fact (accepted without proof) · Circularity (loops back — FLAGGED) · Open (doesn't terminate — FLAGGED)
Fragility
If this foundation fails, what happens?
Immediate collapse · Graceful degradation · Independent survival. A structural engineering load test applied to ideas.
GR Example
Hidden dependency: smooth manifold.
GR assumes spacetime is continuous. At Planck scale (~10⁻³⁵ m), it might be discrete. This unexamined root has driven unsolved physics for 100 years. Q5 surfaced it in one question.
▼ grounded → generative
▲ STEP 2: Force — extract what the survivor produces
6 Q6 — WHAT DOES IT FORCE? (Consequences)
Predictions
What must be true if this claim is true?
Confirmed vs untested prediction ratio = generative maturity. A claim that forces nothing downstream explains nothing.
Cross-Domain
Where do consequences land in other fields?
If a consequence appears in another domain and preserves structural relationships → isomorphism candidate detected. Loops back to Q2.
GR Example
GR bore fruit its planter didn't foresee.
Black holes (Schwarzschild 1916), gravitational waves (detected 2015), GPS corrections (daily). Einstein didn't predict most of these — the math forced them.
▼ generative → testable
▲ STEP 1: Destroy — show every way it could die
7 Q7 — WHAT KILLS IT? (Falsification)
Death #1
Self-refutation
Destroys itself when stated. The denial presupposes what it denies.
Death #2
Infinite regress
Pushes the question backward without resolving it. Each answer generates a new instance.
Death #3
Empirical contradiction
Contradicts observed data. The traditional mode — necessary but not sufficient alone.
Death #4
Logical incoherence
Contradicts something already established in the chain — a dependency, a consequence, itself.
Death #5
Explanatory failure
Cannot answer forced downstream questions from Q6. Stops generating. Runs out of road.
