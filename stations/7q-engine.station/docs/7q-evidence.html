The 7Q Framework — Evidence Layer

Three Channels.
One Score.
No Shortcuts.

Modern science asks one question about evidence: "Is it repeatable?" That's not enough. Repeatable without explained is incomplete. Explained without measured is untested. Experienced without structured is unverifiable. You need all three — and they multiply, not add.

A claim is only as strong as its evidence — and evidence is only as strong as its explanation.
The Problem
Science Rewards Prediction. It Should Also Reward Understanding.
Pre-1927, the scientists who moved the needle — Newton, Maxwell, Faraday, Einstein, Planck, Lemaître — all pursued explanatory closure. They asked why. Post-1927, instrumentalism correlated with proliferating interpretations, fragmentation, and unresolved foundational disputes. Twenty-plus interpretations of quantum mechanics. No unification in a hundred years. The evidence was strong. The explanations were optional. That produced a science that can predict without understanding.
The fix is not to reject measurement. The fix is to make explanation a first-class scoring channel alongside observation. If you only measure "does it repeat?" you get modern physics. If you also measure "do we understand why?" — you get a filter that rewards the kind of work that actually closes gaps.
The Three Channels
What We Observe. Why It Happens. How It Lives.
Evidence is not one thing. It arrives through three independent channels, and each answers a different question. Scoring them separately prevents the blind spots that come from collapsing everything into "is it significant?"
🌧
Channel 1
Phenomenon Strength (PS)
"What do we reliably observe?" — This is what science already does well. Measurement. Replication. Signal detection. The rain that falls on the tree.
  • Reproducibility× 0.4
  • Effect size× 0.3
  • Measurement quality× 0.3
PS = repro×0.4 + effect×0.3 + quality×0.3
☀️
Channel 2
Explanatory Depth (ED)
"Do we understand WHY?" — This is what science systematically avoids. Every dead framework refused this question. Every breakthrough came from someone who held it. The sunlight that tells the tree which direction to grow.
  • Mechanism clarity× 0.4
  • Constraint consistency× 0.3
  • Scope× 0.3
ED = mechanism×0.4 + constraints×0.3 + scope×0.3
💧
Channel 3
Experiential Coherence (EC)
"Does it produce stable structure in lived systems?" — Not "I feel strongly." This is: does the evidence produce consistent downstream effects across time, behavior, and agents? The soil moisture the roots actually draw from.
  • Internal consistency× 0.25
  • Longitudinal stability× 0.25
  • Behavioral transformation× 0.25
  • Intersubjective pattern× 0.25
EC = consistency×.25 + stability×.25 + transform×.25 + pattern×.25

The Why-Penalty

If ED = 0 — if there is no explanation at all — evidence strength is capped at 50% no matter how repeatable it is. This is the mechanism that enforces the principle: observed ≠ explained. Repeatable ≠ understood.

The penalty is not punishment. It's honesty. A phenomenon that repeats but nobody can explain why is genuinely less useful than one that repeats AND we know why. The score should reflect that. Science that only asks "does it work?" without asking "why does it work?" produces reliable local predictions inside a structurally fragmented landscape. The why-penalty catches that.

The Formula
Three Channels Multiply Through Completeness Gates
The channels don't add. They multiply. This means you can't compensate for zero in one channel by maxing another. A tree with infinite rain but no sunlight dies. A tree with perfect sunlight but no water dies. You need all three present for anything to grow.
Completeness Factor
CF = (0.5 + 0.5 × ED) × (0.5 + 0.5 × EC)
Final Evidence Score
E_final = PS × CF
When ED = 0 and EC = 0, CF = 0.25 — evidence is capped at 25% no matter how strong the phenomenon. When both ED and EC are at 1.0, CF = 1.0 — full credit earned.

The Five Gates
Every Evidence Unit Passes Through Five Gates
No raw evidence enters the system unstructured. Before a piece of evidence can connect to a claim, it must declare what it is, what kind of knowing it represents, how strong each channel is, what it connects to, and what could invalidate it.
E1
Gate
Identity — What IS This Evidence?
Not the claim. The evidence itself. Is it a dataset, a paper, an observation, a proof, a protocol, a prediction result, a testimony, an isomorphism detection?
Fields: id (UUID) · label · evidence_format
E2
Gate
Type — What Kind of Knowing?
Three layers of classification. Epistemic class: empirical, logical, structural, or experiential. Evidence type: experimental, observational, statistical, mathematical, historical, scriptural, inferential, isomorphic. Reliability: replicated, single-study, anecdotal, simulated, inferred.
Fields: epistemic_class · evidence_type · reliability
E3
Gate
Strength — How Strong Is Each Channel?
Score PS, ED, and EC using guided dropdowns. The system computes CF and E_final. No manual judgment needed — the math handles nuance.
Fields: PS components · ED components · EC components → scores auto-computed
E4
Gate
Linkage — What Does It Connect To?
Which claims does this evidence support, contradict, or contextualize? Is the connection direct or inferential? Critical sub-question: could this same evidence equally support a competing explanation?
Fields: supports_claims · contradicts_claims · connection_type · competing_support
E5
Gate
Vulnerabilities — What Could Invalidate This Evidence?
Not the claim — the evidence itself. Selection bias? Confounding variables? Unreproducible conditions? Measurement error? Cherry-picking? Has anyone actually tried to invalidate this evidence?
Fields: vulnerability conditions · severity · adversarial_tested
Non-Negotiable
Seven Rules of Epistemic Honesty
Rule 1
"Observed" ≠ "Explained." High PS with low ED gets capped. Always.
Rule 2
"Repeatable" ≠ "Understood." The system will not let you score high without answering why.
Rule 3
"Evidence" ≠ "Complete evidence." Every unit declares its limits.
Rule 4
"Personal" ≠ "Transferable." Experiential evidence routes through consequences. You measure downstream effects.
Rule 5
"Strong evidence" ≠ "Correct claim." Evidence strength and claim validity are never collapsed into one number.
Rule 6
"Coherent" ≠ "True." A system can be internally coherent and still false.
Rule 7
"Unsupported" ≠ "False." Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence.
The Historical Thesis
Predictive Success Alone Is Insufficient
A theory can be predictively competent and still be explanatorily immature. This protocol catches that by making explanation a first-class channel.
