# Path 1: Autonomy (s = −1)

The neurochemistry of “I got this.”
      At s = −1, the system is fully decoupled from the Logos Field. The coupling function α(s) = 0. Grace is available but unreceived. The equation reduces to pure decay: dC/dt = −S·C.
      Dopamine dominance. The autonomous brain runs on the reward circuit — nucleus accumbens, ventral tegmental area, mesolimbic pathway. Every decision: does this feel good now? The problem is tolerance. Repeated spikes cause receptor downregulation — fewer D2 receptors, requiring more stimulation for the same effect. This is the neurological equivalent of S·C — entropy accelerating over time.
      Prefrontal cortex dims. The PFC — executive control, impulse regulation — progressively loses influence as the reward circuit dominates. Imaging studies of addiction show precisely this. The part of the brain capable of recognizing “this isn’t working” gets quieter. This is Gödel’s incompleteness at the system level. You can’t see the problem from inside the problem.
      Cortisol rises. Chronic stress. The autonomous system is anxious because it knows — at some level below conscious awareness — that it’s decaying. Sleep disrupts. Inflammation increases.
      The spiral. Dopamine tolerance → need more stimulation → PFC suppression → less self-regulation → more impulsive behavior → more tolerance. This is dC/dt = −S·C expressed as neurochemistry. Exponential decay with no internal brake.
      This is what Paul describes in Romans 1:24-28. “God gave them over in the sinful desires of their hearts.” The “giving over” isn’t punishment — it’s the removal of resistance. The system at s = −1 does what the equation says it will do: decay.
