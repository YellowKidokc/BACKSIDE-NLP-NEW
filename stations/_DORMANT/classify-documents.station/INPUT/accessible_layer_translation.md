# Accessible Layer Translation Prompt

Use this prompt for turning a deep Theophysics article into a 9th-grade-accessible layer.

## Goal

Rewrite the article so a smart 9th grader can follow it without losing the original argument.

## Rules

- Stay close to the original article's structure and sequence.
- Do not remove the argument's force.
- Do not turn technical claims into vague inspiration.
- When the original uses one compressed sentence, expand it into a paragraph if needed.
- Use fatherly explanation: patient, direct, clear, not childish.
- Define technical words the first time they matter.
- Use analogies when they clarify the same structure, not when they replace it.
- Preserve kill conditions and falsification language.
- Preserve grace as the final landing, not as decoration.
- Mark any place where the original needs source-checking or tighter proof as `[needs source/proof check]`.

## Voice

Plain, serious, warm, and concrete.

Avoid:

- Academic fog.
- Sermon padding.
- Overclaiming.
- "This is basically saying..." filler.

Prefer:

- "Here is the simple version."
- "That matters because..."
- "The hard part is..."
- "If this is wrong, here is where it breaks."
