# Executive Summary

**One-sentence thesis:**  
Quantum mechanics as currently understood describes a *fallen* physics—post-Fall reality with broken direct coupling to the Logos—while pre-Fall reality was a determinate, coherent substrate that quantum indeterminacy and radiometric clocks did not exist in.

**5-8 executive bullets:**  
- Pre-Fall and post-Fall physics are fundamentally different, not just different perspectives on the same reality.  
- Eden’s substrate was deterministic, directly coupled to God’s Logos, maximally coherent, and timeless in the sense of no irreversible decay.  
- Quantum indeterminacy (superposition, Born Rule, wavefunction collapse, tunneling) is a post-Fall phenomenon; structural quantum features (Pauli exclusion, energy quantization) predate and survive the Fall.  
- The probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics reflects a broken coupling between knower and known, not fundamental randomness in creation.  
- Radiometric decay (alpha decay via quantum tunneling) and thus radiometric clocks began at the Fall, retroactively backdated through the substrate’s χ-field dynamics.  
- The Earth’s age is 4.5 billion years within fallen physics but not necessarily in the pre-Fall substrate; both young-earth and old-earth views are correct within their respective physics but make a category error by assuming a single substrate.  
- The image of God and structural physical laws survived the Fall; what was lost is proximity—direct coupling to God’s Logos—which the Cross and Spirit restore.  
- The framework rejects gnosticism and Omphalos, affirming matter’s goodness and the reality of radiometric ages within fallen physics.

**Why it matters:**  
This framework dissolves the longstanding young-earth vs. old-earth debate by showing they address different physics substrates. It reframes quantum mechanics and cosmology theologically, proposing a physics history with a substrate fracture at the Fall, impacting how we interpret Scripture, science, and their integration.

**Key claims:**  
- Quantum indeterminacy is a signature of a broken coupling, not fundamental reality.  
- Radiometric decay and clocks are post-Fall phenomena introduced retroactively.  
- The substrate change at the Fall is a global, retrocausal event affecting physics backward and forward in time.  
- Structural quantum features and the image of God remain invariant across substrates.

**Evidence used:**  
- Biblical narrative and theological interpretation (Genesis, Luke, Revelation).  
- Known physics distinctions between quantum structure and indeterminacy.  
- Bell’s theorem and experimental violations of local hidden variables.  
- Quantum tunneling’s role in radiometric decay and its implications.  
- Analogies to delayed-choice quantum eraser experiments demonstrating retrocausality.  
- Formal framework papers on χ-field substrate dynamics and measurement collapse.

**Risks, gaps, or assumptions:**  
- The clean separation between quantum structure and indeterminacy may not hold under future physics developments.  
- Retrocausal substrate restructuring is a strong, unconventional claim requiring further formal and experimental support.  
- Hermeneutical claims about Genesis interpretations may be contested by biblical scholars.  
- Experimental tests of coherence effects on tunneling rates remain to be done.  
- The framework’s metaphysical assumptions about Logos coupling and χ-field dynamics are not standard physics.

**Best next action:**  
- Conduct targeted experiments to test coherence influence on quantum tunneling rates.  
- Develop sharper formal distinctions between quantum structural and indeterminacy features.  
- Engage biblical scholarship to test hermeneutical claims about substrate fracture compatibility.  
- Publish and peer-review the retrocausal substrate-change model to invite critique and refinement.  
- Prepare accessible summaries to facilitate dialogue between scientific and theological communities.
