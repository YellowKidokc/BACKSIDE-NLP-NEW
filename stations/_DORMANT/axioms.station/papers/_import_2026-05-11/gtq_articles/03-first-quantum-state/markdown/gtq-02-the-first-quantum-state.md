## I. What Superposition Actually Is

Before the mapping, the formalism has to be stated without evasion.

A quantum system in superposition occupies multiple eigenstates simultaneously. This is not a statement about observer ignorance --- it is not that the particle has a definite position we don\'t know yet. It is that the particle genuinely has no definite position until measurement. The mathematical object representing the system is a wavefunction:

:::: eq-block
::: eq-label
Quantum Superposition State
:::

\$\$\|\\psi\\rangle = \\alpha\|0\\rangle + \\beta\|1\\rangle\$\$

where \$\|\\alpha\|\^2 + \|\\beta\|\^2 = 1\$ and both eigenstates are simultaneously real until measurement forces resolution.
::::

The wavefunction evolves according to the Schrödinger equation --- a perfectly smooth, deterministic, reversible evolution. Nothing strange happens. The system drifts through Hilbert space in a perfectly orderly way. Then measurement occurs, and everything that was smooth and reversible becomes discontinuous and irreversible. The wavefunction \"collapses\" to a single eigenstate. The probability amplitudes vanish. One outcome becomes actual. The others are gone.

This event has five properties that every serious interpretation of quantum mechanics has to accommodate:

1.  **Multiple states coexist.** Before measurement, all eigenstates are simultaneously real. The system is not in one state with unknown probability --- it is in all states with weighted amplitude.
2.  **No definite outcome exists until measurement.** The system evolves deterministically in superposition. Measurement, not passage of time, forces resolution. Without a measurement event, superposition persists indefinitely.
3.  **Measurement is irreversible.** Once the wavefunction collapses, it does not spontaneously restore itself. There is no quantum un-measurement. The direction of time imposed by measurement is strict.
4.  **Observer choice determines outcome.** The measurement basis --- what property you choose to measure --- determines which eigenstates are available as outcomes. Choosing to measure spin-z gives different results than choosing to measure spin-x, even on the same particle.
5.  **Post-collapse evolution differs.** The system after collapse evolves from a definite eigenstate, not from a superposition. The subsequent dynamics are qualitatively different from pre-measurement evolution. The system has entered a different regime.

::: insight
[--- The Structural Claim]{.label}

These five properties are not arbitrary. They constitute the minimum description of superposition that any interpretation of quantum mechanics must preserve. Any mapping that captures all five is not an analogy. It is an isomorphism.
:::

## System Audit

This paper introduces structural correspondence between Eden and quantum superposition. The pattern recognition is strong, but claims of equivalence are overstated.

### What Holds {#what-holds .audit-holds}

- The identification of multiple shared properties between Eden and superposition is compelling.
- The "no measurement, no collapse" framing is logically coherent.
- The Tree as a trigger mechanism is a strong conceptual bridge.

### What Needs Proof {#what-needs-proof .audit-proof}

- Whether these correspondences constitute true structural equivalence remains unproven.
- The informational fidelity variable requires formal definition and measurement.
- The observer role needs clearer distinction from standard quantum interpretation.

### Where We Overreached {#where-we-overreached .audit-overreach}

- "This is not analogy" exceeds what has been demonstrated.
- "Five complete matches" overstates the strength of alignment.
- Claims about entropy activation at collapse conflict with established physics framing.
