# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
The story of the Fall in the Bible isn’t just about people making bad choices or feeling guilty. It’s about something breaking deep down in the very fabric of reality — the physical foundation of everything. Because the problem is so deep, the fix has to be just as deep. The Cross and Resurrection aren’t just good advice or spiritual ideas; they are the exact, physical repair to that broken foundation. Jesus’ resurrection wasn’t just a symbol — it had to be real and physical to fix the damage. Even the thief on the cross, who had no good record, was promised paradise “today,” showing that this fix doesn’t depend on earning it but on simply connecting with Jesus.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Substrate:** The basic physical foundation or “ground floor” of reality.  
- **Entropy:** A measure of disorder or decay in a system.  
- **Negentropy:** The opposite of entropy; a force that brings order and repair.  
- **Coherence:** The connectedness and unity of reality’s parts.  
- **Symmetry requirement:** The rule that a fix must be as deep and strong as the damage it repairs.  
- **Grace:** A free gift from God that repairs the broken system without needing us to earn it.

**What this is really saying:**  
The Fall broke reality’s physical foundation, causing decay and separation. Human efforts like better morals or therapy can’t fix this deep break. Only God’s action through Jesus — his life, death, and physical resurrection — can repair the damage exactly and fully. This repair is immediate and available to anyone who turns to Jesus, no matter their past.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If the Cross only fixed spiritual or moral problems but left the physical foundation broken.  
- If Jesus’ resurrection was not a real, physical event but only a metaphor or spiritual idea.  
- If the repair didn’t match the depth of the Fall’s damage.  
- If restoration required earning or waiting rather than being immediate and free.
