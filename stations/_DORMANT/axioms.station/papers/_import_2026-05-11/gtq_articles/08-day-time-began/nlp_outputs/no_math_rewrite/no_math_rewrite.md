# The Day Time Began

**A Note Before We Begin**  
This article treats the Bible and science as two ways of describing the same reality. When Genesis says God created the heavens and earth, that is taken literally. When physics describes the universe’s beginning and laws, that is also taken literally. The question is not whether they conflict, but what it means that they agree. This article explores where time itself begins, finding the same story in both the Hebrew text and physics equations.

> "For dust you are, and to dust you shall return." — Genesis 3:19  
> This is not a moral judgment but a physics statement. It describes a process: dust becomes living being and returns to dust. This shows sequence, irreversibility, and the arrow of time.

Before God spoke these words, there was no "before." No ticking clock, no entropy pushing matter toward disorder, no death scheduled. The main physics equation describing reality had no time term. The system was perfectly coherent and static—not frozen, but complete. Nothing changed because there was no time.

Previous articles showed Eden as a quantum superposition—a perfectly coherent state that collapsed when Adam measured it by eating the fruit. The will survived but entered a noisy, degraded state. This article goes deeper: the collapse did not happen within time; it created time. The first wavefunction collapse is the first tick of the clock. And we have data to support this.
