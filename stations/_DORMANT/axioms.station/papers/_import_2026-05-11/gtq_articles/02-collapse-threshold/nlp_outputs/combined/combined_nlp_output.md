## EXPLAIN IT SIMPLE

**The 2-minute version:**  
Imagine two people hear the same message, but one hears it clearly and the other hears it with static. The one who hears it clearly can make a big change happen, but the one who hears it with static can’t. This idea helps explain a puzzle in quantum physics and the Bible about why the same action can have different effects depending on who does it.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Collapse:** When a quantum system changes from many possibilities to one actual outcome.  
- **Informational Fidelity:** How clearly someone receives the original message or command.  
- **Measurement:** Interacting with a system to find out its state.  
- **Decoherence:** Noise or interference that makes the message unclear.  
- **Federal Headship:** A theological idea that one person’s actions affect a whole group.

**What this is really saying:**  
Not all observers are equal in causing reality to change. Whether a measurement causes reality to “collapse” depends on how directly and clearly the observer received the instructions or information. In the Bible, Adam got the command straight from God, so his action caused reality to change for everyone. Eve got the message secondhand and with interference, so her action didn’t cause the same change. This idea connects physics and theology and suggests that reality-making is a special authority, not something everyone automatically has.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
If experiments showed that all observers, regardless of how they receive information, always cause the same collapse effect, or if the biblical narrative was proven unrelated to physical measurement processes, this framework would be undermined.

# The Collapse Threshold

**A Note Before We Begin**  
In a previous article, we encountered a puzzle: Eve ate the fruit and nothing happened, but when Adam ate the same fruit, reality collapsed for both of them at once. Two identical actions, two very different results. This article explores the mechanism behind that difference—not to dismiss it, but to clearly define it so it can be tested.

---

## The Signal Strength Problem

Why does one person’s measurement collapse the system, while another’s identical measurement does not? Standard quantum mechanics says this can’t happen—measurements are measurements, no matter who does them. But the Bible describes exactly this situation, and theological analysis confirms it. So either the text is wrong, or physics is missing something.

The missing piece is called **Informational Fidelity**. Think of it like a radio signal: if your receiver is perfectly tuned, you get a clear message. If there’s noise or interference, you only get fragments and can’t act with full authority. Informational fidelity measures how clearly an observer receives the system’s instructions from the source.

---

## Two Observers, Two Fidelity Values

Adam received the command directly from God—no middleman, no noise. The instruction arrived perfectly clear. Eve received the command secondhand, likely from Adam, but the serpent introduced noise, making the message unclear. By the time Eve acted, her informational fidelity was degraded.

The claim is that there is a threshold of informational fidelity needed to cause the system to collapse. Eve’s measurement was real but below that threshold—a weak measurement that did not collapse reality. Adam’s measurement crossed the threshold, causing an instantaneous, non-local collapse that affected both of them.

This reflects the idea of federal headship in theology: the observer with primary authority causes systemic change; others can interact but cannot authorize it.

---

## What This Means

This idea challenges modern physics: reality-making is not something all conscious observers can do equally. The ability to cause collapse depends on how directly and clearly the observer receives the system’s instructions.

Neurochemical studies show different brain states correspond to different levels of coupling to the source. Civilizational history also reflects this variable. Experimental results from the PEAR-LAB show consciousness quality varies among observers in affecting physical systems, supporting the idea of informational fidelity.

## The Complete Picture

Before the Fall, reality was fully coherent and ordered. The Tree of Knowledge acted as a boundary between potential and actual reality.

The serpent introduced noise into Eve’s channel, lowering her informational fidelity below the collapse threshold. She measured but the system held. Adam measured with perfect fidelity, crossing the threshold and causing collapse. This collapse was systemic, affecting the entire entangled human system.

Later, Christ acts as a Second Federal Observer, absorbing the entropy caused by the Fall. He does not undo the collapse but restores the connection that was severed, paying the cost introduced by the Fall.
