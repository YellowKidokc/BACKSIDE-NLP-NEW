# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Scientists have done an experiment where what you decide to do in the future can actually change what happened in the past—this sounds impossible, but it’s real. This article says that the Christian idea of Jesus dying on the Cross isn’t just a story or a one-time event; it’s something that exists outside of time and reaches both backward and forward in time to fix everything that went wrong. Time itself was created when humans first sinned, and it’s the thing that makes fixing that sin possible. So, the Cross is like a cosmic eraser that wipes away sin across all time.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Delayed-choice quantum eraser:** A physics experiment showing future choices can affect past events.  
- **Retrocausality:** When the future influences the past.  
- **Cross/Atonement:** Jesus’ death that fixes humanity’s sin.  
- **Eternal frame:** A way of looking at reality where all times exist at once, not one after another.  
- **Temporal frame:** Our everyday experience of time moving forward.  
- **Spirit actualization:** The process that makes possibilities real, not limited by time.  
- **Entropy:** Disorder or decay, which increases over time.  
- **Redemption:** Being saved or made right again.

**What this is really saying:**  
The Cross isn’t just a past event; it’s an eternal event that exists outside of time and affects all moments in history. The strange quantum experiment shows that the future can influence the past, which helps explain how the Cross can reach backward to fix sins before it happened and forward to cover sins after. Time was created by human sin, but it also makes salvation possible because it allows “before and after.” This means physics and theology are describing the same reality from different angles.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If future events could never influence past events, even at the quantum level.  
- If the Cross were shown to be only a historical event with no eternal significance.  
- If time were proven to be strictly one-way and irreversible at all scales.  
- If the quantum eraser experiment’s retrocausal interpretation were conclusively disproven.  
- If theological texts were shown to contradict the eternal nature of the Cross.
