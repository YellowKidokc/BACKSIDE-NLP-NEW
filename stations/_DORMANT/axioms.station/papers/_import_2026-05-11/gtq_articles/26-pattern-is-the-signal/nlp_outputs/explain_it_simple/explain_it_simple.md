# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Some people argue Jesus never really lived but was made up like other ancient heroes with similar stories. A historian named Richard Carrier used math to say there’s only about a 32% chance Jesus was real. But this article says Carrier made a big mistake: he treated the shared story pattern—like dying and coming back to life—as a sign people just copied myths. Instead, this article says that pattern is actually a real signal from how the universe works, not just a story people made up. When you fix that mistake, the math shows it’s very likely Jesus was a real person.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Bayes’ Theorem:** A way to update how likely something is based on new evidence.  
- **Reference class:** The group of things you compare your subject to when figuring out how likely something is.  
- **Projection Principle:** The idea that the universe’s physical laws reflect the nature of the divine, so patterns in stories reflect real cosmic truths.  
- **Death-resurrection-salvation pattern:** The common story of a hero who dies and comes back to save people.  
- **Independent attestation:** Evidence from sources that don’t depend on each other, showing something really happened.  
- **Hostile attestation:** Evidence from people who didn’t want to support the story but still mentioned it.  
- **Consciousness coupling:** Experiments showing that human consciousness can influence physical systems.

**What this is really saying:**  
The common story of a dying and rising hero isn’t just a myth people copied. It’s a real pattern built into reality itself. Jesus fits this pattern better than anyone else because of strong historical evidence and unique markers. When you take this into account, the chance Jesus really lived is much higher than some skeptics say.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If the Projection Principle is shown to be false or just made up after the fact.  
- If the death-resurrection story can be proven to come only from cultures copying each other with no real event behind it.  
- If the experiments showing consciousness affects physical systems are proven wrong.  
- If another ancient figure shows the same strong historical evidence as Jesus.  
- If Carrier’s reference class can be defended without assuming the pattern is fake.
