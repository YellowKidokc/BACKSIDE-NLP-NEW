# No-Math Rewrite

### Why the Pattern Is the Signal

This article responds to Richard Carrier’s serious and methodical argument that Jesus probably didn’t exist, based on Bayesian probability. Carrier’s math is correct given his assumptions, but the problem lies in one key assumption: the reference class he uses to estimate how likely Jesus was historical.

Carrier groups Jesus with other ancient savior figures like Osiris and Hercules, who share a common pattern of incarnation, death, resurrection, and salvation. He assumes this pattern is a mythological template cultures invent repeatedly. Because many such figures exist and are likely fabricated, Carrier assigns a low prior probability that Jesus was real.

But what if this assumption is wrong? What if the pattern itself is real, not just a story people copied?

This article introduces the Projection Principle, which says that the universe’s physical laws reflect the nature of the divine Logos. The death-resurrection-salvation pattern is not just a myth but a physical signature of coherence restoration—what happens when entropy (disorder) is reversed by a grace input. This pattern appears in many cultures not because they copied myths but because it reflects a real cosmic process.

If the pattern is real, then the reference class Carrier uses is contaminated by signal. The pattern’s frequency does not lower the chance that any individual instance is historical; it raises the question of which instance is the actual source of the signal.

Jesus stands out uniquely among these figures because of several historical markers:

- **Independent attestation within one generation:** Paul's letters, written about 20 years after Jesus’s death, mention people who knew Jesus personally. No other figure in the class has such early independent sources.

- **Hostile attestation:** Non-Christian historians like Tacitus and Josephus mention Jesus in contexts hostile to Christianity, which supports historicity.

- **A movement that grew rapidly despite no political or military power:** Christianity started among common people under occupation and persecution but became dominant within centuries, unlike other savior figures tied to ruling powers.

- **Specific, falsifiable claims:** Paul’s letters mention details about Jesus’s death and resurrection that contemporaries could have disproved but did not.

Each of these markers increases the likelihood that Jesus was historical.

The article also points out that Carrier treats the resurrection as a supernatural miracle with a near-zero prior probability. However, the framework here treats resurrection as a predicted physical event—a local reversal of entropy driven by maximum coherence coupling with the Logos. This is supported by experimental evidence from PEAR-LAB and the Global Consciousness Project, which show consciousness can measurably affect physical systems at statistically significant levels.

When these corrections are applied to Carrier’s Bayesian framework—raising the prior probability, adjusting the scoring of extra-biblical evidence, and reinterpreting Paul’s letters—the posterior probability that Jesus was historical rises dramatically, potentially above 90%.

The article stresses it does not claim mathematical proof of Jesus’s existence, only that Carrier’s reference class assumption is flawed and that correcting it changes the probability significantly. The pattern is not noise but signal, and Jesus is the best candidate for the source event behind it.

### Falsification Criteria

The argument would fail if:

- The Projection Principle is disproven or shown to be cherry-picked.  
- The death-resurrection pattern is shown to be purely cultural borrowing with no independent origin.  
- The consciousness experiments are invalidated.  
- Another figure shows comparable early and hostile attestation and movement growth.  
- Carrier’s reference class can be defended without assuming the pattern is fabricated.

### The Audit

The article acknowledges that the reference class critique is strong and that Jesus’s evidentiary markers are unique. The math is correct when the prior is corrected. However, the Projection Principle is the foundational assumption and not independently verified outside this framework. The consciousness experiments support the mechanism but do not prove resurrection occurred. The 97.4% probability figure is aspirational, depending on all corrections being accepted simultaneously. The article offers this as a reasoned argument, not definitive proof.
