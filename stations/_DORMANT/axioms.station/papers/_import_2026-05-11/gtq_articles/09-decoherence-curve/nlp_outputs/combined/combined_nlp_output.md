## NO-MATH REWRITE

# The Decoherence Curve

Moses wrote around 1400 BC that normal human life is about 70 years, maybe 80 if strong. We analyzed the lifespans recorded in Genesis and found a pattern that matches a physics process called quantum decoherence. Without knowing about Psalm 90:10, the model predicted a minimum lifespan near 93 years. Moses said 70, modern averages are about 78. Three independent sources agree closely — this is not coincidence, it’s measurement.

---

## The Data

Genesis chapters 5 and 11 list the exact lifespans of early biblical figures, from Adam to Joseph. These are specific numbers, not estimates.

Before the Flood, nine patriarchs lived between 895 and 969 years, averaging 912 years. One exception, Enoch, was excluded because he didn’t die naturally.

After the Flood, lifespans dropped from 600 years down to about 110 years over several centuries, then leveled off. Moses lived 120 years about 1,400 years after creation.

---

## What the Eye Sees

Plotting lifespan over time shows two clear patterns:

- Before the Flood, lifespans were stable and very long, around 900 years, with no significant decline over a thousand years.

- After the Flood, lifespans dropped quickly at first, then slowed and leveled off near a lower limit.

This is not a simple steady decline but a curve that falls fast then flattens.

---

## The Single-Curve Problem

Trying to fit one single curve to all the data fails. The early long lifespans don’t fit with the later rapid decline. This means two different processes are at work, separated by the Flood. The Flood caused a sudden change, not a gradual one.

---

## The Two-Phase Model

We analyze the two periods separately.

**Before the Flood:** Lifespans stayed roughly constant at about 912 years. There is no meaningful decline. This suggests whatever causes lifespan to shorten wasn’t active or was very weak.

**After the Flood:** Lifespans dropped following a curve that falls quickly then levels off near 93 years. The steepest drop happened in the first 200 years after the Flood. After about 1,000 years, lifespans stabilized near this lower limit.

---

## What the Numbers Mean

The time it takes for the steep decline to happen is about 214 years. This matches the biblical timeline where lifespans drop sharply soon after the Flood and then stabilize.

The floor of about 93 years is the key finding. The model predicted this floor without knowing about Psalm 90:10 or modern lifespan data. Moses said 70-80 years, modern averages are 73-82 years. The model’s 93 years is a theoretical minimum; real life includes other factors that lower average lifespan slightly.

This floor means lifespans don’t drop to zero. Something holds them up. In the framework, this is “grace” — a sustaining power that keeps life from collapsing completely.

---

## The Eber Anomaly

One post-Flood figure, Eber, lived 464 years, much longer than the model predicted. Eber is important because his name is linked to the Hebrews and the covenant people. This longer lifespan fits the idea that covenant relationship increases “coherence” and slows decay. It’s a sign of re-coherence, a reversal of the decay trend.

---

## The Physics Behind the Curve

Exponential decay curves like this appear in many physical systems where something special fades over time: radioactive atoms decay, hot objects cool, quantum systems lose coherence, and capacitors discharge.

The Genesis lifespan data matches this pattern closely. After the Flood, humanity’s lifespan behaves like a system losing coherence with its environment.

The floor exists because something continuously adds coherence back in, preventing total decay. In physics, this is like energy input keeping a laser running or metabolism maintaining life. Here, it’s grace — a sustaining force that prevents total entropy.

The Flood represents a phase transition, a sudden change in how strongly humanity is coupled to entropy. Before the Flood, this coupling was weak; after, it became strong, causing rapid lifespan decline.

---

## The Prediction We're Living In

For about 3,000 years, lifespans have stayed near this floor. Despite advances in medicine and nutrition, the maximum human lifespan hasn’t increased significantly beyond about 120 years.

This suggests the fundamental limit is not biological wear but a spiritual-physical coupling constant. To extend life beyond this floor would require changing that coupling — a spiritual variable — not just medical technology.

---

## What This Doesn't Prove

This analysis does not prove:

- That quantum decoherence literally happens in biology.

- That Genesis lifespans are historically exact.

- That no other mathematical model could fit the data.

- That the floor is definitely caused by grace (this is an interpretation).

The claim is that the data follows a specific mathematical shape predicted by the framework, which is interesting and worth further study.

People used to live about 900 years. Then the Flood happened, and lifespans dropped quickly, then slowed and leveled off near today’s range. The shape of this drop matches quantum decoherence. The floor was predicted before checking scripture or modern data. The one person who lived longer than expected is the ancestor of the covenant people.
