# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
This study checks if a new scientific model about how God reveals truth over time matches what we see in the Bible and history. The model says God reveals more as people get ready to understand, and this readiness grows steadily. The study tested this idea in many ways and found strong evidence that the Bible’s language and ideas get more complex over time, that evil strategies get more clever as people get smarter, and that the Bible’s story fits a unique plan that respects free will, grace, and justice. One test didn’t work out, but most did. More tests are planned.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Theophysics Master Equation:** A scientific formula that tries to explain how spiritual and physical realities connect.  
- **Preparation function:** How ready people are to understand God’s messages, which grows over time.  
- **Coherence:** How well things fit together or make sense in the Bible and history.  
- **Grace:** God’s help or gifts given to people.  
- **Entropy:** A measure of disorder or decay, like things falling apart over time.

**What this is really saying:**  
The Bible’s story isn’t random or just a collection of old writings. Instead, it shows a clear pattern where God reveals more as people become ready, evil adapts to this growth, and the whole plan respects important rules like free will and justice. This pattern is so specific that no other way of doing it fits all the rules as well. The study uses math and data to back this up.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If the Bible’s language and ideas did not get steadily more complex over time.  
- If evil’s strategies did not get more sophisticated as people’s readiness increased.  
- If another religious text showed the same steady growth pattern as the Bible.  
- If a simpler or “better” divine plan could satisfy free will, grace, and justice better than the biblical pattern.  
- If grace response time clearly increased with readiness in a simple way (which it did not).
