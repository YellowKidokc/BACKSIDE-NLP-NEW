# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
This article says that the way ancient Canaanites built their society—through rituals, sacrifices, laws, and beliefs—is very similar to how modern Western society works today, just in new forms. Things like abortion, social media, cancel culture, and debt are like modern versions of old religious practices that helped people survive hard times. But instead of helping, these new versions are breaking down our society. The Bible’s laws tried to stop these problems by making rules about fairness and mercy, but today we’ve mostly ignored those rules. If we don’t notice this, society will keep falling apart.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- *Ritual*: A repeated action with special meaning, often religious.  
- *Sacrifice*: Giving up something valuable, often to a god, to gain favor or protection.  
- *Blood feud*: When families or groups take revenge on each other, causing ongoing violence.  
- *Cancel culture*: When people online try to punish or exclude someone harshly for a mistake.  
- *Debt slavery*: When people owe so much money that it controls their lives and future.  
- *Coherence*: How well a society or person stays connected to its core values and order.  
- *Grace*: A positive force that helps restore order and healing.  
- *Entropy*: The natural tendency toward disorder and breakdown.

**What this is really saying:**  
Even though technology and culture have changed a lot, the basic spiritual and social problems humans face haven’t changed. We still try to control life’s chaos by making deals with higher powers or systems, but today those deals are hidden behind things like technology and money. These hidden deals often harm us and our communities. The Bible’s old laws tried to stop this harm by teaching fairness and mercy, but modern society has mostly forgotten those lessons. If we don’t remember and apply them, society will keep getting worse.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If modern social problems had completely new causes unrelated to ancient spiritual or social patterns.  
- If data showed that modern practices like abortion or social media had no negative impact on social cohesion or spiritual health.  
- If societies that reject these ancient patterns and Mosaic principles thrived without decay.  
- If the biblical lifespan and decay data were shown to be inaccurate or unrelated to social entropy.  
- If proportional justice and mercy mechanisms were found ineffective or harmful in modern contexts.
