## 2. THE JUSTICE VECTOR (J)

**Operator definition:** Restore coherence by requiring the offender to pay the cost of the violation.

$$\mathbf{J}(\text{offense}) = (\text{response proportional to offense}) \;\big|\; (\text{cost-bearer} = \text{offender})$$

**Component decomposition:**

$$\vec{\mathbf{J}} = \begin{pmatrix} \text{Proportionality} \\ \text{Impartiality} \\ \text{Truth-naming} \\ \text{Restoration of victim} \\ \text{Cost-borne-by-offender} \end{pmatrix}$$

| Symbol | Component | What It Measures | Failure Mode When Absent |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Pr | Proportionality | Response magnitude matches violation magnitude | **Disproportion** — punishment exceeds or trivializes the offense |
| Im | Impartiality | Same response regardless of who the offender is | **Favoritism** — the powerful escape, the weak are crushed |
| Tn | Truth-naming | The violation is correctly identified, not minimized | **Denial** — the offense is renamed, softened, or erased |
| Rv | Restoration of victim | The victim is made whole to the extent possible | **Victim-neglect** — justice serves the system but abandons the harmed |
| Co | Cost-borne-by-offender | The offender bears the cost of restoration | **Scapegoating** — an uninvolved party is forced to pay |

**Perfect Justice** is the state where all five components are at maximum simultaneously. Drop any single component and you get the named failure mode. The five failure modes — disproportion, favoritism, denial, victim-neglect, scapegoating — are not random pathologies. They are the **specific dimensional collapses** of the Justice vector.

$$\mathbf{M}(\text{offense}) = (\text{response proportional to offense}) \;\big|\; (\text{cost-bearer} = \text{third party})$$

**Component decomposition:**

$$\vec{\mathbf{M}} = \begin{pmatrix} \text{Proportionality} \\ \text{Impartiality} \\ \text{Truth-naming} \\ \text{Restoration of victim} \\ \text{Cost-borne-by-third-party} \end{pmatrix}$$

| Ct | Cost-borne-by-third-party | A willing third party absorbs the cost voluntarily | **Coerced sacrifice** — an unwilling party is forced to bear the cost |

**Perfect Mercy** is the state where all five components are at maximum simultaneously. Drop any single component and you get the named failure mode.
