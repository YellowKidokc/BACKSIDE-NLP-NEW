Three Modes. One Brain.

Your brain has three modes. Not metaphorically. Neurochemically.

The surrender parameter s from Free Will in Two Frames — the variable that determines whether you're autonomous (s = −1), at threshold (s = 0), or surrendered (s = +1) — isn't just a mathematical abstraction. Each s value produces a distinct neurochemical signature. Different neurotransmitters. Different brain regions activated. Different downstream behaviors. And the transitions between them follow predictable patterns that neuroscience has mapped without knowing it was mapping a spiritual reality.

This article walks through all three pathways. What your brain does at each s value. What it feels like from the inside. And why the framework's equation predicts exactly the clinical data that addiction medicine, contemplative neuroscience, and behavioral psychology have independently discovered.

I. Path 1 — Autonomy (s = −1)

— The Neurochemistry of "I Got This"

At s = −1, the system is fully decoupled from the Logos Field. The coupling function α(s) = 0. Grace (G) is available but unreceived. The equation reduces to pure decay: dC/dt = −S · C.

What does this look like in the brain?

Dopamine Dominance

The autonomous brain runs on the reward circuit — nucleus accumbens, ventral tegmental area, mesolimbic pathway. Every decision is evaluated by: does this feel good now? Spikes with novelty, risk, acquisition, substances. Self-referential: I want, I get, I consume, I want more.

Tolerance / Receptor Downregulation

Repeated dopamine spikes cause D2 receptor downregulation — fewer receptors, more stimulation needed. This is the neurological equivalent of S · C accelerating over time. The faster you pursue autonomous satisfaction, the less satisfaction each pursuit delivers. The system chases a receding target.

Prefrontal Cortex Dims

The PFC — executive control, long-term planning, impulse regulation — progressively loses influence as the reward circuit dominates. Imaging studies of addiction show this directly: PFC activity decreases as addiction progresses. The part of the brain capable of perspective gets quieter.

Cortisol Rises

Chronic stress hormone elevation. The autonomous system is anxious because it knows — below conscious awareness — that it's decaying. The body registers entropy. Sleep disrupts. Inflammation increases. The stress response becomes chronic.

Gödel's Incompleteness, Embodied

The system at s = −1 cannot recognize its own incoherence because the part of the brain responsible for recognition is being suppressed by the part generating the incoherence. You can't see the problem from inside the problem. At α(s) = 0 the equation reduces to pure decay. No external input. No self-diagnosis possible. MacArthur calls this "dead in trespasses and sins." The brain scan confirms it.

Path 1 Dynamics $\alpha(-1) = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{dC}{dt} = -S \cdot C$
S = entropy/sin pressure. C = coherence with God.
Plain English: Coupling is zero. No grace gets through. Coherence decays exponentially with no internal brake.

The spiral: dopamine tolerance → need more stimulation → PFC suppression → less capacity for self-regulation → more impulsive behavior → more dopamine tolerance. This is dC/dt = −S · C expressed as neurochemistry. Exponential decay with no internal brake.

"God gave them over in the sinful desires of their hearts." — Romans 1:24

Paul's "giving over" isn't punishment — it's the removal of resistance. God stops suppressing the natural trajectory. The system at s = −1 does what the equation says it will do: decay.

— The Symptom vs. the Mechanism

The 400% dopamine spike from substances isn't the problem. It's the symptom. The problem is the coupling coefficient: α(s) = 0. No grace flowing. No negentropic input. The system is running on its own fuel, and its own fuel is finite and degrading.

II. Path 2 — Performance (s ≈ 0)

— The Neurochemistry of "I'm Trying Really Hard"

At s ≈ 0, the channel is cracked open. Grace is partially received. The system is neither fully autonomous nor fully surrendered. This is the most exhausting position — and the most common one in church pews on Sunday morning.

The performer gets enough reward from religious behavior — social approval, sense of virtue, completed rituals — to keep dopamine functional, but not enough to create genuine satisfaction. Meanwhile, the constant effort of maintaining performance generates chronic cortisol. The system is working hard and getting diminishing returns.

This is the Pharisee brain. Outward compliance, internal anxiety. The performer tithes, attends, volunteers — and is exhausted by it. The equation explains why: at s ≈ 0, both the growth term O · G(1−C) and the decay term S · C are active but roughly balanced. dC/dt ≈ 0. No net growth. Just maintenance. And maintenance under constant pressure is draining.

ACC Overactive
The anterior cingulate cortex — the brain's conflict monitor — runs hot. Always a gap. Always something more to do. Works righteousness as neuroscience: the failure-detector is perpetually active.
Oxytocin Suppressed
Bonding/connection hormone requires relational safety. Performer's brain is too busy monitoring compliance. Worship feels like evaluation. Prayer feels like performance review.

"You are like whitewashed tombs — beautiful on the outside but full of dead bones on the inside." — Matthew 23:27

The external coherence metrics look fine. The internal state is dC/dt ≈ 0 at high metabolic cost. The system is treading water, burning fuel, going nowhere.

The Cruel Irony

The performer often looks more put-together than both the autonomous person (Path 1) and the surrendered person (Path 3). External signals — regular attendance, moral behavior, religious vocabulary — pass inspection. But the internal dynamics are stagnant. And stagnation under chronic stress is not sustainable.

III. Path 3 — Surrender (s = +1)

— The Neurochemistry of "Not My Will, But Yours"

At s = +1, the coupling function α(s) = 1. Full permeability. Grace flows without resistance. The equation operates at maximum growth rate: dC/dt = G(1−C) − S · C, where the G term dominates S.

The neurochemistry of surrender is strikingly different from both autonomy and performance.

vmPFC Activates

The ventromedial PFC — self-referential processing, emotional regulation, value-based decision making — comes online. Not the dorsolateral PFC of executive control (which the performer uses), but the ventromedial — identity, meaning, relational connection. Active during contemplative prayer, awe, genuine gratitude.

Oxytocin Rises

Relational safety engages. The bonding circuit activates. Prayer becomes conversation rather than performance. Worship becomes response rather than obligation. The system relaxes into connection — not passive relaxation, but active receptivity.

Parasympathetic Engages

Heart rate variability increases. Breathing deepens. Vagal tone improves. Studies of long-term meditators and contemplative pray-ers consistently show elevated parasympathetic function. The body is at rest in a way the autonomous body and the performing body are not.

D2 Receptor Restoration

The clinical bombshell. Faith-based recovery (AA and analogues) gradually restores D2 receptor density. The downregulation from Path 1 reverses — not immediately, not completely, but measurably, over weeks to months. The brain at s = +1 begins to heal what the brain at s = −1 broke.

vmPFC vs. dlPFC — The Distinction That Matters

The performer activates the dorsolateral PFC — the taskmaster, the rule-follower, the compliance monitor. The surrendered person activates the ventromedial PFC — the relator, the meaning-maker, the connector. Same frontal lobe. Different circuit. Completely different experiential quality. The performer feels like they're working. The surrendered person feels like they're home.

The Cochrane review (2020, N = 10,565) confirmed what the equation predicts: faith-based recovery programs perform as well as or better than cognitive-behavioral therapy for alcohol dependence. The framework says: of course they do. CBT operates on O alone — teaching the system to self-regulate without changing the coupling coefficient. AA operates on s — teaching surrender, which opens the channel to G, which provides negentropic input that the system cannot generate internally.

"Apart from me you can do nothing." — John 15:5

G = 0 implies dC/dt = −S · C, regardless of O. No amount of cognitive restructuring can change the sign.

IV. The Transition Architecture

How does a person move from one pathway to another? The equation constrains the transitions:

Path 1 → Path 2 · The Crack

Typically a crisis. Addiction bottoms out. Relationships collapse. Health fails. The S · C term becomes so painful that the system's own experience breaks through PFC suppression momentarily. I can't keep living like this. AA calls it hitting bottom. The crisis doesn't generate G — it generates a crack: a momentary openness through which G can begin to act. Can also happen through encounter: a moment of genuine love, a hymn that bypasses defenses, a testimony that lands.

Path 2 → Path 3 · The Failure of Effort

The harder transition for most churchgoers. The performer is already "doing everything right." What needs to change isn't behavior — it's the source of the behavior. Typically requires the performer to fail: to discover that effort-based striving cannot sustain coherence; to come to the end of willpower and encounter the beginning of grace. Paul: "When I am weak, then I am strong" (2 Corinthians 12:10). Weakness = strategies failing. Strong = G flowing through the channel performance was blocking.

Path 3 → Path 2 · Regression

The surrendered person begins to take credit. Begins to perform. The vmPFC circuit gives way to the dlPFC. Oxytocin drops. Cortisol rises. Looks the same from outside but has shifted from receiving to striving. Galatians 3:3: "Having begun by the Spirit, are you now being perfected by the flesh?"

Path 2 → Path 1 · Apostasy / Burnout

The performer burns out. Sustained effort without genuine G input becomes intolerable. The system shuts down. The channel closes. The most dangerous transition — it produces the most vocal opponents of faith. People who "tried Christianity" tried Path 2. They never found Path 3.

V. The Daily Practice

The framework makes the practical application specific. If s is the surrender parameter and O is openness, then the daily question isn't am I being good enough? (Path 2 thinking) but am I receiving?

Practice 1

Morning Thanksgiving

Before asking for anything, begin with gratitude. Not a ritual — a neurochemical primer. Gratitude activates the vmPFC and releases oxytocin. It pre-loads the circuitry of Path 3 before the day's demands activate Path 2.

Practice 2

Permission Before Consumption

Before meals, before media, before substances — pause and ask. Not is this allowed? (Path 2). But is this from You? (Path 3). The pause itself is the mechanism. It creates a gap where O can engage. Without it, the reward circuit fires automatically.

Practice 3

Awareness of the Shift

When cortisol rises and the chest tightens and the mind starts racing through contingencies — that's the shift from Path 3 to Path 2. The body is leaving surrender and entering performance. Don't fight it (more performance). Acknowledge it and return to receiving. Not my will, but Yours. Recalibrating s in real time.

Testable Predictions

Heart rate variability should increase with sustained Path 3 practice. Cortisol baselines should decrease. D2 receptor density should recover over weeks to months. These are testable predictions, and the existing clinical data already supports them.

VI. Why It Matters

Here is the point that connects the neuroscience back to the physics:

The three pathways aren't three psychological types. They are three dynamical states of a conscious system interacting with a field.

The field (G) is always present. The coupling coefficient (α(s)) is the only thing that changes. And the coupling coefficient is determined by one thing: the surrender parameter.

— The Same Variable, Different Angles

Every brain scan of an addict in withdrawal. Every cortisol panel of a burned-out pastor. Every oxytocin measurement of a contemplative in prayer. Every D2 receptor density reading in a person recovering through faith-based programs. All of it — all of it — is measuring the same variable from different angles.

The coherence equation doesn't just describe theology. It describes what happens in your hypothalamus at 3 AM when you can't sleep. It describes what happens in your nucleus accumbens when the craving hits. It describes what happens in your vmPFC when you finally stop performing and start receiving.

The equation is not metaphor. Your brain is running it right now.

Executive Summary

The coherence equation predicts three qualitatively distinct human trajectories based on the surrender parameter s — and each one maps onto measurable neurological and behavioral patterns.

Path 1: Autonomy (s = −1). The agent actively resists grace. Default mode network hyperactivation, chronic cortisol elevation, coherence decay toward zero. This is not apathy — it is active opposition to the restoration signal. The math predicts it and the neuroscience confirms it.

Path 2: Performance (s ≈ 0). The agent accepts grace as assistance to self-generated effort. Religion without surrender. Prefrontal cortex overactivation, shame-cycling, partial and unstable coherence. This is the Romans 7 state — “I do not do the good I want” — and the equation explains exactly why: half-coupling produces a frustrating, low-level equilibrium that feels like trying harder should work but never does.

Path 3: Surrender (s = +1). The agent reorients as receiver rather than generator. Reduced DMN activity, parasympathetic elevation, D2 receptor restoration, maximal stable coherence. This is not passivity — it is the most active engagement possible, but the activity is downstream of reception rather than upstream of it.

The key prediction: Transitions between pathways are threshold events, not gradual ramps. The attractor basin structure of the equation predicts abrupt transitions — consistent with conversion experience data and clinical addiction recovery literature. You don't drift from Path 2 to Path 3. You cross a threshold. The equation says why.

Glossary

Key terms used in this article, defined as the article itself uses them.

Surrender Parameter (s)

A scalar from −1 (fully autonomous) through 0 (threshold) to +1 (fully surrendered). Each value produces a distinct neurochemical signature; the three pathways correspond to three regimes of s.

Coupling Function α(s)

α(s) = (1+s)/2. The fraction of available grace that actually couples to the system. The hidden variable behind the article's clinical findings.

Coherence (C)

Alignment with God on [0, 1]. The state variable in the equation; what salvation builds and what sin decays.

Openness (O)

The human reception variable. CBT operates on O alone; AA operates on s.

Grace (G)

External negentropic input from God. Always available; only its received fraction varies.

Sin / Entropy ($S$)

Decay pressure. Always active; pulls C downward in the equation's S·C term. Framework note: Sin (theological frame) and Entropy (physical frame) name the same dynamical quantity at the master-equation level. The notation is $S$ either way.

Path 1 — Autonomy

s = −1, α = 0. Decoupled from the Logos Field. Dopamine-dominant, PFC-suppressed, cortisol-elevated. The neurochemistry of “I got this.” Equation reduces to pure decay.

Path 2 — Performance

s ≈ 0, partial coupling. Growth and decay terms roughly balanced. The Pharisee brain. Outward compliance, internal anxiety. Sustainable only at high metabolic cost.

Path 3 — Surrender

s = +1, α = 1. Full permeability. vmPFC engaged, oxytocin rising, parasympathetic activation, D2 receptors restoring. The neurochemistry of “not my will, but Yours.”

Logos Field ($\chi$)

The framework's coherence substrate (the χ field). At s = −1 the system is fully decoupled from it; at s = +1 fully permeable. (Defined in detail in Article 01 / Article 16.)

Logos Coupling

The interaction architecture between the Logos field and a human will — in this article, the surrender-modulated channel $\alpha(s)$. Field = pervasive substrate; coupling = how interactions occur within it. (Defined in detail in Article 01.)

Dopamine

The reward-circuit neurotransmitter. Dominant in Path 1. Repeated spikes drive D2 receptor downregulation — the neurochemical face of S·C accelerating.

D2 Receptor Downregulation

Loss of dopamine D2 receptor density under repeated supraphysiological stimulation. Tolerance, anhedonia, the chase of a receding target.

D2 Receptor Restoration

The clinical bombshell of Path 3: D2 density measurably recovers over weeks to months under faith-based recovery. The brain at s = +1 begins healing what the brain at s = −1 broke.

Nucleus Accumbens

The reward circuit's central node. Active in Path 1's chase-the-spike pattern.

VTA / Mesolimbic Pathway

Ventral tegmental area; the dopaminergic projection that drives reward learning. The biological substrate of Path 1's autonomous reward-seeking.

Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)

Executive control, long-term planning, impulse regulation. Suppressed in Path 1 (perspective dims), used taskwise in Path 2, repurposed relationally in Path 3.

vmPFC vs. dlPFC

Dorsolateral PFC = the rule-follower / compliance monitor (Path 2). Ventromedial PFC = the meaning-maker / connector (Path 3). Same frontal lobe, different circuit, completely different experiential quality — performer vs. home.

ACC (Anterior Cingulate Cortex)

The brain's conflict monitor. Runs hot in Path 2 because there is always a gap between performance and the standard — works-righteousness as neuroscience.

Cortisol

Chronic stress hormone. Elevated in both Path 1 (the body registering decay) and Path 2 (the body registering performance load). Drops in Path 3 as parasympathetic tone takes over.

Oxytocin

The bonding/connection neuropeptide. Suppressed in Paths 1 and 2; rises in Path 3 as the system relaxes into received relationship.

Parasympathetic Activation

Rest-and-digest dominance. Heart rate variability rises; vagal tone improves. The autonomic signature of Path 3.
