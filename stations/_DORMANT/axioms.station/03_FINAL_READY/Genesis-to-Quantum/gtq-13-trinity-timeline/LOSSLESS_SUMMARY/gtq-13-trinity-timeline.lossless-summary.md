### Glossary

Key terms used in this article. State Vector ($|\psi\rangle$) The complete quantum description of a system. Encodes all possible outcomes weighted by their amplitudes. In this article: the Father's signature. Measurement Basis ($|\phi\rangle$) The orthonormal set of eigenstates against which a system is measured. The structure that makes possibility legible. ### Born Rule

The quantum-mechanical rule giving the probability of measurement outcome $a$ as $P(a) = |\langle a|\psi\rangle|^2$. The foundational equation this entire article maps onto Trinitarian theology. Coherence ($\chi$, $C$) The structural integrity of a system — the degree to which it maintains itself against entropy. Pre-Pentecost: coherence depended on external pulses. Post-Pentecost: continuous internal grace. Entropy / Sin ($S$) The decay parameter that drives coherence toward zero. Same Master-Equation variable, two coordinate names: “entropy” in the physics frame, “sin” in the theological frame. Activated in the human domain at the Fall. Decoherence Time Constant ($\tau_d$) The characteristic time over which coherence degrades. The Flood marks the phase transition at which $\tau_d$ shifts from near-infinity to 214 years.

### Phase Transition

An abrupt structural change in a system's regime. The Flood, the Cross, and Pentecost are all phase transitions in the Trinity-coupling architecture.
