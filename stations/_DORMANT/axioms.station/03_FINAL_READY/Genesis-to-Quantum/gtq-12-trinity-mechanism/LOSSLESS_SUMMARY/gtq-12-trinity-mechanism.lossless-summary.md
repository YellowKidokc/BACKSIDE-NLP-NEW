### Glossary

Key terms used in this article. Hover any underlined gold term in The Paper for the same definition. Wave Function ($|\psi\rangle$) The complete quantum-mechanical description of a system as a vector in Hilbert space. Encodes all possible outcomes weighted by their amplitudes.

### Superposition

A linear combination of basis states. Before measurement, a quantum system is in superposition — it has not chosen a single value for its observable. Eigenstate ($|\phi_n\rangle$) A basis state of an observable; the “definite-value” states a system can be in after measurement. The eigenstates are the items in the “possible-outcomes” list.

### Born Rule

The rule that gives the probability of measurement outcome $a$ as $P(a) = |\langle a|\psi\rangle|^2$. Discovered by Max Born in 1926; the cornerstone of quantum probability.
