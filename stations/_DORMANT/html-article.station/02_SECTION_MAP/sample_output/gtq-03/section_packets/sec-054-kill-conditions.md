Kill Condition 1 — Coupling Structure
        If G and O are NOT multiplicatively coupled — if the relationship is additive ($G + O$) rather than multiplicative ($G \times O$) — the dissolution of the Calvinist–Arminian debate fails.
        The dissolution depends on the structural fact that $O = 0$ zeroes the entire grace term. If the actual relationship is additive, grace can operate independently of openness, which restores the Calvinist position on its own terms. The form of the coupling is the load-bearing element.
      

      
        Kill Condition 2 — Path Hierarchy
        If Path 3 does NOT outperform Path 2 in clinical and psychological data — if surrender-oriented spirituality shows no measurable advantage over performance-oriented spirituality — the framework's most important practical claim is wrong.
        Existing data from psychology of religion (intrinsic vs. extrinsic religiosity, Allport; secure vs. anxious attachment to God, Granqvist; self-determination theory applied to religion, Ryan & Deci) is broadly consistent with the hierarchy. But the equation makes specific quantitative predictions that have not yet been directly tested.
      

      
        Kill Condition 3 — Transition Architecture
        If pathway transitions are smooth gradual ramps rather than threshold crossings, the attractor basin model is wrong and the equation's phase structure doesn't apply.
        The prediction of abrupt transitions is specific and testable in conversion research, addiction recovery data, and clinical studies of value restructuring. Gradual-change data would falsify the threshold model but would not falsify the equation's steady-state predictions.
