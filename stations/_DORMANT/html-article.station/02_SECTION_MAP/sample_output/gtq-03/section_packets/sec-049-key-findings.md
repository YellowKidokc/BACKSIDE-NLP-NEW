# Key Findings

The equation requires both divine grace and human openness. Remove either term and the coherence collapses to zero. Neither camp is wrong about the existence of their variable; both camps are wrong to treat their variable as the whole equation.
        The surrender parameter $s \in [-1, +1]$ explains both theological camps. Calvinist outcomes arise naturally when $G$ is large relative to $O$ (grace overwhelms resistance). Arminian outcomes arise when $O$ is the limiting factor. Both are special cases of the same dynamics.
        Neuroscience confirms three distinct pathways. Path 1 (Autonomy, $s = -1$): DMN hyperactivation, chronic stress, coherence decay. Path 2 (Performance, $s \approx 0$): PFC overactivation, shame-cycling, partial and unstable coherence. Path 3 (Surrender, $s = +1$): reduced DMN, parasympathetic elevation, D2 receptor restoration, maximal stable coherence.
        Paul's Philippians 2:12–13 is structurally correct. "Work out your salvation" is the $O$ term. "God who works in you" is the $G$ term. Paul does not choose between them because they are not in competition; they are in multiplication.
        Transitions between pathways are threshold events, not gradual ramps. The attractor basin structure of the equation predicts abrupt transitions, consistent with conversion experience data and clinical addiction recovery literature.
