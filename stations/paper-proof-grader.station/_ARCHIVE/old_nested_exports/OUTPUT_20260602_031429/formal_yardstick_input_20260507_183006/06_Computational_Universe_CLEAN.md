## Overview {#-overview}

The Computational Universe hypothesis suggests that the physical universe is fundamentally computational in nature. Rather than being composed of particles or waves operating according to fixed laws, the universe might itself be understood as a vast computation—a digital information processor evolving according to algorithmic rules.

## Core Idea {#-core-idea}

The hypothesis posits that:
1. Physical reality is substrate-independent (like software on hardware)
2. The universe executes deterministic or probabilistic algorithms
3. Time evolution represents computation steps
4. Physical laws are computational rules
5. Reality is discrete at the fundamental level

## Philosophical Foundations {#-philosophical-foundations}

### Information as Fundamental {#-information-as-fundamental}
- Universe built on information, not matter or energy
- Physical properties emerge from information states
- Computation creates structure from information

### Digital Physics Principle {#-digital-physics-principle}
- The universe is fundamentally discrete and finite
- Continuous mathematics is approximation of discrete processes
- Reversibility implies universe computes without loss

### Planck-Scale Discreteness {#-planck-scale-discreteness}
- Space and time quantized at Planck length (~10^-35 m) and Planck time (~10^-44 s)
- Fundamental discreteness required for consistency
- Emerges naturally from computational framework

## Theoretical Frameworks {#-theoretical-frameworks}

### Cellular Automata Models {#-cellular-automata-models}
- Universe as 3D cellular automaton evolving in time steps
- Local interactions create global behavior
- Conway's Game of Life demonstrates complexity from simple rules

### Rule-Based Systems {#-rule-based-systems}
- Physical laws as rewrite rules on data structures
- Deterministic evolution from initial state
- Examples: Wolfram's cellular automaton universe models

### Quantum Computation Perspective {#-quantum-computation-perspective}
- Quantum superposition as parallel computation
- Quantum decoherence as measurement collapsing possibilities
- Many-worlds interpretation as computational branching

## Mathematical Representation {#-mathematical-representation}

The computational universe can be formalized as:
- **State Space**: All possible configurations of information
- **Transition Rules**: Deterministic or probabilistic evolution
- **Time**: Discrete steps or continuous differential equations
- **Complexity**: Emerges from simple underlying rules

## Key Predictions and Implications {#-key-predictions-and-implications}

### Discreteness {#-discreteness}
- Spacetime fundamentally discrete
- Continuous physics is mathematical approximation
- Quantum field theory emerges from discrete microscopic theory

### Reversibility {#-reversibility}
- Physical processes reversible at fundamental level
- Thermodynamic arrow emerges from coarse-graining
- Information never truly destroyed

### Information Limits {#-information-limits}
- Maximum information storage density bounded
- Holographic principle: surface area limits information
- Computational complexity bounds on physical processes

### Testability {#-testability}
- Small violations of conservation laws possible
- Finite speed of light emerges from computation limits
- Randomness from pseudorandomness in computation

## Connections to Established Physics {#-connections-to-established-physics}

### Quantum Mechanics {#-quantum-mechanics}
- Superposition as concurrent computation
- Entanglement as information correlation
- Wave function as probability distribution over computational states
- Measurement as classical readout of quantum computation

### Relativity {#-relativity}
- Finite speed of light as information propagation limit
- Spacetime curvature as computational complexity variation
- Black holes as information sinks and computation barriers

### Thermodynamics {#-thermodynamics}
- Entropy as information theoretical measure
- Second law emerges from information theory
- Heat as disordered information

### String Theory {#-string-theory}
- Strings as information carriers
- Extra dimensions as computational degrees of freedom
- Duality as computational equivalence

## Evidence and Observations {#-evidence-and-observations}

### Supporting Indicators {#-supporting-indicators}
- Universe's apparent fine-tuning for complex structures
- Information conservation in quantum mechanics
- Success of discrete approximations in physics
- Self-similarity across scales

### Challenges {#-challenges}
- Gap between continuous differential equations and discrete rules
- Difficulty explaining quantum mechanics emergence
- Computational complexity apparently intractable for full simulation

- Digital physics conferences and research

### Technology {#-technology}
- If universe is computable, technology cannot exceed computational bounds
- Suggests limits to computation and energy efficiency

### Metaphysics {#-metaphysics}
- Explains regularity of physical laws
- Addresses fine-tuning questions
- Provides mechanism for physical reality

### Epistemology {#-epistemology}
- Knowledge is discovering computational algorithms
- Scientific understanding equals finding compression algorithms
- Explains success of mathematics in physics

## Open Questions {#-open-questions}

1. What substrate runs the cosmic computation?
2. Why these particular algorithms rather than others?
3. How does consciousness relate to computation?
4. Can the hypothesis be experimentally tested?
5. Does computation precede or follow from mathematics?

## Integration with Theophysics {#-integration-with-theophysics}

The computational universe framework aligns with theophysical principles:
- Information as primary ontology
- Logos as cosmic algorithm/word
- Creation as computation initialization
- Physical law as manifestation of logical structure
- Emergence of complexity from simple deep rules

## Criticisms and Limitations {#-criticisms-and-limitations}

- Untestable in current form
- Doesn't clearly explain quantum indeterminacy
- Requires arbitrary choice of "what computes"
- Doesn't eliminate fine-tuning problem
- May be equivalent to existing physical theories
