# Theophysics Framework: Ontological Rebranding Protocol

## 1. Objective

To create a unique, proprietary lexicon for the Theophysics framework by systematically identifying and redefining key scientific and theological terms. This process aims to avoid the semantic baggage, "bad connotations," and built-in assumptions of existing paradigms, thereby forcing engagement with the framework on its own internally consistent terms. The goal is to build a new vocabulary for a new paradigm.

## 2. Core Principle: The 70-80-90 Rule

The selection of new terms shall be governed by a principle of conceptual correlation:

- **70-80% Correlation (The Sweet Spot):** A new, framework-specific term should be proposed if it shares a 70-80% conceptual overlap with an existing term. This ensures the new term is intelligible and can be mapped to the old concept, but is distinct enough to carry the unique meaning of the Theophysics framework.
- **>90% Correlation (Redundant):** If a potential new term is over 90% correlated with an existing term, it is likely redundant. The existing term may be used, but with a rigorous framework-specific definition.
- **<70% Correlation (Confusing):** If the correlation is too low, the new term will be unintelligible and create confusion.

## 3. Scanning & Redefinition Criteria

The scan of the source texts shall identify terms based on the following criteria:

1.  **Old Paradigm Terms:** Identify standard scientific or theological terms that carry problematic connotations or assumptions that are inconsistent with the Theophysics framework (e.g., "consciousness" implying an emergent property of brains; "measurement problem" implying a problem rather than a feature).
2.  **Inconsistent Usage:** Flag terms used with multiple or ambiguous meanings within the text (e.g., using "entropy" to mean both thermodynamic and moral entropy without a unifying term).
3.  **Bridge Concepts:** Give special priority to terms where the framework unifies previously separate concepts (e.g., the identity of Sin and Entropy). These are the most critical candidates for new, unifying terminology.
4.  **Neologisms & Placeholders:** Identify terms already created for the framework (e.g., "Logos Field") and ensure they are rigorously defined and consistently applied.

## 4. Required Output

The final output should be a comprehensive list structured as follows for each term identified for rebranding:

- **Old Term:** The existing scientific or theological term.
- **Problematic Connotations:** A brief explanation of the semantic baggage the old term carries.
- **Proposed New Term:** The new, framework-specific term.
- **Justification:** An explanation of why the new term is a better fit for the Theophysics framework, aligning with the 70-80% rule.
- **Formal Definition:** A precise, axiomatic definition of the new term as it should be used within the framework.
