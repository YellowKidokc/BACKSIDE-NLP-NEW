# Coherence (C)

Integrated wholeness; the state of unified, non-contradictory alignment across a system.

## Definition

Coherence is the measure of internal consistency and integration within a system. High coherence means all parts work together without contradiction; low coherence indicates fragmentation, conflict, or dissolution. Coherence is both the goal and the metric of Grace's action.

---

## Trinity Connection
**Aspect:** Son (Form/Logos)

The Son is the image of coherence - the perfect integration of divine and human, the Word that holds all things together (Colossians 1:17). Coherence reflects the Son's ordering nature.

---

## Physics <-> Theology Bridge
**Physics side:** Quantum coherence, phase alignment, constructive interference, system integration

**Theology side:** Unity of the body, alignment with divine will, integrity of being, shalom

**The bridge:** Both describe the same reality - parts working in unified harmony rather than fragmented opposition. Physical coherence is the material expression of theological wholeness.

---

## Key Insight

Coherence is measurable. We can quantify it across Scripture, theology, physics, and information theory. This makes theophysics empirical - we can track whether our understanding is becoming more coherent or fragmenting.

---

## Mathematical Expression

$$
C = 1 - \frac{\sum_{i<j} |v_i - v_j|}{N(N-1)/2}
$$

Where C measures alignment across N components, with perfect coherence = 1.
