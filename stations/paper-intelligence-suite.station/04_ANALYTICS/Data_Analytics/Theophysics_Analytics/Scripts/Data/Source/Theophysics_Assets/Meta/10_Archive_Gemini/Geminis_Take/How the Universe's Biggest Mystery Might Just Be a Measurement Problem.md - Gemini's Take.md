The core argument is that the discrepancy in measurements of the universe's expansion rate (H₀) is not due to different expansion rates, but rather to the evolution of the "meter" itself as the universe transitioned from a hot, structureless plasma to its current highly structured state. The paper employs relatable analogies, makes a timely and testable prediction for the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), and explores profound philosophical implications regarding the nature of space, time, and reality as fundamentally relational and information-driven.

### 2. Novelty of the "Ruler Problem" Hypothesis

The "ruler problem" hypothesis is highly novel and offers a compelling, elegant solution to the [[Theophysics_Glossary#Hubble Tension|Hubble Tension]].

*   **Fundamental Reinterpretation:** Instead of seeking new physics to explain a changing expansion rate, the hypothesis reinterprets the problem as a change in the fundamental units of measurement themselves. This is a profound shift in perspective.
*   **"Thermal Rulers vs. Structural Rulers":** The distinction between "thermal meters" (early universe, based on particle bounce distances) and "structural meters" (today's universe, based on atomic bonds, stellar sizes) provides a concrete mechanism for the evolving ruler.
*   **Explains "In-Between" Values:** The hypothesis naturally explains why measurements at medium distances yield "in-between" values, which is a significant strength.
