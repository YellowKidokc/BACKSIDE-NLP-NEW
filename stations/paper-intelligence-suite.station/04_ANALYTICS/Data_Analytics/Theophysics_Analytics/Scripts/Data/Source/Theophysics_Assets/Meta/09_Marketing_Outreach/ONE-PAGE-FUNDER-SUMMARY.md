## THE QUESTION

**Can structured information physically couple to quantum systems?**

The Resonant Coupling Hypothesis (RCH) predicts that information structure—quantified via algorithmic complexity—measurably affects physical observables in proportion to how well the information "matches" a system's internal model. This would represent a fundamental extension of physics with profound implications for consciousness studies, information theory, and the science-religion interface.

---

## THE APPROACH

### Unified Framework
Rather than asking "does scripture do something special?" we ask: **"Do all physical systems respond monotonically to a computable information metric?"**

**Information Resonance Metric (IRM):**
$$
\text{IRM}(s) = \frac{\alpha}{K(s)} \cdot C(s)^\beta
$$
- Low complexity + high coherence → high IRM
- Predicts effect size before testing

### 4-Rung Falsification Ladder

| Rung | Test | Stop Condition |
|------|------|----------------|
| A | Pure math vs. chaos | No monotonic trend → STOP |
| B | Text degradation curve | Hebrew = random → STOP |
| C | System cross-overs | No model-dependence → STOP |
| D | Blinded scripture test | All texts equivalent → STOP |

**The program can fail cleanly at any stage**

## THE EXPERIMENT

### Minimal Viable Experiment (MVE)

**Equipment ($9K):**
- Quantum Random Number Generator (photon-based)
- Magnetic modulation system
- Environmental controls (Faraday cage, temperature)

**Procedure:**
- 1 million test blocks (3 seconds each)
- Input bitstreams of varying complexity
- Measure entropy modulation

**Observable:** Change in QRNG entropy ∝ IRM(input)

**Success Threshold:** 6-sigma effect, Bayes Factor > 10³

### Rigor Safeguards

✅ **Pre-registered:** Protocol & analysis locked before data (Registered Report)  
✅ **Blinded:** Cryptographic commitment, third-party randomization  
✅ **Adversarial:** Skeptic PI + neutral PI co-own equipment  
✅ **Null-tested:** Four independent null models must all be passed  
✅ **Reproducible:** Open hardware/software, automatic publication of results
