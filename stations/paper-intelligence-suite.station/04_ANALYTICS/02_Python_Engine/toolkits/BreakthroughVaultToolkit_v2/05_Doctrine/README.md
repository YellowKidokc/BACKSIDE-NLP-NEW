# 05_Doctrine — Codified Principles & Laws

## Purpose
This is your **canon** — validated, defensible, foundational principles you're willing to stake your reputation on.

## What Goes Here
- **Laws** — repeatable, validated principles
- **Core Equations** — Master Equation, fundamental formulas
- **Doctrines** — theological principles with strong grounding
- **Axioms** — self-evident foundational truths
- **Frameworks** — overarching structures (e.g., Logos Field Theory)

## The Law Standard
A concept becomes a Law when:
- ✅ Grounded in validated atoms/molecules
- ✅ Tested in multiple analyses
- ✅ Has falsification criteria
- ✅ Makes testable predictions
- ✅ Consistent with other Laws
- ✅ Demonstrates Trinity structure
- ✅ Supported by evidence (physical, theological, mathematical)

## Examples
- `LAW 1 — Information Conservation Principle`
- `LAW 2 — Observer-Participant Duality`
- `LAW 3 — Negentropic Grace Function`
- `LAW 5 — Resonant Coupling Hypothesis`

## Workflow
1. Have a strongly validated molecule or analysis
2. Verify it meets Law criteria
3. Create doctrine note
4. Use template: `Ctrl+T` → "05_Doctrine_Template"
5. Write formal statement
6. Document foundation (atoms/molecules/analyses)
7. Define falsification criteria
8. Map Trinity structure
9. Link to papers where it's demonstrated
10. Prepare defense strategy for peer review

## Status Progression
- `proposed` → candidate for Law status
- `validated` → passed internal testing
- `canonical` → published, peer-reviewed, defended
- `challenged` → under dispute (requires re-examination)

## Falsification
Every Law MUST have clear falsification criteria.

If you can't define what would prove it wrong, it's not a Law — it's speculation.

## Priority Levels (1-10)
- **10** — Core framework laws (Master Equation)
- **7-9** — Primary supporting laws
- **4-6** — Secondary principles
- **1-3** — Exploratory doctrines

## Key Principle
**This is what you're willing to defend publicly.**

If you wouldn't stake your academic reputation on it, it stays in `04_Integration/` or `03_Analysis/`.
