Standard scientific method plus a humility prior:

| Q | Question |
|---|---|
| Q0 | **Humility / surrender** — am I open to being wrong here? (not logic — posture) |
| Q1 | What is observed? |

| Q5 | What is the test? |

## 6. Theopoetic Engine — output format for public-facing work

Every public-facing article, GTQ episode, or layperson-pitched piece follows this 5-beat structure:

| Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Human Anchor** | A real, concrete human moment the reader recognizes |
| **Principle Revealed** | The physical/mathematical principle hiding underneath it |
| **Jesus Story** | The gospel narrative that mirrors the principle |
| **Core Insight** | The synthesis — what the three above produce together |
| **Truth Bomb** | One sentence the reader carries away |

### Exemplar

> **Human Anchor:** A man stares at his coffee cooling on the counter. He didn't watch the heat leave, but he knows it left.
>
> **Principle Revealed:** The second law of thermodynamics — entropy rises in any closed system. The cup didn't get cold. It surrendered its order to the room.
>
> **Jesus Story:** "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit." (John 12:24) Christ didn't escape entropy. He entered it. He surrendered to it on purpose.
>
> **Core Insight:** Surrender is not weakness. It's the only mechanism by which order propagates outward instead of decaying inward. Entropy isn't the enemy of meaning — refusing to die is.
>
> **Truth Bomb:** The cup of coffee on the counter and the cross on Golgotha obey the same law. Surrender is the physics of grace.

Technical/academic work uses formal voice instead — Theopoetic format is for public reach.
