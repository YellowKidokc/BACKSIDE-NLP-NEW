Article 01 named the puzzle: Eve ate first and nothing collapsed. Adam ate second and the eyes of both were opened. ### The Signal Strength Problem

Standard quantum mechanics treats observers as interchangeable. A measurement is a measurement. The apparatus does not care who is operating it. But Genesis preserves a sequence that does not behave that way. Genesis 3:6–7 Eve takes and eats. Adam takes and eats. Then the eyes of both are opened.

### Two Observers, Two Fidelity Values

Adam received the command directly from God. Genesis 2:16–17 places the boundary condition before Eve's creation. No intermediary. No transmission loss. The system specification arrives at full fidelity: $I_f = 1$. Eve received the command secondhand. She was not present when the original boundary condition was given. Then the serpent introduced active noise into the channel: Genesis 3:1 , "Did God actually say..." By the time Eve reaches for the fruit, her informational fidelity has been degraded. Eve's interaction was real. It was not system-authorizing. In physics language, Eve's act behaves like a weak measurement: information is exchanged, but the system does not resolve into the final state. Adam's act is the strong measurement. It carries the original specification, federal headship, and full responsibility. When he crosses the threshold, collapse propagates non-locally.
