# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Before Adam ate the forbidden fruit, there was no time as we know it—no before or after, no aging or death. Reality was like a perfect, unchanging quantum state. When Adam took a bite, it caused the first “collapse” from this perfect state into a world where things change, decay, and time flows. This event created time itself, making death and entropy possible. The Bible’s story and modern physics both point to this moment as the start of time. Lifespans recorded in Genesis show a pattern that matches how quantum systems lose their perfect state over time. Even after the Fall and the Flood, God’s grace keeps life going, preventing total decay. The Trinity—Father, Son, and Spirit—are all involved in this first moment, showing that time and redemption are deeply connected.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Wavefunction collapse:** The moment a quantum system changes from many possibilities to one definite reality.  
- **Coherence:** How “perfect” or “ordered” a quantum system is.  
- **Entropy:** The natural tendency for things to become disordered over time.  
- **Decoherence:** The process of losing that perfect order, leading to aging and death.  
- **Trinity:** The Christian belief in one God in three Persons—Father, Son, and Spirit.  
- **Grace:** God’s unearned kindness that sustains life and order.

**What this is really saying:**  
Time, death, and decay didn’t exist before the Fall. The Fall caused the first irreversible change in reality, creating time and making death possible. The Bible’s genealogies reflect this change in human lifespans, which match patterns predicted by quantum physics. God’s grace keeps life from completely falling apart. The Trinity’s involvement shows that time and redemption are part of God’s plan from the start.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- Evidence that time existed before the Fall event.  
- Lifespan data that does not fit the predicted decoherence pattern.  
- Biblical texts showing prophecy or sequence before the Fall.  
- A physical explanation for the Flood’s phase transition that contradicts entropy coupling changes.  
- Demonstration that Genesis genealogies are purely symbolic and not historical.
