# Executive Summary

**One-sentence thesis:**  
The capacity of an observer to cause quantum reality to collapse depends on the quality of information they receive from the source, introducing a threshold of informational fidelity that explains asymmetries in measurement outcomes.

**Executive bullets:**  
- Standard quantum mechanics treats all observers as equal in causing wavefunction collapse, but biblical narrative and theological analysis suggest otherwise.  
- The concept of **Informational Fidelity** measures how cleanly an observer receives the system’s boundary conditions from the source.  
- Adam received the command directly from God with perfect fidelity, triggering collapse; Eve received it secondhand with noise, below the collapse threshold.  
- Collapse is a systemic event affecting the entire entangled subsystem, not just the individual observer.  
- This framework aligns with theological concepts like federal headship and is supported by experimental data showing observer-dependent effects on physical systems.  
- The Fall introduced decoherence (noise) that degraded informational fidelity, preventing some measurements from collapsing reality.  
- Christ’s role is described as restoring the coupling and coherence lost in the Fall, not reversing the collapse but paying its thermodynamic cost.  
- The model challenges the assumption that all conscious observers have equal reality-making authority.

**Why it matters:**  
This framework redefines the role of consciousness and measurement in quantum mechanics, linking physics and theology to explain why identical measurements can yield different outcomes. It suggests reality-making is a delegated authority, not an intrinsic property of all observers, with implications for understanding consciousness, free will, and the nature of reality.

**Key claims:**  
- Informational fidelity is a measurable variable that determines whether a measurement collapses the quantum state.  
- Collapse is triggered only when informational fidelity crosses a threshold.  
- Biblical events reflect this physical principle, with Adam’s direct reception causing collapse and Eve’s indirect, noisy reception failing to do so.  
- Experimental data (PEAR-LAB) supports observer-dependent variations in consciousness’s effect on physical systems.

**Evidence used:**  
- Biblical narrative from Genesis and New Testament passages analyzed through theological and physical lenses.  
- Theoretical framework connecting decoherence, measurement, and informational fidelity.  
- PEAR-LAB experimental results showing statistically significant differences in observer effects on physical systems.

**Risks, gaps, or assumptions:**  
- The framework assumes a direct correspondence between theological concepts and physical measurement processes.  
- Informational fidelity is a theoretical construct not yet fully quantified or experimentally isolated.  
- The interpretation of biblical texts as describing physical phenomena may be contested.  
- The neurochemical and civilizational scale analogies require further empirical support.

**Best next action:**  
Develop experimental protocols to measure informational fidelity in controlled quantum systems and test whether varying the quality of information received by observers affects collapse outcomes. Further interdisciplinary study combining neuroscience, quantum physics, and theology to refine the model and its predictions.
