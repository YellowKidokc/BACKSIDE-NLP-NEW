# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Physics today has two main theories that don’t fit together: one explains big things like planets and gravity (General Relativity), and the other explains tiny things like atoms and particles (Quantum Mechanics). They don’t agree, and scientists have been stuck on this for a hundred years. This article says that’s because the universe was made by a perfect, orderly source called the Logos, but something broke that order. The big stuff still follows the perfect rules, but the tiny stuff is messy and uncertain because it’s like a broken signal. The Bible’s story of the Fall explains this break, and the Cross and Resurrection show how the broken world can be fixed. So, physics isn’t just broken by accident—it’s showing us a deeper spiritual truth.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Logos:** The perfect, rational source of everything, like a divine blueprint.  
- **General Relativity (GR):** The physics of big things, smooth and predictable.  
- **Quantum Mechanics (QM):** The physics of tiny things, uncertain and affected by observation.  
- **Fall:** The event that broke the perfect connection between the spiritual source and the physical world.  
- **Cross and Resurrection:** Unique events that show how the broken world can be healed.

**What this is really saying:**  
The reason physics has two incompatible theories is because the universe itself is split—part of it still follows the original perfect design, and part of it is broken and noisy. This split matches the spiritual story of creation, fall, and redemption. Understanding this helps us see that physics points beyond itself to a spiritual reality.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If physicists successfully unify GR and QM purely through physics without needing an external source, that would challenge this idea.  
- If quantum randomness is shown to be truly fundamental and unaffected by consciousness or spiritual coherence, that would weaken the claim.  
- If the Cross and Resurrection can be fully explained without invoking both physics frameworks or spiritual repair, the argument loses force.
