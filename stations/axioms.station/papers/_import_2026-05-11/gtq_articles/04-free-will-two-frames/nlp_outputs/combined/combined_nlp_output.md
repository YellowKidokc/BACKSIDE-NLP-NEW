- Both Calvinist and Arminian views are correct but describe different transitions: Calvinism the initiation of openness by God, Arminianism the genuine human response once openness exists.

It clarifies how divine sovereignty and human responsibility coexist without contradiction and offers predictive power for spiritual growth and recovery.

## NO-MATH REWRITE

### A Note Before We Begin

For sixteen centuries, Christians have debated whether God’s control or human free will is primary in salvation. This article argues that both views are correct but describe different aspects of the same reality. Like two people standing on opposite sides of a mountain describing different views of the same rock, Calvinists and Arminians see different parts of the spiritual process. The Bible shows both divine sovereignty and human responsibility working together, not in conflict. The key is a mathematical framework—a differential equation—that models how these forces interact continuously since the Fall.

Paul’s words in Philippians 2:12-13 capture this tension perfectly: we are told to “work out your own salvation” while also recognizing “God works in you.” Both happen simultaneously. Calvinists emphasize God’s work; Arminians emphasize human effort. Both are right but incomplete alone. The equation shows both are necessary and operate together.

### The Equation (Explained in Words)

The spiritual state of a person’s coherence with God changes over time based on three factors:

- **Openness (O):** How willing a person is to receive God’s help.  
- **Grace (G):** God’s active help or input that promotes spiritual growth.  
- **Entropy or Sin (S):** The spiritual resistance or decay that pulls a person away from God.

The change in coherence depends on two competing forces:

1. **Growth force:** This increases coherence and requires both openness and grace working together. If either is missing, growth stops. It’s like a radio signal—you need both the broadcast (grace) and the receiver turned on (openness) for the message to get through.

2. **Decay force:** This pulls coherence down, representing sin and resistance. The more coherent you are, the stronger this pull becomes, which explains why spiritual growth often feels like a battle.

If grace is removed, no matter how open you are, your coherence declines. If openness is removed, no matter how much grace is available, coherence also declines. Both are essential and must operate simultaneously.

### The Calvinist-Arminian Dissolution

The debate can be understood by introducing a “surrender parameter” that ranges from fully autonomous (self-directed, no openness) to fully surrendered (maximum openness).  

- When a person is fully autonomous, they cannot open themselves to grace. Only God can initiate this opening. This matches the Calvinist view of irresistible grace.  
- Once the channel is open, the person can respond by choosing to surrender or resist. This matches the Arminian view of genuine human choice.

Thus, Calvinism accurately describes the initial divine initiation of openness, and Arminianism accurately describes the subsequent human response. Both are phases of the same process.

### Free Will Before and After the Fall

Before the Fall, there was no spiritual resistance (entropy). The channel between God and humans was clear, making the right choice obvious though still freely made. After the Fall, entropy introduced noise and interference, making spiritual growth harder but not impossible. The will itself did not break; the environment in which it operates changed.

Paul’s experience in Romans 7—wanting to do good but doing evil—reflects this noisy environment where the will is intact but the channel is corrupted.

### Three Pathways: The Neuroscience Confirms the Equation

The model predicts three distinct modes of moral decision-making, each with unique brain and neurochemical signatures:

1. **Autonomous mode:** No openness, driven by reward circuits and addiction pathways. Spiritual coherence decays.  
2. **Self-righteous performance:** Partial openness but relying on willpower alone, causing stress and exhaustion without lasting growth.  
3. **Surrendered mode:** Full openness, relational brain areas activate, stress decreases, and biological markers of healing appear. Spiritual coherence grows sustainably.

The brain changes are effects of grace working through the body, not the cause of spiritual transformation.

### What the Equation Predicts

- Faith-based recovery programs that engage grace and openness outperform willpower-only programs.  
- The most spiritually coherent people face the strongest temptations because resistance scales with coherence.  
- Sustained spiritual growth requires ongoing openness; a one-time decision is insufficient.  
- Group spiritual coherence exceeds the sum of individuals due to shared coupling with God’s presence, making community essential.

### What You Just Read

Free will is not a new gift from the Fall nor destroyed by it. The same will operates before and after, but the environment changed from perfect clarity to one filled with spiritual noise. The Calvinist-Arminian debate reflects two phases of a single process, not opposing truths. This model integrates theology, physics, psychology, and neuroscience into a coherent framework explaining spiritual growth and struggle.
