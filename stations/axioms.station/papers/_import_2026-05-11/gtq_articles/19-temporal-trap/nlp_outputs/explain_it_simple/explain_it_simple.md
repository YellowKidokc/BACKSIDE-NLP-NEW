# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Satan fell from heaven in a way that changed his location but not his time—he’s stuck in a permanent state. Humans, however, fell in a way that created time itself, with past, present, and future. Satan understands space well but doesn’t fully grasp time. He caused Jesus to be crucified, thinking it was a win for him. But the meaning of the Cross wasn’t clear until Jesus rose from the dead, which changed the whole story backward in time. This is like a science experiment where a future event changes how we understand a past event. The Cross is not just history; it’s happening now, breaking Satan’s trap and offering freedom. All the different ways Christians explain the Cross are just different views of this one big event.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Spatial fall:** Being moved from one place to another permanently.  
- **Temporal fall:** The start of time with before and after, cause and effect.  
- **Retrocausal:** When something in the future changes how we understand something in the past.  
- **Cross:** Jesus’s death on the cross, central to Christian salvation.  
- **Resurrection:** Jesus rising from the dead, which reveals the true meaning of the Cross.  
- **Spirit:** God’s presence that makes the Cross real and active now.  
- **Temptation, despair, accusation, addiction:** Ways evil uses time to trap people.

**What this is really saying:**  
Satan’s biggest mistake was misunderstanding time. He thought killing Jesus would win, but because time works differently in God’s eternal plan, the Cross actually defeats him. Time itself is the battlefield where God’s rescue plan unfolds, and the Cross is the weapon that breaks evil’s hold not just in the past but right now.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If the Cross’s meaning was fixed only at the moment of crucifixion with no retroactive effect.  
- If Satan fully understood time and still lost, undermining the claim of his blindness.  
- If evil strategies worked without relying on time or sequence.  
- If the Resurrection did not change the meaning of the Cross in any way.  
- If the Spirit’s work did not make the Cross present and active now.
