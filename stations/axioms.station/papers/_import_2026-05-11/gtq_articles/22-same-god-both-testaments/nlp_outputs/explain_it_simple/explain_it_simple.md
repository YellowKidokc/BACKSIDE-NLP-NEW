# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
The Bible’s Old and New Testaments show the same God, but He acts differently because He’s working with people who lived in very hard times. Long ago, life was brutal—many children died, diseases were everywhere, and there was no police or hospitals. People even sacrificed their babies because they thought it was the only way to survive. The Old Testament shows God dealing with this tough world, setting rules that were actually kinder than what others had. Over many centuries, God slowly taught people how to love and live better. The New Testament shows the result of that teaching, with Jesus showing perfect love. The hard parts of the Old Testament aren’t God being mean; they’re God working within justice, mercy, and human freedom all at once. The Cross is where all these come together perfectly.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Justice:** Fair consequences for actions, like how sin causes real harm that must be dealt with.  
- **Mercy:** God’s kindness and grace that helps people heal and grow.  
- **Free will:** People’s ability to choose to accept or reject God’s help.  
- **Slavery:** In ancient times, a survival option, not the cruel system we think of today.  
- **Blood feud:** Endless family fights that the law tried to stop by limiting revenge.  
- **Tophet:** A place where people sacrificed babies to false gods, a terrible practice God opposed.  
- **Cities of Refuge:** Safe places where people who accidentally killed someone could be protected until a fair trial.  
- **Progressive revelation:** God teaching people step-by-step over centuries, not all at once.  
- **Cross:** The event where Jesus paid for all sin, showing justice, mercy, and free will together.

**What this is really saying:**  
God didn’t change between the Old and New Testaments. Instead, He worked patiently with people who lived in a harsh world, balancing fairness, kindness, and freedom. The Bible’s tough stories make sense when you see them as part of a long teaching process that leads to Jesus. The Cross is the moment when everything God was doing becomes clear and complete.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If the Old Testament laws and stories were shown to be arbitrary or cruel without any moral or historical context.  
- If justice, mercy, and free will could not coexist logically or practically in the same framework.  
- If the Cross did not embody justice, mercy, and free will simultaneously.  
- If the slow moral progress was due to God changing His mind rather than human readiness.  
- If archaeological or historical evidence contradicted the described Bronze Age conditions or Mosaic legal innovations.
