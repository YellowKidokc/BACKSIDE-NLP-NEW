# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
For over 1600 years, Christians have argued about whether God chooses who will be saved or if people really get to choose for themselves. Both sides have good reasons and Bible verses to support them. The truth is, both are right — but they’re talking about different parts of the same story. First, God makes the first move because people are spiritually "dead" and can’t choose on their own. Then, once God opens the door, people can choose to follow Him or not. This two-step process explains why the Bible seems to say both things at once. A simple math idea shows how God’s grace and our choices work together in order.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Sovereignty:** God is in control and makes the first move.  
- **Free will:** People genuinely choose to follow God after He opens the door.  
- **Dead in sin:** People can’t choose God on their own because they’re spiritually lifeless.  
- **Grace:** God’s help that makes it possible to respond to Him.  
- **Effectual calling:** God’s action that brings a person from spiritual death to life.  
- **Antinomy:** Two truths that seem to contradict but actually don’t.  

**What this is really saying:**  
God starts salvation by giving life to people who can’t help themselves. After that, people really do have a choice to accept or reject God’s gift. Both God’s control and human choice are true, but they happen in order, not at the same time. This explains why the Bible talks about both ideas without contradiction.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If people could choose God before God made the first move, then the idea of being "dead in sin" wouldn’t fit.  
- If God didn’t actually open the door first, then people’s choice wouldn’t be real or possible.  
- If the Bible’s verses about God’s election and human choice couldn’t be separated into phases, the two-phase idea would fail.  
- If salvation were only one phase, either God’s choice or human choice, the tension would remain unresolved.
