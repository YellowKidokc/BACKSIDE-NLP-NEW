# Executive Summary

**One-sentence thesis:**  
The recurring death-resurrection-salvation pattern across cultures is not mere mythological noise but a real signal rooted in physical and theological reality, which, when properly accounted for, significantly raises the probability that Jesus of Nazareth was a historical figure.

**5-8 executive bullets:**  
- Richard Carrier’s Bayesian argument for Jesus’s non-historicity hinges on a reference class that treats the death-resurrection-salvation pattern as fabricated myth.  
- The Projection Principle argues that this pattern is a real physical and metaphysical signature projected by the Logos, not a cultural invention.  
- If the pattern is real, Carrier’s reference class is contaminated by signal, invalidating his low prior probability for Jesus’s historicity.  
- Correcting the prior to reflect a reference class of historically attested religious founders with early independent sources raises the prior probability substantially.  
- Independent attestation, hostile sources, and the rapid growth of Christianity uniquely mark Jesus as the source event for the pattern.  
- Experimental evidence from consciousness research (PEAR-LAB, Global Consciousness Project) supports the physical mechanism underlying resurrection as a coherence-restoration event.  
- Adjusting Carrier’s Bayesian inputs with these corrections yields a posterior probability of Jesus’s historicity well above 50%, potentially as high as 97%.  
- The argument depends critically on the Projection Principle and the interpretation of the pattern as signal, not noise.

**Why it matters:**  
This challenges a leading mythicist argument by showing that the pattern shared by Jesus and other savior figures is not evidence of fabrication but evidence of a real metaphysical and physical phenomenon. This reframes the historicity debate and integrates theology with physics and consciousness research.

**Key claims:**  
- Carrier’s reference class assumption is flawed because it treats a real signal as noise.  
- The death-resurrection-salvation pattern is a universal physical-metaphysical signature (Projection Principle).  
- Jesus uniquely meets multiple historical criteria that identify him as the source event behind the pattern.  
- Consciousness coupling experiments provide a plausible physical mechanism for resurrection.  
- Correcting the Bayesian framework accordingly reverses Carrier’s conclusion.

- Historical markers: early independent attestation, hostile sources, movement growth.

**Risks, gaps, or assumptions:**

- The argument depends on the uniqueness of Jesus’s evidentiary markers; discovery of comparable markers for others would weaken it.
