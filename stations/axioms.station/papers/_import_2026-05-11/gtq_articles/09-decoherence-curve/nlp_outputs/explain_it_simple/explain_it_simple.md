# Explain It Simple

**The 2-minute version:**  
Long ago, people lived for hundreds of years. Then, after a big event called the Flood, lifespans started dropping quickly but slowed down and stopped dropping at about 90 years. This pattern matches a special kind of decay seen in physics called “quantum decoherence,” where things lose their special qualities over time but never completely disappear. The Bible even says people live about 70 to 80 years now, which fits this pattern. One person named Eber lived longer than expected, which fits with his special role in the Bible. Today, even with medicine, people still mostly live near that lower limit. This suggests there’s a kind of “grace” that keeps life from getting worse, and that the Flood changed how life works in a big way.

**Key terms in plain language:**  
- **Decoherence:** A process where something special slowly fades away but never fully disappears.  
- **Phase transition:** A sudden big change, like water freezing or melting.  
- **Lifespan floor:** The lowest average age people tend to live to, which doesn’t go lower.  
- **Grace:** A sustaining power that keeps life going even when things get worse.  
- **Covenant:** A special relationship or promise that can affect how long someone lives.

**What this is really saying:**  
The Bible’s record of how long people lived isn’t random or made up. It follows a clear pattern that matches a known physical process. After the Flood, something changed that made lifespans drop quickly but then stop dropping. This stopping point matches what the Bible and modern science say about how long people live. This suggests that life is kept going by a kind of grace that can’t be measured by just biology or medicine.

**What would prove it wrong, if present:**  
- If lifespans after the Flood didn’t follow this clear pattern or if the data were random.  
- If a better mathematical model explained the data without needing a grace floor.  
- If modern lifespans could be pushed well beyond 120 years by medicine or technology.  
- If the Flood didn’t correspond to any sudden change in lifespan patterns.  
- If Eber’s longer life was just random and not connected to covenant identity.
